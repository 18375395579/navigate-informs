Title: A Model for Optimal Allocation of HIV Prevention and Treatment Funds

Author: Margaret Brandeau, Stanford University, Huang Engineering Center, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, brandeau@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Sabina Alistar, Elisa Long, Eduard Beck

Year: 2012

Abstract: We develop a model to determine the optimal allocation of resources between HIV prevention and treatment services to minimize the basic reproduction number. Our model includes multiple populations with different transmission modes, as well as production functions that relate investment in prevention and treatment to changes in transmission and treatment rates. We apply our model to examine HIV epidemic control in two different settings, Uganda and Russia.
