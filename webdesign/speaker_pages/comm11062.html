Title: Optimizing Provider Recruitment for Public Health Surveillance Networks

Author: Samuel Scarpino, Santa Fe Institute, 1399 Hyde Park Road, Santa Fe, NM, United States of America, scarpino@utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Lauren Ancel Meyers, Nedialko Dimitrov

Year: 2013

Abstract: Public health agencies use surveillance systems to detect and monitor diseases. These systems often rely on data sources that are chosen based on loose guidelines or out of convenience. Here, we present a new, data-driven method for designing surveillance systems. Our approach is a geographic optimization of data sources designed to achieve specific surveillance goals. The resulting networks outperformed existing systems when predicting seasonal and pandemic influenza hospitalizations.
