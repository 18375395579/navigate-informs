Title: A Regression Tree Approach for Assessing Perceived Usefulness of Computerized Clinical Reminders

Author: Sze-jung Sandra Wu, Graduate Assistant, Purdue University, School of Industrial Engineering, 315 N Grant Street, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States, swu@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Lehto, Bradley Doebbeling, Yuehwern Yih

Year: 2007

Abstract: Computerized clinical reminders (CCRs) are useful tools for alerting healthcare providers of upcoming or overdue events. We conducted a cross-sectional survey of CCRs users from 104 different VA facilities using a 5-point Likert scale. The survey data were analyzed using a cross-validated regression tree algorithm to measure the factors affecting clinician’s perceived usefulness of CCRs.
