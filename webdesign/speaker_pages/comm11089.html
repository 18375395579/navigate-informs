Title: A Dynamic Infrastructure Performance Model for Evaluating Maintenance Effectiveness and Poolability

Author: Chih-Yuan Chu, Student, Northwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States, jameschu@northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Pablo L. Durango-Cohen

Year: 2006

Abstract: The paper uses state-space specifications of time series and develops dynamic, stochastic, multivariate infrastructure performance models that capture the effectiveness of maintenance activities, exogenous factors and uncertainty. A fully and a partially aggregated specifications are proposed. Their predictive power and poolability of data are evaluated by a benchmark model.
