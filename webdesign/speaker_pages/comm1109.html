Title: On Simpler Procedures for Multi-attribute Choice

Author: Paula Sarabando, Escola Superior de Tecnologia de Viseu, Dep. Matemática Campus Politécnico, Viseu, 3504-510, Portugal, psarabando@mat.estv.ipv.pt

Coauthor(s): Luis Dias

Year: 2007

Abstract: Building multiattribute value functions requires translating the preferences of a Decision Maker into precise numbers, which is often problematical even in the additive case. Hence, procedures such as SMART and Even Swaps have been proposed. We discuss elicitation of information which, albeit not precise, may facilitate the cognitive burden on the analysis. The incomplete information can then be used to provide guidance on the best choice using tools like simulation or mathematical programming.
