Title: Optimal Policy for Plug-In Hybrid Electric Vehicles Adoption

Author: Emrah Ozkaya, Purdue University, 315 N. Grant Street, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States of America, eozkaya@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Preckel, Andrew Liu

Year: 2013

Abstract: This study focuses on the dynamics between adoption of Plug-In Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) and government policies. To find an optimal policy to promote such adoption, we build a discrete choice model to represent consumers?behavior in choosing a conventional vehicle versus a PHEV. To further capture the interaction between the transportation and energy sector (fuel, electricity, etc), we propose an iterative approach to integrate our model with an energy-system model (MARKAL).
