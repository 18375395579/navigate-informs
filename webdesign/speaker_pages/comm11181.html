Title: Consistency-seeking for Behavior-based Traffic Routing Strategies

Author: Alexander Paz, Senior Professional, Cambridge Systematics, 218-15 Nimitz Drive, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States, apaz@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Srinivas Peeta

Year: 2007

Abstract: A fuzzy control framework is used to enhance, based on real-time data measurements, the consistency of a controller-estimated driver behavior model which is a key component in the determination of information-based traffic routing strategies. Enhancing the consistency of the controller-estimated driver behavior model results in more consistent/reliable information-based traffic routing strategies.
