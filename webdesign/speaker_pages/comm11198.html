Title: The Relationship between Workplace Practices and Mortality and Health Costs in the United States
Author: Joel Goh, Stanford University, Faculty Building East E105, 655 Knight Way, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, joelgoh@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Jeffrey Pfeffer, Stefanos Zenios
Year: 2013
Abstract: Empirical evidence has linked workplace practices to health outcomes, but the aggregate contribution of these factors to mortality and health spending in the U.S. is not known. We use a model to estimate the excess mortality and health spending associated with workplace exposures. Our model was designed to overcome inherent data limitations. We find that 120,000 deaths and 5-8% of healthcare costs per year are associated with how U.S. companies manage their work force.
