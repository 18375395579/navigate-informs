Title: Challenges of Using a Client’s Data Set: A Skill Students Were Not Taught

Author: Kenneth Chelst, Wayne State University, Detroit, MI, United States of America, kchelst@wayne.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Students typically learn data analysis with high quality data sets. In the real world obtaining data from a client and then processing it is fraught with many pitfalls. We describe our experiences in guiding students in a) identifying the available data, b) requesting the data, c) assessing the quality and completeness of the data d) linking together multiple data files, e) submitting a revised request for data and e) dealing with missing data, inaccurate data, and outliers. This iterative process usually takes months.
