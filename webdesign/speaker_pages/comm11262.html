Title: Jumps in Equity Returns before and after the Financial Crisis
Author: Haowen Zhong, Columbia University, Room 350, Mudd Building, New York, United States of America, hz2193@columbia.edu
Coauthor(s): Steven Kou, Cindy Yu
Year: 2013
Abstract: We find that a simple AJD model fits equity daily return data well; the model outperforms models with Levy jumps during the crisis, and is at least as good before the crisis. Based on the model and the data sets, we conclude that: (i) Jump rates increased significantly during the financial crisis, while there is little evidence that jump sizes have changed. (ii) The empirical evidence favors finite number of large jumps in equity returns.
