Title: A New Model for Incorporating Interfraction Motion in Radiation Therapy Treatment Plan Optimization
Author: Chunhua Men, Ph.D Student, University of Florida, Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering, 303 Weil Hall, Gainesville, FL, 32611-6595, United States, chhmen@ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Edwin Romeijn, James Dempsey
Year: 2007
Abstract: We present a new stochastic programming model for IMRT treatment plan optimization to account for the interfraction motion due to set-up errors. In contrast with the traditional approaches, which employ a margin around the clinical target, our optimization is based on a finite number of generated scenarios, where each daily treatment (fraction) can be viewed as a realization. We test the efficacy and efficiency of our model, as well as the effect of the number of scenarios, using clinical data.
