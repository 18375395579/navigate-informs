Title: Forecasting Demand in Hotel Food and Beverage Outlets

Author: Gary Thompson, Professor, Cornell University, 352 Statler Hall, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, gmt1@cornell.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We look at whether lodging properties can use occupancy data to improve demand forecast accuracy in their food and beverage outlets, using 4 hotels with a total of 41 outlet & day part combinations. We examine 27 forecasting methods, which we test using an 8 week holdback. In 34 cases, the best forecast originated with one of the models incorporating occupancy, and the accuracy improved over 14% on average. Our findings will be of interest to food and beverage managers in lodging properties.
