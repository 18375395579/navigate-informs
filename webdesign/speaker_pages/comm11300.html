Title: Optimization to Select Green Building Options

Author: John F Dickson, University of Texas at Arlington, 2420 S Fielder rd, ARLINGTON, TX, 76015, United States of America, john.dickson@mavs.uta.edu

Coauthor(s): Victoria C Chen, Jay Rosenberger, Anthony Robinson, Pin Kung

Year: 2013

Abstract: A mixed integer linear programming (MILP) model is presented to optimize green building design over multiple performance objectives. Performance objectives are predicted via treed regression models developed using design and analysis of computer experiments with eQUEST software as the computer experiment. Results of single story building case study were studied. Future study includes sequential design of experiments to minimize the computer runs and improve the optimization.
