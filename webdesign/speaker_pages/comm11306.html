Title: Choosing a Good Probability Estimation Method for Classification-based Collaborative Filtering

Author: Jong-Seok Lee, Assistant Professor, Department of Systems Management Engineering, Sungkyunkwan University, 2066 Seoburo Jangangu, Suwon, Korea, Republic of, jongseok@skku.edu

Coauthor(s): Wook-Yeon Hwang, Jungmin Lee

Year: 2013

Abstract: Classification-based collaborative filtering is a product recommendation scheme, which performs well for the case of utilizing market basket data in the form of binary user-item matrix. Since predicted purchasing probabilities affect quality of recommendations, it is required to use a good probability estimation method. In this research, we propose to employ random forest regression that was shown in our experiments to perform well and be robust to high-dimensionality and cold-starting problem.
