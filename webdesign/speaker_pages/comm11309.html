Title: Process Monitoring of Ultra Precision Machining (UPM) using Evidence Theory based Sensor Fusion

Author: Omer Beyca, Ph.D. Student/Research Associate, Oklahoma State University, 322 Engineering North, Stillwater, OK, 74074, United States of America, omer.beyca@okstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Zhenyu (James) Kong, Satish Bukkapatnam

Year: 2013

Abstract: Surface integrity is vital for Ultra Precision Machining (UPM) processes. Therefore monitoring the UPM process is crucial to detect the process anomalies at their early stages, and thus, to improve surface integrity. In this study we developed a Dirichlet process based sensor fusion method for UPM process monitoring. The case study demonstrated its effectiveness in capturing process variability in the UPM.
