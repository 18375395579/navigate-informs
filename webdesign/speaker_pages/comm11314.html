Title: A Quasi Experimental Study on the Effects of Website Interactivity on Online Shopping Behavior
Author: Hafizul Islam, PhD Student, University of Melbourne, PhD Student, MBS, 200 Leicester Street, Carlton, Vi, 3056, Australia, h.islam@mbs.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: Multimedia rich online retail websites can influence online consumers shopping motivations and browsing behavior through sophisticated and aesthetic levels of interactive features. This study proposes an attitudinal framework based on the Technology Acceptance Model and uses a quasi-experimental analysis to explore the effects of design characteristics of interactive online shopping sites on online consumer’s purchase intentions.
