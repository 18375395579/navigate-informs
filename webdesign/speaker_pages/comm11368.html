Title: Initial Attack Dispatching Configurations: Standard Responses vs. Perfect Knowledge Solutions

Author: Yu Wei, Colorado State University, Boulder, CO, United States of America, wei@cnr.colostate.edu

Coauthor(s): Erin Belval

Year: 2013

Abstract: A series of complex decisions need to be made at a dispatch center upon the occurrence of one or more fires. Pre-designed standard response rules help shorten response reaction time by providing intuitive dispatch guidelines based on fire weather, ignition locations and local experience. Stochastic optimization models provide a systematic and consistent way to evaluate existing standard response rules, compare it with perfect knowledge solutions, or to generate new and more efficient rules under possible fire scenarios. This study explores new knowledge in designing and implementing initial attack response rules.
