Title: Sales Forecasting with Financial Indicators and Experts' Input
Author: Vishal Gaur, Associate Professor, Cornell University, Sage Hall, The Johnson School, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, vg77@cornell.edu
Coauthor(s): Nikolay Osadchiy, Sridhar Seshadri
Year: 2008
Abstract: The volume of retail sales is commonly understood to be correlated with the state of the economy. This information could be employed in aggregate forecasting, inventory decisions, and risk management. We present a model in which total sales is a function of sales forecast, forecast term, and financial market return over the forecast term. We show results using panel data of sales forecast evolution for 214 retailers over 10 years, each year containing many forecasts of varying terms.
