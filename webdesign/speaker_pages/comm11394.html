Title: Economics of Free under Perpetual Licensing: Implications for the Software Industry

Author: Marius Florin Niculescu, Georgia Institute of Technology, Scheller College of Business, 800 West Peachtree St. NW, Atlanta, GA, 30308, United States of America, Marius.Niculescu@scheller.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): DJ Wu

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this paper, we explore the economics of free under perpetual licensing for modular software. We focus on two emerging software business models: feature-limited freemium and seeding. We benchmark their performance against a conventional business model under which software is sold without free offers. Our model accounts for WOM effects and cross-module synergies. Our analysis uncovers conditions under which freemium and seeding are the dominating models under both constant and dynamic pricing.
