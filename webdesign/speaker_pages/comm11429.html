Title: Biomechanics Data Mining to Support Human Performance and Injury Prevention

Author: Kaizhi Tang, Research Scientist, Intelligent Automation, Inc., 15400 Calhoun Dr., Suite 400, Rockville, MD, 20850, United States of America, ktang@i-a-i.com

Coauthor(s): Atul Phadke

Year: 2007

Abstract: Biomechanics with its experimental, simulation and domain rules provides an excellent arena for data mining applications. The unique characteristics of biomechanics datasets including isolated feature space and multiple dependent variables call for innovative data mining solutions. This paper presents the research efforts of Intelligent Automation Inc. on biomechanics data mining, especially summarizes how the unique characteristic and requirements in biomechanics data mining are handled.
