Title: Optimizing Contact Tracing for a Pertussis Outbreak in a Local Health Department
Author: Emine Yaylali, North Carolina State University, 375 Daniels Hall, Raleigh, NC, 27695, United States of America, eyaylal@ncsu.edu
Coauthor(s): Julie S. Ivy, Reha Uzsoy
Year: 2012
Abstract: We developed a discrete-event simulation model of a local health department and its response to a pertussis outbreak. Contact tracing, a key mitigation method for pertussis, is modeled using the NC DHHS case data. The impact of the time to initiate response and the resource availability of the health department on outbreak management policies are explored and optimal response policies are developed.
