Title: An International Supply Chain Network Design Model for Rafael-de-Mexico

Author: Gopalakrishnan Easwaran, Associate Professor, St. Mary's University, One Camino Santa Maria, MS 34, Engineering Dept, St. Mary's Univ, San Antonio, TX, 78228-8534, United States of America, geaswaran@stmarytx.edu

Coauthor(s): Archana Shankarnarayanan, Nestor Moreno, Alejandra Mireles

Year: 2013

Abstract: Considering an international business expansion problem for a small scale, home décor products fabrication firm located in Mexico, namely, Rafael-de-Mexico, we develop and solve a comprehensive supply chain network design model for maximizing the company’s return on investment. The model includes explicit consideration of constraints pertaining to procurement, aggregate planning, international shipments, warehousing, resource planning, accounting regulations, sales, and corporate finance.
