Title: Making High Quality Clinical Decisions: Influence Diagrams for Cost-effectiveness Analysis

Author: Ram Duriseti, Clinical Assistant Professor, Stanford University, 1890 Oak Knoll Lane, Menlo Park, CA, 94025, United States, ram1@stanford.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Medical decisions involve the interplay of many probabilistic variables, and decision alternatives with disparate outcomes, making model design and implementation difficult. Structural conditions on the ID related to d-separation can determine if a variable's presence or absence will impact the scope and/or the normative results of the model. We demonstrate the impact of these conditions by constructing two versions of a decision model for the diagnosis of pulmonary embolism.
