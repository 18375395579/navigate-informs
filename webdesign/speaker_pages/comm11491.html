Title: Model-based Correction of Overprecision Bias in Subjective Confidence Intervals

Author: Venkat Prava, Johns Hopkins University, 3400 N. Charles Street, 313 Ames Hall, Baltimore, MD, 21218, United States of America, avadhanulu@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Robert Clemen, Benjamin Hobbs, Melissa A. Kenney

Year: 2012

Abstract: Elicited confidence intervals tend to be overly narrow. We propose a model to correct this bias based on the normal and bi-normal distributions. Model parameters are estimated by adjusting expert responses using a sufficient number of questions with known answers. We apply these models to datasets of expert elicitations, testing model validity with holdout analysis and quantile-quantile plots. The bi-normal model fits better, indicating an asymmetric overprecision bias.
