Title: Modeling the Performance of the Low Latency Queueing Router Discipline

Author: Martin Fischer, Senior Fellow, Noblis, 3150 Fairview Park Drive South, Falls Church, VA, 22042, United States of America, mfischer@noblis.org

Coauthor(s): John Shortle, Denise Masi

Year: 2008

Abstract: Low Latency Queueing (LLQ) is an Internet Protocol (IP) router discipline that is being used to ensure that performance-sensitive high priority traffic receives a high level of performance, while allowing less performance-sensitive traffic to receive some portion of the bandwidth. We present a discussion of our experiences in modeling the performance of this system. The discussion involves the roles analytics, simulation, and the use of approximations have had in this development.
