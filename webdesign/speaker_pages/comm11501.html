Title: A Dynamic Model for the Mitigation of Transmission Failure Risk

Author: Johannes Enders, Ph.D Candidate, Princeton University, Engineering Quadrangle, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States, jenders@princeton.edu

Coauthor(s): Warren Powell

Year: 2007

Abstract: Catastrophic failures of high-voltage transformers constitute an increasingly important operational risk as failures adversely affect system economics and reliability. We present a model that supports risk mitigation efforts by determining optimal policies for transformer purchasing and replacement. The model supports the capital investment planning performed by RTOs and transmission owners. The problem presents methodological challenges that we address with approximate dynamic programming.
