Title: An Approximate Dynamic Programming Approach to Developing Strategies for a Multi-species Stand

Author: Jules Comeau, Dalhousie University, 37 Braemar Dr., Moncton, NB, E1A3S7, Canada, jcomeau5@dal.ca

Coauthor(s): Eldon Gunn

Year: 2007

Abstract: In forest stand optimization, most whole stand models use multi-dimensional state descriptors. The discretization of this state space becomes a major issue when using dynamic programming (DP) in developing optimal harvest policies. This paper introduces the application of a modern dynamic programming approach in developing multi-species, even-aged decision policies in a stochastic environment.
