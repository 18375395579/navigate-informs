Title: Tests of Approximations to Unit Commitment in Planning Models

Author: Saamrat Kasina, Student, The Johns Hopkins University, 3400 N Charles Street, Ames 319, Baltimore, MD, 21218, United States of America, bkasina1@jhu.edu

Coauthor(s): Sonja Wogrin, Ben Hobbs

Year: 2013

Abstract: Planning models must consider the impacts of generator and transmission investments upon operating costs. It is often impractical to solve full unit commitment models as subproblems. We ask what approximations to the unit commitment problem make it computationally efficient while still accurately characterizing energy and operating reserve prices and total costs. Numerical test cases are used to compare approximations and full commitment models.
