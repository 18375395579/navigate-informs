Title: Using Cumulative Propagation to Schedule Resources on Projects with Release Dates to Pick-up Goods
Author: Thiago Serra, Operations Research Analyst, PETROBRAS, Avenida Paulista, 901, São Paulo, SP, 01311-100, Brazil, thiago.serra@petrobras.com.br
Coauthor(s): Fernando J. M. Marcellino, Gilberto Nishioka
Year: 2012
Abstract: The development of constraint-based scheduling solvers has been biased towards the satisfaction of the global constraint cumulative. Hence, a successful use of them to tackle many problems depends on leveraging such constraint in the model. In this work, we consider the scheduling of vessels to convey pipes that will connect offshore oil wells to producing units. To guarantee the loading of pipes by activities scheduled after their release, a qualitative property is modeled with such constraint.
