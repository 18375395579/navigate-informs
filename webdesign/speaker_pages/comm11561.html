Title: Advanced Modeling Techniques in Python

Author: William E. Hart, Sandia National Laboratories, P.O. Box 5800, Albuquerque, NM, 87185, United States of America, wehart@sandia.gov

Coauthor(s): Jean-Paul Watson, Carl Laird, John D. Siirola, David L. Woodruff

Year: 2012

Abstract: Recently, a variety of algebraic modeling tools have been created within high-level programming languages, including C++, Java, Python and Ruby. We have previously argued that this allows modelers to leverage sophisticated language constructs and standard libraries, and that this enables a more expressive modeling environment. But is this claim true? This presentation critiques examples of modeling with the Pyomo software, which is built in Python.
