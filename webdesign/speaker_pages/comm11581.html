Title: NLP Approaches for Estimation of Seasonal Transmission Parameters in Childhood Infectious Diseases

Author: Carl Laird, Professor, Texas A&M University, 3122 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843, United States of America, Carl.Laird@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Daniel Word, Derek A.T. Cummings

Year: 2013

Abstract: Childhood infectious diseases remain a serious public health issue. Efficient estimation techniques are required to understand temporal dynamics and develop effective control strategies. In this work, we use nonlinear interior-point methods to estimate seasonal parameters in infectious disease models using measles case count data. Our results show seasonal patterns that are strongly correlated with school term holidays across very different social settings and holiday schedules.
