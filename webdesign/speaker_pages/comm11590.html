Title: Implementing ARFORGEN: Installation Capability and Feasibility Study of Meeting ARFORGEN Unit Reset Guidelines

Author: Steven Wilcox, Senior Operations Research Analyst, Serco-NA, 1818 Library Street, Suite 1000, Reston, VA, 22090, United States of America, steven.wilcox@serco-na.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: The Army’s new Army Force Generation (ARFORGEN) model promotes decentralized decision-making and redefined readiness, based on a brigade-based structure. These smaller units are designed to be agile, expeditionary, tailored to specific circumstances (e.g., nature of threat, climate, terrain, etc.), and capable of rapid assembly. This study, completed in July 2007, based on a discrete event simulation model of the unit reset process and installation resource constratins, identified adjustments to resources and/or procedures that appear to be necessary for units to meet ARFORGEN guidelines.
