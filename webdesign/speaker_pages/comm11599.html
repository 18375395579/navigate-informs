Title: Modeling Location Choices for Discretionary Activities using Large-scale Data Collected from Online Social Media
Author: Samiul Hasan, Purdue University, 550 Stadium Mall Drive, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, samiul@purdue.edu
Coauthor(s): Satish Ukkusuri
Year: 2012
Abstract: A significant amount of traffic is generated due to the non-work related discretionary activities such as eating out, shopping, recreational, social visits, and so forth. Realistic behavioral models of individual location choices for these secondary activities have great importance in activity-based travel demand analysis. However, large-scale empirical observations for secondary activity location choices are not very common. In this work we develop a location choice model for discretionary activities using a large collection of check-in information shared in online social media.
