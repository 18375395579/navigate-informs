Title: Market Engineering and Agent-based Simulation
Author: Clemens van Dinther, Department Manager, Forschungszentrum, Informatik an der Universität Karlsruhe, Haid-und-Neu-Str. 10-14, 76131, Karlsruhe, Germany, vanDinther@fzi.de
Coauthor(s): Christof Weinhardt
Year: 2007
Abstract: Bidders normally are not perfectly informed. Rather, people acquire relevant information over time. The problem of uncertainty about one’s own valuation is modeled as a Markov Game and studied with an agent-based model using reinforcement learning. Different settings have been studied in order to understand changes in the information cost structure, the participants structure (number and type), and institutional rules. The data is analyzed regarding 16 hypotheses that were derived from 5 general research questions and propositions from literature.
