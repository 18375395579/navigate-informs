Title: Application of Uncertainty Models to Evaluate Laboratory Assay Calibration Protocols

Author: Varun Ramamohan, Purdue University, 315 N Grant St, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, vramamoh@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): George Klee, Yuehwern Yih, James Abbott

Year: 2012

Abstract: Laboratory protocols used to calibrate clinical chemistry systems affect test result quality. System uncertainty models are used to evaluate such protocols: calibration using mean of replicate measurements; calibration based on acceptance when quality control specimens were within one standard deviation of target. The effect of calibrator concentrations on assay uncertainty was studied, and calibrator concentrations minimizing uncertainty at specific concentration levels were identified.
