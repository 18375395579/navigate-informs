Title: Ranking Pandemic Interventions via Multilevel Sensitivity Analysis of Model Results
Author: Patrick Finley, Complex Systems Engineer, Sandia National Laboratories, PO Box 5800 MS 1198, Albuquerque, NM, 87110, United States of America, pdfinle@sandia.gov
Coauthor(s): Thomas Moore, Greg Lambert, Leland Evans, Richard Martinello, Carter Mecher, Robert Glass, Victoria Davey
Year: 2013
Abstract: Multilevel sensitivity analyses (SA) of pandemic disease models are used to rank potential mitigation strategies. Novel SA methods based on weighted Levenschtein-edit distances, penalized multinomial logistic regression, and stochastic dominance of Gaussian process surfaces produce mitigation effectiveness rankings which vary in response to demographic and contact-network parameters. If the ranking criterion is changed from total infected to $/QALY, intervention strategy rankings also change.
