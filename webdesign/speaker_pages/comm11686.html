Title: The Credits that Count: Understanding the Drivers of Higher Education Credit Exposure and Defaults

Author: Katharina Ley Best, PhDCandidate, University of Michigan Industrial and Operations Engineering, 1205 Beal Avenue, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, katley@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Jussi Keppo

Year: 2012

Abstract: Using a two-stage least squares model, we build a macroeconomic model of supply and demand for US higher education. We find that credit factors (student loan amounts and household debt) are primary drivers of demand. Further, we construct a hazard-based model of student loan defaults to estimate default probabilities for different types of students. We account for frailty correlation due to unobserved factors, macroeconomic conditions, and individual student characteristics.
