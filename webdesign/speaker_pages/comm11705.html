Title: Quantification of the Benefits of Real-time Information for Individual Travelers

Author: Dongyoon Song, NEXTRANS Center, USDOT Region V Regional UTC, School of Civil Engineering, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, song50@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Srinivas Peeta

Year: 2012

Abstract: This study seeks to quantify the benefits of real-time travel information in terms of both conventional travel time savings as well as psychological effects for individual travelers. Revealed preference (RP) data from an experiment with a driving simulator and stated preference (SP) data from a survey will be utilized in behavioral analysis to estimate the value of the real-time information.
