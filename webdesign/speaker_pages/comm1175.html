Title: Flight Cancellation Behavior and Delay Savings Estimates

Author: Michael Seelhorst, University of California Berkeley, 1710 Delaware St., Berkeley, CA, 94703, United States of America, mseelhorst@berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Hansen

Year: 2012

Abstract: A discrete choice model was used to infer airline flight cancellation preferences from a sample of on-time performance flight data. The predicted cancellation probabilities were then used as inputs to delay savings estimates, which were approximated with a continuous technique. The results from this work will be useful for incorporating flight cancellation decisions into system-wide delay forecasting models.
