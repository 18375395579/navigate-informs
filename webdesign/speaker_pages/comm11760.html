Title: Optimal Sample Size Allocation for Multi-stress Tests Using Extreme Value Regression

Author: Hon Keung Tony Ng, Assistant Professor, Southern Methodist University, Department of Statistical Science, 3225 Daniel Avenue, Dallas, TX, 75275, United States, ngh@mail.smu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ping Shing Chan, N. Balakrishnan

Year: 2007

Abstract: The optimal sample size allocation in a multi-group life-testing experiment with complete or Type-II censored samples when the extreme value regression model is used for statistical analysis will be discussed. We will derive the maximum likelihood estimators (MLEs), expected Fisher information and the asymptotic variance-covariance matrix of the MLEs. Three optimality criteria and different settings will be introduced and some numerical results of the optimality allocations will be presented.
