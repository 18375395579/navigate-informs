Title: Developing Measures of North American Container Ports Productivity: Using DEA and FDH Approaches

Author: Evangelos Kaisar, University of Maryland, 1173 Glen L. Martin Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, kaisar@wam.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Ali Haghani, Somchai Pathomsiri

Year: 2006

Abstract: Measuring port productivity is an interesting issue, especially if an automated system across ports is required. Amongst other methods, the efficiency of port production can potentially be analyzed by Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) or by the Free Disposal Hull (FDH) model. This paper aims to evaluate the efficiency of major North American ports using DEA and FDH models. The results show that the above two techniques lead to different conclusions.
