Title: Continuously Monitoring a Fraction in Service Processes and Achieving the Nominal False Alarm Rate

Author: Rodrigo Duran, Teaching Assistant, Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering, Rutgers University, 173 Clinton St., South Bound Brook, NJ, 08880, United States of America, rodduran@eden.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Susan Albin

Year: 2008

Abstract: In service processes where we continuously monitor fractions, traditional p-charts may not be useful. The fraction may be anywhere between 0 and 1 and rules of thumb such as Np>5 may not be helpful. We propose a Cusum chart where the nominal false alarm rate is achieved, and the parameters are calculated easily (without tables, simulation, or Markov Chain analysis). The new method also has a superior sensitivity compared with other easily designed existing methods.
