Title: Adaptive Design for PHEV Electric Charging Stations: Considering Impact of Demand on Service Level

Author: Amirhossein Khosrojerdi, The University of Oklahoma, 202 W. Boyd St., Room 124, Norman, OK, United States of America, akhosrojerdi@ou.edu

Coauthor(s): Janet Allen, Farrokh Mistree, Shabnam Rezapoor, Paul Hoang

Year: 2013

Abstract: We propose a two-step method to design an adaptive system for electric charging stations. First, a multi-attribute regression model based on a discrete-event simulation is developed to model the impact of PHEV demand on the service level of charging stations. Second, a multi-objective, mixed-integer, non-linear model is formulated to find the location, number of chargers and capacity of charging stations, and the allocation of electricity between charging stations and demand nodes.
