Title: Bounding Wrong-way Risk

Author: Linan Yang, PhD Student, Columbia University, IEOR Department, New York, NY, 10027, United States of America, ly2220@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Glasserman

Year: 2013

Abstract: Counterparty risk depends on the interaction between market and credit risk. Positive dependence between the two creates “wrong-way risk? The dependence between market and credit risk is difficult to model, so we develop methods for bounding it by finding the worst-case joint distribution given constraints on the marginal models of market and credit risk. We also develop a method to interpolate between the worst case and the independent case based on the degree of uncertainty in the dependence.
