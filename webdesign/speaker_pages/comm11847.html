Title: Sequential and Simultaneous Decision Making Under Dual Resource Flexibilities

Author: Adelina Gnanlet, PhD Candidate, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, CB#3490 McColl Building, Kenan-Flagler Business School, Chapel Hill, NC, 27599, United States, adelina@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Wendell Gilland

Year: 2006

Abstract: We model simultaneous and sequential decision making for capacity and staffing decisions in two hospital units. We use four flexibility configurations to model downward substitution of bedspaces (capacity) and cross-training (flexibility) of nursing staff. We use two-stage stochastic programming to minimize costs, and assess the impact of configurations and cost parameters on decision variables and performance.
