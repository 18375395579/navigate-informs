Title: A Probabilistically Constrained Model for Wildfire Initial Response Planning

Author: Julian Gallego, Texas A&M University, Austin, TX, United States of America, kamizama77@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Lewis Ntaimo, Curt Stripling

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a probabilistically constrained stochastic integer program model for wildfire initial response planning that incorporates both wildfire risk and the decision-maker’s risk averseness. The model uses wildfire risk indices based on the Texas Wildfire Risk Assessment system. We report on the application of the model to one of the Texas A&M Forest Service fire planning units in East Texas.
