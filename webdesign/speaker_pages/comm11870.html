Title: Predictive Analytics for a Safer Food Supply
Author: Lynda Kelley, Senior Advisor for Food Defense, Office of Food Defense and Emergency Response, FSIS, USDA, Russell Research Center, 950 College Station Road, Athens, GA, 30604, United States of America, Lynda.Kelley@fsis.usda.gov
Coauthor(s): Artur Dubrawski, Lujie Chen, Paula J. Fedorka-Cray, Adrienne Dunham, Beth McGlinchey, Mark Huckabee
Year: 2008
Abstract: Science based risk analysis improves the USDA Food Safety Inspection Service’s ability to combat threats to public health from food-borne illness by allowing the Agency to focus resources on hazards that pose the greatest risk. Innovative algorithms enable detection and containment of threat by analysis of multiple streams of microbiological and sub-typing data, as well as data representing other risk factors such as inspection noncompliance, establishment sizes, and types of produced food.
