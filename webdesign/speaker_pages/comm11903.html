Title: Optimality of Control Limit Policies (CLPs) for a Sensor-based Single-unit Replacement Problem

Author: Alaa Elwany, Ph.D. Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, elwany@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Nagi Gabraeel, Lisa Maillart

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose a sensor-based model for the finite horizon single-unit replacement problem. The replacement decision is based upon the component's underlying state of degradation. Information about the degradation state is gathered from real-time sensory signals acquired using condition monitoring techniques. We formulate the model as a Markov Decision Process (MDP) and show that a control limit policy (CLP)is optimal for this model.
