Title: On Marginal Decision Rule in Day-to-Day Traffic Assignment
Author: Xiaozheng He, Research Associate, Purdue University, NEXTRANS Center, 3000 Kent Ave, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, seanhe@purdue.edu
Coauthor(s): Srinivas Peeta
Year: 2013
Abstract: We propose a marginal decision rule based on the law of diminishing marginal utility to model traveler day-to-day route choice behavior. We investigate the relationship between the marginal decision rule and traveler?s rational and boundedly rational behaviors. A day-to-day traffic assignment model based on the marginal decision rule is explored in terms of the evolution of the traffic pattern towards the classical or boundedly rational user equilibrium cases.
