Title: The Development and Use of Forest Fire Detection Probabilities for Detection Patrol Route Planning

Author: David Martell, University of Toronto, Faculty of Forestry, Toronto, ON, Canada, david.martell@utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Robin Santiago, Douglas Woolford, Colin McFayden, Mike Wotton

Year: 2013

Abstract: Forest and wildland fire managers seek to find fires while they are small to increase the chance that should a fire pose a threat, it will be contained by the initial attack system while still small, to minimize its destructive impact. We describe how we combined real-time GPS aircraft tracking data and the estimated ignition times of lightning-caused forest fires to estimate fire detection probabilities and how those detection probabilities can be used to help route aerial detection patrols.
