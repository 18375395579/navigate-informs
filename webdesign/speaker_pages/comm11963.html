Title: Global Millennium Development Goals: Which Policies are Effective?

Author: Nicole Adler, Assistant Professor, School of Business Administration, Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Mount Scopus 91905, Israel, msnic@huji.ac.il

Year: 2006

Abstract: We benchmark developing countries and analyze which are most effective in achieving the United Nations agreed Millennium Development Goals using data envelopment analysis. These goals range from eradication of extreme poverty, provision of universal primary education, promotion of gender equality and reduction in child and maternal mortality rates. The second stage analysis utilizes regression to explain the efficiency scores in an attempt to identify useful policy measures.
