Title: Fixing Phantom Stockouts: A POS-Based Shelf Inspection Model
Author: Li Chen, Assistant Professor, Duke University, 100 Fuqua Drive, Durham, NC, 27708, United States of America, li.chen@duke.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: Phantom stockout is a retail stockout phenomenon caused by shelf execution failure and/or product shrinkage (e.g., theft and spoilage). In this paper, we propose a simple but effective partially-observable Markov decision process (POMDP) model to tackle this problem. We show that the optimal shelf inspection policy is a threshold policy based on the number of consecutive zero-sales periods. We further extend the analysis to models with time-varying parameters.
