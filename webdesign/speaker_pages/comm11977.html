Title: Prepositioning of Supplies in Preparation for a Hurricane with Forecast Information Updates

Author: Rajan Batta, University at Buffalo (SUNY), Dept of Industrial & Systems Engg, Buffalo, NY, 14260, United States of America, batta@buffalo.edu

Coauthor(s): Gina Galindo

Year: 2012

Abstract: It is possible to use prediction models to obtain periodic forecasts about a hurricane’s main characteristics such as its path, wind force and time of landfall. Using this information we present a dynamic model for prepositioning supplies in such a setting. Our model has two distinguishing features: possible destruction of supply points during the disaster event, and the use of forecast information updates in order to strategically modify and improve the prepositioned quantities and locations.
