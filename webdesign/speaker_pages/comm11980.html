Title: Cost-effectiveness of Darunavir for Treatment-experienced Adults with HIV Infection in the US
Author: Anita Brogan, Senior Director, Health Economics, RTI Health Solutions, 3040 Cornwallis Rd, RTP, NC, 27709, United States of America, abrogan@rti.org
Coauthor(s): Erik Smets, Silas Martin, Josephine Mauskopf
Year: 2008
Abstract: Darunavir is a novel protease inhibitor used with other antiretroviral medications for the treatment of HIV infection. We developed a Markov model to estimate the cost-effectiveness of darunavir versus other protease inhibitors in treatment-experienced adults. The model tracks the remaining lifetime of a treatment-experienced cohort by virologic and immunologic response to treatment. We discuss this model with an emphasis on the application of modeling techniques to HIV.
