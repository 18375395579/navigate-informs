Title: Optimal Resource Capacity Management for Stochastic Networks
Author: Ton Dieker, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr NW, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, ton.dieker@isye.gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Soumyadip Ghosh, Mark Squillante
Year: 2012
Abstract: We develop a general framework for determining the optimal resource capacity for each station comprising a stochastic network, motivated by applications arising in computer capacity planning and business process management. In this talk we propose an iterative methodology that relies only on the capability of observing the queue lengths at all network stations given any resource capacity allocation. We theoretically investigate the proposed methodology for single-class Brownian tree networks, and further illustrate the use our methodology and the quality of its results through extensive numerical results.
