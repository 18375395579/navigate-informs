Title: Cutter Tilt Modeling and Monitoring in Face Milling Using High-deifinition Metrology

Author: Hui Wang, University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, United States of America, huiwz@umich.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: This research uses surface data measured by high-definition metrology to monitor machine spindle setup and deflection that impact surface quality and part functions. Procedures were developed to estimate and monitor the hard-to-measure cutter tilts by integrating cutting force modeling and surface data patterns. A Ford case study has demonstrated the effectiveness of the proposed method. This research also establishes a theoretical framework of surface characterization driven by process physics.
