Title: From theory to practice: A resource allocation model for health departments
Author: Emine Yaylali, PE Fellow, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 1600 Clifton Road NE, MS E-36, Atlanta, GA, 30333, United States of America, wqq3@cdc.gov
Coauthor(s): Arielle Lasry, Stephanie Sansom
Year: 2013
Abstract: Resource allocation models can synthesize surveillance, programmatic and research data in a transparent way to guide the optimal distribution of a fixed budget among people and programs. Although a number of resource allocation models for HIV prevention have been created over the years, they seldom are used in practice. In this study, we describe the challenges of designing and executing an HIV resource allocation model for state and local health departments.
