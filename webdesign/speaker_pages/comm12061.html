Title: Multi State Markov Models and Currency Options Implied Volatility Regimes

Author: Adam Duncan, Cambridge Associates, Boston, MA, United States of America, aduncaninmotion@gmail.com

Year: 2013

Abstract: The multi-state Markov model is a useful way of describing a process in which an individual moves through a series of states in continuous time. Data often consist of observations of the process at arbitrary times, so that the exact times when the state changes are unobserved. For example, the progression of chronic diseases is often described by stages of severity, and the state of the patient may only be known at doctor or hospital visits. We conduct an exploratory study using multi-state Markov models to examine various implied volatility regimes using data on currency options. Volatility regimes are nothing more than states of the world defined by volatility characteristics. We also estimate transition probabilities of going from one volatility regime to another, as well as 'lengths of stay' - the length of time of each regime. Our results have implications in selecting an optimal trading strategy from a set of rules-based strategies.
