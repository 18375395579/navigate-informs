Title: Modeling Dynamic Information Flow and Processing from Sensors to Commanders

Author: Darryl Ahner, US Army, PO Box 8695, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States, darryl.ahner@us.army.mil

Year: 2006

Abstract: The Dynamic Model of Situated Cognition is a cognition model that puts the complex human-machine interaction on the battlefield into a conceptual framework. This framework lacks a mathematical rigor that models the processing and feedback processes in a capacity constrained system. This research develops a dynamic mathematical model and solution approach for representing dynamic information flow and processing from sensors to commanders within this Dynamic Model of Situated Cognition framework.
