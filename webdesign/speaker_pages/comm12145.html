Title: Effects of Estimation Errors on Cause-Selection Charts

Author: Fugee Tsung, Professor, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, IELM Department, Kowloon, Hong Kong - ROC, season@ust.hk

Coauthor(s): Lianjie Shu, K-L Tsui

Year: 2006

Abstract: The cause-selecting chart (CSC) is a statistical process control (SPC) tool for analyzing multistage processes. It distinguishes between incoming and outgoing quality problems by establishing a relationship between input and output measurements. In practice, the model relating the input and output must first be estimated before the CSC is implemented. This paper investigates the effect of parameter estimation errors on the performance of CSCs and presents some useful results.
