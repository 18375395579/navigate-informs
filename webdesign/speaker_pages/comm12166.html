Title: Airport Capacity: Aggregate vs. Disaggregate Modeling

Author: Jasenka Rakas, University of California at Berkeley, NEXTOR, 107B McLaughlin Hall, Berkeley, CA, 94720, United States, jrakas@berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Yu Zhang

Year: 2006

Abstract: We develop a comprehensive methodology for assessing airport performance and establishing airport efficiency metrics for runway and airport utilization using PDARS. We focus on the estimates of landing time intervals (LTIs) for each consecutive pair of aircraft for each runway and then aggregate micro aircraft-landing data to runway level, airport-configuration level and airport level for five major airports in the U.S. We discus the advantages of disaggregate airport capacity modeling.
