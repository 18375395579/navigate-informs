Title: A Modeling Framework for Life History-based Conservation Planning

Author: Sandor Toth, Assistant Professor, University of Washington, School of Environmental and Forest Scien, Seattle, WA, 98195, United States of America, toths@uw.edu

Coauthor(s): Eileen S. Burns, Robert Haight

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present a new population protection function for reserve site selection models that determines whether minimum habitat requirements are present in a given reserve network. We embedding the function in an integer programming-based reserve selection model and apply it to a case study of Myotis bat conservation on Lopez Island, United States. Finally, we highlight the need in future reserve design models to capture intra- and inter-species competition for habitat resources.
