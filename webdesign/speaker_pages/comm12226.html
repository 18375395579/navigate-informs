Title: Optimizing the Care of Children with Acute Liver Failure: The Decision to List for Transplantation

Author: Mark Roberts, Professor, University of Pittsburgh, 130 De Soto Street, A620 Crabtree Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States of America, mroberts@pitt.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Pediatric acute liver failure (PALF) is a complex, devastating, often rapidly fatal disease for which liver transplantation is a potentially lifesaving treatment. However, many children with PALF will recover: these patients are not benefited by transplantation. Therefore, the decision to list a child for transplantation represents a complex optimal timing question. We present the development and early results of a physiologically based individual microsimulation model of PALF that can be used to evaluate different transplantation decisions.
