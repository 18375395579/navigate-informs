Title: Choice of Best-fitting Inputs in Mutli-target Model Calibration: Implications for Economic Analysis
Author: Lauren Cipriano, Assistant Professor, Western University, Ivey Business School, London, ON, Canada, lcipriano@ivey.uwo.ca
Coauthor(s): Eva Enns, C. Torrey Simons, Chung Yin Kong
Year: 2013
Abstract: We describe a new approach for selecting best-fitting sets from a multi-target model calibration effort which applies the principles of Pareto-optimality. We demonstrate the new approach using a simple model, developed for illustrative purposes, and a previously-published model used for a cost-effectiveness analysis of transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR). We explore the implications of how best-fitting sets are selected on which policy is preferred and decision uncertainty.
