Title: Derivative-free Software for Multiagent Hybrid Optimization in Parallel

Author: Joshua Griffin, Sandia National Laboratories, 4970 Owens Drive Apt 637, Pleasanton, CA, 94588, United States, jgriffi@sandia.gov

Coauthor(s): Tamara Kolda

Year: 2007

Abstract: We discuss strategies for accelerating convergence of Generating Set Search (GSS) by providing the ability to evaluate externally generated points. These modifications provide several new capabilities: 1) the user may runmultiple local searches simultaneously, 2) the user may concurrently use additional (non-GSS) optimization strategies while sharing common point evaluations, and 3) the ability to extend capabilities to global optimization.
