Title: Modeling the Electricity Supply Stack with Time-subordinated Processes

Author: Stathis Tompaidis, Associate Professor, McCombs School of Business, IROM Dpt., B6500, University of Texas at Austin, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, Stathis.Tompaidis@mccombs.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Rafael Mendoza-Arriaga, Vishwakant Malladi

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a framework for modeling the supply stack of electricity generators using time-subordinated processes. We demonstrate how the use of time-subordinated processes can be adapted to a factor structure that allows for correlated outages across generator types, geographical location, and links to the transmission network. We illustrate our model with a calibrated example for the ERCOT region.
