Title: A Patrol Problem from the Adversary's Perspective

Author: Kyle Lin, Associate Professor, Naval Postgraduate School, 1411 Cunningham Rd, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States of America, kylin@nps.edu

Coauthor(s): David Alderson, Michael Atkinson, Tim Chung

Year: 2012

Abstract: Consider a patrol problem, where a defender uses patrols to deter attacks, while an adversary observes and studies the patrol strategy before deciding whether to attack. We use a game-theoretic model to study these interactions and draw insights into the effectiveness of patrols.
