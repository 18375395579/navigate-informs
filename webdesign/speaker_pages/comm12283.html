Title: Modeling Ambiguity Attitudes in Decision Analysis

Author: Emanuele Borgonovo, Professor, Bocconi University, Via Roentgen 1, Milan, Italy, emanuele.borgonovo@unibocconi.it

Coauthor(s): Massimo Marinacci

Year: 2013

Abstract: In several decision analysis problems, state of knowledge does not allow decision-makers to assign a certain value to the involved probabilities. Experimental evidence has shown that this uncertainty (ambiguity) is accompanied by behavioral consequences. We explore the explicit modelling of these attitudes using minimax, subjective expected utility and smooth ambiguity functionals in combination with the traditional decision-analysis representation of choice problems.
