Title: Modeling Effects of Counterterrorism Initiatives on Reducing Adversary Threats

Author: Heather Rosoff, University of Southern California, Los Angeles, CA, United States of America, rosoff@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Richard John

Year: 2012

Abstract: We describe a general methodology for evaluating counterterrorism alternatives that accounts for an adaptive adversary planning to attack a U.S. target. The central focus of the methodology is a model of terrorist objectives and values using a proxy multi-attribute utility model. Results suggest that terrorist preferences for attack mode are sensitive to certain counter-terrorism strategies and insensitive to others.
