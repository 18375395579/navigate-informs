Title: A Simulation-optimization Approach for Large-scale Wildfire Extended Attack Response Planning

Author: Michelle M. Alvarado, PhD Student, Texas A&M University, 3131 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843, United States of America, michelle.mcgaha@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Lewis Ntaimo

Year: 2012

Abstract: Our simulation-optimization approach integrates a stochastic integer programming (SIP) model for wildfire extended attack planning with DEVS-FIRE, a fire spread and suppression discrete event simulation model. The SIP model optimizes the location and timing of the deployment of firefighting resources to an escaped large-scale wildfire. The SIP decisions and weather forecasts are used in DEVS-FIRE to generate fire spread scenarios, which become input for the SIP model in the next decision period.
