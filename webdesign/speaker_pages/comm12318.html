Title: Incorporating Endogenous Demand into Capacity Expansion Models for Developing Countries

Author: Rhonda Jordan, PhD Candidate, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 143 Albany Street, #201A, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, rjordan@mit.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Using a novel combination of approximate dynamic programming, system dynamics and linear programming, this research develops a power system capacity expansion model that incorporates both the technical details of power grid operation and the salient features of electricity demand in developing countries. Unlike traditional planning models, demand is represented endogenously. The capacity expansion strategies identified in this research are compared against that of more traditional approaches.
