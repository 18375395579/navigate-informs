Title: Open Pit to Underground: Optimizing the Transition

Author: Barry King, Graduate Student, Colorado School of Mines, 1500 Illinois Street, Golden, CO, 80401, United States of America, barking@mines.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Current industry practice of scheduling extraction in an open pit mine, followed by making underground extraction decisions for the same deposit, leads to suboptimal extraction in the transition zone. We can use an optimization model to determine these decisions for maximizing net present value for the mining operation, and adhering to precedence and operational constraints. We illustrate our technique numerically and contrast our work with previous less detailed and/or less formal efforts.
