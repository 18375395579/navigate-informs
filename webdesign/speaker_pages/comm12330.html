Title: The Robust Approach to Simulation Optimization

Author: Ilya Ryzhov, University of Maryland, 4322 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 21044, United States of America, iryzhov@rhsmith.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Boris Defourny, Warren Powell

Year: 2012

Abstract: Simulation can be used to learn about a decision-maker’s environment and improve a future implementation decision (a system design, management policy, investment decision, etc.). The simulation optimization literature often assumes that decision-makers are risk-neutral. Robust optimization can be used to approach the problem from a risk-averse perspective. We formulate the risk-averse problem and propose a way to allocate the simulation budget based on an economic improvement criterion.
