Title: Deriving Infectious Disease Contact Networks through Agent-based Simulation

Author: Charles Macal, Senior Systems Engineer, Argonne National Laboratory, 9700 S. Cass Avenue, Bldg 221 - DIS, Argonne, IL, 60439, United States of America, macal@anl.gov

Year: 2013

Abstract: An agent-based activity simulation is used to derive an infectious disease contact network. A synthetic population of individuals and households for Chicago is developed based on census and survey data. Agents are matched to activity profiles relevant to disease transmission, specifically community-associated Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (CA-MRSA). The presentation describes the development of the network and analysis of its topological properties.
