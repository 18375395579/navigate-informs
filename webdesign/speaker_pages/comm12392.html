Title: Outcome and State Transition Modeling for Adaptive Interdisciplinary Pain Management
Author: Aera LeBoulluec, PhD Student/GRA, University of Texas-Arlington, 2205 Hunter Place Lane, Arlington, TX, 76006, United States of America, aera.leboulluec@mavs.uta.edu
Coauthor(s): Li Zeng, Victoria Chen
Year: 2012
Abstract: In this research, we seek adaptive treatment strategies for interdisciplinary pain management using data from the Eugene McDermott Center for Pain Management at the University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center at Dallas. An adaptive dynamic programming approach is formulated to improve current and future pain outcomes. Outcome and state transition modeling handle the problem of endogeneity via an inverse probability of treatment weighting approach.
