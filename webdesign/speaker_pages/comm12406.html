Title: Optimizing the Dose and Delivery of Influenza Vaccine Using A Multi-Objective Simulation Approach
Author: Wei Bao, Graduate Assistant, University of Arkansas, 4207 Bell Engineering, Fayetteville, Ar, 72701, United States of America, weibao@email.uark.edu
Coauthor(s): Shengfan Zhang
Year: 2013
Abstract: We present a multi-objective simulation optimization model to determine the number of doses and delivery of influenza vaccine before seasonal influenza outbreaks. The demand and timing for flu shots is uncertain, especially for areas with high risk of infection. We develop a simulation model for an optimal vaccine production and delivery policy considering three different perspectives: individual, society and vaccine manufacture.
