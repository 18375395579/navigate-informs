Title: Predicting Binary Choices from Probability Phrase Meanings

Author: Thomas Wallsten, twallsten@psyc.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Yoonhee Jang

Year: 2008

Abstract: One way to validate scaled probability phrase meanings is to use them within a model to predict independently observed binary choices. We scaled participant’s individually-selected probability phrases and developed a model that predicts choice probabilities by using the scaled meanings as proxies for confidence distributions associated with sampling from memory. Predictions were sustained for 34 of 41 participants. The results have both theoretical and applied implications.
