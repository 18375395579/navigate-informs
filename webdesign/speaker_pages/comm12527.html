Title: Bilevel and Trilevel Models for Electric Power Grid Interdiction and Defense
Author: Javier Salmeron, Associate Professor, Naval Postgraduate School, Operations Research Dept., Monterey, CA, 93933, United States, jsalmero@nps.edu
Coauthor(s): Kevin Wood
Year: 2007
Abstract: We describe defender-attacker-defender (DAD) models for protecting an electrical power grid physical infrastructure. We first discuss the attacker-defender (AD) problem as one of interdiction: Use limited attacks on system components to maximize energy disruption. The DAD model uses limited defensive resources to protect components: We interdict the interdictor. We discuss decomposition techniques to solve realistic electric power-transmission instances of AD, and smaller instances of DAD.
