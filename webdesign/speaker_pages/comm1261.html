Title: Study on Power Grid Defend-Attack-Defend with Line Switching
Author: Bo Zeng, Assistant Professor, University of South Florida, 4202 E Fowler Ave ENB118, Tampa, FL, 33620, United States of America, bzeng@usf.edu
Coauthor(s): Wei Yuan, Long Zhao
Year: 2012
Abstract: Defend-Attack-Defend (DAD) Models are widely used in power grid contingencies and security studies. In this study, we consider line switching, one of the most effective operations to mitigate deliberate outages or attacks, in DAD model. We evaluate the benefits of transmission line switching by comparing the case without line switching.
