Title: Ghost Model in Simulation based Optimization

Author: Felisa Vázquez Abad, Professor, Hunter College, 695 Park ave, New York, NY, 10021, United States of America, felisav@hunter.cuny.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a simulation model for large two-time scale networks that increases the efficiency compared to a discrete event simulation model. Faster point processes are replaced by a fluid (ghost process) to accelerate the execution time. When a local clock advances due to a slower event, retrospective calculations (Filtered Monte Carlo) recover the fine detail lost in the fluid model. Efficiency improvement results not only from the speed of execution, but also from variance reduction.
