Title: Estimating Congestion Impacts on Rail Lines via Statistical Methods
Author: Michael Gorman, Associate Professor, University of Dayton, School of Business, Dayton, OH, United States, Michael.Gorman@notes.udayton.edu
Year: 2007
Abstract: As railroads become more and more congested with increasing traffic base, it becomes paramount to estimate the congestion impact of new traffic. Well-tested, specialized simulation software is often used for such predictions. However, simulations have some drawbacks and are time consuming to run. We test the viability of statistical methods as a low cost alternative for prediction the impact of additional trains on train running times. Empirical results are presented.
