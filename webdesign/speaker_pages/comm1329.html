Title: A Preliminary Comparison of European & U.S. Performance

Author: Dave Knorr, FAA Liasion at DFS, FAA, 800 Independence Avenue, Washington, DC, 200008, United States of America, Dave.Knorr@faa.gov

Coauthor(s): Marc Rose

Year: 2008

Abstract: Anecdotal comparisons have been made comparing United States with Europe ATM. Many believe the European system applies more stringent ground delays to flights to control for demand exceeding capacity. Taxi times are believed to be longer in the U.S. To date a systematic approach to comparing performance trends by phase of flight has not been performed. Detailed flight data in both Europe and the US to compare real performance is used. A normalizing technique the “DLTA Method?is used
