Title: Pricing American Options By Numerical Replication: Quadratic Programming Approach
Author: Timofey Turko, University of Florida, jk13@ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Stanislav Uryasev, Valeriy Ryabchenko
Year: 2007
Abstract: The paper considers a quadratic programming approach to pricing American options in an incomplete market. The algorithm replicates an option by a portfolio consisting of the underlying security and a risk-free bond. To make the model robust we incorporate a set of American option specific monotonicity constraints that are theoretically justified for general price dynamics processes. We populate the model with historical sample paths of the underlying security or with Monte Carlo simulated paths.
