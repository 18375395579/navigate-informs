Title: Mining Consumer Network Data for Targeted Marketing

Author: Shawndra Hill, New York University, 44 W 4th Street, New York, NY, 10012, United States, shill@stern.nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Chris Volinsky, Foster Provost

Year: 2006

Abstract: This project analyses networks formed from direct interactions between consumers. The data come from a telecommunication firm’s targeted marketing campaign, which form a natural testbed for studying network-based marketing because both direct consumer interactions and product adoption are observable. We show that models built using attributes constructed from the consumer interaction network enable better prediction of product adoption than traditional targeted marketing techniques.
