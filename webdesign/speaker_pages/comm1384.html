Title: Routing to Prevent Congestion in Rail-Based AMHS Systems

Author: Kelly Bartlett, Doctoral Candidate, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr., Atlanta, GA, United States of America, kelly.bartlett@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): George Nemhauser, Shabbir Ahmed, Joel Sokol, Junho Lee, Bungsoo Na

Year: 2013

Abstract: In rail-based Automated Material Handling Systems (AMHS), such as those used to transport wafers in semi-conductor manufacturing, vehicular congestion results in reduced production efficiency. We present vehicle routing strategies to prevent congestion including dynamic routing, where routes are adjusted based on current congestion, and intentional route distribution using a multi-commodity flow model. We provide results from a simulated prototype facility.
