Title: Active Learning via Sequential Design with Applications to Anti-money Laundering

Author: Xinwei Deng, Ph.D Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, xdeng@isye.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Roshan Vengazhiyil, Agus Sudjianto, C. F. Jeff Wu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Anti-money laundering is an important responsibility. There are millions of transactions happening each day. Investigating each transaction and classifying an account to be suspicious or not is time-consuming. In this article, we propose an active learning method using Bayesian sequential designs to detect money-laundering. The sequential nature helps to identify suspicious accounts with minimal effort. An application to real banking data is used to demonstrate the performance of the method.
