Title: Impact of a Potential H7N9 Pandemic Outbreak in the U.S.

Author: Walter Silva Sotillo, PhD Student, University of South Florida / Pontificia Universidad Catolica del Peru, Industrial and Management Systems Engine, 4202 E. Fowler Avenue, Tampa, FL, 33620, United States of America, silvasotillo@mail.usf.edu

Coauthor(s): Tapas Das

Year: 2013

Abstract: Till June 27, 2013, WHO reported 131 confirmed cases (with 30% fatality) of influenza in China from bird-to-human transmission of H7N9 virus. Hence, the concern is high that H7N9 may become human-to-human transmittable. We use data from recent reports and a simulation model to predict impact of a potential H7N9 pandemic outbreak in the U.S.
