Title: On the (Non-Linear) Probability of Winning a (College) Football Game

Author: Eric Huggins, Associate Professor of Management, Fort Lewis College, Durango, CO, 81301, United States of America, huggins_e@fortlewis.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this paper, we estimate the probability that a football team wins a game based on the point spread and the over/under. This work is an extension of the Hal Stern's 1991 paper with several key differences: 1) We analyze data for both college and professional football, using a much larger data set. 2) We factor in the over/under as well as the point spread. 3) We explore several non-linear models for the probability function.
