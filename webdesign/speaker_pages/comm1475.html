Title: Multiple Objective Model to the Retail Store Design Using Tabu Search

Author: Alice Smith, Professor and Chair, Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering, Auburn University, 207 Dunstan Hall, Auburn, AL, 36849, United States, aesmith@eng.auburn.edu

Coauthor(s): Haluk Yapicioglu, Bryan A. Norman

Year: 2006

Abstract: This study presents a multiobjective model for the spatial design of a retail store. The model combines ideas from both the shelf space allocation and the facility layout literature. The model considers the revenue and the adjacency requirements among departments. The revenue aspect consists both of department size and department exposure to the main traffic patterns of the customers. Therefore, the problem includes several objectives along with constraints on the departments and on the aisling.
