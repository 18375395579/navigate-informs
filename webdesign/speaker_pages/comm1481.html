Title: Robustness and Performance of Threshold-based Resource Allocation Policies

Author: Atul Bhandari, Post-Doctoral Associate, University of Pittsburgh, 1178c Benedum Hall, University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States, abhandari79@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Alan Scheller-Wolf, Mor Harchol-Balter, Takayuki Osogami

Year: 2007

Abstract: We provide the first analytical study of the mean response time and robustness of a wide range of threshold-based resource allocation policies for a multi-server queuing system with flexible routing such as those commonly used in modeling call centers. We find that using multiple thresholds can have significant benefit over using only a single threshold with respect to robustness, but that multiple thresholds surprisingly offer only small advantage with respect to mean response time.
