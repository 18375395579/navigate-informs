Title: A Directional Distance Function Approach to Identifying the Input/Output Status of Medical Residents

Author: Michael Vardanyan, IÉSEG School of Management, Socle de la Grande Arche, 1, Parvis de la Défense, Paris La Défense, 92044, France, M.vardanyan@ieseg.fr

Coauthor(s): Vivian Valdmanis, Gary Ferrier, Herv?Leleu

Year: 2013

Abstract: The application of the human capital theory, the production theory, and the comparison of costs between teaching and non-teaching hospitals all point to the difficulties in identifying whether medical residents should be considered as inputs or outputs. We add to the debate by using a data-driven parametric approach based on the directional distance function to determine the status of residents. American Hospital Association data from 1994 to 2008 is used in the empirical illustration.
