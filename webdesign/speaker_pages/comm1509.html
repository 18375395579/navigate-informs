Title: Optimizing State Access to Trauma Centers

Author: Charles Branas, Assistant Professor, University of Pennsylvania, 936 Blockley Hall, 423 Guardian Drive, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, cbranas@cceb.med.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Justin Williams

Year: 2006

Abstract: The TRAMAH model seeks to optimize the locations of trauma centers and ambulances in terms of the number of severely injured people that can reach a trauma center in a short period of time. The first phase of the model was tested in a single state. The second phase was applied to 13 states in a multi-objective framework that considers coverage of severely injured people and patient volumes per trauma center. The third phase applies portions of the model to all states in a web-based platform.
