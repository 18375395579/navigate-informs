Title: An Integer Programming Model for Breast Cancer Treatment Scheduling

Author: Illya Hicks, Associate Professor, Texas A&M University, Industrial and Systems Engineering MS 3131, Zachry Engineering Center, College Station, TX, 77843-3131, United States, ivhicks@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Elif Kolotoglu, Kathleen Diehl

Year: 2006

Abstract: Tamoxifen and several types of Aromatase Inhibitors (AI) have been used for the prevention and adjuvant treatment of breast cancer. However, there is currently conflicting evidence and several unanswered questions regarding which inhibitor is most effective for use in post menopausal patients. In this study, our aim is to design effective 5-year drug treatment plans considering Tamoxifen and AIs for postmenopausal women with breast cancer using integer programming techniques.
