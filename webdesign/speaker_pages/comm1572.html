Title: Stochastic and Robust Optimization of Multi-OR Surgical Schedules
Author: Brian Denton, Assistant Professor, Edward P. Fitts Dep. of Industrial & Systems Engr., North Carolina State University, Campus Box 7906, 111 Lampe Dr, Daniels Hall 400, Raleigh, NC, 27695-7906, United States, bdenton@ncsu.edu
Coauthor(s): Hari Balasubramanian, Andrew Miller, Todd Huschka
Year: 2007
Abstract: We describe two types of models for scheduling of multi-OR surgical suites under uncertainty. The first is a two-stage stochastic linear program with binary decisions in the first stage. The second is a robust counter-part, in which the objective is to minimize the maximum cost associated with an uncertainty set for surgery durations. We describe the mathematical models, structural properties, methodologies employed, and sample numerical results based on real data to contrast the two models.
