Title: Radiation Therapy Treatment Decision Making for Prostate Cancer Patients Based on PSA Dynamics

Author: Mariel Lavieri, University of British Columbia, 2053 Main Mall, Vancouver, BC, Canada, Mariel.Lavieri@sauder.ubc.ca

Coauthor(s): Steven Shechter, Scott Tyldesley, Martin Puterman

Year: 2008

Abstract: In this talk we model the decision of when to start radiation therapy (RT) in prostate cancer patients who are undergoing neoadjuvant hormone therapy. We develop and validate a dynamic Kalman filter model for within patient serial PSA values. Our decision making model trades off the risk of beginning RT too soon so that hormone therapy has not achieved its maximum effect vs. waiting too long to start therapy so that there is an increased risk of tumor cells becoming resistant to the treatment.
