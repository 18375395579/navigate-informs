Title: Patient Experience Improvement at UPMC Eye Center

Author: Tinglong Dai, PhD Candidate, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States of America, dai@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Sridhar Tayur, Joshua Rheinbolt, Robert Noecker

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this case, we describe a patient improvement initiative at University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC) Eye Center, where both long waiting times and equipment underutilization were observed. The improvement project featured a videotaping of the service process in the UPMC Eye Center’s imaging room for an extended period. Based on analyzing the videos, the researchers at Carnegie Mellon University’s Tepper School of Business, together with the physicians at UPMC Eye Center, proposed a simple “dome-shaped?strategy that used different appointment interval before, during, and after the lunchtime, and determined the optimal service parameters using queueing network modeling and simulation analysis. Students learn through this case how to address a real-life service design problem through detailed observation, quantitative modeling, and simulation. They are also taught how to make recommendations matching the needs and the expectations of real service environments. This case is intended for both undergraduate and MBA students in operations management, service management, or healthcare analysis courses, as well as health professionals who are interested in improving the efficiencies in their workplaces.
