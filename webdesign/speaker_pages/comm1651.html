Title: Reducing Ward Congestion at Vancouver Island Health Authority Through Improved Surgical Scheduling

Author: Vincent Chow, Operations Research Analyst, British Columbia Cancer Agency, 600 West 10th Avenue, Vancouver, BC, V5Z 4E6, Canada, vchow@bccancer.bc.ca

Coauthor(s): Neda Salehirad, Derek Atkins, Wenhai Huang, Martin Puterman

Year: 2008

Abstract: This talk describes a significant study carried out at Royal Jubilee Hospital in Victoria BC, Canada involving surgical scheduling and its impact on downstream ward utilization. We developed a portable MS EXCEL based simulation model (BUS) which we combined with an integer program to help surgical planners reduce ward congestion. Results from the project are currently being used within the health authority.
