Title: Airport Terminal Design Using Simulation

Author: Cenk Tunasar, PhD, Senior Associate, Booz Allen Hamilton, 8283 Greensboro Drive, McLean, VA, 22102, United States, tunasar_cenk@bah.com

Coauthor(s): Rahmi Evlioglu

Year: 2006

Abstract: We demonstrate the use of simulation in airport terminal facility design via examples from JFK, Baltimore-Washington and Atlanta Airports. The models, developed by languages Arena and Automod, cover detailed movements of passengers and baggage. Working with architects and planners, we show that simulation can become part of the design process by evaluating multiple design options quickly. We will also share the challenges we faced and lessons learned in implementing simulation modeling.
