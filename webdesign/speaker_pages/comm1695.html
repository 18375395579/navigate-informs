Title: Bayesian Model Averaging in Benchmark Dose Estimation

Author: Susan Simmons, Professor of Statistics, University of North Carolina Wilmington, 401 S. College Rd, Wilmington, NC, United States of America, simmonssj@uncw.edu

Coauthor(s): Cuixian Chen, Xiaosong Li, Qijun Fang, Walter Piegorsch

Year: 2013

Abstract: The use of Benchmark Dose Estimation has dramatically increased over the past decade. Many US government agencies and research laboratories use benchmark dose methodology to estimate the allowable dose of hazardous agents. The main drawback of this methodology is that a dose-response curve must be specified in order to estimate the benchmark dose. Herein, we illustrate an alternative to specifying one benchmark dose curve and utilized Bayesian model-averaging.
