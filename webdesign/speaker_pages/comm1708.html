Title: Data Mining Approach using Hemodynamic Parameters
Author: Monica Puertas, University of South Florida, 18125 Birdwater Dr., Tampa, FL, 33647, United States of America, mpuertas@mail.usf.edu
Coauthor(s): Peter Fabri, Jose Luis Zayas-Castro
Year: 2013
Abstract: The accurate assessment of the circulatory system is important for critically ill patients. Often changes in circulatory status occur rapidly, requiring continuous monitoring. This study is focused on how to extend the usability of hemodynamic parameters such as cardiac output and systemic vascular resistance, using a data mining approach with available clinical metrics to produce individual predictive hemodynamic models.
