Title: Spreadsheet vs. Algebraic Approaches for Optimization Modeling

Author: Larry LeBlanc, Owen Graduate School of Management, Vanderbilt University, TN, United States of America, Larry.LeBlanc@owen.vanderbilt.edu

Coauthor(s): Michael Galbreth

Year: 2007

Abstract: Because of their familiarity to managers, spreadsheets have been suggested as preferable to specialized mathematical packages for optimization modeling in some contexts. However, spreadsheets often require extensive use of VBA to accommodate large models. Thus, for large models the choice is not a specialized package vs. simple Excel, but rather a specialized package vs. very complex Excel and VBA. We discuss the pros and cons of this decision and suggest the best approach in various situations.
