Title: Toward a Complete Active Tomography Framework
Author: Earl Lawrence, Los Alamos National Laboratory, earl@lanl.gov
Year: 2007
Abstract: Active network tomography is concerned with inferring link-level packet metrics in a network based upon end-to-end probing. There are two outstanding problems. The first is the need for a framework that can model loss and empty-queue probabilities with a flexible continuous component for the heavy-tailed delays. The second problem is the need for a spatio-temporal modeling component. I will discuss the use of mixture models in the first problem and a multivariate probit model in the second.
