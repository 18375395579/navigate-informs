Title: A Three-level Model for Generation and Transmission Expansion Planning

Author: Javier Contreras, Professor, University of Castilla - La Mancha, E.T.S. de Ingenieros Industriales, Ciudad Real, 13071, Spain, Javier.Contreras@uclm.es

Coauthor(s): Enzo Sauma, David Pozo

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present a three-level model for the expansion of an electric network. The lower-level model represents the equilibrium of a pool-based market; the intermediate level represents the Nash equilibrium in generation expansion, and the upper-level model represents the anticipation of transmission expansion to the investment in generation and the pool-based market equilibrium. The model is applied to a realistic power system in Chile to illustrate the methodology and proper conclusions are reached.
