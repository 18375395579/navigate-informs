Title: Air-Passenger Travel Time Demand
Author: Gregory Coldren, President, Coldren Choice Consulting Ltd., 123 South Adams Street, Rockville, MD, 20850, United States, gregorycoldren@yahoo.com
Coauthor(s): Frank Koppelman
Year: 2006
Abstract: This talk examines air-travel itinerary share models focusing on time-of-day demand. The models hypothesize that itinerary choice is based on a traveler’s effort to minimize schedule delay. Functions are estimated to represent passenger departure time preferences. The models differentiate between outbound vs. inbound passengers and between early vs. late schedule delay. Departure time-of-day preference patterns across the different days of the week, service offerings and distances are compared.
