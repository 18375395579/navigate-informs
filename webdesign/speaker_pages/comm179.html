Title: What do Bayesian Testing of a Sharp Hypothesis and Reliability Models Have in Common?

Author: Ehsan Soofi, Distinguished Professor, University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, Sheldon B. Lubar School of Business, P.O. Box 742, Milwaukee, WI, 53021, United States of America, esoofi@uwm.edu

Coauthor(s): Nader Ebrahimi, Nima Jalali

Year: 2013

Abstract: Bayesian testing and some well-known reliability models involve singular distributions. The traditional indices of relationship fail to distinguish the dependence of singular models such as Marshall-Olkin and the exponential autoregressive bivariate distributions. The information measure of dependence is not defined. A weighted average of the mutual information indices for the singular and absolutely continuous parts provides a common metric for comparing the dependence of these models.
