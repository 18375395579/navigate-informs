Title: Some Recent Developments of Stochastic Kriging Methodology

Author: Xi Chen, Assistant Professor, Virginia Commonwealth University, Statistical Sciences & Operations Resear, 1015 Floyd Avenue, Grace E. Harris Hall, Richmond, VA, 23284-3083, United States of America, xchen4@vcu.edu

Coauthor(s): Kai Wang, Kyoung-kuk Kim, Feng Yang

Year: 2013

Abstract: Stochastic kriging (SK) has been studied as a useful metamodeling tool for approximating the mean response surface implied by a stochastic simulation. We propose to extend the standard stochastic kriging in two directions, respectively, to enable SK to handle stochastic simulations involving both qualitative and quantitative decision variables and to upgrade SK with methods such as sectioning for better performance in predicting extreme quantiles and tail conditional expectations.
