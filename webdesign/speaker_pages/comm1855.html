Title: Finding Alternative Routes between Airports under Weather Uncertainty

Author: Wanjira Jirajaruporn, UC Berkeley, 107-E McLaughlin Hall, University of California, Berkeley, CA, 94720, United States of America, iamaung@yahoo.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: The stochastic dynamic ground-holding and rerouting model has been developed to enhance strategic planning tools, Ground Delay Programs and National Playbook for rerouting. The model combines the ground-holding and rerouting and is formulated as a heuristic shortest path problem. The probabilistic model generates flyable probabilities of storms from weather forecast. The ground-holding and rerouting model is implemented on the NAS network using results of the probabilistic model as its input.
