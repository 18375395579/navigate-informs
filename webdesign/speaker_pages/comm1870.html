Title: Allocating CDC’s HIV Prevention Funds

Author: Katherine Hicks, RTI International, 3040 Cornwallis Rd, RTP, NC, 27709, United States of America, khicks@rti.org

Coauthor(s): Arielle Lasry, Stephanie Sansom, Vladislav Uzunangelov

Year: 2008

Abstract: The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) funds most HIV prevention programs in the U.S. To guide CDC funding decisions, we developed a resource allocation model aimed at minimizing new infections over five years. New infections are calculated using a dynamic compartmental model structured by infection status, risk group, gender and race. We discuss this model, with a focus on challenges in applying optimization models to public health issues.
