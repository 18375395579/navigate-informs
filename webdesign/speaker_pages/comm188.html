Title: Psychological Benefits of Real-time Travel Information on Route Choice Behavior ?Preliminary Insights from Driving Simulator Experiment Data

Author: Dong Yoon Song, Graduate Research Assistant, Purdue University, 3000 Kent Ave, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, song50@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Srinivas Peeta

Year: 2013

Abstract: This study proposes a comprehensive framework to understand the psychological mechanisms associated with real-time travel information provided. A structural equation model with latent variables is adopted for analysis based on the revealed preference data from a driving simulator experiment and stated preference data from a survey. Three types of information-related stresses are identified as important factors to infer on driver’s benefits from real-time travel information as well as conventional travel time savings.
