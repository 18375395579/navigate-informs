Title: Applying Air Traffic Flow Management Optimization Models to Railroad Train Scheduling

Author: Lingyun Meng, School of Traffic and Transportation, 8507D, No.8 Teaching Building, No.3 ShangYuanCun, HaiDian District, China, lymeng@bjtu.edu.cn

Coauthor(s): Xuesong Zhou

Year: 2012

Abstract: By analyzing a variety of air traffic flow management models, this paper proposes a cumulative flow count based optimization model for train scheduling problem under stochastic segment capacity breakdowns. The “capacity aggregation?technique is used to capture the uncertainty of capacity breakdown incidents. A Lagrangian relaxation based solution approach is presented to solve this model. Numerical experiments are conducted to demonstrate the performance of the proposed solution approach.
