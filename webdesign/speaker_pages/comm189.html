Title: Health Index Development Based on Sensory Data Fusion for Degradation Modeling and Prognostics

Author: Kaibo Liu, Research Assistant, H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering, 765 Ferst Drive, Room 109, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, kbliu@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Nagi Gebraeel, Jianjun (Jan) Shi

Year: 2012

Abstract: Due to the rapid development of sensor technology, integrating multiple sensor information to improve residual life prediction becomes more promising and challenging. This paper develops a novel data-level fusion technique that includes a generic chain of data selection, processing and fusion steps, specifically for degradation modeling and prognostic purposes. Case studies are conducted based on the aircraft gas turbine engine, which is generated by the C-MAPSS simulator at NASA.
