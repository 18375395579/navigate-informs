Title: Economics of Herd Behavior: Local Conformity, Global Diversity and Punctuated Equilibrium Effect
Author: Steve Kou, Associate Professor, Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, Columbia University, 312 S.W. Mudd, New York, NY, 10027, United States, sk75@columbia.edu
Coauthor(s): Nan Chen
Year: 2006
Abstract: In this paper we propose a simple, analytically tractable, stochastic model to incorporate local conformity, global diversity and punctuated equilibrium effect in a rigorous way. In doing so, the model provides a better understanding of the economics of herd behavior by characterazing precise meanings of the above three features. In addition, we discuss differences between the current stochastic model and some popular deterministic approximations.
