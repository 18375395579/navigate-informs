Title: The Effect of Budgetary Restrictions on Breast Cancer Diagnostic Decisions

Author: Mehmet Ayvaci, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas at Dallas, Jindal School of Management, 800 West Campbell Road, SM33, Richardson, TX, 75080-3021, United States of America, mehmet.ayvaci@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Elizabeth Burnside, Oguzhan Alagoz

Year: 2012

Abstract: We develop a finite-horizon constrained Markov Decision Process to model diagnostic decisions after mammography where we maximize the total expected quality adjusted life years (QALYs) of a patient under resource constraints. Comparing to actual clinical practice, using optimal thresholds may result in 22% cost savings without sacrificing QALYs. Our modeling framework could be used for evaluating cost-effectiveness of diagnostic procedures.
