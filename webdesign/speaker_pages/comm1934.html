Title: Combining Metamodel Techniques and Bayesian Selection Procedures to Derive Computationally Efficient Traffic Management Algorithms
Author: Hoda Bidkhori, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Ave, Room 1-172, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, bidkhori@mit.edu
Coauthor(s): Carolina Osorio
Year: 2012
Abstract: This talk introduces simulation-based optimization (SO) methods to address the problems in Traffic Management. It proposes an SO technique that uses metamodel information together with a Bayesian framework, where the parameters of the prior distributions are estimated based on probabilistic metamodel information. In order to derive an SO algorithm that achieves a good trade-off between detail, realism and computational efficiency, the metamodel combines information from a highresolution traffic simulator with information from a lower-resolution yet computationally efficient analytical queueing network model. We use this approach to address an urban traffic management problem using a detailed microscopic traffic simulator of the Swiss city of Lausanne.
