Title: How to Define the Decision Variables: Model Building for Allocation Type Problems

Author: Boris Goldengorin, Visiting Professor, University of Florida, 7951 NW 47th Way, Gainesville, United States of America, goldengorin@ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Panos Pardalos

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this talk we show why a usual choice of decision variables in the Simple Plant Location Problem (SPLP) is less efficient than inverse one. Based on the corresponding pseudo-Boolean polynomial of the SPLP we derive the most compact MILP model for the SPLP and its variations. Our computational study of the MILP model for the SPLP and its variations shows that many large sized SPLP type instances are solved exactly by means of a general purpose solver like Cplex.
