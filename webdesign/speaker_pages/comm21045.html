Title: A Comparison of Approaches for Solving the Product Family Platform Selection and Design Problem

Author: Aida Khajavirad, Graduate Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave., Hamerschlag Hall B129, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, aida@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jeremy Michalek

Year: 2008

Abstract: An important challenge in product development is to design a set of products that target distinct market segments while maximizing component sharing to reduce manufacturing cost. This joint platform-selection and design problem is a large-scale nonconvex MINLP. We propose and compare several approaches to solving the joint problem, including a heuristic NLP, a decomposed genetic algorithm, and a deterministic global optimization approach using branch-and-reduce with Lagrangian decomposition.
