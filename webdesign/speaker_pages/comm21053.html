Title: Progressive Hedging Innovations for Stochastic Mixed-integer Programming

Author: David Woodruff, Prof, UC Davis, Graduate School of Management, One Shields Avenue, Davis, CA, 95616, United States of America, dlWoodruff@ucdavis.edu

Coauthor(s): Jean-Paul Watson

Year: 2008

Abstract: Progressive hedging (PH) is a scenario-based decomposition technique well-suited to solving stochastic mixed-integer programs. A variety of issues arise when implementing PH in practice. We investigate these issues in the context of a large-scale, real-world stochastic mixed-integer problems. We introduce a mathematically-based heuristic for setting the algorithm parameter, methods for detecting convergence, and novel techniques for convergence acceleration.
