Title: Multi-objective Optimization g for a Novel Batch Scheduling Problem
Author: Jun Pei, Ph.D, University of Florida, 401 Weil Hall P.O. Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32611-6595, United States of America, peijun@ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Xinbao Liu, Wenjuan Fan
Year: 2013
Abstract: This paper investigates a novel multi-objective model for a batch scheduling problem with mould capability constraint that minimizes both the total completion time of the jobs and the total cost of the moulds. We develops a new multi-objective algorithm based on the features of gravitational search algorithm. The computational results demonstrate that our proposed MOGSA algorithm outperforms NSGA-II and SPEA-II in terms of the convergence and diversity level of solutions.
