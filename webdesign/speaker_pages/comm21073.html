Title: An Augmented Lagrangian Method for Conic Convex Programming

Author: Necdet Serhat Aybat, Assistant Professor, The Pennsylvania State University, 220 Leonhard Bldg., University Park, PA, 16802, United States of America, nsa10@psu.edu

Coauthor(s): Garud Iyengar

Year: 2013

Abstract: We propose an algorithm for solving (P): min{g(x)+f(x): Ax-b in K, x in Q}, where g and f are convex, and f has a Lipschitz cont. gradient, K is a closed convex cone, and Q is a convex compact set s.t. (SP): min{g(x)+|x-x0|_2^2: x in Q} is easy to solve. We show that any limit point is optimal, and the dual iterates converges to a KKT point of (P); and for any e>0, the iterates are e-feasible and e-optimal within O(log(1/e)) iterations which require solving O(1/e log(1/e)) many (SP).
