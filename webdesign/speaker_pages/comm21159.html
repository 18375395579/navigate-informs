Title: A More Compact Polyhedral Formulation for Minimum Cut

Author: Ojas Parekh, Emory University, ojas@mathcs.emory.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: For a graph (V,E), existing linear formulations for the minimum cut problem require \Theta(|V||E|) variables and constraints. These formulations can be interpreted as a composition of |V|-1 polyhedra for minimum s-t cuts paralleling early algorithmic approaches to finding globally minimum cuts, which relied on |V|-1 calls to a minimum s-t cut algorithm. We present the first formulation to beat this bound, one that uses O(|V|^2) variables and O(|V|^3) constraints. Our formulation directly implies a smaller compact formulation equivalent to the subtour relaxation of the Traveling Salesman Problem. This talk covers research conducted jointly with Robert Carr, Greg Little, Goran Konjevod, and Venkatesh Natarajan.
