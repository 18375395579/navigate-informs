Title: Stochastic Dynamic Itinerary Interception Refueling Location for EV Taxi Charging Stations

Author: Jaeyoung Jung, Assistant Project Scientist, University of California, Irvine, Institute of Transportation Studies, 4000 Anteater Instruction and Research B, Irvine, CA, 92697-3600, United States of America, jyoungjung@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Joseph Chow, R. (Jay) Jayakrishnan, Ji Young Park

Year: 2013

Abstract: A facility location model for EV taxi charging station is proposed, which considers: (1) itinerary-interception; (2) stochastic demand as dynamic passenger requests; (3) queue delay at stations. A bi-level simulation-optimization solution method is used to combine upper level multi-server allocation model and lower level dispatch simulation. The solution algorithm is tested on a fleet of 600 shared-taxis in Korea, a budget of 100 stations, and 22 locations, against a benchmark genetic algorithm.
