Title: Selecting a Small Set of Diverse Solutions to Mixed Integer Programming Problems

Author: David Woodruff, Professor, University of California, GSM, One Shields Avenue, Davis, CA, 95616, United States, dlwoodruff@ucdavis.edu

Coauthor(s): Emilie Danna

Year: 2007

Abstract: When solving a mixed-integer programming model, it is useful to obtain multiple feasible solutions of good quality rather than just one optimal solution. However, it is often impractical in applications to make use of a very large number of solutions. Instead, a small set of diverse solutions is more valuable. Given an oracle that generates a large number of solutions (CPLEX), we introduce several algorithms to select a small subset of solutions with high diversity.
