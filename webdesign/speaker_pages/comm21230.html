Title: Bounding the Range of Implicit Functions

Author: Paul I Barton, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Process Systems Engineering Laboratory, 77 Massachusetts Ave, Rm 66-464, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, pib@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Achim Wechsung

Year: 2013

Abstract: Deterministic global optimization methods are known to scale worst-case exponentially with the number of variables. Reduced-space approaches attempt to counter this by reducing the number of variables branched on, e.g., by using equality constraints to define implicit functions. In light of the cluster effect, it is important to bound their range using methods that have quadratic convergence order. Here, we present recent advances designed with this key property in mind.
