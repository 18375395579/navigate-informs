Title: A Scenario-based Branch-and-cut Algorithm for Stochastic Programs with Probabilistic Constraints

Author: Matthew Tanner, Texas A&M University, Zachry Hall, College Station, TX, 77842, United States, mtanner@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Lewis Ntaimo

Year: 2007

Abstract: We present reformulations of deterministic equivalents to stochastic programs with discretely distributed probabilistic constraints. We derive new cuts for this problem based upon irreducibly infeasible subsets of the constraint set. Finally, we present a scenario-based branch-and-cut implementation that includes these cuts and others from prior research. We give computational results using test instances from optimal vaccine allocation.
