Title: Using Renewable Energy to Power Hi-tech Manufacturing Facilities: A Simulation Study

Author: Jesus Jimenez, Associate Professor, Texas State University, Ingram School of Engineering, 601 University Dr, San Marcos, TX, 78666, United States of America, Jesus.Jimenez@txstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Victor Santana-Viera, Tongdan Jin

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this research, simulation and optimization models are applied for designing distributed energy generation systems, the paradigm for producing electricity through solar photovoltaic (PV) and wind turbines (WT). Distributed generation can be integrated into manufacturing facilities, particularly in semiconductor wafer fabs, seeking to reduce electricity costs and carbon footprints. A case study is presented to demonstrate opportunities and obstacles of the distributed generation approach.
