Title: Regularization for Constraint Reduction under Degeneracy

Author: Luke Winternitz, University of Maryland, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, 20770, United States of America, lukewinternitz@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Andre Tits

Year: 2008

Abstract: Constraint-reduced primal-dual interior-point algorithms for linear programming consider only small working subsets of the (dual) constraints in constructing search directions. Such algorithms can yield dramatic speedup on some classes of LPs with large numbers of inequality constraints. In this talk, we discuss a type of degeneracy that arises when applying constraint-reduced methods to even well-posed problems and we investigate a regularization designed to deal with the problem.
