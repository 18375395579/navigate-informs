Title: Integrating Short-term Unit Commitment Constraints into Transmission Planning

Author: Saamrat Kasina, Student, Johns Hopkins University, 3400 North Charles Street, Ames Hall 319, Baltimore, MD, 21218, United States of America, k.saamrat@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Francisco Munoz, Benjamin Hobbs

Year: 2012

Abstract: Transmission planning studies for renewable integration usually apply heuristic methods to decide which transmission lines to build, but optimization has been proposed. We ask whether operational constraints such as plant ramp-rates, zonal reserve requirements, and start-up decisions would significantly affect investments chosen by a two-stage stochastic transmission optimal expansion model. The value of integrating these features (in terms of improved expected performance) is also estimated.
