Title: An Approximate Dynamic Programming Approach to a Rolling-horizon Appointment Scheduling Problem

Author: Dan Zhang, University of Colorado at Boulder, 995 Regent Dr, Boulder, CO, 80305, United States of America, Dan.Zhang@Colorado.EDU

Coauthor(s): Thomas Vossen

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider approximation strategies for a rolling horizon appointment scheduling problem formulated as an infinite horizon discount dynamic program. First, we show that the approximate linear program from affine functional approximation admits a compact representation. Second, we consider a relaxation based on probabilistic allocation of demand, which can be solved as a one-dimensional dynamic program. Numerical results are provided.
