Title: Reclaimed Water Network Design via Stochastic Integer Programming

Author: Weini Zhang, Systems and Industrial Engineering, University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ, 85721, United States of America, wzhang@email.arizona.edu

Coauthor(s): Guzin Bayraksan, Gunhui Chung, Kevin Lansey

Year: 2012

Abstract: We model a reclaimed water distribution network for supplying treated water to the public users or agricultural areas using two-stage stochastic binary programming with random recourse. Both construction and energy costs expanded are considered. The network has spatial growth and reclaimed water demands are uncertain. A specialized algorithm is developed to exploit the structure of the network to efficiently solve the problem. Computational results are presented on a realistic problem.
