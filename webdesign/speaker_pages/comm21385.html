Title: Maximizing Resilience of Airport Runway and Taxiway Pavement Networks through Stochastic Programming

Author: Reza Faturechi, PhD Candidate, University of Maryland, 1173 Glenn L. Martin Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, reza@umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Xiaodong Zhang, Eyal Levenberg, Elise Miller-Hooks

Year: 2012

Abstract: A two-stage binary integer, stochastic program and integer L-shaped method proposed for its solution are presented for assessing and maximizing the resilience of an airport given the possibility of future, randomly arising events affecting airport runway and taxiway pavement networks. Their application provides insights into optimal allocation of limited resources for response and preparedness actions that facilitate them. The approach is demonstrated on a case study involving Laguardia Airport.
