Title: Scheduling with Multitasking

Author: Nicholas Hall, Professor, Ohio State University, 2100 Neil Avenue, Columbus, OH, United States of America, hall.33@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Joseph Leung, Chung-Lun Li

Year: 2012

Abstract: This paper considers scheduling under multitasking, i.e. the processing of a selected job is interrupted by other jobs. Multitasking occurs in administration, project management, and manufacturing. Most classical solution methods no longer apply under multitasking. We develop optimal algorithms for several classical single machine problems under multitasking. For one intractable problem, we describe and test a heuristic. For four problems, we study the cost increase due to multitasking.
