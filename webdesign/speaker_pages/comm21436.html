Title: Impact of Different Interaction Patterns and Human Inconsistencies on Hybrid Evolutionary Multi-Objective Algorithm

Author: John Fowler, Professor and Department Head, Arizona State University, School of Business, Tempe, United States of America, John.Fowler@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Esma Gel, Jyrki Wallenius, Pekka Korhonen, Jone Marquis, Murat Koksalan

Year: 2013

Abstract: We investigate the impact of inconsistent DM responses and different interaction patterns on the convergence of an interactive, evolutionary multi-objective algorithm developed by authors. As test problems we use 2-4 objective knapsack problems, multi-criteria scheduling problems, and multi-objective facility location problems. The results indicate that the algorithm is robust to minor inconsistencies in DM responses. There is also surprising robustness towards different interaction patterns.
