Title: Minimizing Activity Costs in Networks with Resource Options

Author: George Vairaktarakis, Professor, Case Western Reserve University, CWRU, Cleveland, OH, United States of America, gxv5@case.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider the problem of minimizing the weighted flowtime of a set of activities processed by unrelated processors subject to network constraints. This problem subsumes important objectives like project duration, activity-in-progress costs, and manufacturing settings like flow shops and job shops. We develop efficient tight lower bounds, near-optimal heuristics, and perform extensive computational experiments to assess the performance of our algorithms on randomly generated networks.
