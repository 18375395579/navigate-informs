Title: High Performance Computation and Approximate Dynamic Programming for the Energy Storage Problem

Author: Somayeh Moazeni, Research Associate, Princeton University, Sherrerd Hall, Charlton Street, Princeton, NJ, 08536, United States of America, somayeh@princeton.edu

Coauthor(s): Warren Powell, Hugo Simao

Year: 2012

Abstract: The energy storage problem deals with finding the flow of electricity among a grid operator with stochastic prices, a wind generator with stochastic supply, and a storage device, to maximize the total revenue and meet a stochastic demand. Direct policy search and a model reference adaptive search are then adopted to solve the resulting optimization problem. Parallel implementations on a multi-cluster supercomputer are explored and numerical experiments to demonstrate scalability are provided.
