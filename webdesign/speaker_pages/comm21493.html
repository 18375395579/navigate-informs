Title: Applications of A Probability Programming Language (APPL)

Author: Diane Evans, diane.evans@rose-hulman.edu

Coauthor(s): Lawrence Leemis, Andrew Glen, John Drew

Year: 2007

Abstract: Probability theory uses algebra and calculus to solve problems involving random variables from the sciences, engineering, and business. Many results in probability have been derived as a means to approximate quantities that are difficult to determine exactly due to lengthy or intractable mathematics. This talk introduces the Maple-based software tool APPL that was designed to manipulate random variables. The functionality of APPL will be highlighted through examples and applications.
