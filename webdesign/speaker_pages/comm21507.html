Title: Reformulation Techniques for Solving Non-linear Workforce Planning Models with Learning
Author: Mike Hewitt, Assistant Professor, Loyola University Chicago, 1 E. Pearson, Chicago, IL, 60611, United States of America, mrheie@rit.edu
Coauthor(s): Scott Grasman, Austin Chacosky, Barrett Thomas
Year: 2013
Abstract: The literature on human learning suggests that quantitative, descriptive models of human learning are non-linear. As a result, prescriptive planning models are difficult to solve. We present a reformulation technique that models non-linear learning functions with sets of binary and linear variables and constraints. With this technique, we are able to solve problems in reasonable runtimes that are significantly larger than what has been previously possible.
