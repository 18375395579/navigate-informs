Title: In Pursuit of a Root

Author: Michael Friedlander, Prof., University of British Columbia, 2366 Main Mall, Vancouver, BC, Canada, mpf@cs.ubc.ca

Coauthor(s): Ewout van den Berg

Year: 2008

Abstract: The basis pursuit problem seeks a minimum 1-norm solution of an underdetermined least-squares problem. Basis pursuit denoise fits the least-squares problem only approximately. A single parameter determines a curve that traces the optimal trade-off between the least-squares fit and the 1-norm of the solution. We show how to derive a root-finding algorithm for finding arbitrary points on the optimal trade-off curve. We give extensions to more general norms, including complex-valued problems.
