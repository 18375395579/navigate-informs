Title: A Wide Branching Strategy for Branch-and-Price Algorithms

Author: David Morrison, University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, Siebel Center, Dept. of Computer Science, 201 N. Goodwin Ave., Urbana, IL, 61821, United States of America, morris50@illinois.edu

Coauthor(s): Sheldon Jacobson, Jason Sauppe, Edward Sewell

Year: 2012

Abstract: The usual branching strategy for branch-and-price algorithms forces a single variable to either 0 or 1 and attempts to preserve the pricing problem structure. When this structure cannot be preserved, a wide branching strategy, which branches on all available variables and assigns them value 1, can be used. This strategy uses delayed branching techniques to limit the search tree width to a manageable size, and is competitive with the best-known solvers for graph coloring problems.
