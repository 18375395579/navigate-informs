Title: Heuristic Solution Algorithms for a Traffic Sensor Optimization Problem

Author: Praveen Edara, Assistant Professor, University of Missouri-Columbia, E 3502 Lafferre Hall, Dept of Civil &Environmental Engineering, Columbia, MO, 65211, United States of America, EdaraP@missouri.edu

Coauthor(s): Milica Selmic, Dusan Teodorovic

Year: 2008

Abstract: A traffic sensor placement problem addressing the travel time estimation on freeways is studied. Two relatively new heuristic algorithms, 1) Variable Neighborhood Search and 2) Bee-Colony Optimization are explored in a case study of Interstate 66 in Northern Virginia. Ground truth travel times were obtained using GPS probe vehicles driven during the peak periods. The performed numerical experiments show that the proposed algorithms generate high-quality solutions in reasonable CPU times.
