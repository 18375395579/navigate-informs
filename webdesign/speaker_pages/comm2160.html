Title: An Interactive Evolutionary Approach Based on Convex Preference Cones to Multiobjective Optimization

Author: John Fowler, Professor of Industrial Engineering, Arizona State University, PO Box 87-5906, Tempe, AZ, 85287-5906, United States, John.Fowler@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jon Marquis, Jyrki Wallenius, Esma Gel, Pekka Korhonen, Murat Köksalan

Year: 2006

Abstract: We present an interactive evolutionary algorithm for multiobjective combinatorial optimization problems. The algorithm seeks to find the solution most preferred by the decision maker (DM). The fitness of a solution reflects preference information elicited from the DM and is based on a partial order obtained from pairwise preference comparisons of solutions and implied preferences based on preference cones. We describe test results for randomly generated multidimensional knapsack problems.
