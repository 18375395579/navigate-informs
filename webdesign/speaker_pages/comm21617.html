Title: Scheduling Meets Random Graphs: A Probabilistic Analysis of Precedence-Constrained Scheduling

Author: Nelson Uhan, School of Industrial Engineering, Purdue University, 315 N. Grant Street, Grissom Hall 262, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States of America, nuhan@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Andreas S. Schulz

Year: 2008

Abstract: We study the classic precedence-constrained single-machine scheduling problem with the weighted sum of completion times objective. In particular, we study so-called 0-1 bipartite instances of this problem, whose approximability is virtually identical to the approximability of arbitrary instances (Woeginger 2003). We use random graph models to show various almost all-type results for these instances, including that almost always, all feasible schedules are arbitrarily close to optimal.
