Title: A New Relative Robust Optimization Approach for Full Factorial Scenario Design of Data Uncertainty

Author: Tiravat Assavapokee, Assistant Professor, Industrial Engineering Department, University of Houston, 206 Engineering Building 2, Houston, TX, 77204-4008, United States, tiravat.assavapokee@mail.uh.edu

Coauthor(s): Jane Ammons, Matthew Realff

Year: 2006

Abstract: We apply bilevel programming to develop an effective relative robust optimization algorithm for two stage decision making under uncertainty. The first stage is 0-1 MILP and the second stage is LP. Each uncertain parameter can independently take its values from a finite set of real numbers with unknown probability distribution. The algorithm solves the relative robust optimization problem with 1.22 x 10^19 scenarios. All results illustrate impressive performance of the proposed algorithm.
