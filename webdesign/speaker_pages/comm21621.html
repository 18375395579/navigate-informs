Title: Very Large-Scale Neighborhood Search for the K-Constraint Multiple Knapsack Problem

Author: Claudio Cunha, Associate Professor, University of Sao Paulo, Av. Prof. Almeida Prado, Trav.2, n,83, Sao Paulo, SP, 05508-970, Brazil, cbcunha@usp.br

Coauthor(s): Ravindra Ahuja

Year: 2006

Abstract: The K-Constraint Multiple Knapsack Problem (K-MKP) is a generalization of the multiple knapsack problem. In K-MKP, each item has K types of weights and each knapsack has K types of capacity. We describe very large-scale neighborhood search (VLSN) algorithms to solve K-MKP, including an approach that consists of randomly perturbing the current solution in order to efficiently produce a set of simultaneous non-profitable. Computational results are also presented.
