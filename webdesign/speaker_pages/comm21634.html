Title: Long-term Planning for an Underground Mine
Author: Michel Gamache, Professor, Polytechnique Montréal, C.P. 6079 succ. Centre-Ville, Montreal, QC, H3C3A7, Canada, michel.gamache@polymtl.ca
Coauthor(s): Jean Collard
Year: 2012
Abstract: In this presentation, we will present a model of long-term planning for Raglan Mine, a large nickel mining complex in the Nunavik region at the extreme limit of Northern Quebec, Canada. We will discuss the mixed integer linear programming model and the constraints that are related to the various operating characteristics of this mine in the sub-Arctic Circle. Results of solutions will be presented.
