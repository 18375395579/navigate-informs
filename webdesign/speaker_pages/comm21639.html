Title: Inventory Rebalancing and Vehicle Routing in Bike Sharing Systems

Author: Willem-Jan van Hoeve, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, United States of America, vanhoeve@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jasper Schuijbroek, Robert Hampshire

Year: 2013

Abstract: A major cost driver in bike sharing systems is rebalancing the bike inventory at the stations over time. We propose a new cluster-first route-second heuristic in which the polynomial-size clustering problem simultaneously considers the service level requirements and approximate routing costs. Extensive computational results on real-world data are provided, which show that our heuristic outperforms a pure mixed integer programming formulation and a constraint programming approach.
