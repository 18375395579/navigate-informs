Title: Optimization Models to Control Infectious Disease Spread in Networks

Author: Apurba Nandi, Graduate Research Assistant, Mississippi State University, 1000 Campus trails Apt 151, Starkville, MS, 39759, United States of America, akn77@msstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Hugh Medal

Year: 2013

Abstract: Minimizing the spread of an infectious disease in a network using limited resources is a challenging problem. We describe several mixed-integer programming models that seek to minimize the expected number of new infections during an outbreak by strategically removing links. Then, we compare the results of our models with those of an existing optimization model. Finally, we develop an algorithm to solve larger problems and show that it outperforms two well-known link selection heuristics.
