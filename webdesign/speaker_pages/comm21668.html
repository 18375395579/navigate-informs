Title: High-contrast Imaging via Fast Fourier Optimization
Author: Robert Vanderbei, Professor, Princeton University, Operations Research & Financial Engineer, 209 Sherrerd Hall, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States of America, rvdb@princeton.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: Many interesting and fundamentally practical optimization problems involve constraints on the Fourier transform of a function. Because the fast Fourier transform is recursive, it is difficult to embed into a linear optimization problem. However, one can adapt the main idea behind the fast Fourier transform so as to make it encodable as constraints in an optimization problem. We demonstrate on a real-world problem from the field of high-contrast imaging.
