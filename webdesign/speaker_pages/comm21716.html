Title: An AC-Feasible Linear Approximation Approach to Finding the Optimal Power Flow

Author: Paula Lipka, University of California-Berkeley, 1035 Kains Ave, Apt 1, Albany, CA, 94706, United States of America, plipka@berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Shmuel Oren

Year: 2013

Abstract: Large energy markets use the solution to the optimal power flow (OPF) problem to determine wholesale energy prices and dispatch of generators. We use a linear approximation of the voltage-current alternating current OPF formulation to find an AC-feasible solution. This method finds solutions faster than and yields total power costs close to the nonlinear benchmark. We also explore incorporating transmission switching into this approach to produce AC feasible transmission switching solutions.
