Title: A Globally Convergent Primal-dual Active-set Framework for Large-scale Convex Quadratic Optimization

Author: Zheng Han, Ph.D. Candidate, ISE, Lehigh University, 200 West Packer Avenue, Bethlehem, PA, 18015, United States of America, zhh210@lehigh.edu

Coauthor(s): Frank Curtis, Daniel Robinson

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a primal-dual active-set framework for large-scale convex quadratic optimization that can make multiple simultaneous changes in the active-set estimate and converge from arbitrary initial points. Each iteration of the framework involves solving a reduced subproblem, the cost of which can be reduced by incorporating inexactness. Preliminary results illustrate that this method often leads to rapid identification of the optimal active-set even for poorly conditioned problems.
