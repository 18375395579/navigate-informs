Title: Intelligent Mechanisms to Escape Local Optima
Author: Michele Samorani, PhD Candidate, Leeds School of Business, Bldg #4, UCB 419, Boulder, CO, 80309-0419, United States of America, Michael.Samorani@Colorado.EDU
Coauthor(s): Manuel Laguna
Year: 2008
Abstract: Existing mechanisms to escape local optimality, such as randomization, restarting and neighborhood modifications, are not strategically designed to lead the search to promising regions of the solution space. We propose the application of data mining techniques to discover effective escape directions from a training set of problem instances with known optimal solutions. Tests on a set of task allocation problems are conducted to assess the merit of our proposal.
