Title: Made-to-Order Production Scheduling using Bayesian Updating

Author: Keith Womer, Professor, University of Missouri, College of Business Administration, One University Blvd, St. Louis, MO, 63121, United States of America, womerk@umsl.edu

Coauthor(s): J Camm, C. Osterman, R. Radhakrishnan, Haitao Li

Year: 2013

Abstract: Production planning for made-to-order (MTO) manufacturing optimizes the production rate in each period before a promised delivery date. As products in MTO are often unique and customized, the associated learning curve slope and other production parameters cannot be accurately estimated before production starts. In this paper, a dynamic and adaptive solution approach is developed to estimate the effects of learning and production rate in conjunction with optimization. This approach offers a closed-loop solution through dynamic programming. Monthly production data are used to update the joint probability distributions of production parameters via Bayesian methods. Our approach is illustrated using historical data from the U.S. Army’s Black Hawk Helicopter Program. Managerial insights are obtained and discussed.
