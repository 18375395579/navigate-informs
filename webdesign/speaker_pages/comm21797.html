Title: Algorithms for Network Interdiction Under Suboptimal Enemy Behavior
Author: J. Cole Smith, Associate Professor, University of Florida, ISE Department, 303 Weil Hall, Gainesville, FL, 32611-6595, United States, cole@ise.ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Churlzu Lim
Year: 2006
Abstract: We examine the problem of fortifying networks to defend against enemy attacks. The enemy can destroy any portion of any arc, yielding a two-level program with an integer first-stage and a bilinear second-stage problem. We also discuss and provide algorithms for the case of suboptimal enemy behavior.
