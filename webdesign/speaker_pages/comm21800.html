Title: Rectangle Stabbing, Scheduling Split Intervals, Lot Sizing, Rectilinear Partitioning, and Topology

Author: Will Ma, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Operations Research Center, Cambridge, MA, United States of America, willma353@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): S. Ben-David, Michael Sharpe, E. Grant

Year: 2012

Abstract: We obtain an LP-relative d-approximation for the Rectangle Stabbing problem in d dimensions. This problem is important for Lot Sizing and Rectilinear Partitioning, and is a celebrated application of Topological methods, which we turn into an algorithm. Finally, we construct asymptotically tight integrality gap lower bounds for both Rectangle Stabbing and the dual problem of Scheduling Split Intervals, proving that the current approximation ratios cannot be improved by LP-based approaches.
