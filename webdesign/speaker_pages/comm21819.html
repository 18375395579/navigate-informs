Title: Clustering Multiple Instance Data
Author: Erhun Kundakcioglu, University of Florida, 303 Weil Hall P.O. Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States of America, erhun@ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Onur Seref, Panos Pardalos
Year: 2007
Abstract: The multiple instance (MI) setting was introduced by Dietterich et al. for drug activity prediction and became a popular concept in supervised learning applications where incomplete information is available. We propose a novel MI setting for clustering, where a subset of data points are to be neglected during the regular clustering procedure. This approach is useful for biomedical applications where the aim is to discover the time of stimulus and detect the semantic features.
