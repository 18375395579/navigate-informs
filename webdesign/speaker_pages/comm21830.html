Title: Trimmed and Winsorized Means for Aggregating Probability Forecasts

Author: Victor Richmond Jose, Assitant Professor, Georgetown University, McDonough School of Business, 37th and O Sts NW, Washington, DC, 20057, United States of America, vrj2@georgetown.edu

Coauthor(s): Yael Grushka-Cockayne, Casey Lichtendahl

Year: 2012

Abstract: The literature on aggregating point forecasts has shown that trimmed & Winsorized means can offer some improvements to the simple mean. Here, we extend this notion to the case of prob forecasts by introducing two simple methods for trimming & Winsorizing. Using data from the SPF, we compare these robust means to the mean and find that therobust means outperform the simple mean when it exhibits underconfidence. We also propose a novel interior approach to deal with the case of overconfidence.
