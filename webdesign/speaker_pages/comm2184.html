Title: Dynamic System Optimal for Multiple O-D Networks Embedding Cell Transmission Model

Author: Kien Doan, Purdue University, 550 Stadium Mall Drive, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, dtkien@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Satish Ukkusuri

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this paper, the route-choice System Optimal Dynamic Traffic Assignment (SO-DTA) problem for general network of multiple O-D pairs is formulated. We incorporate the Path-based Cell Transmission Model as the underlying network loading procedure to propagate traffic from different paths of different O-Ds. This model guarantees that the traffic moves without holding-back phenomenon. Since the general optimization solvers are not able to solve the SO-DTA problem for medium-size network, we propose a heuristic algorithm based on method of successive average which can solve the SO-DTA problem for general networks. Extensive experiment results will be shown to illustrate the benefits of the proposed model and algorithm
