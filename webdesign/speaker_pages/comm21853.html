Title: Using the PATH Solver with Open-Source Factorization Routines
Author: Michael Ferris, Computer Sciences Department, University of Wisconsin, 1210 West Dayton Street, Madison, WI, 53706, United States of America, ferris@cs.wisc.edu
Coauthor(s): Qian Li
Year: 2008
Abstract: The PATH solver is designed for solving Mixed Complementarity Problems and has been used extensively for problems in applied general equilibrium, economics and engineering. It is currently not open-source due to the underlying linear solver technology that is used for pivoting. The extension of PATH to incorporate a number of alternatives to the current (LUSOL) routines will be described, along with their benefits and drawbacks on a number of test problems arising in the literature.
