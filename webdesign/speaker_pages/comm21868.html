Title: Randomized Heuristics for Handover Minimization in Mobility Networks

Author: Mauricio Resende, AT&T Labs Research, 180 Park Avenue, C241, Florham Park, NJ, 07932, United States of America, mgcr@research.att.com

Coauthor(s): Luis Morán-Mirabal, Jos?Luis González-Velarde, Ricardo SIlva

Year: 2013

Abstract: The Handover Minimization Problem (HMP) in Mobility Networks assigns base stations (BS) to radio network controllers (RNC) such that RNC capacity is not violated and the number of handovers between BSs connected to different RNCs is minimized. We show that state-of-the-art IP solvers fail on all but the smallest instances of the HMP. We propose several heuristics for the HMP, including a GRASP with path-relinking (PR), a GRASP with evolutionary PR, and a biased random-key genetic algorithm.
