Title: Single Array Designs for Robustness Studies

Author: Lulu Kang, Ph.D Student, H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering, 350440 Gatech Station, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States, lulu@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Roshan Vengazhiyil

Year: 2007

Abstract: In robust parameter design, single array designs usually have much smaller run size than the cross array designs and thus are more economical. But designing efficient single arrays is hard, because it is very important to estimate interactions between control and noise factors for parameter design. In this work we show that the importance of effects and hierarchy of effects are two different concepts and propose a Bayesian approach to integrate the two concepts in the design of single arrays.
