Title: Inverse Mixed Integer Linear Programming

Author: Lizhi Wang, Iowa State University, 3016 Black Engineering, Iowa State University, Ames, IA, 50011, United States of America, lzwang@iastate.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: An inverse optimization problem is to minimally perturb the objective function of a mathematical program to make a feasible solution optimal. Inverse linear programming has been well studied. This paper presents cutting plane and heuristic algorithms for inverse mixed integer linear programming. Computational experiment results are reported.
