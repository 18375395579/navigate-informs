Title: On a Class of Bilevel Linear Assignment Problems

Author: Vladimir Stozhkov, University of Florida, 303 Weil Hall, Gainesville, FL, 32611-6595, United States of America, vstozhkov@ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Vladimir Boginski, Eduardo Pasiliao, Oleg Prokopyev

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider a bilevel extension of the linear assignment problem. The follower solves an assignment problem maximizing his profit, while the leader may interdict removing some of the nodes of the graph. The leader’s task is to minimize the total cost given by the cost of interdiction plus the cost of the assignments made by the follower. The problem is shown to be NP-hard. We also derive equivalent linear mixed 0-1 programming reformulations and discuss possible heuristic solution approaches.
