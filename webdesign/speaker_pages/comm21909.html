Title: Optimal Information Blending with Measurements in the L-2 Sphere

Author: Boris Defourny, Post-doctoral associate, Princeton University, Sherrerd Hall, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States of America, defourny@princeton.edu

Coauthor(s): Ilya Ryzhov, Warren Powell

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a sequential information collection problem where a risk-averse decision-maker updates a Bayesian belief about the unknown objective function of a linear program. The information comes as a linear combination of the objective coefficients, to be selected. Because the belief determines the uncertainty set of a robust linear program used to optimize a worst-case performance, the information collection problem is affected by the possible evolution of the uncertainty set.
