Title: Constrained Optimization of Computer Experiments Using an Expected Improvement Criterion

Author: Brian Williams, Los Alamos National Laboratory, brianw@lanl.gov

Coauthor(s): Thomas Santner, William Notz, Jeffrey Lehman

Year: 2006

Abstract: In a computer experiment, deterministic responses are computed according to an experimental design of input settings. We introduce a sequential design for computer experiments where the goal is to find an input setting optimizing one output subject to a constraint determined by a second output. The idea of the methods is to compute the posterior expected improvement over the current optimum for each untested site; the design selects the next site to maximize the expected improvement.
