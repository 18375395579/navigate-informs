Title: Computational Stochastic Programming for Trade Execution under Downside Risk Measures

Author: Somayeh Moazeni, Princeton University, Princeton, NJ, United States of America, somayeh@Princeton.EDU

Coauthor(s): Thomas Coleman, Warren Powell, Yuying Li

Year: 2013

Abstract: Computing an optimal stochastic dynamic programming solution under a downside risk measure and constraints is a challenging task. We develop two tractable computational approaches to derive approximate stochastic trading strategies in the presence of temporary and permanent price impacts, possibly several constraints, and under a downside risk measure particularly VaR or CVaR. We compare performance of these approaches and discuss structure of computed trading strategies and their costs.
