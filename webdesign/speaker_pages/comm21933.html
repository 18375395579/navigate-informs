Title: A Python Environment for Nonlinear Programming
Author: Dominique Orban, Professor, Ecole Polytechnique de Montréal, Math & Industrial Eng Department, 2900 Boulevard Edouard Montpetit, Montréal, H3T 1J4, Canada, dominique.orban@polymtl.ca
Coauthor(s): Michael Friedlander
Year: 2008
Abstract: NLPy is an environment that eases development of numerical methods for linear and nonlinear programming. The far reaches of object-oriented programming and the power of the Python language promote reusability, readability and rapid development without sacrificing numerical efficiency. NLPy provides the building blocks on which optimization methods are built. We illustrate the implementation of some well-known and novel algorithms routinely used in optimization and their performance.
