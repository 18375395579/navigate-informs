Title: Computing Bounds and Solutions to the Traveling Salesman Problem with Approximate Linear Programming

Author: Michael Poremba, Ph.D. Student, Industrial and Systems Engineering, University of Southern California, 3715 McClintock Ave, GER 240, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, poremba@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Alejandro Toriello

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study the asymmetric traveling salesman problem (TSP) using approximate linear programming (ALP) to restrict the dynamic programming formulation. The second author has previously applied ALP to the TSP to obtain a nested family of polyhedral lower bounds. We discuss two computational methods for solving these formulations, constraint generation and a primal-dual procedure. We also present a price-directed tour generation algorithm that uses ALP solutions as approximate costs-to-go.
