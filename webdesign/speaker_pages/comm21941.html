Title: Exploring Stochastic Optimization Solutions with Underspecified Discrete Joint Distributions.

Author: Luis Montiel, PhD, The University of Texas as Austin, 1 University Station, Austin, TX, 78703, United States of America, lvmontiel@gmail.com

Year: 2012

Abstract: We address the problem of optimization under uncertainty when the joint probability mass function (pmf) is underspecified. In such cases the pmf is itself a random vector with support in a convex polytope. Our approach uses a simulation procedure (JDSIM) that explores these polytopes and create a sampled collection of distributions. We show that JDSIM lead to solutions that in expectation provide better results than the traditional robust approach.
