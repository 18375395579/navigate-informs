Title: Optimal Job Splitting on a Multi-slot Machine with Applications in the Printing Industry
Author: Ali Ekici, Ph.D Student, H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332-0205, United States, aekici@isye.gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Pinar Keskinocak, Ozlem Ergun, Michail Lagoudakis
Year: 2007
Abstract: Motivated by practices in the printing industry, our goal is to assign the demand for n items to an m-slot machine to minimize the waste and setup cost. We show that the problem is NP-hard and propose two linear integer programming formulations, several preprocessing steps, and two heuristics. Extensive tests on real life and randomly generated instances show that the heuristics are both fast and effective, finding near-optimal solutions.
