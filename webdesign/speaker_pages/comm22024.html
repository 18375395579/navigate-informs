Title: Semi-definite Programming Based Receding Horizon Methods for Dynamic Hedging

Author: James Primbs, Assistant Professor, Stanford University, 444 Terman Engr. Ctr., Mail Code 4026, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, japrimbs@stanford.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: In this talk we present a receding horizon approach to dynamic hedging of high dimensional options using semi-definite programming methods. The receding horizon approach solves on-line optimization problems at each time in order to construct a policy. We show that the dynamic hedging problem for various options can be formulated so that the on-line optimizations are semi-definite programs. Thus, we are able to effectively hedge high dimensional options. Examples illustrate the approach.
