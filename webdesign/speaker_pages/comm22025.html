Title: Strengthened Quadratic Programming Bounds for QAP

Author: Kurt Anstreicher, Professor, Dept. of Management Sciences, University of Iowa, S210 PBB, Iowa City, IA, 52242, United States of America, kurt-anstreicher@uiowa.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: The Quadratic Programming Bound (QPB) has proved useful in solving large-scale QAP problems to optimality within a Branch-and-Bound (B&B) framework. For many problems QPB provides a good quality bound at modest computational expense. However it would be desirable to strengthen QPB on problems where the bound is less good, especially near the root of the B&B tree. We describe methodologies for strengthening QPB that continue to utilize convex quadratic programming for the bound computation.
