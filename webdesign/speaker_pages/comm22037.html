Title: Cutting-plane Representations of Convex Functions Over Nonconvex Sets

Author: Daniel Bienstock, Columbia University, 500 W 120th St, New York, United States of America, dano@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Alexander Michalka

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider sets of the form conv(S), where S = {(x, q) : q >= Q(x), x in F} where Q is an arbitrary convex function and F is an arbitrary closed set in R^n. When Q is differentiable (and in other cases) we show that conv(S) can be obtained by lifting (in the mixed-integer programming sense) the gradient approximation to Q(x) on points of the boundary of F. We describe some cases where this leads to polynomial-time separation and present computational experiments.
