Title: Determining Protein Structures by Semidefinite Programming
Author: Nathan Krislock, INRIA Grenoble Rhône-Alpes, 655 Avenue de l'Europe, Montbonnot, Saint-Ismier Cedex, 38334, France, nathan.krislock@inria.fr
Coauthor(s): Babak Alipanahi, Ali Ghodsi, Henry Wolkowicz, Logan Donaldson, Ming Li
Year: 2012
Abstract: Euclidean distance matrix methods based on semidefinite programming (SDP) are a natural approach for protein structure determination. Our contribution is a new SDP formulation that overcomes the difficulty of the high complexity of SDP solvers by using facial reduction to reduce the problem to approximately one quarter the size of the original problem. The reduced SDP can be solved approximately 100 times faster, and it is more resistant to numerical problems from erroneous and noisy distances.
