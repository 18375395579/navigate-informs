Title: A Dynamic Evacuation Network Optimization Problem: Lane Reversal and Crossing Elimination

Author: Chi Xie, Research Fellow, The University of Texas at Austin, 1 University Station, C1761, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, chi.xie@mail.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Dung-Ying Lin, Travis Waller

Year: 2008

Abstract: We define and solve a dynamic evacuation planning problem with two planning strategies: lane reversal and crossing elimination. The formulated network optimization model has a bi-level structure: the upper-level part is aimed at optimizing the allocation of network capacity and connectivity; the lower-level problem serves as a network flow evaluation framed into a cell transmission model. An integrated Lagrangian relaxation and tabu search method is developed to search for optimal solutions.
