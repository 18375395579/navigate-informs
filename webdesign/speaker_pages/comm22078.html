Title: A Branch-and-cut Algorithm to Solve a Kidney Exchange Model

Author: Amin Dehghanian, PhD student, University of Pittsburgh, Department of Industrial Engineering, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States of America, AMD120@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Murat Kurt, Andrew Schaefer

Year: 2012

Abstract: We propose a decomposition based branch-and-cut algorithm to solve a large scale two-stage stochastic mixed binary linear program available in the literature for a kidney exchange problem. We are using the idea of minimal infeasible subsystem cut generation in the literature. In addition, we present two classes of combinatorial valid inequalities. We also dynamically update notorious big-M coefficients and binary variables as the branch-and-cut algorithm proceeds.
