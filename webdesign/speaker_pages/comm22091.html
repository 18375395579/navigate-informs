Title: Cook, Kannan and Schrijver's Example Revisited
Author: Yanjun Li, Assistant Professor, Purdue University, 403 W. State Street, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States, li14@purdue.edu
Coauthor(s): Jean-Philippe Richard
Year: 2006
Abstract: Cook, Kannan and Schrijver (1990) studied the split closure of polyhedron. They showed on a 3-dimensional example that the 1-term disjunctive rank of mixed-integer polytopes can be infinite. We generalize this example to a family of high-dimensional polytopes and present a close-form description of the split closures of all ranks for these polytopes. We conjecture that the k-term disjunctive rank of the (m+1)-dimensional polytope in this family is infinite for any k
