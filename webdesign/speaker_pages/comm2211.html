Title: A Hybrid Methodology for Freeway Work Zone Optimization with Time Constraints
Author: Ning Yang, University of Maryland, Department of CEE, Glenn L. Martin Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, ningyang@umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Paul Schonfeld
Year: 2007
Abstract: In this paper, optimization techniques are applied to determine the appropriate work zone lane-closure schedules and traffic control strategies which can minimize the total costs, subject to working time constraints. A heuristic optimization algorithm, named two-stage modified simulated annealing (2SA), is developed to search for an optimized solution. A macroscopic analytic model and a microscopic simulation program are used to evaluate the objective function in the two stages, respectively.
