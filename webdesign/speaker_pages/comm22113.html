Title: Kinked Linear Utility Functions in Portfolio Optimization
Author: Michael J. Best, Professor of Mathematics, University of Waterloo, Waterloo, ON, N2L 3G1, Canada, mjbest@math.uwaterloo.ca
Coauthor(s): Robert R. Grauer, Jaroslava Hlouskova
Year: 2007
Abstract: In the portfolio selection problem, utility functions are typically differentiable. There has been recent interest in kinked, or non differentiable utility functions. We consider the problem of maximizing a kinked linear utility function subject to linear constraints. This can be formulated as a typically, large LP . We present a modification of an active set LP algorithm which efficiently solves this LP by doing calculations in the much smaller space of original problem assets.
