Title: Valuing Multiple Exercise Options using Term Structure Models and Approximate Dynamic Programming
Author: Selvaprabu Nadarajah, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, snadarajah@cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Francois Margot, Nicola Secomandi
Year: 2012
Abstract: We develop computationally efficient least squares Monte Carlo (LSM) and approximate linear programming (ALP) methods for valuing multiple exercise, such as energy swing and storage, options, using term structure price models. Our theoretical and numerical investigation shows the superiority of an LSM version based on value, rather than continuation value, function approximation. We also structurally relate our LSM and ALP methods. This suggests further research on optimal ALP relaxations.
