Title: Infinite-Horizon Dynamic Programming with Non-Convex Risk-Sensitive Measures
Author: Lin Kun, Research Assistant, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, United States of America, klin@umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Steve Marcus
Year: 2013
Abstract: The dynamic performance measure approach is a generalization of the classical work using expected value. One drawback of this approach is that it has only been developed for coherent performance measures, which exclude a large class of important non-convex performance measures. We remedy this drawback by proving the optimality of the dynamic programming equation for infinite-horizon problems with non-convex performance measures.
