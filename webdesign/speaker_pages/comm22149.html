Title: Network Equilibrium Pricing Under Uncertainty

Author: Fernando Ordonez, Assistant Professor, University of Southern California, 3715 McClintock Avenue, GER-240, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States, fordon@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Nicolas Stier-Moses

Year: 2006

Abstract: Traffic network models assume that utilities only depend on congestion, however in most applications they also include an uncertain component. We explore the use of tolls for managing traffic on networks and encouraging players to select socially-efficient paths. The solution concept we use is a robust Wardrop equilibrium in which players take into account the uncertainty by solving robust shortest path problems. We study conditions under which socially optimal solutions can be enforced.
