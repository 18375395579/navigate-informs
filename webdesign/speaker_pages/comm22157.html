Title: A Real-time Model for the Stochastic Vehicle Routing Problem

Author: Clara Novoa, Assistant Professor, Texas State University, Engineering and Technology Department, 601 University Drive, San Marcos, TX, 78666, United States, cn17@txstate.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: We develop a stochastic integer programming model for solving the vehicle routing problem with random customer demands in real-time. Every time a vehicle arrives to a new customer, we compare the actual demand against the range of predicted demands for which a current route plan remains valid and decide if it is necessary to solve the model again. We compare our solutions to ones from an approximate dynamic programming approach.
