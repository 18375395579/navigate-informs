Title: VLSN Search Approach for Load Planning Problem at Intermodal Rail Terminal

Author: Ashish Nemani, Research Assistant, University of Florida, 303 Weil Hall, P.O. Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32608, United States, aknemani@ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Krishna Jha, Ravindra Ahuja

Year: 2007

Abstract: The load planning problem at a rail terminal is to assign containers and trailers to the scheduled trains to maximize the train utilization. We formulate the problem as a multicommodity flow problem on an underlying network. We solve the problem using two variants of very large scale neighborhood search heuristics. We present the computational results comparing heuristics and exact approaches.
