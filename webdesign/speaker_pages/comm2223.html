Title: A Stochastic Integer Program for Prioritization

Author: David Morton, Associate Professor, The University of Texas at Austin, Graduate Program in Operations Research, Austin, TX, United States, morton@mail.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Ali Koc, Dennis Michalopolous

Year: 2007

Abstract: Priority lists are common in industry and government. In contrast, a combinatorial optimization problem with a knapsack constraint takes as input a budget and binary activity costs. Such models provide a prioritization only in that an activity is selected or not. If the budget or activity costs are uncertain when selecting the activities then it is sensible to create a partially-ordered priority list. We describe a stochastic integer program to do so in the context of motivating applications.
