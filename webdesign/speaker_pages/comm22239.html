Title: Component Design within Information Technology Service Portfolios: A Risk Management Perspective

Author: Ryan Sougstad, Carlson School of Management, University of Minnesota, 319 19th Ave S., Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States of America, sougs002@umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Robert Kauffman

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose a method to design information technology (IT) services which balances risk and return trade-offs between capabilities and future demand. We apply a technique building on value-at-risk methods from financial economics, IT Services Profit-at-Risk, to design IT service components. We consider the benefits of reuse vs. the quality and market differentiation of the IT services offerings. We present analytical results and simulations to derive optimal design strategies for providers.
