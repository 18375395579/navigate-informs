Title: On a Transportation Problem with Market Choice

Author: Pelin Damci-Kurt, The Ohio State University, 1971 Neil Avenue, 210 Baker Systems Building, Columbus, OH, 43210, United States of America, damci-kurt.1@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Simge Kucukyavuz, Santanu Dey

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study a transportation problem with market choice (TPMC) where suppliers choose a subset of potential markets to serve. We prove that the problem is NP-hard in general and present polynomially solvable cases. For a special case, we show that adding a constraint on the number of unsatisfied markets to the convex hull of solutions to TPMC gives an integral polyhedron. We present strong valid inequalities for TPMC and show their effectiveness in a branch-and-cut algorithm.
