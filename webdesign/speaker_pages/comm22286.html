Title: The Impact of Overfitting and Overgeneralization on the Classification Accuracy in Data Mining

Author: Huy Pham, Louisiana State University, Department of Computer Science, 298 Coates Hall, LSU, Baton Rouge, LA, 70803, United States, hpham15@lsu.edu

Coauthor(s): Evangelos Triantaphyllou

Year: 2007

Abstract: Many classification studies have been developed in the two last decades. No single method outperforms all methods all the time. The performance of a classification method in terms of its false-positive and false-negative rates may be unpredictable. The root to the problem is the overfitting and overgeneralization behaviors. However, it has not been studied from a comprehensive view. This study proposes the Homogeneity-Based Algorithm for optimally controlling the rates with the promising results.
