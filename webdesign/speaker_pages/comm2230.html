Title: Game-theoretic Network Interdiction: Heterogeneous Searchers
Author: R. Kevin Wood, Distinguished Professor, Operations Research Dept., Naval Postgraduate School, 1 University Circle, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States of America, kwood@nps.edu
Coauthor(s): Alan Washburn, Lee Ewing
Year: 2012
Abstract: A two-person zero-sum game models an interdictor who must allocate heterogeneous searchers to maximize the probability of detecting an evader moving through a network. We first solve an LP for an optimal marginal strategy for the interdictor, and then efficiently derive a valid mixed strategy from that: a polynomial-time, overall solution results. When assumptions must be relaxed, dynamic row and column generation solve the problem.
