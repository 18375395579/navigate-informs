Title: The Effect of Rule Ordering in Heuristic Performance: A Preliminary Examination

Author: Kevin Boston, Oregon State University, kevin.boston@oregonstate.edu

Coauthor(s): John Sessions

Year: 2007

Abstract: The effect of rule ordering in a simulated annealing heuristic is examined in a tactical harvest scheduling model. The effect is demonstrated by solving a 91-harvest unit for a twenty period problem of approximately 2690 acre problem from a logging plan prepared for the southern portion of the Oregon State University McDonald-Dunn Research Forests. Results for this case study indicate a 14-40% saving in computational time if spatial feasibility is evaluated before calculating the objective function. Quality of the solution was similar in each case.
