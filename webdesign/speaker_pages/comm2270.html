Title: Multiple Solutions for Two-phases MIP Decompositions
Author: Diego Olivier Fernandez Pons, Optimization consultant, ILOG, 1195 West Fremont Avenue, Sunnyvale, CA, United States of America, dofpons@ilog.com
Coauthor(s): Emilie Danna
Year: 2008
Abstract: We use the solution pool feature of CPLEX 11 to solve two industrial problems by decomposition. For a quadratic packing application, we use a column generation approach to remove the quadratic constraints. For a large-scale timetabling problem, we solve an approximation of smaller size then the detailed allocation. By generating alternate solutions in the first phase, we can focus on promising solutions and still recover from an infeasibility if the first phase was too optimistic.
