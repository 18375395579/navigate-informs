Title: Convergence Rate Analysis of the Simplex Algorithm for Non-stationary Markov Decision Processes

Author: Ilbin Lee, PhD student, University of Michigan, 1205 Beal Ave., Ann Arbor, Mi, 48109, United States of America, ilbinlee@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Marina Epelman, Edwin Romeijn, Robert Smith

Year: 2013

Abstract: A nonstationary Markov decision process (MDP) is an infinite-horizon MDP with time-varying problem data. It can be formulated as countably infinite linear program (CILP), and recently, a simplex algorithm for the CILP was suggested. Given a desired accuracy epsilon, we show that the number of pivots of the simplex algorithm with a particular pivoting rule to achieve this accuracy is O(N^3S^2A log(N+log S)(log S)^4) where N=log(epsilon) and S and A are the number of states and actions, resp.
