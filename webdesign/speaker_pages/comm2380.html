Title: Multiple Instance Classification through Linear and Nonlinear Robust Support Vector Machines
Author: Mohammad H. Poursaeidi, University of Houston, E 209 Engineering Building, Houston, TX, United States of America, mhpoursaeidi@uh.edu
Coauthor(s): Erhun Kundakcioglu
Year: 2012
Abstract: We investigate a multiple instance classification problem that is used for drug activity prediction and image annotation. Hard margin loss formulations that minimize the number of misclassified instances are proposed to model a more robust representation of outliers. Due to NP-hardness of the problem, we develop a three-phase heuristic algorithm for larger problems. Computational results show that the proposed method provides better cross validation accuracy compared to hinge loss formulation.
