Title: The Symphony Callable Library
Author: Menal Guzelsoy, Lehigh University, 200 West Packer Ave, Room 362, Bethlehem, PA, 18015, United States, megb@lehigh.edu
Coauthor(s): Ted Ralphs
Year: 2007
Abstract: SYMPHONY is an open source callable library for solving mixed integer linear programs. It is fully customizable through user-callback functions, a large assortment of parameter settings and compile time options and can be run on a variety of architectures including parallel environments. In this talk, we will give an overview of SYMPHONY and its advanced features such as the ability to solve bicriteria MILPs and to warm start the solution procedure.
