Title: Parallel Approximation, and Integer Programming

Author: Gabor Pataki, Department of Statistics and Operations Research, UNC Chapel Hill, Chapel Hill, NC, United States, pataki@email.unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Mustafa Tural

Year: 2007

Abstract: The Parallel Approximation Problem is: Given an integral vector a, find an integral vector p, which is near parallel to a, and has much smaller norm. More generally, the vector a should be approximated with a combination of k integral vectors. We prove a bound on the quality of the approximaton, and as a corollary we show in subset sum problems with sufficiently large norm of the left hand side for almost all right hand sides the problem has a simple proof of infeasibility.
