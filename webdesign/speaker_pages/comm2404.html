Title: A Heuristic Model for Highway Interdiction

Author: Tolga Seyhan, Operations Research Scientist, Amazon.com, 345 Boren Ave. N, Seattle, WA, 98109, United States of America, tolgaseyhan@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Lawrence V. Snyder

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider a network interdiction model in which a smuggler uses a network of roads and an interdictor deploys patrol units to increase the smuggler’s cost of using particular roads. We heuristically reformulate the smuggler’s problem, which is a prize-collecting Steiner tree problem, and solve the interdictor’s problem as a single-level mixed-integer program.
