Title: Large-scale Testing for the Design of Genetic Algorithms Using High Throughput Computing

Author: Mary Beth Kurz, Clemson University, 110 Freeman Hall, Clemson, SC, 29634, United States, mkurz@CLEMSON.EDU

Year: 2007

Abstract: Design of effective genetic algorithms depends on several factors, perhaps foremost on how the solution is represented. Several solution representations are generally possible for each problem, with various implications for solution quality and running time. Through the use of Condor (a high throughput computing solution) and over 250,000 CPU hours, we are able to explore a new solution representation designed for flexible flow line scheduling (extensions to other problems are easily conceived).
