Title: Pricing Problem Based Sampling in Metaheuristics for Mixed Integer Programs

Author: Chun Wang, University of Wisconsin - Madison, 1513 University Avenue, Room 4126, Madison, 53705, United States, wang26@wisc.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrew Miller, Leyuan Shi

Year: 2007

Abstract: Nested Partitions (NP) is a metaheuristic strategy that focuses on promising feasible subregions while maintaining a global view of the whole solution space. An important feature of NP is the sampling scheme, which is used to generate good feasible solutions. We discuss ways to use information from the pricing problem from a Dantzig-Wolfe reformulation to bias the sampling. This approach can help NP find better samples and thus improve computational convergence.
