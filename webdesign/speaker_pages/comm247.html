Title: Infeasibility Detection in Nonlinear Programming

Author: Frank Curtis, Postdoctoral Researcher, New York University, 251 Mercer Street, New York, NY, 10012, United States of America, curtis@cims.nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jorge Nocedal, Richard Byrd

Year: 2008

Abstract: Optimization algorithms, which are primarily designed to solve feasible problem instances, can behave inefficiently when applied to infeasible problems. Meaningful termination points are those minimizing a feasibility measure, but convergence to such points can be exceedingly slow. In this talk we present a SQP penalty method that provides fast convergence guarantees for both feasible and infeasible problems by transitioning continuously between the goals of optimality and feasibility.
