Title: Distributed Adaptive Steplength SA Schemes for Cartesian Stochastic Variational Inequality Problems
Author: Farzad Yousefian, PhD Candidate, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 104 S. Mathews Ave, Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, yousefi1@illinois.edu
Coauthor(s): Angelia Nedich, Uday Shanbhag
Year: 2012
Abstract: We consider a class of cartesian stochastic variational inequality problems. Motivated by the naive choice of stepsizes in stochastic approximation (SA) methods, we provide almost-sure convergence theory for a distributed SA scheme with an adaptive steplength rule. Additionally, to address problems where the mapping is not Lipschitz, we present a distributed local randomization scheme that constructs a Lipschitzian approximation. Preliminary numerical results are seen to be promising.
