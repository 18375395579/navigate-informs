Title: A Heuristic Procedure for Optimal Open Access Scheduling Templates
Author: Xiuli Qu, Assistant Professor, North Carolina A&T State University, 1601 E. Market Street, 424 McNair Hall, Greensboro, NC, 27411, United States, qu@ecn.purdue.edu
Coauthor(s): Ronald Rardin, Julie Ann Williams
Year: 2007
Abstract: The purpose of open access scheduling is to improve the accessibility to outpatient clinics and reduce patient no shows. In this talk, we formulate a stochastic integer programming (SIP) model to determine an optimal provider scheduling template, and then develop a procedure to search a heuristic optimal solution to the SIP model. Our sensitivity analysis results show that heuristic optimal scheduling templates are sensitive to the cost coefficients and the no-show rates.
