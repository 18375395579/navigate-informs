Title: Performance Variability in Mixed Integer Programming

Author: Emilie Danna, Ilog Inc., 1195 W Fremont Ave, Sunnyvale, CA, 94087, United States of America, edanna@ilog.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: The solving time for a given MIP model sometimes varies dramatically if the branch-and-cut algorithm used to solve it is modified slightly, even if the algorithmic change is theoretically neutral with respect to performance. We will present evidence of this performance variability, examine its causes, describe its consequences, propose a few ideas to address it, and raise several related questions.
