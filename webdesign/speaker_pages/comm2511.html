Title: Decomposition Methods Applied in Refinery Scheduling

Author: Marianthi Ierapetritou, Associate Professor, Rutgers University, 98 Brett Rd, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, marianth@soemail.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): George Saharidis

Year: 2008

Abstract: Scheduling in refineries is a difficult task. In general the developed models are large scale mixed integer models. The application of decomposition methods seems to be a good approach for the resolution of this type of problems. The question is which decomposition method gives the faster convergence in each case. We present a comparison of classical decomposition methods and some extension of them applied in two case studies of scheduling in refineries.
