Title: Gradient-based Algorithms for Finding Nash Equilibria in Extensive Form Games

Author: Andrew Gilpin, Carnegie Mellon University, Computer Science Department, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, gilpin@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Javier Pena, Samid Hoda, Tuomas Sandholm

Year: 2007

Abstract: We present a gradient-based algorithm for finding Nash equilibria of two-person, zero-sum sequential games. The algorithm is based on modern smoothing techniques for non-smooth convex optimization and requires O(1/eps) iterations to compute an eps-equilibrium. Fast iterations enable the approximation of equilibria for games with a tree representation of 100 billion nodes. We develop heuristic improvements and demonstrate their efficacy on a range of real-world games.
