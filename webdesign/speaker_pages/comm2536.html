Title: Optimal Systemic Risk Mitigation in Financial Networks

Author: Agostino Capponi, Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, MD, United States of America, acappon1@jhu.edu

Coauthor(s): Peng Chu-Chen

Year: 2013

Abstract: We propose a multi-period bilateral clearing framework, where systemic risk is mitigated through an optimal bailout strategy. Interbank liabilities evolve stochastically over time, and default events have a persistent impact on the the network. The optimal bailout policy and clearing payments are recovered as solution of a stochastic dynamic programming problem. Our analysis provides a tool to support regulator decisions of when and where to intervene to prevent financial stability.
