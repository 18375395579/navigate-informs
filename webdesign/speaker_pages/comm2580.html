Title: Dynamic Software Release Management

Author: Angelo Mancini, Ph.D. Student, University of Chicago Booth School of Business, 5807 South Woodlawn Ave., Chicago, United States of America, amancini@chicagobooth.edu

Coauthor(s): Dan Adelman

Year: 2012

Abstract: Software release management is the process by which firms decide on the timing, composition and development schedule of their future releases. We construct a semi-Markov decision process (SMDP) that models these decisions in the presence of a stochastically improving competitor. We then formulate the static problem as a mixed integer nonlinear program, whose parameters we obtain by making a value function approximation in our SMDP. We report numerical results.
