Title: Integer and Constraint Programming Methods for a Dynamic Network Interdiction Problem

Author: Thomas Sharkey, Assistant Professor, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, 110 8th St. CII, Suite 5015, Troy, NY, 12180, United States of America, sharkt@rpi.edu

Coauthor(s): Chase Rainwater

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a dynamic network interdiction problem where law enforcement resources must be scheduled to remove criminals from a drug trafficking network. The objective of this problem is to minimize the cumulative amount of flow through the network over a finite horizon. We propose a hybrid integer and constraint programming method that takes advantage of the structure of the interdiction and scheduling decisions. Our method is tested on realistic instances based on a small city.
