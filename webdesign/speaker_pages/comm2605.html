Title: The Maximum Visibility/Shortest Path Problem Applied to Routing of UAVs
Author: Eric Delmelle, Assistant Professor, University of Idaho, Department of Geography, McClure Hall 203, Moscow, ID, 83844, United States, delmelle@uidaho.edu
Coauthor(s): Irene Casas
Year: 2007
Abstract: A bi-objective problem known as the maximum visibility/shortest path, applied to routing of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) is presented. The inputs are a network and an elevation model of the underlying terrain. The network is constructed to reflect the terrain topography i.e., greater density where there is a strong variation in altitude. With the built network, heuristic techniques are applied to solve the bi-objective problem.
