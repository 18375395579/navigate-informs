Title: Optimally Locating Charging Stations for Serving Plug-in Electric Vehicular Flows
Author: Nan Jiang, University of Texas Austin, Austin, TX, United States of America, jiang@utexas.edu
Coauthor(s): Chi Xie
Year: 2013
Abstract: An optimal location problem of where to build public charging stations for plug-in electric vehicles (PEVs) in an equilibrium network is formulated and solved. The model is of a bi-level structure, with its upper level minimizing the network-wide travel cost subject to the budget constraint and its lower level characterizing PEV driver’s route choice behavior shaped by the en-route charging requirement. A nested partition method is implemented and evaluated for solving the problem.
