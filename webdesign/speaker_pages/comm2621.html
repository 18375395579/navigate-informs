Title: A Polyhedral Study of the Unit Commitment Problem with Ramping Constraints

Author: Pelin Damci-Kurt, The Ohio State University, Dept. of Integrated Systems Eng., 210 Baker Systems Building 1971 Neil Ave, Columbus, OH, 43210, United States of America, damci-kurt.1@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Alper Atamturk, Deepak Rajan, Simge Küçükyavuz

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study a unit commitment problem in which the goal is to minimize the operational cost of power generators over a finite horizon subject to physical constraints. In particular, we consider a relaxation involving the ramping constraints. We present several classes of facet-defining inequalities, describe the convex-hull for the two-period case, and report our computational experiments with using the multi-period inequalities in a branch-and-cut algorithm.
