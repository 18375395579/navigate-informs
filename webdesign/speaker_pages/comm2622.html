Title: A Preliminary Study of Binary Quadratic Optimization Under Uncertainty

Author: Nan Kong, Purdue University, 206 S. Intramural Drive, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States of America, kong@eng.usf.edu

Coauthor(s): Oleg Prokopyev

Year: 2007

Abstract: Many combinatorial optimization programs can be formulated as unconstrained quadratic 0-1 programs (Q01), such as the minimum cut problem. Due to this motivation, many corresponding solution methods are developed. We present several constrained stochastic integer programming (SIP) models to study these problems under uncertainty. We compare deterministic Q01 and SIP algorithms in this context. We also attempt to generalize the modeling framework to stochastic pseudo-Boolean optimization.
