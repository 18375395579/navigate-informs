Title: Concurrent Root Cut Loops to Exploit Random Performance Variability

Author: Andrea Tramontani, IBM Italy, CPLEX Optimization, Via Martin Luther King 38/2, Bologna, 40132, Italy, andrea.tramontani@it.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Matteo Fischetti, Andrea Lodi, Domenico Salvagnin, Michele Monaci

Year: 2013

Abstract: Performance variability is a well known issue in Mixed Integer Programming. We propose a possible way to exploit random variability at the root node of a branch-and-cut algorithm, by performing multiple root cut loops in a parallel fashion. The idea is to enforce some random diversification in the alternative cut loops, in order to collect more cutting planes and a (possibly) better incumbent solution. We provide benchmark results to analyze the performance impact of the proposed approach.
