Title: Logical Bender's Cuts Guided Local Search and a Greedy Heuristic for the Traveling Umpire Problem
Author: Hakan Yildiz, Ph.D Candidate, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, hakanyil@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Michael Trick
Year: 2007
Abstract: This paper introduces the use of Logic-Based Bender's Cuts to guide a Large Neighborhood Search to solve the Traveling Umpire Problem, a sports scheduling problem inspired by the real-life needs of the officials of a sports league. At each time slot, a Greedy Matching heuristic is used to construct a schedule. When an infeasibility is recognized Bender's cuts are generated, which guides a Large Neighborhood Search to ensure feasibility and to improve the solution.
