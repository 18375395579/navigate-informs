Title: Branch-and-Cut for Probabilistically Constrained Stochastic Integer Programs

Author: Julian Gallego, Texas A&M University, Austin, TX, United States of America, kamizama77@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Lewis Ntaimo

Year: 2013

Abstract: Probabilistically constrained stochastic integer programs (SIP-C) are challenging since its feasible region is generally non-convex. In addition, very weak lower (upper) bounds on the objective function are obtained from the linear relaxation to deterministic equivalent reformulations of SIP-C. We present a new branch-and-cut method for SIP-C with random technology and right-hand side based on irreducible infeasible subsystems (IIS). We report computational results based on standard instances.
