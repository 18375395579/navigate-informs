Title: Global and Local Approaches for Large-scale Security-constrained Optimal Power Flow

Author: Dzung Phan, Research Staff Member, IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, 1101 Kitchawan Road, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States of America, phandu@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Jayant Kalagnanam

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present global and local methods for solving the security-constrained optimal power flow problem. We propose a class of global methods based on Lagrangian duality to solve the problem to optimality. For practical uses when dealing large-scale instances, two decomposition algorithms based on Benders cut and ADMM are investigated. These schemes often generate solutions whose objective values are smaller than the conventional approach and are very close to the optimal points.
