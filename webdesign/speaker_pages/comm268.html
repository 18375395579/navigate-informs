Title: Adaptive Transit Routing under Uncertainty
Author: Stephen Boyles, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas, Austin, 1 University Station C1761, Austin, TX, United States of America, sboyles@mail.utexas.edu
Coauthor(s): Tarun Rambha, Travis Waller
Year: 2012
Abstract: An adaptive transit routing (ATR) problem on a stochastic transit network is defined and is formulated as a Markov Decision Process. State space reduction through pre-processing is achieved by solving variants of time dependent shortest paths and applying rules of dominance. Sampling based approximation techniques are used to solve an instance of the problem to demonstrate its application.
