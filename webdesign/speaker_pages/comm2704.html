Title: Stochastic Model for Pricing Optimization

Author: Yihua Li, Disney, Orlando, FL, United States of America, Yihua.Li@disney.com

Coauthor(s): Bruce Wang, Luz A. Caudillo-Fuentesa

Year: 2012

Abstract: Given Price-Demand elasticity forecasting, optimally determine price to maximize total revenue plays an key role for most public service providers, such as: hotels, car rental, retails, etc. By the nature of the problems, stochastic optimization model will counter all uncertainties. Traditional SLP with dynamic programming (DP) is not solvable for most realistic networks because of the large dimensionality of the state space (e.g., typical numbers for even a modest-sized hotel with hundreds of thousands products and for each product DP will go along each of stages = 180-500, each unit of capacities = 500-3000, each of time slots = 500 ?10000, leading to at least 10100 states). Moreover, DP cannot directly deal with constraints, such as upgradings(UP) and overbookings (OB). We will develop a stochastic model which is solvable together with RU and GB. We accomplish solving it in real time by suggesting a heuristic that generates a good initial feasible solution.
