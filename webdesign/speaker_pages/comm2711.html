Title: Stochastic and Robust Optimization Approaches to the Design of Evacuation Systems in Buildings

Author: Reza Faturechi, University of Maryland, 1173 Glenn L. Martin Hall, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, reza@umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Elise Miller-Hooks, Shabtai Issac, Lei Feng

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this work, techniques of bilevel programming, stochastic programming and robust optimization are applied in the robust design of shelters and exits in building environments under multi-hazard disaster scenarios. Shelters and exits are designed with the aim of minimizing risk exposure during hazard events while adhering to budgetary and other constraints. User equilibrium and system optimum solutions from the lower level of a bilevel program formulation are explored and interpreted.
