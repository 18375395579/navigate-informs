Title: Mathematical Programming Models for the Bandwidth Coloring Problem

Author: Bernard Gendron, Universit?de Montréal, C.P. 6128, Succ. Centre-ville, Montréal, QC, Canada, gendron@iro.umontreal.ca

Coauthor(s): Alain Hertz, Patrick St-Louis

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider the bandwidth coloring problem, a generalization of the well-known graph coloring problem. For the latter problem, a classical theorem states that the chromatic number of a graph is bounded from above by the number of vertices in the longest elementary path in any directed graph derived by orienting all edges in the graph. We generalize this result to the bandwidth coloring problem by deriving a series of equivalent mathematical programming models.
