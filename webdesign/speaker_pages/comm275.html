Title: Designing Price Incentives in a Network with Social Interactions

Author: Maxime Cohen, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Cambridge, MA, United States of America, maxcohen@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Pavithra Harsha

Year: 2013

Abstract: The recent ubiquity of social networks allows firms to collect vast amount of data on social interactions. We formulate the problem of optimizing prices for an indivisible good sold to consumers that are embedded in a social network. We derive an LP based procedure to efficiently solve the case of uniform and discriminative prices. We also propose a model where the retailer designs incentives to guarantee the influence of the buyers on neighbors and provide insights with computational examples.
