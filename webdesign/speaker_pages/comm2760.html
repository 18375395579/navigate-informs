Title: A Message-passing Paradigm for Decentralized Control and Optimization
Author: Ciamac Moallemi, Stanford University, Electrical Engineering, Packard 274, Stanford, CA, 94305-9510, United States, ciamac@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Benjamin Van Roy
Year: 2007
Abstract: We propose a message-passing paradigm for decentralized resource allocation problems. This is a conceptual framework that generalizes price-based systems. We demonstrate that this is equivalent to approaches based on shadow prices when the utility functions are concave. However, the message-passing approach also extends to more general cases, and yields an incentive-based framework with certain optimality guarantees even when utilities are not concave or resources are indivisible.
