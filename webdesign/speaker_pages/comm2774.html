Title: A Two-stage Stochastic Transmission Planning Model Considering Dependent Load and Wind

Author: Heejung Park, The University of Texas at Austin, 1 University Station C0803, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, tohjpark@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Ross Baldick, David Morton

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a two-stage stochastic model for transmission system expansion planning with two dependent random variables, load and wind. Sample realizations of load and wind are generated using Gaussian copula. Candidate solutions for 200 and 300 random samples are represented. A 95% confidence interval on the optimality gap is assessed for the solution quality of a candidate stochastic solution. Optimal power flow is solved based on the linearized direct current (DC) power flow.
