Title: Improving Performance of Linear and Mixed Integer Programs

Author: Alexandra Newman, Associate Professor, Colorado School of Mines, 1500 Illinois Street, Golden, CO, 80401, United States of America, anewman@mines.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Even with state-of-the-art hardware and software, linear and mixed integer programs can require a significant amount of computing time to reach an optimal or near-optimal solution. Careful modeling constructs and specific use of commercial algorithms can vastly improve performance. In this talk, we present examples of correct use of modeling languages, good model formulation, and algorithmic selection and parameter tuning that enhance optimization model performance.
