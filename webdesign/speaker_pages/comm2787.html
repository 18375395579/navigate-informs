Title: Solving the Pricing Problem in Branch-and-Price using Zero-Suppressed Binary Decision Diagrams

Author: David Morrison, University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, 201 N Goodwin Ave, Urbana, IL, United States of America, morris50@illinois.edu

Coauthor(s): Edward Sewell, Sheldon Jacobson

Year: 2013

Abstract: Zero-suppressed binary decision diagrams (ZDDs) are data structures that compactly encode families of sets; in this talk, it is shown how to use ZDDs to characterize valid solutions to the pricing problem in a branch-and-price setting. A linear-time algorithm is given to add restrictions to the ZDD so that standard branching rules can be employed without destroying pricing problem structure. Computational results for the graph coloring problem are given to show the method's effectiveness.
