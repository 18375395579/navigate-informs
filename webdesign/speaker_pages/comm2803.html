Title: Recent Progress in Disjunctive Programming

Author: Anureet Saxena, Tepper School of Business, CMU, 5624 Fifth Ave, Appt C13, Pittsburgh, PA, 15232, United States of America, anureets@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Egon Balas

Year: 2007

Abstract: Given a polyhedron P, a disjunction D whose terms are systems of linear inequalities, and a point x in P, we address the problem of finding an inequality that separates x from the convex hull of the intersection of P and D . We state this separation problem as minimizing a convex piecewise linear function of the multipliers associated with the inequalities of D, and give an iterative row generation procedure for solving it.
