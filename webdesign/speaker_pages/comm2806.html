Title: Mechanism Design for Stochastic Optimization Problems

Author: Samuel Ieong, Stanford University, 353 Serra Mall, Rm 120, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, sieong@cs.stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Mukund Sundararajan, Anthony Man-Cho So

Year: 2007

Abstract: We design economic mechanisms for eliciting optimization parameters privately known to selfish agents in two-stage stochastic optimization problems. We show that the expected welfare maximizer can be implemented in perfect Bayesian equilibrium. We study algorithmic issues that arise in implementation, and demonstrate a tradeoff between computational efficiency and incentive guarantee.
