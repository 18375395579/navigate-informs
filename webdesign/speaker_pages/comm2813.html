Title: Static and Dynamic Resource Allocation for Perimeter Security
Author: Matthew Bailey, University of Pittsburgh, Department of Industrial Engineering, Pittsburgh, PA, 15243, United States, mdbailey@engr.pitt.edu
Coauthor(s): Nan Kong
Year: 2006
Abstract: We present a model to assist in the allocation of resources for perimeter security, while accounting for its impact on dynamic operations. More generally, we introduce a framework for optimally allocating static interdiction devices in a zero-sum, one player controlled stochastic game. We first introduce the general model, then illustrate the technique on a problem of perimeter security, and finish with a computational illustration.
