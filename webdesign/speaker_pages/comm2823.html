Title: Bounds on the Value of Stochastic Mixed-Integer Programs

Author: Nan Kong, Assistant Professor, Industrial and Management Systems Engineering, University of South Florida, ENB 118, Tampa, FL, 33620, United States, kong@eng.usf.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrew Schaefer, Burhaneddin Sandikci

Year: 2006

Abstract: We consider a general two-stage stochastic mixed-integer program with discrete distribution and finite support. We derive a class of bounds for the optimal objective value of this problem by solving a number of truncated deterministic equivalent problems, each of which corresponds to a subset of scenarios. For the cases where the number of scenarios is large, we apply sampling techniques. Computational results for several stochastic linear and integer programs are presented.
