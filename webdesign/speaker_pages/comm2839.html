Title: Variance Reduction Techniques for Adaptive Nonlinear Stochastic Programming

Author: Fabian Bastin, Assistant Professor, Unviersity of Montreal, C.P. 6128, Succursale Centre-Ville, Montreal, QC, H3C 3J7, Canada, bastin@iro.umontreal.ca

Coauthor(s): Pierre L'Ecuyer

Year: 2007

Abstract: Stochastic programs are often approximately solved by considering sample average approximations, based on Monte Carlo samplings. We have previously constructed an adaptive trust-region algorithm that allows the use of sub-samples when possible, adjusting the sample size at each iteration. We here consider extensions of this algorithm that are based on variance reduction techniques, such as quasi-Monte Carlo sampling, and show that substantial numerical gains can then be achieved.
