Title: Dantzig-Wolfe-Jacobi Decomposition of Equilibrium Models: Algorithm, and Formulation Aid

Author: David Fuller, Professor, Univesity of Waterloo, Dept. of Management Science, Waterloo, On, N2L3G1, Canada, dfuller@engmail.uwaterloo.ca

Year: 2007

Abstract: Equilibrium models contain submodels linked by constraints and by the influence of one submodel's variables on another submodel's function values, e.g., competitive multicommodity models, and Nash-Cournot models. Dantzig-Wolfe decomposition relaxes linking constraints, and the Jacobi idea fixes other variables in functions, to produce optimization subproblems and an equilibrium master problem. In addition to potential computational efficiencies, decomposition can help in model formulation.
