Title: Gradient Projection for Nonlinear Optimization

Author: Richard Waltz, University of Southern California, rwaltz@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Richard Byrd

Year: 2007

Abstract: Gradient projection is often the most efficient method for large-scale nonlinear optimization problems with simple bound constraints because of its ability to modify the active-set estimate by many constraints at a time. We will investigate attempts to extend gradient projection-type methods to general nonlinear optimization problems. The relationship between gradient projection methods and methods that estimate the active set by solving linear programming subproblems will be established.
