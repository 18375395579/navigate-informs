Title: Inverse Cutting Plane Methods for Problems with Second Order Stochastic Dominance Constraints

Author: Andrzej Ruszczynski, Professor, Rutgers University, 94 Rockefeller Road, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, rusz@business.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Darinka Dentcheva

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose new cutting plane methods for solving optimization problems with second order stochastic dominance constraints. The methods are based on the inverse formulation of stochastic dominance constraints using Lorenz functions. Convergence of the methods is proved for general probability distributions. In the case of discrete distributions convergence is finite, without the assumption of equal probabilities of the realizations. Comparison with primal cutting plane methods is provided.
