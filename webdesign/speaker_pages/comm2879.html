Title: Probabilistic Constrained Multiobjective Programming -- Models and Formulations: Part I

Author: Miguel A. Lejeune, George Washington University, Duques Hall, Room 406, Washington, DC, United States of America, mlejeune@gwu.edu

Coauthor(s): Siqian Shen

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study probabilistically constrained multiobjective stochastic programming problems. We use a Boolean programming method to derive a series of equivalent deterministic mixed-integer programming formulations. The method applies to all types of chance constraints, including the joint probabilistic constraints with random technology matrix and random right-hand sides.
