Title: Bounds on the Size of Quadratizations of Pseudo-Boolean Functions

Author: Endre Boros, Distinguished Professor, Rutgers University Center for Operations Research, 640 Bartholomew Road, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, boros@rci.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Martin Anthony, Yves Crama, Aritanan Gruber

Year: 2013

Abstract: There are several techniques to reduce a nonlinear binary minimization problem to a quadratic one. It is however not clear how large the resulting quadratic problems can be. In this talk we consider all possible quadratizations(not necessarily depending on a particular construction), and prove sharp lower and upper bounds on the size of the resulting quadratic functions. Our upper bounds are in fact constructive, resulting in new quadratization algorithms.
