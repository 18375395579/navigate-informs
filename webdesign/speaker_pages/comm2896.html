Title: Monotone Policies in Dynamic Medical Treatment Planning
Author: Archis Ghate, University of Washington, Industrial and Systems Engineering, Seattle, WA, United States of America, archis@uw.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: A central problem in medicine requires physicians to choose dose levels that achieve adequate disease control while limiting adverse effects over a treatment course with multiple decision points. We propose a stylized stochastic dynamic programming formulation of this problem and prove the existence of a monotone optimal policy in which dose levels increase with disease severity. This result is illustrated using an example from cancer radiotherapy.
