Title: Simultaneous Assignment & Sequencing of Product Families to Minimize Sequence-Dependant Setup Costs

Author: Susan Heath, Assistant Professor, Naval Postgraduate School, 1 University Circle, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States of America, skheath@nps.edu

Coauthor(s): Douglas Morrice, Jonathan Bard

Year: 2007

Abstract: This paper presents a mixed-integer non-linear program (MINLP) as a next-generation solution to a known production scheduling problem. The scheduling problem consists of scheduling multiple product families on parallel, identical production lines so as to minimize complex sequence-dependant setup costs. This problem is represented as a multiple traveling salesman subset-tour problem (MTSSP). We use a greedy randomized adaptive search procedure (GRASP) to generate good quality solutions.
