Title: Applying High Performance Computing to Multi-area Stochastic Unit Commitment
Author: Anthony Papavasiliou, Assistant Professor, Catholic University of Louvain, Voie du Roman Pays 34, Office b.114, Louvain la Neuve, 1348, Belgium, anthony.papavasiliou@gmail.com
Coauthor(s): Shmuel Oren
Year: 2013
Abstract: We present a parallel implementation of Lagrangian relaxation for solving stochastic unit commitment subject to wind supply uncertainty and component failures. We present a scenario selection algorithm inspired by importance sampling and validate its performance by comparing it to a stochastic formulation with a very large number of scenarios. We examine the impact of narrowing the duality gap on performance and running time and compare it to the impact of increasing the number of scenarios.
