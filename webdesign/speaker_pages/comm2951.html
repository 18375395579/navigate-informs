Title: Optimization of Electrical Efficiency using Mixed AC/DC Distribution

Author: Stephen Frank, PhD Student, Electrical Engineering, Colorado School of Mines, 1500 Illinois St., Golden, CO, 80401, United States of America, stfrank@mymail.mines.edu

Coauthor(s): Steffen Rebennack, Pankaj (PK) Sen

Year: 2012

Abstract: The majority of electrical loads and distributed generation sources in modern buildings require direct current (DC), but grid-level electricity generation, transmission, and distribution systems continue to use alternating current (AC). A significant amount of energy is lost due to AC/DC conversion. This presentation explores formulations and solution methods for the optimal design of mixed AC/DC distribution systems to minimize the losses due to AC/DC conversion.
