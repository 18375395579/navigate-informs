Title: Solving Stochastic Quadratic Programs Via Iterative Sampling
Author: Uday Shanbhag, Asst. Professor, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 216 Transportation Bldg, Urbana, Il, 61822, United States of America, udaybag@uiuc.edu
Coauthor(s): Peter W. Glynn, Gerd Infanger
Year: 2008
Abstract: The L-shaped method is a standard approach for solving stochastic linear programs. However, when the second-stage distribution governing the randomness of the second-period problems is continuous, computing the expectations of cuts becomes difficult. In this paper, we construct estimators in the context of quadratic programming and provide confidence intervals for the upper and lower bound of the optimal value. We report computational results on a quadratic programming test problem set.
