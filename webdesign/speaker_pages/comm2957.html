Title: A Bi-level Stochastic Programming for Natural Gas Market Network Based on EPEC Methodology

Author: Parviz Darvish, ESSEC Business School, 1, Ave. Bernard Hirsch, Cergy, 95000, France, Parviz.darvish@essec.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this presentation, we consider a natural gas market network with five tiers of players and propose a bi-level stochastic programming in terms of equilibrium problem with equilibrium constraint (EPEC) methodology to attain an equilibrium condition. The model tries to find out both optimal primal and dual decision variables of Cournot competitors regarding the agents'profit maximization and loss risk minimization.
