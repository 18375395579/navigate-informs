Title: Optimal Scheduling of Critical Peak Pricing Events with Day-ahead Wind Energy Commitments

Author: Xiaoxuan Zhang, IBM TJ Watson Research, 1101 Kitchawan Road, Route 134, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States of America, zhangxia@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Bo Zhang

Year: 2012

Abstract: We investigate the optimal scheduling of critical peak pricing from the perspective of a load serving entity who has wind energy to commit. The goal is to minimize the total cost for the whole planning horizon, including energy purchasing cost, revenue from the critical peak pricing, wind sales, and imbalance penalties due to wind over- and under-commitments. We propose a multi-stage stochastic mixed integer nonlinear programming model and present an optimal stopping view of this model.
