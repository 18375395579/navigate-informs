Title: The Iterative Linear Current Voltage Formulation of the AC Optimal Power Flow Problem and Testing Results

Author: Richard O'Neill, Chief Economic Advisor, FERC, 888 First Street, NE, Washington, DC, 20426, United States of America, richard.oneill@ferc.gov

Year: 2013

Abstract: In the over 50 years since it was first proposed, we have yet to find commercially viable software to solve the AC optimal power flow (ACOPF). The ACOPF is non-convex. The current software is either too slow or fails to find an optimal solution. Consequently, we solve linear approximations. In this presentation, we review the history of attempts to solve the ACOPF including different formulations and algorithms. We present the iterative linear current voltage formulation and computational results on four standard IEEE test problems.
