Title: The Optimality of Security-level Strategies in Adversarial Settings
Author: Jacomo Corbo, Chief Race Strategist (Visiting Scholar), ING Renault F1 Team Ltd. (The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania), 33 Oxford Street #219, Cambridge, MA, 02138, United States of America, jacomo@eecs.harvard.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: We consider devising a pit stop strategy in Formula One (F1) racing. We formulate an F1 race as a full-information game, show how to solve for competitor risk aversion from data, and note that competitors are strongly risk averse. We show that the game compels risk averse play and matches well with a game class where security-level strategies are optimal. We conclude by showing that our game-theoretic approach outperforms other methods, and discuss its extension to R&D portfolio investment.
