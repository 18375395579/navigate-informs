Title: Performance Evaluation for Current Aeromedical Base Locations of New Mexico with Managerial Insights

Author: Elif Tokar Erdemir, Research Assistant, SUNY at Buffalo, 60 Maple Court Apt. 1, Amherst, NY, 14226, United States, et5@buffalo.edu

Coauthor(s): Peter Rogerson, Alan Blatt, Rajan Batta, Seth Spielman, Marie Flanigan

Year: 2006

Abstract: The current aeromedical system of New Mexico is designed without considering path demand, i.e. traffic roads with a weight signifying relative crash occurrence. From this perspective, we analyze the loss in accident coverage and location error for current aeromedical bases. We also provide managerial insights that will show the relevance of considering path demand. Additionally, we address the tradeoff issues in adding new trauma centers vs. new aeromedical bases to the current system.
