Title: Identification of Primary Bottleneck Clusters in a Job Shop

Author: Jun-qiang Wang, Professor, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Box 554,Northwestern Polytechnical Univ., 127 Youyi Xilu, Xi'an, 710072, China, siemwjq@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): shudong sun, yingfeng zhang, yong kang, Jian Chen, George Q. Huang

Year: 2013

Abstract: Clearly distinguishing between bottlenecks and non-bottlenecks, as well as scientifically identifying the significance of each bottleneck, is a difficult task in a job shop. We propose a identification model of primary bottleneck cluster (PBC) to determine the exact size, importance, and hierarchy of bottleneck candidates, and use hierarchical clustering and TOPSIS to identify multi-order PBCs. Feature attributes of Machines and distance metric of clustering impact PBC identification.
