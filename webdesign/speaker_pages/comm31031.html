Title: The Difficulty of Applying Fuzzy Set Number Crunching to Decision Making

Author: Thomas Saaty, University Professor, University of Pittsburgh, 322 Mervis Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15260, United States, saaty@katz.pitt.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Fuzzy logic has difficulty producing valid answers in decision-making. Absent are theorems to prove its workability by crunching numerical representations of judgments to produce results already known and estimated with judgments. The numerical representation of judgments in the AHP is already fuzzy. Fuzzifying judgments does not lead to a better outcome and often a worse one.
