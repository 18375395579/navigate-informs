Title: Estimating Usable Habitat for the California Spotted Owl

Author: Richard Church, Department of Geography, University of California, Santa Barbara, Santa Barbara, CA, 93106, United States, church@geog.ucsb.edu

Coauthor(s): Ross Gerrard

Year: 2007

Abstract: Activities such as thinning and fuels removal to reduce the risk of fire alter the extent and quality of specific habitat types. The focus of this paper deals with estimating the impact of such activities on the habitat of the California spotted owl. This paper develops a modeling framework, including a spatial optimization model that attempts to estimate effective or useful habitat both pre- and post-treatment.
