Title: Understanding Statistical Thinking in the eSCM-SP

Author: Mark Paulk, Senior Systems Scientist, Carnegie Mellon University, IT Services Qualification Center, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, mcp@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Keith Heston, Elaine Hyder

Year: 2006

Abstract: The eSourcing Capability Model for Service Providers (eSCM-SP) version 2 is a “best practices?framework with explicit requirements for statistical thinking and quantitative management. The purpose of this paper is to identify what was expected by the authors of the Model for a complete implementation of statistical thinking; what the Model explicitly requires; and what has been empirically observed, both good and bad, in service providers implementing the relevant Practices.
