Title: Long-term, System-wide Impact Analysis of High-speed Rail in the Midwest Corridor

Author: Jeffrey Peters, Purdue University - Nextrans, 3000 Kent Ave, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, peters83@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): En-Pei Han, Daniel DeLaurentis, Srinivas Peeta

Year: 2012

Abstract: This study describes a methodology for forecasting system-wide ridership and the corresponding user and community impacts of a high-speed rail mode under the existing transportation infrastructure. Experiments with various social, economic, technological, and political trends over the next few decades are conducted using the proposed Chicago-Hub network to analyze the potential viability in the long-term. Both the methodology and the impacts of the proposed system will be discussed.
