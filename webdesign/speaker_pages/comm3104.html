Title: HIV Models: Contraception and Extended High Viremics
Author: Benjamin Armbruster, Assistant Professor, Northwestern University, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, armbruster@northwestern.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: We discuss two issues about HIV in Africa. First, hormonal contraceptives are very common have recently been shown to double the risk of HIV. We look at the births, HIV transmission, and mother-to-child transmission under various scenarios of contraceptive substitution. Second, a fraction of HIV-1 type C infections have high viral loads for an extended duration. These individuals are more infectious and have a shorter life span. We discuss their impact on the HIV epidemic.
