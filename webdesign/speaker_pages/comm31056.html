Title: FAST Copper: Optimizing Fiber/DSL Broadband Access

Author: Mung Chiang, Assistant Professor, Princeton University, B328 Engineering Quad, Olden Street, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States, chiangm@princeton.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: FAST Copper is a joint project by Princeton (Mung Chiang), Stanford(John Cioffi), and Fraser Research Labs (Alexander Fraser). It aims at providing the next revolution in wired broadband access networks through a hybrid fiber-to-the-curb and DSL last mile architecture. A main component is the joint and dynamic optimization of resources in Frequency, Amplitude, Space, and Time. This talk will overview this project, with a focus on interesting optimization problem formulations.
