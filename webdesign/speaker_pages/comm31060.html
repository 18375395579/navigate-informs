Title: Wind Power Forecasting toward the Adaptive Design of PHEV Charging Stations by SVM

Author: Piampoom Sarikprueck, University of Texas at Arlington, 416 Yates Street, Nedderman Hall Room 517-518,, Arlington, TX, 76019, United States of America, piam_13@hotmail.com

Coauthor(s): Asama Kulvanitchaiyanunt, Victoria C Chen, Jay Rosenberger, Wei-jen Lee

Year: 2013

Abstract: The design of a PHEV charging station with advanced control algorithm and distributed storage devices is proposed to maximize the utilization of the renewable energy and provide supports to the grid. To improve the performance of the charging station, the predictions of availability and/or prices of energy sources are essential. This research discusses the wind power forecasting using Support Vector Machines (SVM). Future research will study the prediction of solar energy and market prices.
