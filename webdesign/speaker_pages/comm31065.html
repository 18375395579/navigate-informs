Title: Using Real-Time and Crowdsourced Data to Improve Disaster Relief Operations
Author: Luis de la Torre, PhD Candidate, Northwestern University, Technological Institute C236, 2145 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, 60208-3119, United States of America, ledelatorre@u.northwestern.edu
Coauthor(s): Jennifer Chan, Irina Dolinskaya, Karen Smilowitz
Year: 2012
Abstract: Recent efforts such as Crisis Mappers, Ushahidi, and the Humanitarian Open Street Map team have facilitated rapidly disseminated and frequently updated geospatial data, which can be of great benefit to disaster relief and other humanitarian operations. We present an overview of available crowdsourced data and an example of its use. We show how incorporating unstructured real-time data, collected in Haiti in the week following the 2010 earthquake, can improve relief operations in Port-au-Prince.
