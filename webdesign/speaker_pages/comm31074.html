Title: Open Source and Software Publication

Author: Matthew Saltzman, Clemson University, Department of Mathematical Sciences, Clemson, SC, United States of America, mjs@clemson.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Open source software (OSS) is arguably the most efficient technology transfer mechanism available today. We explain what OSS is and what its benefits are to the OR community. We describe various flavors of OSS licenses, and we discuss various issues related to publishing and using OSS. We also describe several examples of OSS resources available in the OR field.
