Title: Sub-optimal Assimilation of Clinical Information Systems in Healthcare Organizations: Antecedents

Author: Roopa Raman, PhD Candidate, Emory University, Goizueta Business School, 1300 Clifton Road, Atlanta, GA, 30322, United States of America, roopa_raman@bus.emory.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Process improvement enabled by clinical information systems is often challenged in hospitals due to sub-optimal and uneven assimilation of the technology across different patient care units within the same organization. In an empirical study, a large hospital faced persistent inter-unit assimilation problems, even though all units were using the same technology features, had access to the same resources, and were subject to the same concurrent implementation effort. I seek to understand why.
