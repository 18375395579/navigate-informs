Title: Characterization of a Capacity Investment Strategy for a Global Firm
Author: Chester Xiang, PhD Candidate, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Posner Hall 360, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, ccx@cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Bahar Biller
Year: 2007
Abstract: We study the optimal flexible capacity investment decision a global firm has to make under demand and exchange rate uncertainties. We restrict our attention to a two-market setting and identify the structure of the optimal investment strategy. We investigate the impact of various investment drivers on the structure of this strategy using a comprehensive input-modeling framework for demand and exchange rate processes.
