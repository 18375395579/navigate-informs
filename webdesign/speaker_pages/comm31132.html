Title: Planning Navy Humanitarian Medical Missions

Author: Anke Richter, Associate Professor, DRMI Naval Postgraduate School, 699 Dyer Rd., Bldg. 234, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States of America, arichter@nps.edu

Coauthor(s): Kathleen Cooperman, Linda Houde

Year: 2008

Abstract: The Navy provides humanitarian medical assistance throughout the world. To anticipate the demand for manpower specialties, we need to capture country-centric health care requirements using standard health statistical indicators. The majority of the need is focused on prevention of infectious diseases such as TB, malaria, and yellow fever. Once a medical manpower workload is established, delivery can be discussed among NGOs, volunteer organizations and military medical personnel.
