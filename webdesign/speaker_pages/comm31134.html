Title: A Logistic Regression Model for Assessing Usefulness of Computerized Clinical Reminders

Author: Sze-Jung Wu, Research Assistant, Purdue University, School of Industrial Engineering, 315 N. Grant Street, W. Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States, swu@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Lehto, Yuehwern Yih, Mindy Flanagan, Alan Zillich, Brad Boebbeling

Year: 2006

Abstract: Computerized clinical reminders (CCR) offer clinicians instructions such as which diagnostic or screening tests to order. Previous studies reported that CCR could improve health outcomes, but had limited effect on changing physicians' behavior. In order to study clinicians' adherence to CCR, we conducted a survey instrument to measure users' demographics and attitudes toward CCR. Logistic regression was applied to identify and model the prognostic predictors affecting users' adherence to CCR.
