Title: Do it Right the First Time or Revisit and Rework? An Empirical Study of ICU Performance

Author: Diwas Kc, PhD Candidate, The Wharton School, 3730 Walnut St., Suite 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, diwas@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Christian Terwiesch

Year: 2007

Abstract: We find that the discharge decision in a cardiac ICU is not only medical, but also operational. A capacity constrained ICU discharges patients early and this decision has eventual capacity and quality implications. In some cases, an early discharged patient develops further complications leading to a longer ICU revisit. In this paper, we explore the quality-capacity tradeoff to determine if a hospital should “do it right the first time?or “revisit and rework.?
