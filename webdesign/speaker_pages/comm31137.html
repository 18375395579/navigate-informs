Title: Isolating the Effects of Health IT on Performance: Empirical Test of Complementarities and Learning
Author: Ritu Agarwal, University of Maryland, ragarwal@rhsmith.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Corey Angst
Year: 2007
Abstract: We examine HIT adoption and process-level value, incorporating the effects of learning that occurs through complementarities. We use a dataset of HIT adopted by almost 400 nationally-representative hospitals in the US, matched with quality and financial performance data to examine a specialty IT application related to cardio care. Results show that when a temporal learning component is introduced into the model as a complementary effect, the IT-Performance link becomes stronger.
