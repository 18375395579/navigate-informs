Title: Information and the Value of Execution Guarantees

Author: Krishnamurthy Iyer, Cornell University, 204 Rhodes Hall, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, ki82@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Ramesh Johari, Ciamac Moallemi

Year: 2013

Abstract: We assess the role of information in the choice between certain trade at a price premium in an intermediated dealer market and contingent trade in a dark pool. Our setting consists of traders with heterogeneous private information as to an asset's value, who endogenously decide between the two venues. We analyze how this choice depends on the information available to an individual agent as well as on the distribution of information across all agents, and study the effect of dark pool on welfare.
