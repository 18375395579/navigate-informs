Title: Optimal Selection of Blood Screening Assays under Prevalence Uncertainty

Author: Ebru Bish, Associate Professor, Virginia Tech, Dept of Industrial and Systems Engineeri, 250 Durham Hall, Blacksburg, VA, 24061-0118, United States of America, ebru@vt.edu

Coauthor(s): Prasanna Kumar Ragavan, Douglas Bish

Year: 2013

Abstract: Blood products are an essential component of any health system, and their safety, in terms of being free of transfusion-transmitted infections, is crucial. However, blood screening tests are imperfectly reliable and prevalence rates are uncertain. The budget-constrained decision-maker needs to select a set of screening tests so as to minimize the infection risk in the blood pool. We analyze structural properties of an optimal solution and perform a case study using real data.
