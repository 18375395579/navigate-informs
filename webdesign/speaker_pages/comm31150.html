Title: How to (Can You?) Influence a Population’s Attitude with a Single Whisper

Author: Robert Burks, Naval Postgraduate School, 5125 Ocean Bluff Blvd, Seaside, United States of America, reburks@nps.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Rumor propagation in complex societal networks is studied through a Social Contagion model and Agent Based Simulation to identify key individuals in a network to target with dis-information, ensuring that the dissemination of information reaches the largest possible individuals and triggers an information cascade. This talk provides a modeling framework identifying individuals that are good early adopters who are most effective in supporting the spread of information through the population.
