Title: Modelling Drug Market Supply Disruptions: Where Do All the Drugs Not Go?
Author: Jon Caulkins, Professor of Operations Research and Public Policy, Heinz School of Public Policy and Management, Carnegie Mellon University - Qatar, PO Box 24866, Doha, Qatar, caulkins@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Haijing Hao
Year: 2006
Abstract: This model predicts how illicit drug production deficits will be “allocated?across downstream markets. Plausible parameterization suggests cocaine markets outside the US serve as a sort of “shock absorber? partially shielding US markets from supply fluctuations, limiting US source country control efforts?ability to reduce domestic drug use.
