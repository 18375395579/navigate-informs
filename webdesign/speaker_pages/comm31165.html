Title: When Networks Matter: An Examination of Network Content and Firms?Innovation Performance

Author: Irem Demirkan, Northeastern University, 313 Hayden Hall, College of Business Administration, Boston, MA, 02115, United States, irem@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): David Deeds

Year: 2007

Abstract: In this study we look into the issue of network content - the characteristics of the actors and/or the qualitative nature of the relationships- and its change over time. We explore how the content of relationships within a firm's ego-network affects the focal firm's innovative performance in biotechnology. Data from the biotechnology industry over a span of seventeen years largely support our hypotheses.
