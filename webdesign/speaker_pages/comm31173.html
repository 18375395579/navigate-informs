Title: Appointment Reminder Systems and Patient Preferences

Author: Nan Liu, Columbia University, 600 W 168th St., Room 603, New York, NY, 10032, United States of America, nl2320@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Stacey Finkelstein, Beena Jani, David Rosenthal, Lusine Poghosyan

Year: 2012

Abstract: We utilized a cross-sectional design where patients ranked various appointment reminder systems and indicated their usage of technology and familiarity with other service providers contacting them via text message and e-mail. We found that patient usage and familiarity of technology are the best predictors of perceived effectiveness and responsiveness to text message and e-mail reminders. After these variables are accounted for, age and other demographic variables are not significant predictors.
