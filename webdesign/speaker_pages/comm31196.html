Title: Business Internal Audit Prioritization: A Case Study

Author: Wen Chiang, Professor, University of Tulsa, 118-J BAH, 600 S. College Avenue, Tulsa, OK, 74104, United States, wen-chiang@utulsa.edu

Coauthor(s): Jennifer Shang

Year: 2006

Abstract: Internal audits evaluate internal control of a business through financial, operational, and compliance review. It assesses the risk of asset loss, studies business processes, and identifies opportunities to improve efficiency and effectiveness. In this paper, we apply AHP and DEA to determine the business units that need audit. The framework is comprehensive, flexible, and shows great potential for internal audit prioritization and resource allocation.
