Title: Mining Electronic Medical Records for Adverse Drug Reactions

Author: Mei Liu, Assistant Professor, New Jersey Institute of Technology, University Heights, Newark, NJ, 07102, United States of America, mei.liu@njit.edu

Coauthor(s): Eugenia McPeek Hinz, Michael Matheny, Hua Xu, Joshua Denny, Randolph Miller, Jonathan Schildcrout

Year: 2013

Abstract: Drugs are important part of today’s medicine, but may cause adverse drug reactions (ADRs). Severe ADRs are estimated to cause over 100,000 fatalities in the US each year. This study examines the use of retrospective medication orders and inpatient laboratory results documented in the Electronic Medical Record (EMR) to identify ADRs. It correlated abnormal laboratory results with specific drug administrations by comparing the outcomes of a drug-exposed group and a matched unexposed group.
