Title: Cost Effectiveness of Containment Strategies in an Avian Influenza Epidemic
Author: David Hutton, Ph.D Student, Stanford University, 380 Panama Street, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States, billdave@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Nayer Khazeni
Year: 2007
Abstract: The Asian Influenza A H5N1 virus is one of the primary international public health concerns of the 21st century. Practical, cost-effective strategies for containment at the source of an outbreak are essential to public health preparedness. We present a hybrid decision-analytic and dynamic transmission nmodel to evaluate the cost-effectiveness of strategies for containing influenza A H5N1 in case of an epidemic.
