Title: Social Psychology Experimental Small Group Studies for Management Decisions with Optimal Choice
Author: Franklin Dexter, Professor, University of Iowa, Department of Anesthesia, 6JCP, 200 Hawkins Drive, Iowa City, IA, 52242, United States of America, franklin-dexter@uiowa.edu
Coauthor(s): Andrew Prahl, Michel Braun, Lyn Van Swol
Year: 2013
Abstract: Operating room operations research decisions with an optimal choice have ubiquitous biases. We reviewed systematically experimental social-psychology studies to explore what a leader can do when working with stakeholders lacking interest in learning management science but expressing opinions about decisions, nonetheless. Leaders will find the most success if they do not bring operational decisions to groups but instead act autocratically while obtaining needed information in 1:1 conversations.
