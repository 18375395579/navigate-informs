Title: Peer Effects in Large Social Networks: Generalized Renormalization Group Methods

Author: Eugene Perevalov, Lehigh University, 200 W Packer Ave, Bethlehem, PA, 18015, United States of America, eup2@lehigh.edu

Coauthor(s): Abhijit Deshmukh, Brandon Pope

Year: 2013

Abstract: Peer effects in social networks can be used to either amplify or negate an effect of external intervention, depending on the nature of the latter. To be able to make use of these phenomena one needs to know what the possible large-scale effects of the given type of intervention are, how they depend on network parameters and what intervention policy has to be to take full advantage of the network structure. We propose a generalized renormalization group approach to address these issues.
