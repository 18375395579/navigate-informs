Title: Who's Good this Year? Comparing the Information Content of Games in the Four Major US Sports

Author: Julian Wolfson, Assistant Professor, University of Minnesota Division of Biostatistics, A460 Mayo Memorial Building MMC 303, 420 Delaware St SE, Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States of America, julianw@umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Joe Koopmeiners

Year: 2013

Abstract: The number of regular season games played in the four major US professional sports leagues ranges from 16 to 162, suggesting that the outcome of an NFL game gives more insight about the relative strength of the teams involved than an MLB game. We quantify the amount of information each game yields about relative team strength, and find dramatic differences between sports in the ability of past game outcomes to predict future results.
