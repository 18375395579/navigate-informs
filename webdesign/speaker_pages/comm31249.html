Title: Security: Herding Cats and Leading Elephants

Author: Alan Briggs, Data Scientist, Elder Research Inc., Odenton, MD, United States of America, awbriggs@gmail.com

Year: 2013

Abstract: By comparison to operations research and management science, the field of data science is still very young. While the initial commentary attempted to set the landscape for this emerging capability, a good portion of follow-on talk has highlighted specific technical applications. There is a shortage of discussion about the softer skills of the field. To effectively manage teams of highly technical yet highly disparate skillsets, data science calls for higher order leadership. This talk explores lessons learned and recommendations for leading teams with highly varied skillsets (a herd of cats), as well as narrowly focused skillsets (an elephant).
