Title: Inpatient Bed Capacity Planning from the Perspectives of Hospitals and Regulators

Author: Thomas Best, The University of Chicago, Booth School of Business, 1000 N La Salle Dr Apt 210, Chicago, IL, 60610, United States of America, tbest@chicagobooth.edu

Coauthor(s): Burhaneddin Sandikci, Don Eisenstein, David Meltzer

Year: 2013

Abstract: We first consider a hospital administrator who decides whether to partition care into focused wings. The administrator faces a trade-off between forming large wings to pool demand and capacity, and forming specialized wings to focus on a narrow range of care types. To inform this decision, we provide an optimization model and apply it to data from an urban teaching hospital. We next consider a regulator of bed capacity who aims to predict and control the effects of capacity changes on society.
