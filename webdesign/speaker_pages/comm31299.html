Title: Identifying Issues in Sourcing: Informing Development of Best Practices
Author: William Hefley, Associate Teaching Professor & Associate Director, IT Services Qualification Center, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, hefley@cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Jane Siegel, Jeff Perdue
Year: 2006
Abstract: Sourcing has rapidly grown in recent years, but has been fraught with numerous issues. Our research explored these issues and informed development of the eSourcing Capability Model for Client Organizations (eSCM-CL), a set of best practices to address the critical capabilities needed by clients to source IT-enabled services. The purpose of this paper is to provide an understanding of these issues, as well as an overview of this model, its intent, its audience and scope, and its structure.
