Title: Using Artificial Neural Networks to Predict the Risk of Breast Cancer
Author: Turgay Ayer, University of Wisconsin-Madison, 1513 University Ave. Mechanical Eng., Building, ISyE Department, Madison, WI, 53706, United States, tayer@wisc.edu
Coauthor(s): Charles E. Kahn, Oguzhan Alagoz, Elizabeth Burnside, Jagpreet Chhatwal
Year: 2007
Abstract: Although breast cancer is the most common cancer affecting women in the US, current methods in practice for diagnosis have low positive predictive values. We developed an artificial neural network (ANN) to predict breast cancer risk from standardized mammographic findings and demographic risk factors.
