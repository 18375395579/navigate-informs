Title: Physician Workload in Primary Care
Author: Hessam Bavafa, Ph.D. Candidate, The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, PA, United States of America, bavafa@wharton.upenn.edu
Coauthor(s): Christian Terwiesch
Year: 2013
Abstract: Our first goal in this paper is to shed light on the effect of physician workload on physician productivity and patient outcomes. Our second goal is to see how the effects of workload are impacted by the available workload diversion channels. Using a dataset of patient visits to a large primary care system, we provide a deeper understanding of the effects of workload on primary care physicians and the effectiveness of diversion mechanisms.
