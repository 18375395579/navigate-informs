Title: Network Equilibrium under Information and Nonlinear Route Choice Behavior

Author: Stephen Boyles, Graduate Research Assistant, University of Texas at Austin, 1 University Station, C1761, ECJ 6.202, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, sboyles@mail.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Avinash Unnikrishnan, Travis Waller

Year: 2008

Abstract: Past research gives guidance on how a single user chooses a route in a stochastic transportation network under nonlinear (e.g., risk averse) preferences and online information. This presentation describes methods for determining how multiple users choose routes simultaneously in such networks with congestion effects, and a formal statement of this type of equilibrium.
