Title: Work Design Choices, Project Characteristics & Project Management Style: A Configuration Approach
Author: Anant Mishra, Doctoral Candidate, Carlson School of Management, 321-19th Avenue S., 3-150 CSOM, Minneapolis, MN, 55414, United States, amishra@csom.umn.edu
Coauthor(s): Kingshuk Sinha
Year: 2006
Abstract: The tremendous growth in information technology hardware and software has opened up a number of alternatives for designing knowledge based work (e.g. R & D) with work design transcending the boundaries of organizations, professions and countries. We propose that the performance implications from a particular choice of work design, is depends on its fit with project characteristics and the project management style. We use the configuration theory for exploring the meaning of fit in our study.
