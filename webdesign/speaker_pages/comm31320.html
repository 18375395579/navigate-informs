Title: Improving the Army's Joint Platform Allocation Tool

Author: Emily Craparo, Naval Postgraduate School, 1411 Cunningham Road, Monterey, United States of America, emcrapar@nps.edu

Coauthor(s): John Harrop

Year: 2013

Abstract: The Army’s Joint Platform Allocation Tool (JPAT) is an optimization model that determines the best way to allocate aerial reconnaissance and surveillance assets to fulfill current and future mission requirements. JPAT has informed critical resourcing decisions and guided the Army’s future investment strategy. This talk describes some recent improvements to JPAT that have dramatically increased it computational efficiency while allowing new classes of assets to be modeled.
