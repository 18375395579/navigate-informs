Title: Following a Group of Targets in Large Environments

Author: Jyh-Ming Lien, Assistant Professor, George Mason University, Computer Science, Fairfax, VA, 22030, United States of America, jmlien@cs.gmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Christopher Vo, Sam McKay

Year: 2013

Abstract: The problem of following multiple coherent targets is called the group following problem. While camera tracking is well studied, the group following problem has not gained much attention despite that there are many scenarios where it is desired to maximize the visibility of moving targets. In this work, we address the scalability issue by investigating the idea of cached visibility. In this work, we show that cached visibility provides better balance between efficiency and performance.
