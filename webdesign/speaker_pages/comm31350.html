Title: Accident Analysis of Passenger Trains on Freight Rail Corridors
Author: Chen-Yu Lin, Research Assistant, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Rm B-118, 205 N. Mathews Ave., Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, clin69@illinois.edu
Year: 2013
Abstract: Quantitative understanding of train accidents on shared passenger and freight rail corridors is critical for rational allocation of resources to reduce railroad passenger train risk. Train accident data from the Federal Railroad Administration database are being analyzed to examine the effects of different accident causes. The results can be applied to identify major train accident causes on shared rail corridors, enabling better-informed risk management on shared rail corridors.
