Title: Sequential Screening: A Bayesian Dynamic Programming Analysis

Author: Peter Frazier, Assistant Professor, Cornell University, 106 Burleigh Dr, Ithaca, NY, 14850, United States of America, pf98@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Li Chen, Bruno Jedynak

Year: 2012

Abstract: Sequential screening is the task of allocating simulation effort to identify those input factors having a significant effect on output. Using dynamic programming, we compute the Bayes-optimal method for splitting factors among groups within a sequential bifurcation procedure. We discuss cases in which existing group-splitting strategies are optimal, and other cases in which the optimal policy is substantially better than current practice.
