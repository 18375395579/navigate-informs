Title: Underlying Themes in the Supply Chain Management Literature: An Initial Investigation
Author: Jeremy Brann, PhD Candidate, Texas A&M University, 4217 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843, United States, jbrann@mays.tamu.edu
Coauthor(s): Antonio Arreola-Risa
Year: 2006
Abstract: Supply Chain Management has varied definitions. As such, the extant literature is topically diverse with widespread target audiences. This paper utilizes text mining to attempt to uncover a set of major underlying themes within the Supply Chain Management literature. These themes are subsequently used to develop a framework for identifying areas within the literature that need further research.
