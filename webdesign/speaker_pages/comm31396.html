Title: Last Mile Distribution in Humanitarian Relief

Author: Burcu Balcik, PhD Candidate, University of Washington, Industrial Engineering Box 352650, Seattle, WA, 98195, United States, burcub@u.washington.edu

Coauthor(s): Benita Beamon, Karen Smilowitz

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider the primary operational decisions related to local (or last mile) distribution in a humanitarian relief supply chain, namely: relief supply allocation, vehicle delivery scheduling, and vehicle routing. We present an MIP model, a Simulated Annealing algorithm, and practical heuristics for last mile distribution.
