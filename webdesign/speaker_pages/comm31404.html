Title: Using an Algebraic Modeling Language for Optimization Class Projects

Author: Robert Fourer, Professor, Northwestern University, Dept. of Industrial Eng & Mgmnt Sciences, 2145 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, 60208-3119, United States of America, 4er@iems.northwestern.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: The operations research literature, particularly the journal Interfaces, is full of realistic mixed-integer programming models that have considerable pedagogical value. This talk describes challenges of adapting MIP formulations to multi-week team projects. The AMPL algebraic modeling language allows students to send challenging formulations to solvers. Data tables in spreadsheets also play a key role, though actual data values must often be reconstructed from limited information in the paper.
