Title: Collaborative Decision Making Processes within Dispersed Environments

Author: Joy Oguntebi, Ph.D. Candidate, University of Michigan, 1205 Beal Ave, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, ogunjoy@umich.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We observe a growing interest in distributed work within the small group literature as technical advancements have led to a reduction in operational boundaries. Concurrently, corporations are increasingly using dispersed teams as a mechanism for accomplishing organizational work. This research study explores the relationship-building development within such dispersed settings and related processes that enable collaborative decision making and improved knowledge management.
