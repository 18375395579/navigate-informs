Title: Store Brand Introduction: Role of Uncertain Consumer Tastes and the Effects on Strategic Inventory

Author: Arcan Nalca, Assistant Professor, Queen's School of Business, Queen's University, Kingston, Canada, arcan.nalca@business.queensu.ca

Coauthor(s): Tamer Boyaci, Saibal Ray

Year: 2012

Abstract: While developing store brands, retailers take advantage of their direct customer contact and generate private information about the liked and disliked features of existing brands. In this work, we investigate the optimal retail strategy to capitalize on the private information regarding the desired product characteristics in the context of store brand introduction. We also elaborate on the interaction between the store brand introduction and strategic inventory in a dynamic environment.
