Title: Organizational Influences on Product Architecture and Their Performance Impact

Author: Alan MacCormack, Harvard Business School, Soldiers Field Park, Boston, MA, 02163, United States, amaccormack@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Carliss Baldwin, John Rusnak

Year: 2007

Abstract: Much work posits a relationship between the design of organizations and the products they produce. Yet little evidence confirms this link or explores its impact. We examine these issues in software. We show that software developed by open source teams is more modular than similar products from commercial firms; and modular software is more evolvable. Our results suggest a product design is driven by factors other than function, with long term and unintended consequences for performance.
