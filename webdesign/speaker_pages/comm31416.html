Title: Digital Distribution: The Impact of New Digital Distribution Channels on Internet Piracy

Author: Michael Smith, Associate Professor, Heinz School-CMU, mds@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Rahul Telang, Samita Dhanasobhon

Year: 2008

Abstract: Digital piracy has concern among content creators that if consumers have access to freely available pirated content, they will no longer have an incentive to purchase content through legitimate channels. We test the impact of legitimate digital distribution channels on piracy demand. Our results suggest that the addition of digital distribution channels reduce the demand for pirated content.
