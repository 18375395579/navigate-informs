Title: The Schedulers Balancing Act of Sensing and Reacting
Author: Jose Antonio Larco Martinelli, CENTRUM Católica Business School, Jr. Daniel Alomía Robles 125 Surco, Lima, Peru, jlarco@pucp.pe
Coauthor(s): Jan Fransoo, Vincent Wiers
Year: 2013
Abstract: Schedulers are required to be responsive to changes in the “world?they manage. To be responsive, they need to both, sense changes and react to these. We use a simulation study to assess how schedulers balance sensing and reacting activities in the field, finding significant opportunities for enhancing responsiveness.
