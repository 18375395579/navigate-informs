Title: Quantitative Models for Vaccine Procurement

Author: Aykagan Ak, Georgia Institute of Technology, School of Industrial and Systems Enginee, 765 Ferst Drive, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States, aykagan@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Clarence Wardell III, Ozlem Ergun, Jessica Heier, Pinar Keskinocak

Year: 2007

Abstract: A United Nations Agency serves as a bulk purchasing agent for vaccines and syringes for 37 countries in Latin American and the Caribbean. Its goal and subsequent challenge is to reduce costs while improving service. We examined its current forecasting and procurement process, explored the potential benefits of using bundle bids, and identified opportunities for reducing costs and improving service.
