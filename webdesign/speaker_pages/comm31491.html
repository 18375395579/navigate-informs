Title: Technology Deployment Flexibility in the Natural Gas Industry

Author: Paul Enders, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, 15213, United States, penders@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Nicola Secomandi, Alan Scheller-Wolf

Year: 2007

Abstract: We model the deployment of production and communication technologies in natural gas production as real options. Based on real data from Equitable Resources, we quantify the incremental value of optimally managing the adoption of production and communication technologies, as well as the additional value of modeling price and flow-rate uncertainty. We also provide insight into optimal investment and divestment patterns, and discuss how these generalize to other resource extraction industries.
