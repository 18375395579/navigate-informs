Title: Optimal Hypertension Management for Risk-averse Patients with Scarce Resources

Author: Gregg Schell, University of Michigan, 1205 Beal Ave, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, schellg@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Mariel Lavieri, Jeremy Sussman, Rodney Hayward

Year: 2012

Abstract: We develop a Markov decision process formulation to determine optimal hypertension treatment regimens for a risk-averse patient with scarce resources who seeks to minimize the expected number of coronary heart disease events over the planning horizon. Initial results from the model illustrate the diminishing returns of increased planning horizon resources. We also investigate the effect of risk-aversion, initial pretreatment blood pressure, and initial coronary heart disease risk.
