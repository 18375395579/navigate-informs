Title: Monitoring Patterns of Sex Trafficking in the United States

Author: Emily Kennedy, Carnegie Mellon University Auton Lab, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, United States of America, emilyckennedy1@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Saswati Ray, Artur Dubrawski

Year: 2012

Abstract: In recent years, the internet has become a popular tool for sex traffickers to advertise and sell their victims. We develop statistical algorithms to monitor publicly available sex service advertisements and group, classify, and characterize them in the context of patterns of potential interest to law enforcement and victim rescue organizations. We present approaches taken, summarize results obtained so far, and discuss their potential for mitigating this serious societal challenge.
