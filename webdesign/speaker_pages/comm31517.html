Title: Multiproduct Humanitarian Healthcare Supply Chains: A Network Modeling and Computational Framework

Author: Min Yu, University of Portland, 5000 N Willamette Blvd., Portland, OR, 97203, United States of America, myu@som.umass.edu

Coauthor(s): Anna Nagurney, Patrick Qiang

Year: 2012

Abstract: We develop a model for supply chain network design/redesign in the case of multiple products, with particular relevance to humanitarian healthcare. The model can be utilized for the determination of the optimal allocation of resources for multiple vaccine and medicine production, storage, and distribution to points of need in the case of disasters or epidemics. It may also be used by organizations to quantify the humanitarian healthcare supply chain costs in a transparent way to stakeholders.
