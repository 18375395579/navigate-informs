Title: The Effect of Movie Streaming on the Consumption of TV

Author: Rodrigo Belo, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, PA, United States of America, rbelo@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Miguel Matos, Pedro Ferreira

Year: 2013

Abstract: Time shifted television increases the alternatives that consumers can choose and allows them to bypass programs from different networks for free. This empowers consumers at the expense of content providers and advertisers and raises the marginal cost to provide service. In this paper we characterize some of the changes that time shifted TV is triggering on TV consumption and discuss the implications for consumers and industry stakeholders alike.
