Title: Priorities for Multiple Decisions Multi-Decisions Decision Making

Author: Thomas Saaty, University Professor, University of Pittsburgh, 322 Mervis Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15260, United States, saaty@katz.pitt.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: High level governmental and corporate decisions require choosing, ordering and allocating resources to many decisions in a multi-decisions decision making process. Actions are improvised to deal with crises. Much politics and committee work determine the outcome. Measurement of intangible basic criteria provides a scientific basis for prioritization and resource allocation of multiple decisions.
