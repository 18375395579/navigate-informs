Title: Sequential Clinical Scheduling with Patient Re-entrant: The Case of Skin Cancer Surgery
Author: Haolin Feng, Lingnan College, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou, China, FengHaoL@mail.sysu.edu.cn
Coauthor(s): Stephen Steidle, Seokcheon Lee, Mark Lawley
Year: 2013
Abstract: Mohs Micrographic Surgery is a popular skin cancers excision method yielding low recurrence rate and positive cosmetic results. It repetitively removes one skin layer at a time until a cancer free layer is found, and this brings same-day patient re-entrants. Currently patients often experience long in-clinic waiting due to the stochastic nature of layer removals. We develop a sequential scheduling scheme to improve patient experience and clinic revenues. Numerical study shows great improvements.
