Title: The TSP Race Problem: How to Minimize Completion Time in Real Time TSP Applications

Author: Bahar Cavdar, Georgia Institute of Technology, 755 Ferst Drive, NW, Main Building, Office: 447, Atlanta, Ge, 30332, United States of America, baharca@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Joel Sokol

Year: 2013

Abstract: In real time Traveling Salesman Problem applications, the computation time is as important as implementation time. However, the current literature focuses on minimizing the travel time and deals with the computational complexity as a secondary criterion. We study TSP Race problem by redefining the objective of the TSP as minimizing the total time from getting the instance to finishing the tour and focus on the computation-implementation time tradeoff.
