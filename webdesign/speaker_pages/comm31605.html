Title: Improving Patient Compliance Using Work ReDesign in an Ambulatory Pediatric Nephrology Clinic

Author: Vivian Reznik, Associate Dean, UCSD School of Medicine, 9500 Gilman Drive #0821, La Jolla, Ca, 02010, United States of America, vreznik@ucsd.edu

Coauthor(s): John Fontanesi

Year: 2007

Abstract: Treating nephritic disease is complex with dire results when treatment recommendations re not followed. Following detailed workflow observations using a standardized instrument, patient instruction, urine testing and discharge process’s were redesigned. With no additional expenditures, efficiency (ratio of patient-provider time to all other time in the clinic) improved more then 18%, patient and staff satisfaction by more then 15% and clinical outcomes more then 30%.
