Title: Tweet the Revolution, Information is Power

Author: Rob Schroeder, Naval Postgraduate School, Monterey, CA, United States of America, rcschroe@nps.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Twitter is a place where users are able to post and send short messages about any topic they like, with most messages pertaining to news events. This paper analyzes the network of Twitter conversations during the Egyptian Revolution in 2012 as well as recent conversations about Syria. It focuses on the content being passed around in order to analyze which accounts have the most social capital by bridging structural holes of information in the network.
