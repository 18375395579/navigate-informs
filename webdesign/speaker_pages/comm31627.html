Title: Designing a Conservation Reserve with Optimum Functional Connectivity

Author: Hayri Onal, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 305 Mumford Hall, Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, h-onal@illinois.edu

Coauthor(s): Yicheng Wang

Year: 2012

Abstract: Spatial contiguity of conservation reserves is crucial for effective functioning of the reserved areas and improving species survival chances. Just physical contiguity may not always be adequate, quality of the connecting areas is important also. We present a linear MIP model for determining an optimal functionally connected reserve while meeting other conservation criteria and an empirical application to a real data set.
