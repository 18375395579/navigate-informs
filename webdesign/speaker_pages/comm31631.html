Title: Iterative Combinatorial Exchanges: An Experimental Analysis
Author: Benjamin Lubin, Harvard School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, Maxwell Dworkin 217, 33 Oxford Street, Cambridge, MA, 02138, United States, blubin@eecs.harvard.edu
Coauthor(s): David Parkes
Year: 2007
Abstract: Expressive two-sided exchanges can increase efficiency in important markets. Our Iterative Combinatorial Exchange enables gradual value revelation directed by linear prices and using an expressive bidding language. A VCG-based rule determines the final outcome. Experiments show computational scalability, limited agent computational requirements, and the utility of price feedback.
