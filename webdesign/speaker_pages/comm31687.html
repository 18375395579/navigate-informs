Title: Adoption of Voluntary Environmental Standards: Empirical Analysis of LEED Green Building Standards

Author: Suresh Muthulingan, Ph.D Student, UCLA Anderson School of Management, Leon & Toby Gold Hall, Suite B512, 110 Westwood Plaza, Los Angeles, CA, 90095, United States, suresh.muthulingam.2010@anderson.ucla.edu

Coauthor(s): Charles Corbett

Year: 2007

Abstract: Organizations adopt voluntary standards as they may have an underlying desire to signal their intent or they may derive intrinsic benefits from the standards. We study which of these factors plays the primary role in the adoption of the voluntary LEED standards for Green Buildings. We find evidence neither factor independently explains the observed adoption patterns, however a combination of the two factors does. We also find tentative evidence that the adoption decision is made sequentially.
