Title: Analyzing College Football Overtime Strategy
Author: Rick Wilson, Professor, Department of Management Science and Information Systems, Oklahoma State University, 408 BUS, Spears School of Business, Stillwater, OK, 74078, United States, rick.wilson@okstate.edu
Coauthor(s): Peter Rosen
Year: 2006
Abstract: Division I-A college football adopted 'alternating possession' overtime rules in 1996. Over this 10 year period, very few teams have varied from conventional wisdom that indicates a teams' chances of winning increase if you are on defense first. We analyze game data over the past decade and show that while there is some evidence of an advantage for this strategy, it is not as pronounced as many think.
