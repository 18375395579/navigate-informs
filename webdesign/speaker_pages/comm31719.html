Title: Including Cyber Warfare Effects in Military Mission Effectiveness Studies

Author: George Blaha, Senior Principal Systems Engineer, Raytheon Integrated Defense Systems, 2461 S. Clark St. #1100, Arlington, VA, 22202, United States of America, gblaha@raytheon.com

Year: 2013

Abstract: The growing use of cyber warfare capabilities necessitates the inclusion of cyber effects into mission effectiveness studies. However, the maturity of cyber effects models lags behind that for traditional military elements (e.g., sensors and kinematic weapons). This presentation introduces the analytical challenge of assessing cyber warfare capabilities within a classic integrated air defense analysis and suggests some key considerations for integration of cyber operations into such analyses.
