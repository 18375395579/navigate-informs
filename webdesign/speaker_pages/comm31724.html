Title: “Costs??of Equity in HIV Prevention

Author: Anke Richter, Associate Professor, DRMI, Naval Postgraduate School, 699 Dyer Road, Building 234, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States, arichter@nps.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: The debate about equity considerations in HIV prevention models focuses on proportionality. However, equity considerations can be incorporated as a variety of constraints or even as part of the objective function. As constraints they can be compared on the basis of lost infections averted. As parts of the objective function, the debate turns to the goal of a resource allocation. Inherent in this debate are the consequences of errors in the model, especially for the underserved populations.
