Title: Avoiding the Eye of the Storm

Author: Michael Metzger, MIT, 34 Shadetree Lane, Roslyn Heights, NY, United States, mmetzger@mit.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: With an approaching hurricane, government decision makers face a set of action timing decisions: supply pre-positioning, mobilization and evacuation. The location, time and intensity of the hurricane at landfall are random variables governed by nature. We develop a stochastic dynamic program incorporating economic, social, and logistical impacts of alternative sequential decisions.
