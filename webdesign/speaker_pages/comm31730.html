Title: On a Theme of Gale and Zimmerman

Author: Alan Hoffman, IBM Fellow Emeritus, 115 Old Mill Road, Greenwich, CT, 06831, United States of America, ajh@us.ibm.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: Let G be a totally ordered abelian group. Inspired by a remark in David Gale's famous paper, “A theorem on flows in networks?(Pac. J. Math. 7, 1957), D. Coppersmith and I have, over the past ten years, studied properties of n-dimensional space whose coordinates are in G: polyhedral combinatorics, convexity topics (Carathedory, Helly, ...). Some conjectures have become theorems, and vice versa. I will also discuss some relevant work by others, most notably, Phil Wolfe and Uwe Zimmerman.
