Title: Multi-Shore Sourcing of Services: Impact of Technology on Sourcing Outcomes
Author: Annapurna Valluri, Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, Suite 500 Jon M. Huntsman Hall, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, avalluri@wharton.upenn.edu
Coauthor(s): Ravi Aron, Lyle Ungar
Year: 2006
Abstract: In this paper we use computational methods to investigate how firms allocate production of services between different wage regimes and service characteristics, namely, in-house, onshore, offshore and automated utility. Our model was motivated by a survey we conducted with executives at different Fortune 500 companies. We also study the impact of different service characteristics on the allocation of production across the different wage regimes.
