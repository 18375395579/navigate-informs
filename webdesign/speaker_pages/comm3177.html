Title: Improving Passenger Demand Forecasts Airline Revenue Management

Author: Subhashish Samaddar, Associate Professor, Department of Managerial Sciences, J. Mack Robinson College of Business, Georgia State University, P.O. Box 4014, Atlanta, GA, 30302, United States of America, s-samaddar@gsu.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Airline revenue management involves protecting the right number of seats for late-booking high-revenue-generating passengers from low-valued leisure passengers. Accurate demand forecasts for all flights are critical. Analysts often adjust the system forecasts to improve revenue opportunity. Little has been done to assess the effectiveness of such adjustments. This paper offers a framework to analyze analysts?performance that includes passenger demand characteristics.
