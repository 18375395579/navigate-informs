Title: Rationality Bias in Water Resource Management

Author: Stefanie M. Falconi, PhD Student, Johns Hopkins University, 3400 N. Charles St, Ames 313, Baltimore, MD, 21218, United States of America, stefanie.falconi@jhu.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: In the water resource management field the rationality axioms have been central to systems analysis models. Empirical studies of water management in real political context point to the oversimplifications of these assumptions. What is the implication of this bias and why does it pose challenges to how water gets managed? This talk will explore several case studies to highlight the inconsistencies between the rationality axioms and the objectives of integrative water resource management.
