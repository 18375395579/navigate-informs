Title: The Benefits and Problems of Using Open Source Software in the Classroom

Author: J. Brian Adams, Assistant Professor of Computer Science, Franklin & Marshall College, Department of Mathematics, PO Box 3003, Lancaster, PA, 17604-3003, brian.adams@fandm.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: In teaching OR, we must often make a decision on what software to use in the classroom. Many commercial packages are available, but they come with both a financial and an intellectual cost. An alternative is to teach the course using Open Source software. In this paper, we present the experience of using several different Open Source software packages in the classroom. This discussion includes the potential benefits as well as some of the problems that may result from their use.
