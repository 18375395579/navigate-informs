Title: Additional Ballistic Missile Defense (BMD) Considerations in a Raid Environment
Author: George Mayernik, Raytheon Integrated Defense Systems, 225 Presidential Way, Mailstop 27/2430b, Woburn, MA, 01801, United States of America, george_e_mayernik@raytheon.com
Coauthor(s): John Krasnadevich, Michael McGowan
Year: 2012
Abstract: Recent USA Missile Defense Agency studies have involved defense of regions against multiple ballistic missile threats arriving within a specified period of time. This paper characterizes the key factors related to BMD against a raid of threats and subsequent impact on defense resources. The effect of raid size and density is shown including defense systems effectiveness in mitigating stressing raid conditions. Radar/engagement timelines and defended areas for a scenario are presented.
