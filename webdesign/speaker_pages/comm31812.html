Title: Social Equity and the Transit Scheduling Problem

Author: Rohan Shah, Grad. Research Asst., The University of Texas at Austin, 301 E Dean Keeton Stop C1761, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, rohanshah@utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Jennifer C. Duthie, Stephen Boyles, Randy B. Machemehl

Year: 2013

Abstract: The talk introduces the TSPEq, a multi-step methodology to attempt the traditional transit scheduling problem (TSP), but with additional factors addressing social inequity and inaccessibility issues for disadvantaged or under-served populations. It aims to provide equitable transit access for all sections of the society, while facilitating inclusion in transit planning models.
