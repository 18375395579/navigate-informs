Title: Scripting and Improvisation in Service Environments
Author: Enrico Secchi, University of Victoria, Peter B. Gustavson School of Business, Business & Economics Building Room 254, Victoria, BC, V8P 5C2, Canada, esecchi@clemson.edu
Coauthor(s): Rohit Verma, Aleda Roth
Year: 2012
Abstract: Using the insights of the research on scripting and organizational improvisation, we develop an econometric model of the impact of different degrees of scripting and improvisation on services financial outcomes. We test our model using survey data and self reported financial performance from the hospitality industry.
