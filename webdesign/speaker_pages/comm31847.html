Title: Seven Principles for Effective Communication with Decision Makers
Author: Lawrence Phillips, Professor, London School of Economics & Political Science, Department of Operational Research, Houghton Street, London, NW3 1AH, United Kingdom, larry_phillips@msn.com
Year: 2006
Abstract: Applying seven principles will improve our communication with decision makers: (1) Understand the client’s problem, (2) Work as process consultants, (3) Engage with groups of key players rather than individuals, (4) Ensure meeting room arrangements facilitate interaction, (5) Use metaphor not jargon, (6) Model on-the-spot and display results graphically, and (7) Focus on creating aligned commitment.
