Title: The Role of Political Culture in Port Reform and Governance
Author: Koi Yu Adolf Ng, Assistant professor, Dept of Logistics, Faculty of Business, Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Hong Kong, China, kyng@few.eur.nl
Year: 2007
Abstract: With the perplexing relationship emerging within hybrid organisational entities, the concept of port governance had been introduced. While much concentration was linked to the relation between governance and port functions, attention on how political culture affected settings and strategies of such reformed institutions was limited. In this study, I will investigate how political culture affects port reform and governance, with emphasis on the power distribution among port's stakeholders.
