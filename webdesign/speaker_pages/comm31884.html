Title: Easing Rail Owners?Capital Crunch Using Public-Private Partnerships
Author: Neil Pogorelsky, Principal Economist, HDR Decision Economics, 8403 Colesville Road, Suite 910, Silver Spring, MD, 20910, United States of America, neil.pogorelsky@hdrinc.com
Year: 2008
Abstract: For the most part, transportation has always functioned as a public-private partnership (P3), but the terms of the partnership have historically been arbitrary. Recently, however, the public benefits of increasing rail capacity in lieu of truck transport have garnered wide attention. We present a quantitative decision rule to identify feasible P3 investments, and then to rank those investments.
