Title: Quantitative Analysis of Eye Movement Datasets for Controlling Neurodegenerative Diseases
Author: Petros Xanthopoulos, University of Florida, 303 Weil Hall, P.O. Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States, petrosx@ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): J. Chris Sackellares, Frank Skidmore, Panos Pardalos, Chang-Chia Liu, Onur Seref, Alla Kammerdiner
Year: 2007
Abstract: Eye movement measurement plays an important role in the examination the progress of the neurodegenerative diseases. There are several techniques for measuring eye movement, the Infrared detection technique (IR), Videooculography (VOG), Scleral eye coil and EOG. In this paper methods for utilizing eye movement recordings towards controlling neurodegenerative diseases as Parkinson's disease are presented and evaluated onto real datasets.
