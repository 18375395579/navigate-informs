Title: Judgmental Forecasting and Biases in a Company
Author: Clint Pennings, Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University, Burgemeester Oudlaan 50, T Building, Room 9-30, Rotterdam, 3062 PA, Netherlands, cpennings@rsm.nl
Coauthor(s): Jan van Dalen, Laurens Rook, Stefanie Protzner
Year: 2013
Abstract: Judgmental forecasting is central to the forecasting process at many companies, but is subject to many biases. An experiment was conducted in which respondents forecast demand and provide input to a planning meeting. Psychological traits are examined to analyze unintentional biases; different departmental roles and incentives (asymmetrical cost functions) are used to influence intentional biases. Various dynamic models are constructed and compared to assess the behavior of respondents.
