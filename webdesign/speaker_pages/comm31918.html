Title: Analysis of Local Impacts of the Regional ShakeOut Earthquake Scenario in Southern California

Author: Laura Dinitz, Operations Research Analyst, US Geological Survey, Western Geographic Science Center, 345 Middlefield Rd., MS 531, Menlo Park, CA, 94025, United States of America, ldinitz@usgs.gov

Coauthor(s): Anne Wein, Pauline Cotelle

Year: 2008

Abstract: A focus study for the USGS Multiple Hazards Demonstration Project analyzes the local impacts of a regional earthquake disaster scenario including lifeline and building damages and economic and social consequences for a Coachella Valley community in Southern California. We will present results on the vulnerability of mobile homes, importance of air transportation, short- and long-term tourism industry concerns, and feedback from community-leader workshop participants who received the information.
