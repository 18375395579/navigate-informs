Title: Examining Moderator Use of Activity-tracking in a Nascent Online Community for Adolescents
Author: Kevin White, UMBC, Dept. of Information Systems, 1000 Hilltop Circle, ITE 404, Baltimore, MD, 21250, United States of America, kwhite2@umbc.edu
Coauthor(s): David Gurzick, Lee Boot, Wayne Lutters
Year: 2008
Abstract: Despite the importance of moderation to the success of an online community, minimal attention has been given to understanding how the moderators function. To this end, this study focuses on the strategies surrounding moderators?use of an activity-tracking tool in moderating an adolescent-centric online community. Specifically, it reports on their use of this tool as a window into the entire community operation and as a mechanism to identify indirect opportunities to engage participants.
