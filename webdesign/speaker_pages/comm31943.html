Title: Design of Farm to School Programs to Fight Child Obesity: The Case of Mexico

Author: Erika Murguia, TIS Consulting Group, Phoenix, AZ, United States of America, erika.murguia@tisconsulting.org

Coauthor(s): Rene Villalobos, Gerardo Trevino, Octavio Sanchez

Year: 2012

Abstract: Mexico is considered one of the countries with highest growth in child obesity. To address this problem the Mexican government has undertaken an aggressive program to increase the consumption of fresh fruits and vegetables to substitute food eaten at schools which tend to be high in carbohydrates and fat. We consider the design a logistics network to support direct distribution to schools from the farms while meeting the nutritional requirements mandated by the official guidelines.
