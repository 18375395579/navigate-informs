Title: Successful Application of Search Theory in Northern Minnesota Missing Person Searches

Author: Richard Slatten, St. Louis County Rescue, 3389 Midway Road, Proctor, MN, 55810, United States of America, Rick.Slatten@newpagecorp.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: Valid Search Theory provides a critical advantage in the planning and management of searches for missing persons in order to save lives and bring closure to families. The author will present a case study illustrating the use of Search Theory to solve land search problems, as well as summarize the evolution of a Northern Minnesota Search and Rescue (SAR) unit from “seat of the pants?search planning, to “pseudo-science?to solid operational footing.
