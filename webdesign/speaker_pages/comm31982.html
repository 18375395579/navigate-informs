Title: Applications of Kitting Materials Feeding Policy to Healthcare: A Case Study in a Perioperative Services

Author: Lawrence J. Bonczar, MS Student, University of Virginia, 151 Engineer's Way, Charlottesville, VA, 22903, United States of America, lbonczar@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): K. Preston White

Year: 2013

Abstract: Kitting involves the creation of kits of raw and sub-assembled components that in total amount to all of the material required for final assemblies. Most large hospitals assemble kits of instruments and disposable supplies for use during surgeries. The efficacy of the kitting process directly contributes to the quality of patient care during a surgical event. The paper reviews research on kitting as a materials feeding policy and applies it to a case study in a perioperative services department.
