Title: Conducting Drug Safety Surveillance Using Electronic Data Sources
Author: Ross Martin, Director, Health Information Convergence, BearingPoint, Inc, 18426 Crownsgate Circle, Germantown, MD, 20874, United States of America, ross.martin@bearingpoint.com
Year: 2008
Abstract: Traditional methods for the voluntary reporting of adverse drug events have proven to be suboptimal for the effective conduct of drug safety surveillance. This presentation reviews some of the current US-based efforts to query and analyze various electronic sources of clinical and healthcare claims data in order to detect clearer signals of possible adverse medical events and discusses ways to create a national electronic drug safety surveillance program.
