Title: Managing Transportation Infrastructure Transition in a Dynamic Commodity Flow Model

Author: Kieran Donaghy, Professor, Cornell University, Department of City & Regional Planning, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, kpd23@cornell.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: The U.S. DOT projects exponential growth in freight shipments resulting from globalization. It also identifies a need to replace aging transportation infrastructure and expand capacity for goods movement, while reducing environmental impacts of transport-related activities. This paper introduces a network model for determining the timing and location of investments in infrastructure systems to manage the impacts of evolving goods movement and promote sustainable regional economic development.
