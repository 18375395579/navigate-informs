Title: Measuring Complexity in Mid-range Products

Author: Kevin Caskey, Associate Professor, State Univ of New York at New Paltz, 1 Hawk Drive, School of Business, New Paltz, NY, 12561, United States, caskeyk@newpaltz.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Product complexity impacts sourcing. For mid- complexity and volume products, we have found that some manufacturers prefer to source locally while others source from lower cost countries. Complexity includes: size, capturing the density of the design network or the number of engineering specifications; frequency, quantifying how often and how much of the design is altered; precision, measuring tightness of design specifications. We will discuss complexity measures and initial field results.
