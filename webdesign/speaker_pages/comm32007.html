Title: How do Prediction Markets Predict?
Author: Abraham Othman, Carnegie Mellon University, 5560 Bartlett Street, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States of America, aothman@cs.cmu.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: Prediction markets are remarkable aggregators of information. But are they more accurate than, say, a subsidized average estimate from a handful of their most prescient contributors? In this talk, I discuss the evidence for and against the so-called Marginal Trader Hypothesis, which attributes virtually all of a market's success to a small group of savvy participants. I discuss the implication of this hypothesis in the design of future markets, and computation's vital role in that future.
