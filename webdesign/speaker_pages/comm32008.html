Title: An Empirical Test of Gain-Loss Separability in Prospect Theory

Author: Alex Markle, University of Chicago, 2958 N Clark St, Unit 3, Chicago, IL, 60657, United States, amarkle@chicagogsb.edu

Coauthor(s): George Wu

Year: 2007

Abstract: We investigate a basic premise of prospect theory, that the valuation of gains and losses is separable. In prospect theory, gain-loss separability implies that a mixed gamble is valued by summing the valuations of the gain and loss portions of that gamble. We demonstrate a systematic violation of the double matching axiom, an axiom that is necessary for gain-loss separability.
