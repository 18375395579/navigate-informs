Title: Optimization-based Cost-benefit Analysis: Government vs. Commercial Provision of Transportation

Author: Ronald McGarvey, Assistant Professor, University of Missouri, Lafferre Hall, Columbia, MO, 65211, United States of America, mcgarveyr@missouri.edu

Coauthor(s): Ricardo Sanchez, Tom Light, Brent Thomas

Year: 2013

Abstract: Intratheater airlift delivers time-sensitive supplies, such as blood products for transfusions, to deployed forces. Traditionally, military aircraft have provided this airlift. However, commercial carriers were providing a significant amount of this airlift circa 2009. But was this more cost-effective than using U.S. Air Force aircraft? To explore this question, we utilized econometric and optimization models to identify the most cost-effective combination of commercial and government airlift.
