Title: The Effects of Problem Structure and Team Diversity on Brainstorming Effectiveness

Author: Stylianos Kavadias, Georgia Institute of Technology, College of Management, 800 West Peachtree Street, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30308-0520, United States, Stylianos.Kavadias@mgt.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Svenja Sommer

Year: 2007

Abstract: In this presentation we seek to reexamine a long debated question between the social psychology and the organizational theory literature, i.e. whether nominal groups are more or less effective than brainstorming groups. We show that the answer is not unidirectional but contingent on the basic parameters of the setting, namely the structure of the problem and the diversity of the team members. We also include more complex problem structures where multiple domains of expertise may be required.
