Title: The Benefits of a Time-Varying Objective Function: Harm Reduction Over the Course of a Drug Epidemic

Author: Jon Caulkins, Professor of Operations Research and Public Policy, Heinz School of Public Policy and Management, Carnegie Mellon University - Qatar, PO Box 24866, Doha, Qatar, caulkins@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrea Seidl, Gernot Tragler, Dagmar Wallner

Year: 2006

Abstract: A central debate in drug policy concerns the relative merits of “harm reduction?(e.g., HIV/AIDS control) vs. “use reduction?(controlling use per se). This paper models whether shifting emphasis over the course of a drug epidemic can improve outcomes for heroin in Australia and cocaine in the US.
