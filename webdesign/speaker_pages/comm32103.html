Title: Examples of Profit Distribution of Sequential Trades and Simultaneous Trades in Arbitrage Networks

Author: Enyu Zhuang, PhD Student, City University of Hong Kong, Room 1101C, Hall 8, Student Residence, Hong Kong, Hong Kong - PRC, zhuang.enyu@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Gang Feng

Year: 2012

Abstract: By regarding financial markets as nodes and arbitrageurs?cross-market trades as edges, an arbitrage network is built. These cross-market trades in the arbitrage network benefit the arbitrageurs and the investors in financial markets. However, the profit distributions of the arbitrageurs and the investors are different between two arbitrageurs?trading modes, i.e. sequential and simultaneous. Some examples are given to illustrate the difference.
