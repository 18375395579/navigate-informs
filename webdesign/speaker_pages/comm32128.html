Title: Balancing Block Size, OR Requirements, and Risk

Author: Kevin Taaffe, IE Graduate Coordinator and IIE Faculty Advisor, Clemson University, 130-A Freeman Hall, Clemson, SC, 29634, United States of America, taaffe@clemson.edu

Coauthor(s): Rebecca Weiss, Lawrence Fredendall

Year: 2013

Abstract: Hospitals have unique preferences in how surgical block times are set for each specialty group or practice. We study the impact of flexibility in risk ratios by group, shared block or open posting time across multiple groups, as well as how an organization may characterize a high quality block schedule. Results indicate that inefficiencies introduced by standard block schedules result in additional ORs being open as well as unused staff labor hours. Data was provided by a major local hospital.
