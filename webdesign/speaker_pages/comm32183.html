Title: Information Technology, Risk, and Quality in Hospitals
Author: Nirup Menon, Associate Professor, George Mason University, School of Management, Faifax, VA, 22032, United States of America, Nirup.Menon@gmail.com
Coauthor(s): Rajiv Kohli
Year: 2008
Abstract: IT helps managers take appropriate risks, and improve organizational outcomes. Administrative IT and clinical IT impact risk and quality directly and by moderating non-IT production inputs and outcomes links. The risk of a hospital is measured by the expected rates of mortality and complications, malpractice insurance premiums, and the financial liability incurred. The quality of hospital service is measured as the actual rates of mortality and complications. This study uses data on hospitals in the State of Washington from 1998 to 2001. Results highlight differences in IT types.
