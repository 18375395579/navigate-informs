Title: The Need for (long) Chains in Kidney Exchange

Author: Itai Ashlagi, Assistant Professor, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, Bldg. E62-577, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, iashlagi@mit.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: We explore both empirically and theoretically the benefit of long chains in kidney exchange. We show that long chains can significantly increase eefficiency when opportunities for exchange are sparse. As large cycles of exchange cannot be used in practice, long chains initiated by non-directed donors significant ly increase eefficiency in pools of the size and composition in practice. Importantly, long chains benefit highly sensitized patients without harming low-sensitized patients.
