Title: Applying the Analytic Hierarchy Process to Ethical Decision Making

Author: Donald Adolphson, Professor, Brigham Young University, Romney Institute of Public Management, 764 TNRB, Provo, UT, 84602, United States of America, donald_adolphson@byu.edu

Coauthor(s): Orrin Cooper, Jared Harris

Year: 2012

Abstract: An often overlooked arena for OR tools is in moral & ethical decision making. The use of relative scales in Saaty’s AHP is especially useful in this regard, because it provides a way to quantify things which cannot be easily measured. The fundamental concept in ethical decision making is Stakeholder Analysis. We will argue in this paper that the AHP can serve a vital role in gaining the understanding of stakeholder interests required to find the sought after joint interests.
