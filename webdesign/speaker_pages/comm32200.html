Title: Mean-deviation Analysis in the Theory of Choice
Author: Anton Molyboha, Ph.D Student, Stevens Institute of Technology, Hoboken, NJ, United States, amolyboh@stevens.edu
Coauthor(s): Bogdan Grechuk, Michael Zabarankin
Year: 2007
Abstract: Mean-deviation analysis, along with the existing theories of coherent risk measures and dual utility, has been examined in the context of the theory of choice, which studies individual preferences in terms of ordering relationships. Axiomatic foundation of the theory of coherent risk measures has been obtained as a relaxation of the axioms of the dual utility theory, and a further relaxation of the axioms has been shown to lead to the mean-deviation analysis.
