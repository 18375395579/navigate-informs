Title: Roadblocks to the Efficient Parallelization of Constraint Solvers

Author: Horst Samulowitz, Research Scientist, IBM Research, 1101 Kitchawan Rd, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States of America, samulowitz@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Ashish Sabharwal, George Katsirelos, Laurent Simon

Year: 2013

Abstract: While modern day hardware provides access to 10,000 and more compute cores, so far parallel versions of combinatorial search algorithms are unable to effectively exploit more than 20 or so cores. This talk aims to raise awareness of the fact that the mechanisms underlying such sequential algorithms can sometimes impose major bottlenecks to their efficient parallelization.We provide a novel look into the behavior of the founding principles of sequential SAT solvers in a parallel setting.
