Title: Panel on Teaching Data Mining

Author: Susan Palocsay, Professor, James Madison University, CIS & Bus Analytics Dept, Harrisonburg, VA, 22807, United States of America, palocssw@jmu.edu

Coauthor(s): James Cochran, Ronald Klimberg, Virginia Miori, Stephen Powell, Galit Shmueli

Year: 2013

Abstract: This panel will discuss various aspects of teaching data mining at the undergraduate and graduate levels. We will cover a wide range of pedagogical issues including pre-requisite skills, topic coverage, course materials, software, and assessments. We will also examine the intersection of data mining with traditional statistics and management science education in the context of business analytics.
