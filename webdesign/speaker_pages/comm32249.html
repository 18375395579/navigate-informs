Title: Optimal Design of cDNA Microarray Experiments
Author: Nilgun Ferhatosmanoglu, Doctoral Candidate, The Ohio State University, 1971 Neil Avenue, 210 Baker System, Columbus, OH, 43210, United States, ferhatosmanoglu.2@osu.edu
Coauthor(s): Theodore Allen
Year: 2006
Abstract: cDNA Microarray experiments are a key tool in efforts to understand the relationships of genes and diseases. We propose a generalization of D-efficiency to address model misspecification and explore its benefits for biologists. Also, we clarify circumstances in which the well known “reference?designs are preferable to “loop?design approaches.
