Title: Global Games over Networks

Author: Pooya Molavi, University of Pennsylvania, L475 Levine Hall, 3330 Walnut Street, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, pooya@seas.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Alireza Tahbaz-Salehi

Year: 2012

Abstract: We analyze a coordination game where players' payoffs depend on actions of their neighbors in a social network. As a result of strategic complementary among players' actions, the game has multiple equilibria when the payoffs are common knowledge; however, introducing some uncertainty results in a unique equilibrium. In this paper we provide a characterization of players' equilibrium strategy, and explore its dependence on the network structure.
