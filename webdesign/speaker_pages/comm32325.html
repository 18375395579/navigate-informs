Title: Operations Research of Enemy Ballistic Missile Offensive Action and Adequate Defense to Counter It

Author: Billy Bradley, Senior Systems Engineer, Raytheon Integrated Defense Systems, 235 Presidential Way, Woburn, MA, 01801, United States of America, Billy_W_Bradley@raytheon.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: The problem presented here involves one of tactical ballistic missile defense from the offensive and defensive sides. The effective number of ballistic missiles that have to be fired to hit a particular target will be investigated as well as determining the number of interceptors to adequately defend the target. Unclassified data is used and is presented along with the location of the data as well as if the data is conjectural. High explosives are used, not nuclear weapons.
