Title: Designing Wireless Local Area Networks with Security Considerations
Author: Frederick Kaefer, Associate Professor, Loyola University Chicago, Loyola University Chicago, 1 E. Pearson Room 372, Chicago, IL, 60611, United States, fkaefer@luc.edu
Year: 2007
Abstract: Designers of Wireless Local Area Networks (WLANs) use access points to provide areas of coverage for their wireless users. At the same time, security considerations often cause designers of WLANs to limit coverage of areas that are outside of their organizations physical boundaries. Incorporating restrictions on areas of coverage increases the complexity of design. This research develops a multi-objective model for designing WLANs that address security considerations.
