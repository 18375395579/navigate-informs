Title: Learning Attacker Strategies in Security Games

Author: Albert Xin Jiang, University of Southern California, Dept of Computer Science, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, albertjiang@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Yundi Qian, Albert Xin Jiang, Emma Bowring, Elizabeth Denicola, Joseph DiRenzo

Year: 2013

Abstract: Previous work on Security Games has mostly focused on domains where the payoff matrix is known to both the defender and the attacker. However, in many real world domains, the defender may have few ideas about the attacker's payoffs, which is a big issue for the defender when designing her strategies. Fortunately, multiple rounds of plays provide the defender the opportunity to learn those payoffs. In our work, we develop models and efficient algorithms to deal with this new challenge.
