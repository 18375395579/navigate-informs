Title: The Impact of Disruptions on Supply Chain Strategy and Performance
Author: John Macdonald, University of Maryland, 3411 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, jmacdona@umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Tom Corsi
Year: 2007
Abstract: Disruptions in supply chains have strategic and performance consequences. Some supply chains make strategic changes after a disruption while others choose to maintain the same strategy. Using the theory of strategic persistence and an experimental methodology, we will manipulate the impact of a disruption and observe its effect on strategic persistence and relative performance. This will provide significant managerial insights for determining strategic directions after disruptions.
