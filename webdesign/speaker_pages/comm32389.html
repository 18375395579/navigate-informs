Title: A Prototype Model for Medical Capabilities-Based Disaster Preparedness

Author: James Broyles, RAND Corporation, 1776 Main Street, Santa Monica, CA, United States of America, jbroyles@rand.org

Coauthor(s): Melinda Moore, Adam Resnick, Michael Wermuth

Year: 2013

Abstract: Disaster uncertainties make local planning difficult, but capabilities-based emergency planning is an accepted, effective planning approach. National guidelines exist for hypothetical disasters that are only specific to an assumed scenario; no research generalizes these guidelines. This research creates a prototype capabilities-based planning tool and proposes generic planning functions that can be applied to varying disaster types and magnitudes to inform community emergency planning.
