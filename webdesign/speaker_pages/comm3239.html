Title: Building Legitimacy for Novel Technologies: The Case of WARF and Human Embryonic Stem Cells

Author: Sanjay Jain, University of Wisconsin-Madison, sjain@bus.wisc.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Building legitimacy for novel technologies within a highly institutionalized environment often involves resolving a fundamental paradox: how do actors conform to an institutional regime while simultaneously taking steps to change it? I carry out an in-depth study of the role played by various actors at a prominent mid-West university ?most notably WARF, its patenting and licensing office ?in supporting the emergence of human embryonic stem cell technology. Through my narrative account, I identify two processes ?which I term protecting and influencing respectively ?that play a key role in building legitimacy for novel technologies. The implications of these findings for theories of institutional entrepreneurship, technological change and technology transfer are discussed.
