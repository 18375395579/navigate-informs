Title: Pattern-based Techniques in Integrative Cancer Genomics

Author: Gabriela Alexe, Research Scientist, The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, 7 Cambridge Center, Cambridge, MA, 02142, United States of America, galexe@broad.mit.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Integrative cancer genomics is aimed to integrate multiple forms of molecular information for understanding the mechanisms of cancer. This novel type of coordinated research raises strategic challenges in the identification of relevant genomic cancer signatures. We review a framework for integrative cancer genomics which addresses some of these challenges. The proposed approach is rooted in the Logical Analysis of Data methodology initiated by Peter L. Hammer.
