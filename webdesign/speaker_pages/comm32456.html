Title: Coordination by Sanctions: Internal and External Consequences in the Lab

Author: Matthew Hashim, Assistant Professor, The University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ, United States of America, mhashim@email.arizona.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Contexts such as the piracy of information goods may lead to consequences for the individual (i.e., internal consequences such as litigation), and consequences for society (i.e., external consequences such as job loss). We conduct a lab experiment to study the role of these sanctions on coordination in a public goods game. Sanctions vary in type depending on treatment, having either internal consequences for individual free-riders, or external consequences imposed by free-riders on the group.
