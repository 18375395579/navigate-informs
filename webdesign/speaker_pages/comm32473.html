Title: Sourcing Strategy and High Impact Low Probability Disruption Response: Theory and Experiment.

Author: Kyle Goldschmidt, Pennsylvania State University, 426A Business Building, University Park, PA, 16802, United States of America, khg116@psu.edu

Coauthor(s): Douglas Thomas, Christopher Craighead, Mirko Kremer

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study sourcing decisions in the context of high-impact, low-probability supplier disruptions. We identify two significant biases: 1) over long periods without supply disruptions, decision makers gradually reduce their supply base; and 2) after experiencing a disruption, decision makers re-adjust their supply base according to the severity of the disruption.
