Title: Health 2.0 - A Different Type of Healthcare Reform
Author: Gerald Kane, Assistant Professor, Boston College, 140 Commonwealth Ave, Chestnut Hill, MA, 02467, United States of America, gerald.kane@bc.edu
Coauthor(s): Rob Fichman
Year: 2008
Abstract: Health 2.0 describes a growing people-powered movement that has the potential to provoke very rapid change in the US healthcare industry. It empowers individual patients and caregivers to exert control over how healthcare happens. It employs a robust new technological foundation that allows people to connect, collaborate, and coordinate in new ways. Involving presentations from industry and academia, this panel explores how Health 2.0 could fundamentally change healthcare delivery in the US.
