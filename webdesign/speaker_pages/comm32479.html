Title: Decision Support Model for Metropolitan Disaster Response Planning
Author: Niki Goerger, ERDC Research Associate at USMA, Engineer Research and Development Center, Department of Systems Engineering, United States Military Academy, West Point, NY, 10996, United States, Niki.Goerger@usma.edu
Coauthor(s): Patrick Driscoll
Year: 2006
Abstract: Terrorist attacks and natural disasters pose significant threats to metropolitan areas. Their ability to recover quickly is key. Determining how to respond and assess progress after such events offer serious challenges for decision makers. In light of this, we present a problem formulation for decision support for metropolitan emergency disaster response planning. The model is based on critical infrastructure states, interdependencies, and response over time.
