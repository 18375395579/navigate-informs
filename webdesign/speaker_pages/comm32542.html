Title: Investment Timing with Incomplete Information and Multiple Means of Learning

Author: Nur Sunar, Stanford University, Knight Management Center, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, Sunar_Nur@gsb.stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): J. Michael Harrison

Year: 2013

Abstract: Motivated by applications in oil exploration and investment, we consider a firm that can use one of several costly learning modes to dynamically explore the economic viability of a project. In addition to dynamic decisions about its learning mode, the firm must decide when to stop learning and either invest or abandon the project. Using a continuous-time Bayesian framework, we solve both the discounted and undiscounted versions of this problem.
