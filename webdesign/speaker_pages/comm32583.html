Title: Inference for Clinical Decision Making Policies
Author: Susan Murphy, Professor of Statistics, University of Michigan, 439 West Hall, 1085 S. Univ., Department of Statistics, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109-1107, United States of America, samurphy@umich.edu
Coauthor(s): Bibhas Chakraborty, Lacey Gunter
Year: 2008
Abstract: Policies estimated from randomized clinical trials can vary greatly across training data sets and to reduce implementation costs should only include important patient variables. Thus it is crucial to provide measures of confidence concerning whether a particular patient variable is necessary. We discuss a method for providing a measure of confidence and illustrate the method with STAR*D clinical trial data.
