Title: Strategic Planning of Forest Management and Industrial Capacity Needs Spatial Models
Author: Eldon Gunn, Professor, Dalhousie University, Dept. Industrial Engineering, 5269 Morris St, Halifax, NS, B3H 4R2, Canada, eldon.gunn@dal.ca
Coauthor(s): Narges Sereshti, Andrew Martin, Evelyn Richards
Year: 2013
Abstract: The major strategies in forest management are based on spatial concepts. These involve biodiversity, ecodistricts, watersheds, old growth and other protected areas. They also involve transportation and relative location of mills to the forest harvest and to each other. In this paper we report on large scale modelling approaches that can accommodate a diversity of spatial constraints and location decisions. We report some numerical results.
