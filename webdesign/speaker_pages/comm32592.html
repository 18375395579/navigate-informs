Title: Optimal Topology for Synthetic Gene Circuits

Author: Cheol-Min Ghim, Microbial Systems Biology Group, Lawrence Livermore Nat'l Lab, 7000 East Ave, L-452, Livermore, CA, 94550, United States, ghim1@llnl.gov

Coauthor(s): Eivind Almaas

Year: 2007

Abstract: Functional capabilities of cells can be attributed to the molecular network of interactions. Despite inherent complexity, cellular networks across the organisms share functional modules with conserved interaction patterns, reminiscent of convergent evolution. Here we comparatively assay natural circuit modules for optimal control in cells, such as chemotaxis and circadian/cell cycle clocks, and discuss the implications on the reconstruction of synthetic circuits for engineering purposes.
