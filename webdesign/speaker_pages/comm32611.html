Title: Real-time Policies for Post-disaster Urban Search and Rescue Team Deployment

Author: Mersedeh TariVerdi, Ph.D. Candidate, University of Maryland - College Park, ENCE Department,1173 Glen L. Martin Hall, University of Maryland, College Park, 20742, United States of America, mercedeh.t@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Elise Miller-Hooks, Matti Adan

Year: 2013

Abstract: In post-disaster circumstances, decisions related to the deployment of urban search and rescue (USR) resources must be taken quickly in the face of uncertainty. Thus, simple-to-follow protocols for resource management are needed for efficient response. In this study, real-time strategies for assigning USR teams to work sites are developed and evaluated against optimal off-line strategies. These strategies build on expert knowledge and existing prioritization methods.
