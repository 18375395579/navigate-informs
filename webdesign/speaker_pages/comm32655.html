Title: Impact of Organizational Characteristics on Performance in Quality Improvement Collaborative (QIC)

Author: Sarang Deo, Northwestern University, s-deo@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Bruce Landon, Keith McInnes, Paul Cleary, Ira Wilson, Charles Corbett, Martin Shapiro

Year: 2008

Abstract: QIC is an increasingly popular method for improving chronic care in the US. However, there is significant variation among the intervention choices of clinics that participate in a QIC. Using data from a national QIC of HIV clinics, we find that intervention choices of clinics are associated with organizational characteristics such as an open culture, QI focus and use of multidisciplinary teams.
