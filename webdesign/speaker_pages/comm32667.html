Title: Optimizing the Timing of Prophylactic Antibiotics
Author: Bryan Norman, University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, PA, United States of America, banorman@pitt.edu
Coauthor(s): Bruce Lee, Sarah Bartsch, Matheus Balceiro Rodriques
Year: 2013
Abstract: Surgical site infections are common postoperative complications that affect patient quality of life and increase health care costs. One strategy that can be highly effective in reducing the incidence of surgical site infections is providing prophylactic antibiotics to patients prior to surgical incision. In this analysis we evaluate the effects of administering prophylactic antibiotics during different presurgical activities to determine a time that minimizes surgical site infections.
