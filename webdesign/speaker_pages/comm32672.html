Title: Small Arms Weapons Effective Life Cycle Study

Author: Gary Kramlich, Instructor & Analyst, Operations Research Center of Excellence, Building 752 (Mahan Hall), United States Military Academy, West Point, NY, 10996, United States, Gary.Kramlich@usma.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: As mission essential equipment is utilized under varied conditions, determining if and when complete overhaul of the equipment becomes more difficult. This presentation addresses the questions: can mandatory overhaul of small arms weapons based on new metrics improve overall fleet effectiveness; and can simulations determine if it is more efficient to fix weapons before they break or only after they have defective parts?
