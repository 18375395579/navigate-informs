Title: Impact of Convective Weather on Outages of NAS Equipment
Author: Jasenka Rakas, Institute of Transportation Studies, 107 McLaughlin Hall, Berkeley, CA, United States of America, jrakas@ce.berkeley.edu
Coauthor(s): Zhuolun Yao
Year: 2008
Abstract: The safety and operability of airports and airspace depends on the functionality of critical automation, surveillance, navigation and landing systems equipment. Weather is one of the main contributors to equipment outages, specifically lightning strikes. This study analyzes the impact of convective weather on service interruptions of equipment related to the National Airspace System (NAS) so that resources can be directed at the facilities found to be most vulnerable.
