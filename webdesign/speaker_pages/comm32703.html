Title: Employee Stock Options: Accounting for Optimal Hedging, Suboptimal Exercises, and Contractual Restri

Author: Siu-Tang (Tim) Leung, Princeton University, Dept. of OR&FE, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States of America, siutang@Princeton.EDU

Year: 2008

Abstract: The main difficulty of Employee Stock Options (ESOs) valuation lies in the uncertain timing of exercises, and a number of contractual restrictions of ESOs further complicate the problem. We present a valuation framework that captures the main characteristics of ESOs such as incorporating the holder's risk aversion, and hedging strategies that include both dynamic trading of a correlated asset and static positions in market-traded options.
