Title: Maintenance Scheduling for Shared Freight and Passenger Rail Corridors

Author: Brennan Caughron, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 205 N Mathews, Urbana, IL, 61822, United States of America, bcaughr2@illinois.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Railroad maintenance windows can be scheduled with the goal of minimizing delay to traffic; however this objective often conflicts with the alternate goal of maximizing maintenance productivity and minimizing cost. This study will review past work in the area of maintenance window scheduling and identify challenges and potential areas for future research specific to shared freight and passenger rail corridors.
