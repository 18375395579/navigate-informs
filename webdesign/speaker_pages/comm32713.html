Title: Modeling the Patient Perspective on Liver Acceptance Using an Explicit Model of the Waiting List

Author: Burhaneddin Sandikci, Graduate Student, University of Pittsburgh, Department of Industrial Engineering, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States, bus2@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Roberts, Andrew Schaefer, Lisa Maillart, Oguzhan Alagoz

Year: 2006

Abstract: End-stage liver disease patients are organized via a “waiting list?to utilize the benefits of transplantation. However, little is known about the effect of knowing the waiting list on the optimum accept/reject decisions. We formulate a Markov decision process model for the liver transplantation problem. The first part of the talk presents appealing results by identifying structural properties of the model and the second part focuses on the results from clinically driven numerical study.
