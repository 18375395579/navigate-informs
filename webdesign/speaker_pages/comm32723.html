Title: Minimax Portfolio Optimization for Risk Management

Author: David Rogers, Associate Professor, University of Cincinnati, Quantitative Analysis & Operations Manag, College of Business, 531 Lindner Hall, Cincinnati, OH, 45221-0130, United States of America, David.Rogers@UC.edu

Coauthor(s): George Polak, Dennis Sweeney

Year: 2008

Abstract: Worst-case risk is considered by maximizing return subject to a risk threshold. Postoptimality/parametric analysis reveal insights for financial firms and Lagrangian relaxation quantifies Knightian uncertainty with alternative probabilities (surrogate prior). Minimax models maximize expected return for the surrogate prior providing insight regarding investors?risk. LP for firms to assist investors to hedge against losses by buying insurance is proposed. A 79-stock/10-period example is examined.
