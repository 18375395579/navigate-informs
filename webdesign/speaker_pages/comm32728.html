Title: Checking in to Check it Out: An Empirical Analysis of Customers?Engagement on Location Based Social
Author: Lei Wang, PhD Candidate, University of Connecticut, 2100 Hillside Road Unit 1041, Storrs, CT, 06269, United States of America, Lei.Wang@business.uconn.edu
Coauthor(s): Ram Gopal, Ramesh Sankaranarayanan, Joseph Pancras
Year: 2013
Abstract: Retailers are increasingly utilizing location-based services via mobile devices to enhance customer engagement. Based on a unique dataset of location-based services (Foursquare and Yelp) for restaurants in New York City, we utilize cross-sectional analyses and find that customer engagement exhibits spatial interdependency that persists over time. Our time-series analysis shows that spatial correlation dissipates with distance, indicating a strong agglomeration effect in restaurant industry.
