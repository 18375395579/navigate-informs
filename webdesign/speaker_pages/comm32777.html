Title: Controlling the Invasion of Sericea with Limited Budgets: Insights from an Optimization Model

Author: Esra Buyuktahtakin, Assistant Professor, Wichita State University, 1845 Fairmount St, Wichita, 67260, United States of America, Esra.Buyuktahtakin@wichita.edu

Coauthor(s): Rajesh Kumar Narasimhan, Halil Cobuloglu, Tanner Lampe, Greg Houseman

Year: 2013

Abstract: Native grasslands in the Great Plains are threatened by the spread of sericea (Lespedeza cuneata), an invasive weed from Asia. Using empirical data, we develop an optimization model, which accounts for population growth rates, carrying capacity, seed dispersal, treatment costs, and economic loss due to invasion. The model determines economically efficient treatment strategies for land owners and government officials for controlling sericea invasion.
