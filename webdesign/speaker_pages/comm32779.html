Title: Knowledge Transfer and Quality Practices in the Implementation of a Capability Model for Outsourcing
Author: Bryon Balint, PhD Student, Carnegie Mellon University, Tepper School of Business, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, bbalint@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Chris Forman, Sandra Slaughter, Elaine Hyder, Mark Paulk
Year: 2006
Abstract: We examine the factors influencing the implementation of the eSourcing Capability Model (eSCM-SP) in a large multinational offshore service provider. The eSCM-SP is a quality program that enables outsourcing service providers to improve their existing capabilities in both service design and delivery. We study how implementation success is influenced by such factors as knowledge characteristics, implementation of prior quality practices, and characteristics of the knowledge recipient.
