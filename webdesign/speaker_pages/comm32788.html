Title: Anchoring and Group Dynamics in a Newsvendor Setting: Results From an Experimental Study

Author: Srinagesh Gavirneni, Assistant Professor, Cornell University, Johnson Graduate School of Management, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States, sg337@johnson.cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Yusen Xia

Year: 2007

Abstract: We conducted a newsvendor experiment in which the subjects (individuals or groups) were presented multiple pieces of anchor-able information. We study whether the subjects tended to change their anchor based on the experimental setting. A secondary objective was to study the impact of group dynamics. We observed that (i) the subjects did not anchor on any particular value, but chose an anchor that was closer to the optimal decision; and (ii) the groups demonstrated a lower tendency to anchor.
