Title: Currency Supply Chain- Efficacy of the Fed's New Cash-recirculation Policy

Author: Mili Mehrotra, The University of Texas at Dallas, 800 West Campbell Road, Richardson, TX, 75080, United States of America, mili.mehrotra@student.utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Vijay Mookerjee, Milind Dawande, Chelliah Sriskandarajah

Year: 2008

Abstract: The overuse of its currency processing operations, characterized by the practice of cross-shipping, by depository institutions (DIs) has motivated the Federal Reserve (Fed) to impose a recirculation fee on cross-shipped cash. The Fed intends to use this fee as a coordinating mechanism for inducing DIs to behave in a manner so that the societal cost of providing currency to the public is minimized. We examine the efficacy of this mechanism and propose a new coordinating mechanism.
