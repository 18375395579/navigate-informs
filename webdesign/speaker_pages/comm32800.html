Title: Networks in Nanotechnology

Author: Stine Grodal, Assistant Professor, Boston University, 595 Commonwealth Ave., School of Management, Boston, MA, 02215, United States of America, grodal@bu.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: A central issue during the emergence of an organizational field is the creation of symbols that enables distinct communities to collaborate across institutional barriers. The creation of a field is thus impacted by communities?shared linguistic practice. I use network analysis to examine the linguistic development of five communities in the nanotechnology field. The results show that over time the nanotechnology label became increasingly central to the communities?linguistic practices.
