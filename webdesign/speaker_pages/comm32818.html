Title: Estimating the Impact of Understaffing on Sales and Profitability in Retail Stores
Author: Saravanan Kesavan, University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill, McColl Building, Chapel Hill, NC, 27519, United States of America, skesavan@unc.edu
Coauthor(s): Vidya Mani, Jayashankar M. Swaminathan
Year: 2012
Abstract: In this paper we use micro-level data on store traffic, sales and labor from 41 stores of a large retail chain to identify the extent of understaffing in retail stores and quantify its impact on sales and profitability using structural estimation. Aligning staffing levels with changing traffic patterns can result in a 6.15% savings in lost sales and a 5.74% improvement in profitability.
