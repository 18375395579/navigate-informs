Title: Improving Store Liquidation

Author: Nathan Craig, Doctoral Candidate, Harvard Business School, Wyss House, Soldiers Field Road, Boston, MA, 02163, United States of America, ncraig@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Ananth Raman

Year: 2013

Abstract: Store liquidation is the divestment of retail outlets through an in-store sale of inventory. We introduce the store liquidation problem, discuss its importance to retailers, and give a technique for optimizing key liquidation decisions: markdowns, inventory, and store closings. Through applications, we show that this method improves liquidation outcomes substantially. We identify ways in which current practice differs from the optimal decisions and trace the consequences of these differences.
