Title: Stockpiling vs. Mental Budgets: The case of the retail gasoline industry
Author: Margaret Aksoy-Pierson, Assistant Professor, Dartmouth College, Tuck School of Business, 100 Tuck Hall, Hanover, NH, 03766, United States of America, mpierson@dartmouth.edu
Year: 2013
Abstract: Although consumers vary the volume of gasoline they purchase at each trip to the pump, they stick to a single spend target, regardless of price changes. This paper explores the moderating effect of mental budgeting on stockpiling behavior using transaction level data from 8 locations over 2 years of observation.
