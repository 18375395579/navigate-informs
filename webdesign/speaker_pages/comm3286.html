Title: The Empirical Implications of Complexity for Economic Models
Author: Adam Wierman, Professor, California Institute of Technology, 1200 E California Blvd MC 305-16, Pasadena, CA, 91125, United States of America, adamw@caltech.edu
Coauthor(s): Siddharth Barman, Umang Bhaskar, Federico Echenique
Year: 2013
Abstract: Recently, the task of understanding the complexity of economic models has received considerable focus. Results have emerged for a variety of models, and the general message is that economic models tend to be computationally hard in the worst case. This motivates the question of whether this complexity has empirical implications. In this work, we begin to address this question in the context of consumer choice theory and bimatrix games using tools from revealed preference theory.
