Title: Geographic Alignment of Service Regions for Paratransit in New Jersey

Author: Eric Gonzales, Assistant Professor, Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey, Civil and Environmental Engineering, 96 Frelinghuysen Road, #606, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, eric.gonzales@rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Mahour Rahimi, Mahyar Amirgholy

Year: 2013

Abstract: Access Link, NJ Transit's ADA paratransit service, faces a challenge in increasing the efficiency and effectiveness of its service, which is currently organized in six service regions. This research develops a methodology to analyze the agency’s costs based on the demand and geographic alignment of these regions. A cost model is developed as a component of an optimization to minimize the cost, by choosing the number and coverage of service areas subject to quality of service constraints.
