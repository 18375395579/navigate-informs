Title: Identification of Ancestry Informative Blocks

Author: Dingxi Qiu, Assistant Professor, University of Miami, McArthur Engineering Building, Room 276, 1251 Memorial Drive, Coral Gables, FL, 33146, United States of America, dingxi@miami.edu

Coauthor(s): Richard Morris

Year: 2008

Abstract: Linkage association studies are often used to improve our understanding of genetic causes of complex human diseases. One difficulty lies in the confounding effects caused by the ancestry substructures. In our study, we propose a heuristic procedure to identify the ancestry-informative blocks, and to decompose the human genes into segments at different ancestry-informative levels. This study aims to provide more accurate estimation of the background allele frequencies for the association studies.
