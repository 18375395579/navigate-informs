Title: Reducing Conservatism in Protective-action Strategies Following a Nuclear Power Accident

Author: Vicki Bier, Professor, University of Wisconsin, 1513 University Avenue, Room 3270A, Madison, WI, 53706, United States of America, bier@engr.wisc.edu

Coauthor(s): Greg Hammond

Year: 2012

Abstract: Protective-action strategies to safeguard the public following a nuclear-power accident are purposely conservative to account for the wide range of uncertainty associated with pre-event planning. In this study, we evaluate the efficacy of a proposed strategy that reduces uncertainty by using site-specific and event-specific data to inform decisions (rather than relying on pre-event regulatory bright lines).
