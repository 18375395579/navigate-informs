Title: FAA Future Demand Schedules for Air Traffic Planning

Author: John Gulding, Senior Strategist, Federal Aviation Administration, 800 Independence Avenue SW, Washington, DC, 20591, United States, john.gulding@faa.gov

Year: 2006

Abstract: Models used to assess future shortfalls must adapt a generic forecast to operational schedules that target key limitations in NAS performance. Schedule quality improves by reflecting seasonal variation and risk ranges around alternatives. For 2006, the ATO Office of Strategy developed a set of future schedules that allows for consistent planning among FAA program offices. The purpose of this paper is report on this development including core functions, verification and user implementation.
