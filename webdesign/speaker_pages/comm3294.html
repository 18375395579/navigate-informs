Title: Catalyzing Collaboration Amongst Strangers: Field Experimental Evidence

Author: Karim Lakhani, Assistant Professor, Harvard Business School, Boston, MA, United States of America, k@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Patrick Gaule, Christoph Riedl, Kevin Boudreau, Anita Woolley

Year: 2012

Abstract: We used a field experiment to investigate the emergence of collaboration in 52 groups (5 members) of software developers. We test theoretical predictions from different disciplines ?regarding the factors leading to the emergence of collaboration. We find that monetary rewards powerfully shape the effort that workers exert in their work. However, we also find substantial evidence for the existence of peer effects, whereby individuals exert more effort when other team members work harder.
