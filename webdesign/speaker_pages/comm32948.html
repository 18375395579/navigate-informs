Title: Docs Under Load: The Endogenous Nature of Work Content in an Emergency Department

Author: Robert Batt, The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut St., Huntsman Hall, Suite 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, batt@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Christian Terwiesch, Olan Soremekun

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present an empirical study of an emergency department that focuses on the endogenous nature of work content per patient. We show that triage nurses & physicians adjust the number of diagnostic tests ordered per patient in response to the level of crowding in the system. During times of crowding, more diagnostic testing is ordered at triage in an effort to improve throughput. However, this leads to an overall increase in testing when the system is most congested.
