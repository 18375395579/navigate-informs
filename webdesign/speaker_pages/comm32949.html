Title: Effects of Intermittent Resources on Investment
Author: John Birge, University of Chicago, Booth School of Business, 5807 South Woodlawn Avenue, Chicago, IL, 60637, United States of America, john.birge@chicagobooth.edu
Coauthor(s): Nur Sunar
Year: 2013
Abstract: Increased presence of intermittent resources, such as wind and solar, can increase volatility of electricity prices to the extent of increasing investment incentives for high-cost rapid-response generation. This talk will discuss this phenomenon and present a model for valuing these investment decisions based on an electricity model with jumps.
