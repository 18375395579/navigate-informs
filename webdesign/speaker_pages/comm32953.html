Title: Environmental and Economic Assessment of Remanufacturing Strategies for Product+Service Firms
Author: Anton Ovchinnikov, Darden School of Business, University of Virginia, 100 Darden Blvd, Charlottesville, VA, 22903, United States of America, Ovchinnikova@darden.virginia.edu
Coauthor(s): Gal Raz, Vered Blass
Year: 2012
Abstract: This paper provides a data-driven assessment of economic and environmental aspects of remanufacturing for product+service firms. We present a model, conduct a behavioral experiment to estimate consumer demand, and based on the realistic parameter values obtained from an industial partner examine when and why remanufacturing results in higher profit and lower environmental damage.
