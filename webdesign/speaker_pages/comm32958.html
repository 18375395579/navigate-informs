Title: The Impact of Sustainability Efforts on Firm Financial Performance

Author: Pei-Ju Wu, Northwestern University, Kellogg School of Management, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, pei.ju-wu@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Sunil Chopra

Year: 2013

Abstract: Firms that invest in sustainability perform significantly worse than firms that do not over the first three years. Eco-collaboration (firms that collaborate do better than those that do not relative to the control group), eco-certification (do better), firm size (larger do better), eco-awards (do worse), and entering margin (high margin do worse) are important moderating factors.
