Title: Staffing under Seasonal Variation in Demand for Neonatal Intensive Care

Author: Gino Lim, Assistant Professor, University of Houston, E211, Engr. Bldg 2, 4800 Calhoun Road, Houston, TX, 77204, United States of America, ginolim@uh.edu

Coauthor(s): Sandra Sullivan, Murray Côt?

Year: 2008

Abstract: Staffing for neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) is an ongoing challenge for NICU managers due to limited resources. Seasonal demand for NICU has not been fully explored in scheduling its staff. We present some of our new findings in NICU staffing problem. Our staffing models take into account seasonal demand and staff preferences on scheduling. Results show that our models can not only reduce the burden of staffing scheduling, but also improve efficiency in staff utilization.
