Title: Operations vs. Marketing Strategy: Considering Quality and Capacity Decisions in Customer Equity

Author: Azin Farzan, The University of Washington, The Michael G. Foster School of Business, The University of Washington, Seattle, WA, 98195, United States of America, afarzan@uw.edu

Coauthor(s): Yong-Pin Zhou

Year: 2013

Abstract: We analyze the optimal decision set for a service provider. The firm chooses both capacity and quality levels and also the marketing strategy and investment level. Because the nature of the consumer behavior is heavily dependent on both the operations and marketing strategy, there is a natural tradeoff between these strategies. We model the effects of operations and marketing decisions on both the customer acquisition and retention processes and create an elaborate model of customer equity.
