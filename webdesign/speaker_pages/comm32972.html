Title: Tropical Cyclone Predictability and the Decision Space for Hurricane Preparation

Author: Eva Regnier, Assistant Professor, Naval Postgraduate School, Defense Resources Management Institute, 699 Dyer Road, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States, eregnier@nps.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Hurricane preparation decisions force a difficult trade-off between risks to life and high false alarm rates. This work quantifies tropical cyclone predictability, and the relationship between forecast accuracy and decision-critical lead times, focusing on geographic differences, and highlights the climatological contribution to New Orleans’s distinction as the most hurricane-vulnerable location in the U.S.
