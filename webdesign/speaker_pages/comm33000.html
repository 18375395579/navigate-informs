Title: Reducing Overcrowding in Emergency Departments using Information Sharing

Author: Masha Shunko, Assistant Professor, Purdue University, 403 W. State St, West Lafayette, IN, 47907, United States of America, mshunko@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Alan Scheller-Wolf, Varun Gupta

Year: 2012

Abstract: While many emergency departments (EDs) in the U.S. experience overcrowding, ambulance crews often act independently and make decisions on where to route patients without any real-time information from the EDs. This independent decision-making approach may worsen the overcrowding situation. We analyze the value of information sharing between the EDs and ambulance crews; we show the characteristics of the EDs and arrival process that make the information sharing most valuable.
