Title: All Reviews are Not Created Equal: The Disaggregate Impact of Reviews and Reviewers at Amazon.com

Author: Michael D. Smith, Associate Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, 4800 Forbes Avenue, HBH 2105D, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States, mds@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Samita Dhanasobhon, Pei-Yu Chen

Year: 2007

Abstract: Online product reviews play an important role in Internet commerce. In this research, we extend prior work by analyzing consumer responses to disaggregate review information. We find that reviews that the community finds helpful have a stronger impact on consumer purchase decisions than other reviews do. Moreover, these reviews have a stronger impact on less popular books than more popular books, where consumers may be able to use outside information sources to evaluate products.
