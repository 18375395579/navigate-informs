Title: An Exploratory Investigation of National Culture on the Behavior of Process Improvement Teams

Author: Kevin Linderman, Associate Professor, University of Minnesota, 321 19th Ave S, Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States, KLinderman@csom.umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Adrian Choo

Year: 2007

Abstract: This study investigates Six Sigma teams from a manufacture of computer components. Teams come from countries in North America, Europe, and Asia. Hofstede dimensions of national culture are used to help explain variation in the efficacy process improvement team variables. The study finds that national culture effects critical process improvement team variables. Organizations implementing improvement practices in a global context need to adapt their practices to the national culture.
