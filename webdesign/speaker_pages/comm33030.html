Title: Measuring Individual Decision-making Competence - The Basis for Well-grounded Training in Decision-making

Author: Nadine Oeser, London School of Economics and Political Science, Houghton Street, London, United Kingdom, nadineoeser@gmx.de

Year: 2012

Abstract: Since good decision processes increase the probability of good decision outcomes, individual decision-making competence (IDMC) seems to be an important parameter to influence the achievement of such outcomes. Research on how to adequately measure and assess IDMC can help to create a better understanding of the different aspects of IDMC, capture an individual’s status quo of decision-making performance, describe areas for improvement, and therefore, guide the teaching of decision-making skills. The present research aims to construct a psychometric test for IDMC, which measures mainly the quality of application of decision-making rules as taught by prescriptive decision science.
