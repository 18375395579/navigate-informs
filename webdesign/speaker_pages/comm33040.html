Title: Software Patent Policy: What Does Academic Research Have to Say?

Author: David Pingry, Professor, University of Arizona, 430L McClelland Hall, Management Info Systems, Tucson, AZ, 85721-0108, United States of America, pingry@email.arizona.edu

Coauthor(s): Matt Thatcher

Year: 2012

Abstract: Since the patentability of computer software was first affirmed in 1981 there has been an explosion of software patents and a vigorous, twofold debate about software patent policy: 1. Should there be software patents? 2. If so, what should the policy parameters be? The academic literatures have had little impact on this important policy debate. This paper examines the state of the academic literatures in addressing these questions and proposes future directions for software policy research.
