Title: Collaboration, Interdependency, and Transfer Pricing

Author: Matthew Hashim, Assistant Professor, The University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ, United States of America, mhashim@email.arizona.edu

Coauthor(s): Karthik Kannan

Year: 2013

Abstract: In resource sharing contexts such as peer-to-peer to caching, information security over a cloud infrastructure, etc., rational agents have been theoretically shown to attain social optimality through auctions. Using a similar context, we conduct a lab experiment to evaluate how well the social welfare turns out to be when agents are allowed to bargain under various levels of uncertainty. We also subsequently study the performances of the auction mechanism to attain social-optimality.
