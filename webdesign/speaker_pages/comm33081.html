Title: Business School Management Science Courses Should Teach Spreadsheet Engineering

Author: Thomas Grossman, Associate Professor of Information & Decision Science, School of Business and Management, University of San Francisco, 2130 Fulton Street, San Francisco, CA, 94117, United States, tagrossman@usfca.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: This paper seeks to help faculty deliver spreadsheet engineering abilities to improve student access to spreadsheet management science, and increase student satisfaction. Business students who rely on spreadsheets can use management science only to the extent that they can build a spreadsheet model of an unstructured business situation. Spreadsheets are a rapid development computer programming language that requires spreadsheet engineering principles.
