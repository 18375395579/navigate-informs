Title: Weight a Minute: Do the Alternatives have Something to Say?
Author: Matthew F. Dabkowski, Instructor of Systems Engineering, Department of Systems Engineering, 646 Swift Road, Building 752, West Point, NY, 10996, United States of America, Matthew.Dabkowski@usma.edu
Coauthor(s): Robert Dees, Terry Bresnick, Gregory Parnell
Year: 2008
Abstract: Originally multiple objective decision analysis obtained the measure range using the extreme outcomes of existing alternatives (Alternative-Focused Thinking). Value-Focused Thinking has been used for many studies to identify future desired capabilities. Two potential concerns are less differentiation of alternatives and potentially different ranking of alternatives. This presentation examines these issues.
