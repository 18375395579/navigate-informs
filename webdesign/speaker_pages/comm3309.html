Title: Teaching Computational Discrete Optimization
Author: Andreas Alpers, Visiting Assistant Professor, School of ORIE, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States, alpers@orie.cornell.edu
Coauthor(s): Leslie E. Trotter
Year: 2007
Abstract: In this talk I will share my personal experience in teaching an advanced undergraduate level course in computational discrete optimization at Cornell. Freely available software is used throughout the course to illustrate various aspects of current branch-and-cut technology. In particular, I will discuss the classroom use of CONCORDE, the current world record holder for solving Traveling Salesman problem instances.
