Title: The Effect of Surrogate Networks on the Interdependent Network Design Problem

Author: Andres Gonzalez, Rice University, 6100 Main Street, MS-318, Dep of Civil & Environmental Engineering, Houston, TX, 77005, United States of America, andres.gonzalez@rice.edu

Coauthor(s): Mauricio Sánchez-Silva, Leonardo Dueñas-Osorio, Camilo Gómez, Andrés L. Medaglia

Year: 2013

Abstract: The main objective of the Interdependent Network Design Problem (INDP) is the efficient design of recovery strategies after a damaging event. In this work, we use the INDP to address the problem of reconstructing interconnected networks considering co-location and functional interdependencies. We also study the effects of using simplified surrogate networks on the quality and efficiency of the INDP solutions as a stepping stone towards better multi-system intervention decision support.
