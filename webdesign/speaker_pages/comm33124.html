Title: Can Social Media Information Improve Disaster Relief Routing Plans?
Author: Emre Kirac, PhD Student, University of Arkansas, Department of Industrial Engineering, Fayetteville, AR, United States of America, ekirac@uark.edu
Coauthor(s): Clarence Wardell, Ashlea Bennett Milburn
Year: 2012
Abstract: This research examines the impact of incorporating two distinct classes of information regarding the location and need of individuals impacted by a disaster when routing vehicles to deliver relief supplies: verified requests obtained through traditional means, and unverified requests obtained from social media. Competitive ratio analysis is performed for alternative decision approaches and computational results for a wide variety of disaster scenarios are presented.
