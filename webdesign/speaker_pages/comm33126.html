Title: Speaking Up about Internal Supply Chain Problems: Experiments on Medication Administration
Author: Anita Tucker, Associate Professor and Marvin Bower Fellow, Harvard University, 413 Morgan Hall, Soldiers Field, Boston, MA, 02478, United States of America, atucker@hbs.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: Employees who experience internal supply chain problems can provide valuable information about improvement opportunities. To understand the conditions under which employees are more likely to speak up about problems, we ran experiments using medication administration tasks for which we purposely created problems. Nurse spoke up under two conditions: (1) to obtain materials if there was no other way to work around the issue and (2) when their role identity was primed to include improvement.
