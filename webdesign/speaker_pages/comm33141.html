Title: Simulating Agent Behaviors in a Deregulated Electricity Market

Author: Zhi Zhou, Graduate Student, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, 110 8th St., Troy, NY, 12180, United States of America, zhouz@rpi.edu

Coauthor(s): Wai Kin Chan

Year: 2008

Abstract: In a deregulated electricity market, market participants make their own decisions to maximize their profit. Their decisions are made competitively, cooperatively, or both. Meanwhile, the market administrator needs to design market rules to maintain efficiency and reliability. In this talk, we present an agent-based simulation model that includes a 30-bus transmission network and study the behaviors of generator agents under different market scenarios controlled by the market administrator agent.
