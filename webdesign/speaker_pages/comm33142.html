Title: Schematic Inertia: A Dynamic and Complexity Pespective on Sared Cognition

Author: Benjamin Herndon, Assistant Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, 800 W. Peachtree St., NW, Atlanta, GA, 30308, United States of America, benjamin.herndon@mgt.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Kyle Lewis

Year: 2012

Abstract: This paper extends theories of socially shared cognition by forwarding a framework to explain how ?over time ?teams are affected by dynamic and residual schema and dimensions of shared cognition. We articulate these dimensions and consider how they form, accrue and decay as team tasks and boundaries change over time. Finally, we posit relationships between these dimensions and a team’s ability to realize performance synergy or dissonance on the task at hand.
