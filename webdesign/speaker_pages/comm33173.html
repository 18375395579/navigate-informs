Title: Reliability Mapping of Remanufactured Products

Author: Wilkistar Otieno, Assistant Professor, University of Wisconsin, 3200 N. Cramer St, RM 503, Milwaukee, WI, 53211, United States of America, otieno@uwm.edu

Coauthor(s): Thomas Omwando

Year: 2013

Abstract: Remanufacturing is becoming popular as a disposal option for products at the end of their first-life. This is due to stringent environmental legislation and the relative economic benefits of product take-back. Challenges of remanufacturing include the determination of quality, cost and warranty estimates all of which are dependent on product reliability estimates. We present a simulation model that maps product reliability considering components with varying age distributions and residual life.
