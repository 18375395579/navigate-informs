Title: Statistical Challenges in eCommerce: Modeling Dynamic and Networked Data
Author: Wolfgang Jank, University of Maryland, 4322 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, wjank@rhsmith.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Inbal Yahav, Shu Zhang, Mayukh Dass, Galit Shmueli
Year: 2008
Abstract: Research in electronic commerce is growing due to the availability of rich data originating from different behavioral social or economic processes and resulting in new dependencies that are not supported by classical statistics. In this tutorial, we discuss three key aspects of eCommerce data: dynamics, competition, and user networks. Each aspect raises new challenges for data modeling, and we present three case studies that showcase the various challenges and discuss some solutions.
