Title: Effective Managerial Presentations of Analytical Work

Author: Thomas Grossman, Associate Professor of Information & Decision Science, School of Business and Management, University of San Francisco, 2130 Fulton Street, San Francisco, CA, 94117, United States, tagrossman@usfca.edu

Coauthor(s): Judith Shaul Norback

Year: 2006

Abstract: Business students struggle to give effective managerial presentations of analytical work, tending to discuss their model and their spreadsheet rather than the model outputs and resulting business insights. We integrate empirical research (from the Workforce Communication Program at Georgia Tech's H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial & Systems Engineering) with observations of business student analytical presentations to identify twelve presentation skills that can usefully be taught in the business school management science course. The process of implementing these skills in business and engineering courses at several universities will be described.
