Title: Spot Pricing and Investment Decisions in an Electricity Market Simulator with Adaptive Agents
Author: Nathanael Miksis, University of Massachusetts, Amherst, 220 ELab, Amherst, MA, 01003, United States, nmiksis@engin.umass.edu
Coauthor(s): Erin Baker
Year: 2006
Abstract: We report on implementing adaptive agents in an electricity market simulator created by EPRI called STEMS. Investment issues are central to market restructuring efforts globally and STEMS is unique in its ability to handle these decision processes. We first cover experience with simple adaptive agents competing in a pure spot market. Later we explore the ability of adaptive agents without tactical predispositions to develop efficient and successful multi-time-period strategies.
