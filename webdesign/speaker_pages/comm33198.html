Title: Scheduling Fuels Removal Projects Across a Forest

Author: Richard Church, Department of Geography, University of California, Santa Barbara, CA, 93106, United States of America, church@geog.ucsb.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: To reduce the chance of large devastating fires and protect habitat, The US Forest Service has been been under a court order to develop plans for SPLATs (strategically placed areas for treatment). In order to maximize the Finney effect, such treatements should be clustered whenever possible. We describe a scheduling model to spatially cluster fuels removal projects to the greatest extent possible. Applications will also be discussed along with the implementation of the model in Arc/GIS.
