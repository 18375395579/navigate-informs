Title: Recent Developments in Coopr
Author: John Siirola, Sandia National Laboratories, Albuquerque, NM, United States of America, jdsiiro@sandia.gov
Coauthor(s): William E. Hart, Jean-Paul Watson
Year: 2013
Abstract: In this presentation we highlight recent developments in the Coopr project, including new language constructs, support for differential-algebraic systems, and support for solver callbacks. Coopr is an open-source umbrella project for supporting optimization within Python. It includes the Pyomo modeling environment and several extensions, including support for stochastic programming (PySP), differential-algebraic systems (DAE), and disjunctive programming (GDP).
