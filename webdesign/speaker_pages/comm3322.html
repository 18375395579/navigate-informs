Title: Informing football schedule creation through alternative objective functions

Author: Keith Willoughby, University of Saskatchewan, 25 Campus Drive, Saskatoon, Canada, willoughby@edwards.usask.ca

Coauthor(s): Kent Kostuk

Year: 2013

Abstract: The 8-team Canadian Football League (CFL) features an 18-game regular season played between late June and early November. We report on our experiences with the CFL in creating optimization models to inform schedule development. Using particular objective functions to create various schedules has enhanced our communication with the CFL and enabled league officials to better understand how analytical modeling enlightens practice.
