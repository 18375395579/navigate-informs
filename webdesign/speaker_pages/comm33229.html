Title: Could We Use a Million Cores to Solve a Single Integer Program?

Author: Ted Ralphs, Associate Professor, Lehigh University, 200 West Packer Avenue, Bethlehem, PA, 18015, United States of America, ted@lehigh.edu

Coauthor(s): Thorsten Koch, Yuji Shinano

Year: 2012

Abstract: Given the steady increase in cores per CPU, it is only a matter of time before supercomputers will have a million or more cores. In this talk, we investigate the opportunities and challenges that will arise when trying to utilize the distributed multi-core architectures that have recently become pervasive to solve a single integer linear optimization problem. We raise the question whether best practices in sequential solution of ILPs will be effective in massively parallel environments.
