Title: The Impact of Over-the-Counter Availability of Plan B on Teens' Contraceptive Decision

Author: Tamar Krishnamurti, Graduate Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 208 Porter Hall, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States, tamark@andrew.cmu.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: In ruling on the over-the-counter status of emergency contraception, the FDA must determine whether adolescents can appropriately self-select & self-medicate. We present an approach to this class of regulatory problems, reflecting analytical and empirical methods grounded in behavioral decision research. We inform this approach with findings from interviews with adolescent females, concerning how their choices about contraception will be affected by FDA’s interpretation of drug licensing laws.
