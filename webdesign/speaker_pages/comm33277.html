Title: Methods of Measuring Airline Network Value
Author: Roger A Parker, Chief of Technology, Marketing Commercial Airplanes, The Boeing Companu, P.O. Box 3707 MC 21-33, Seatte, WA, 98124-2207, United States of America, roger.a.parker@boeing.com
Coauthor(s): Fred Ervin, Richard Lonsdale
Year: 2007
Abstract: Several measures of the effectiveness and value of the world’s airline network are described. These include measure of consumer surplus (the Network Value Index), measures of market share structure, including q-derivatives, the Herfindahl index, and entropy notions, and measures of hub and spoke concentration, such as spoke density. These measures will be compared and contrasted, and evaluated as vehicles for in-depth studies of the world’s airline network. Computing algorithms are outlined.
