Title: Operations Management in Health Care
Author: Stefanos Zenios, Professor, Stanford University, Graduate School of Business, 518 Memorial Way, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States, stefzen@exch-gsb.stanford.edu
Year: 2007
Abstract: In the field of Operations Management we study technological systems which execute ongoing tasks though the coordinated function of technology, people, and operating procedures. In this talk I will review three health care systems that exemplify this definition of Operations Management, will discuss research done to date and explore open research questions. These systems are: organ allocation systems; health care services reimbursement systems; OR scheduling systems.
