Title: A Competitive Analysis of an Industry in Transition
Author: Patrick Murphy, Gerchick-Murphy Associates, 1134 29th Street N.W, Washington, DC, 20007, pmurphy@gerchickmurphy.com
Year: 2008
Abstract: We have updated our work documenting the state of domestic airline competition in the U.S. We have found that the industry changed dramatically beginning in 2000. Low Cost Carriers have emerged as the drivers of growth and pricing. Network carriers have lost their previous ability to segment high-end demand and are focusing on international markets. These trends are continuing.
