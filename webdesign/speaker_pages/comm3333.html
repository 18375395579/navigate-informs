Title: A Prediction Model for the United States Presidential Election

Author: Sheldon Jacobson, Professor, University of Illinois, 201 N. Goodwin Avenue (MC258), urbana, IL, 61821, United States of America, shj@uiuc.edu

Coauthor(s): Edward Sewell, Steve Rigdon

Year: 2008

Abstract: It has become a popular pastime for political pundits to predict the winner of the United States Presidential election. This presentation discusses a Bayesian approach that incorporates the effect of third-party candidates and undecided voters for predicting the outcome of this election. Predictions for the 2008 election are discussed.
