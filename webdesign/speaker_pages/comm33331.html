Title: Service Quality in US Hospitals: Endogeneity and Variability

Author: Justin Ren, Assistant Professor, Boston University School of Management, 595 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, MA, 02215, United States, ren@bu.edu

Coauthor(s): Xin Wang

Year: 2006

Abstract: We study the relation between hospitals' patient volume and the associated operations service quality among US hospitals. We focus on both the mean and the variance measure of service quality, and use a structural model to control for the endogeneity between the patient case volume and the service quality of hospitals. We answer the following questions: (1) Is larger patient volume causing, or caused by, better operations quality? And (2) Are larger hospitals really better than smaller ones?
