Title: Dynamic Spatiotemporal Warping for the Detection and Localization of Heart Attacks
Author: Chen Kan, University of South Florida, United States of America, chenkan@mail.usf.edu
Coauthor(s): Hui Yang
Year: 2012
Abstract: Myocardial infarction (MI), also known as heart attack, is the leading cause of death. This paper presents a novel warping approach to quantify the dissimilarity of disease-altered patterns in 3-lead VCG signals. The hypothesis testing shows there are significant space-time signal pattern variations between healthy controls (HC) and various MIs. The new approach yields an accuracy of 94.7% for separating MIs and HCs and an accuracy of 96.5% for anterior-related MIs and inferior-related MIs.
