Title: Affinity Scheduling for Heterogeneous Computing Systems

Author: Douglas Down, McMaster University, 1280 Main Street West, Hamilton, ON, Canada, downd@mcmaster.ca

Coauthor(s): Issam Al-azzoni, Yu-Tong He

Year: 2007

Abstract: The mapping of tasks to servers is an important function in controlling heterogeneous computing (HC) systems. The HC system is modelled as a system of parallel (heterogeneous) queues. Many different mapping heuristics have been proposed in recent years. We discuss modifications to policies from the flexible server queueing literature and discuss tradeoffs in performance (including guarantees) versus implementation concerns.
