Title: Post-disaster Debris Recycling and Disposal
Author: Alvaro Lorca, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr NW, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, alvarolorca@gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Pinar Keskinocak, Ozlem Ergun
Year: 2012
Abstract: The large volumes of debris generated by disasters can generate severe long term financial and environmental problems to the affected communities if not handled appropriately. We study the impact of strategic decisions in the collection and disposal phases of post-disaster debris management such as recycling, collecting debris separately and incinerating debris, on the tradeoff between financial costs and environmental issues.
