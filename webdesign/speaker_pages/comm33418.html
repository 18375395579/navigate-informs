Title: Pedestrian Route Choice Modeling in a Changing Physical Environment

Author: Lei Feng, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, United States of America, lfeng@umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Elise Miller-Hooks

Year: 2012

Abstract: The selection of routes in a congested pedestrian network in which the physical environment is changing is explored. Models of route choice behavior in this environment are developed. The evolving physical conditions, improving pedestrian situational awareness, responsively changing goals, and individual and collective responses are explicitly recognized in these models of route choice.
