Title: Quantifying the Benefits of Improved Flight Predictability
Author: Kleoniki Vlachou, University of Maryland, 1173 Glenn L. Martin Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, kvlachou@umd.edu
Coauthor(s): David Lovell, Michael Ball
Year: 2012
Abstract: In this research we define new flight predictability metrics which will provide a customer centric measurement of system performance. We investigate how airlines set their scheduled block times. We also analyze the benefit of improved flight predictability for the NAS and Identify how NextGen may lead to improved predictability.
