Title: Worst Case Examples for the Central Path: Redundancy and Large Input Length

Author: Tamas Terlaky, Professor, McMaster University, ITB-110, 1280 Main Street West, Hamilton, ON, Canada, terlaky@mcmaster.ca

Coauthor(s): Eissa Nematollahi, Antoine Deza, Yuriy Zinchenko

Year: 2008

Abstract: In this talk we highlight how redundancy affects the central path of the Klee-Minty cube, i.e., how exponentially many redundant constraints may force the central path to make exponentially many sharp turns. We also discuss some non-redundant examples where the number of sharp turns of the central path is linear in the number of inequalities, while all the constraints are facet defining. These constructions lead to the continuous analogue of the Hirsch conjecture.
