Title: Optimal Incentives for Pro-Social Innovation

Author: Yi Xu, Assistant Professor, Robert H. Smith School of Business, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, United States of America, yxu@miami.edu

Coauthor(s): Christian Terwiesch

Year: 2008

Abstract: The adoption of a pro-social innovation or new product (e.g., fuel efficient automobile) has a positive impact on social welfare. However, both firms and consumers may not fully take this positive social impact into account in their decision making. In this paper, we study how a social planner could play an active role in promoting and stimulating such pro-social innovations through various incentive mechanisms.
