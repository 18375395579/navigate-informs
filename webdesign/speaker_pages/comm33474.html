Title: Strategies for Encroaching on the Low End of Existing Markets
Author: Cheryl Druehl, University of Maryland, 4318 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, cdruehl@rhsmith.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Glen Schmidt
Year: 2006
Abstract: Kodak’s early digitals were expensive cameras that appealed to tech-savvy males. Instead, maybe Kodak should have initially pursued cheaper, simpler digitals. We compare and contrast these two possible strategies, developing an analytical framework that relates these strategies to the notions of disruptive innovation, blue oceans, and willingness to cannibalize. We discuss the link between Kodak’s encroachment strategy and its operational and marketing capabilities.
