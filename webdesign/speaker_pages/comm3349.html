Title: Churn and Contagion in Supply Chains: An Empirical Investigation

Author: Jayanth Krishnan, The Wharton School, 3730 Walnut Street, Suite 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, jayanth@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Kleindorfer, Aliza Heching

Year: 2006

Abstract: Suppliers in capacity constrained facilities face two important problems: “churn?and “contagion? Churn refers to significant changes in forecasts (in advance of capacity commit dates), even when the actual forecasted demand distribution has not changed. Forecast contagion happens when forecast churn across customers is positively correlated even when the customers?demands are not correlated. Using proprietary data from a large semiconductor manufacturer, we investigate these phenomena.
