Title: Optimal Mix of Screening and Contact Tracing for Endemic Diseases (Submitted for the 2006 Pierskalla Award)
Author: Margaret Brandeau, Professor, Stanford University, Management Science and Engineering, Stanford, CA, United States, brandeau@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Benjamin Armbruster
Year: 2006
Abstract: Screening and contact tracing are two common means of controlling infectious diseases. We present a mathematical model that determines the cheapest mix of screening and contact tracing necessary to achieve a desired endemic disease prevalence or to identify a specified number of cases. We present theoretical results and discuss their practical implications.
