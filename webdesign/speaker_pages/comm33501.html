Title: Decision Theory Framework for System Wide Information Management Benefits Case

Author: Fred Wieland, Sensis Corp., 600 Maryland Ave., S.W., Suite 603E, Washington, DC, United States of America, fred.wieland@sensis.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: System-Wide Information Management (SWIM) is an FAA initiative that proposes to eventually provide more accurate and timelier information to controllers, pilots, dispatchers, and others involved in flight planning and execution. One of the potential benefits to dispatchers is knowing whether Special Use Airspaces (SUAs) are available for civil operations or not. This paper estimates the dollar value of better SUA information using standard techniques from the science of decision theory.
