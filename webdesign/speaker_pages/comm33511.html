Title: Modeling and Analyzing Supply Chain Viability

Author: Mariah Magagnotti, Graduate Research Assistant, Clemson University, 110 Freeman Hall, Clemson, SC, 29634, United States of America, mariahm@clemson.edu

Coauthor(s): Scott Mason

Year: 2012

Abstract: The supply chain literature reveals many performance measures focused on localized costs and/or throughput. A holistic view is more appropriate when a supply chain is disrupted due to an emergency or disaster, as many of the key decisions to be made are interdependent. We present a capacitated, multi-commodity flow model under two competing objectives and demonstrate how viability-based decision making can impact both performance measures of interest.
