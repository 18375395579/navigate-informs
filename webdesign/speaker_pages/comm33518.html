Title: A New Liver Allocation Mechanism that Increases Patient Autonomy

Author: Gorkem Saka, PhD Student, University of Pittsburgh, 1048 Benedum Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, United States, gos2@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Roberts, Lisa Maillart, Andrew Schaefer

Year: 2006

Abstract: We present a model that gives greater flexibility to liver patients to express their own preferences. A patient with health h is given b(h) points which are to be allocated among L mutually exclusive sets of livers, assuming that b(h) is monotone in factors such as health and waiting time. The primary optimization question is how a patient with given health and preferences should allocate points. We discuss model formulation and several structural properties related to this allocation system.
