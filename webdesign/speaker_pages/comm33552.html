Title: Dose Calculations: Where and How to Measure Dose

Author: Allen Holder, Trinity University Mathematics, One Trinity Place, San Antonio, TX, United States of America, aholder@trinity.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Uhlig, Arthur Hanna

Year: 2007

Abstract: The accuracy of treatment design depends on appropriate estimations of delivered dose. However, the size of the underlying optimization problem increases with the accuracy of dose, often leading to problems of unusual size. So, limiting the dose model is important but needs to be accomplished to reduce the loss of accuracy. We discuss a new technique to measure dose and the supporting data structures.
