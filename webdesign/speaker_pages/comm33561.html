Title: Homeless Family Shelter Policy and Operations Management

Author: Natalie Privett, Assistant Professor of Management and Policy, New York University, Wagner Graduate School of Public Service, 295 Lafayette St, 2nd Floor, New York, NY, 10012, United States of America, natalie.privett@nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Gordon Campbell

Year: 2013

Abstract: This project investigates the intersection of public policy, decision making and operations planning for the New York City homeless family shelter system, exploring the complexities and operational implications of policy decisions as well as the nuances of forecasting and planning in the context of homeless services. We examine data, interviews, and financial information spanning the full lifecycle of Advantage in New York City, the only jurisdiction in the U.S. that provides shelter on demand.
