Title: Bayesian Social Learning with Consumer Reviews

Author: Bar Ifrach, Postdoctoral Researcher, Stanford University, Huang Engineering Center Room 258, 475 Via Ortega, Stanford, Ca, 94305, United States of America, bifrach@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Costis Maglaras, Marco Scarsini

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study a market with heterogeneous customers who rationally learn the quality of an offered product by observing the reviews of customers who purchased the product earlier in time. We explore aspects of this social learning process such as: its success in revealing the true quality over time, and the effect of the order in which reviews are submitted on consumers?beliefs. In addition, we draw conclusions on the seller’s pricing problem when accounting for social learning.
