Title: Wargaming Process for High-intensity Conflicts

Author: Dan Mahoney, OR Analyst, Center for Army Analysis, 6001 Goethals Road, Fort Belvoir, VA, 22060, United States of America, daniel.mahoney1@us.army.mil

Year: 2008

Abstract: The Center for Army Analysis has developed a High-Intensity Wargaming Process, which is a time-step, man-in-the-loop, force-on-force simulation method that seeks to explore the actions and interactions of modern, high-intensity conflict at the operational level of war. As such, this process is designed to provide insights into operational level issues such as joint effects; force sequencing, allocation and sufficiency; theater sustainment; and lines of communications management, etc.
