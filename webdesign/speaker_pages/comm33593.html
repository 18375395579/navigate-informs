Title: Can We Quantify the Risk of Cascading Failure Blackouts with Branching Processes?

Author: Ian Dobson, Professor, University of Wisconsin, ECE Department, 1415 Engineering Drive, Madison, WI, 53706, United States, dobson@engr.wisc.edu

Coauthor(s): Janghoon Kim

Year: 2007

Abstract: Blackouts of the electric power transmission infrastructure are complicated events in a huge network with diverse, interacting failures. The failures cascade by weakening the system and making further failures more likely. We model cascading in a bulk statistical fashion as initial failures propagating probabilistically according to a branching process. We estimate branching process parameters from data and hence compute the probability of cascading failures of various sizes.
