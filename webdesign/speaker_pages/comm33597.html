Title: Quantifying the Impact of Overdiagnosis in Breast Cancer Screening
Author: Paul Griffin, Professor, Pennsylvania State University, 310 Leonhard Building, Department of Industrial and Manufacturi, University Park, PA, 16802, United States of America, pmg14@engr.psu.edu
Coauthor(s): Sai Zhang
Year: 2013
Abstract: Screening programs have been effective at reducing mortality and in early stage detection of breast cancer. The presence of overdiagnosis in breast cancer screening, however, has a potential for harm. We quantify the impact of overdiagnosis on quality adjusted life years and expected number of mammograms over a lifetime under various screening policies. Recommended screening intervals based on a Markov-based model ranged from 2 to 3 years depending on conditions.
