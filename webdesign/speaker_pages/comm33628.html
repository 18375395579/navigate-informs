Title: Cost Effectiveness of Proposed U.S. Climate Change Legislation

Author: Allen Fawcett, Senior Economist, U.S. EPA, 1200 Pennsylvania Ave. NW, Mail Code 6207J, Washington, DC, 20460, United States of America, fawcett.allen@epa.gov

Year: 2008

Abstract: The US EPA has analyzed several of the leading climate change proposals in the 110th U.S. Congress, including the Lieberman-McCain, “Climate Stewardship and Innovation Act? the Bingaman-Specter, “Low Carbon Economy Act? and the “Lieberman-Warner Climate Security Act.? The analyses use two CGE models (ADAGE and IGEM), and focus various economic and emissions impacts. This paper draws out some of the key insights from the individual analyses, and important comparisons of the three bills.
