Title: The Impact of Social Media on Television Viewership: Evidence from a Large Scale Natural Experiment

Author: Dylan Walker, Boston University, School of Management, 595 Commonwealth Ave., Boston, MA, 02215, United States of America, dtwalker@bu.edu

Coauthor(s): Lev Muchnik

Year: 2013

Abstract: The rapid growth of online social media has significantly altered the way that we consume, process and diffuse information. Social media posts on twitter prominently feature discussions about and reactions to scripted televised content. We estimate the causal impact of twitter discourse on television viewership in a large scale natural experiment that measures actual content through NLP techniques and leverages the exogenous lag of premiered televised programming across geographic markets.
