Title: Social Comparisons in Service Cohorts

Author: Guillaume Roels, Assistant Professor, UCLA, 110 Westwood Plaza, Los Angeles, 90095, United States of America, guillaume.roels@anderson.ucla.edu

Coauthor(s): Xuanming Su

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study the effect of social comparisons on individual performance in service cohorts such as arise in education, health care, and sports industry. We consider two kinds of social comparison depending on whether customers experience a disutility from under-performing or a utility gain from over-performing relative to their peers. We discuss how a service provider should manage her service to enhance or mitigate the effect of social comparison.
