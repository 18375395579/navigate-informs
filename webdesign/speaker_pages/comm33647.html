Title: Sequential Infrastructure Investments: Big vs. Small

Author: Tim Leung, Assistant Professor, Columbia University, IEOR Department, 500 West 120th Street MC4704, New York, NY, 10027, United States of America, tl2497@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Eric Dahlgren

Year: 2013

Abstract: The energy & material processing industries are traditionally characterized by very large-scale physical capital with long lead times & long lifetimes. However, recent technological advancement in low-cost automation permits parallel operations of large numbers of small-scale production units. Such a paradigm shift motivates the analysis of the combined effect of lead time & lifetime on infrastructure investment decisions under uncertainty.
