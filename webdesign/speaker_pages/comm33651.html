Title: A Case Study of National Culture and Services Offshoring
Author: Richard Metters, Associate Professor, Emory University, 1300 Clifton Road, Atlanta, GA, 30322, United States, Richard_Metters@bus.emory.edu
Year: 2006
Abstract: National culture is frequently cited by both academics and practitioners as being the most important concern in pursuing offshore operations. Here, a particular U.S. service process offshored to Barbados and the Dominican Republic is reviewed. The research question centers on how national culture affects operational implementation.
