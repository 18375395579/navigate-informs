Title: Service Engineering: A Case Study of Productivity Analysis in the Construction Industry
Author: Timothy Anderson, Portland State University, 1900 SW 4th Ave, Suite LL-50-02, Portland, OR, United States of America, tima@etm.pdx.edu
Coauthor(s): Gerald Williams
Year: 2008
Abstract: As the U.S. economy has shifted from manufacturing to services, the challenge of understanding and improving services has given birth to the field of service engineering. While many of the principles of manufacturing can lend insight, often services are harder to measure. This talk will examine the sources of productivity loss in a segment of the construction industry with a high labor content accounting for close to 1% of GDP
