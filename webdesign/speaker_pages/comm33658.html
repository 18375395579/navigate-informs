Title: Modeling the Psychosocial Effects of Terror Threats and its Social Dynamics
Author: Amy Ding, University of Illinois, IL, United States of America, wxding@uic.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: In the area of information war, information can be used to produce various psychosocial impacts on the society. In this talk, we analyze the working procedure of brain components that are in charge of human memory and decision making. We then use differential dynamics to model how individual generates a response decision when facing a threat and project population-level possible psychosocial consequences.
