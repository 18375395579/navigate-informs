Title: Heterogeneity in Women's Adherence and its Role on Optimal Breast Cancer Screening Policies
Author: Turgay Ayer, Assistant Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, ayer@isye.gatech.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: The existing controversial breast cancer screening policies consider only women’s age but ignore several other personal breast cancer risk factors and women's adherence to screening recommendations. In this study, we propose a model to redesign the controversial breast cancer screening policies by tailoring the screening decisions to women’s personal risk and adherence levels.
