Title: Multi-Shore Sourcing of Judgment and Expertise-Intensive Services: Evidence From Field Research

Author: Ravi Aron, Assistant Professor, The Wharton School, 545 Jon M. Huntsman Hall, 3730 Walnut Street, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, raviaron@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Annapurna Valluri, Lyle Ungar

Year: 2006

Abstract: Based on panel data collected from providers of multi-shore research and show that providers (firms) that use deep-linked inter-organizational information systems are able to migrate their buyers away from costly synchronous channels of communication (telephone, in-person) towards asynchronous channels that are less costly and over a period of time, more effective. Finally, we also study the factors that are best predictors of what makes ad-hoc sourcers convert to contractual sourcing.
