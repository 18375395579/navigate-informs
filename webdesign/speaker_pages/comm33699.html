Title: Engineering Management & Analytics Star in Cost-Saving Modifications for Travel Procedures

Author: John Dulin, Enterprise Ventures Corp, 771 Fairdale Ct, Castle Rock, CO, 80104, United States of America, dulinj@ctc.com

Coauthor(s): Ashlee Knapp

Year: 2013

Abstract: Separately, unexpected travel fees and trip expenditures beyond forecasts may not seem significant; but together they can have a large impact on budgets. We searched internally to recognize and validate areas that contribute to over-spending. Research areas focused on time deltas: planning to booking, booking to travel, and advanced airfare purchase. By modeling travel procedures and analyzing corresponding data, we discovered streamlining opportunities that could lessen excessive travel costs.
