Title: Autonomous Vehicles and Intersection Auctions: Efficiency and Equity
Author: Stephen Boyles, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas at Austin, 301 E Dean Keeton St Stop C1761, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, sboyles@mail.utexas.edu
Coauthor(s): Michael Levin, Dustin Carlino, Peter Stone
Year: 2013
Abstract: Autonomous vehicles admit new mechanisms for managing intersections and pricing travel choices, including decentralized auction-based intersection control. Simulation experiments are used to explore the relationships between total travel time, travel time impacts by value of time, and equity among travelers with different willingness to pay, as auction parameters are varied.
