Title: Conspicuous Consumer Purchases in a Social Media Context
Author: Lauren Rhue, PhD Candidate, New York University Stern School of Business, 44 West 4th St, KMC 8-186, New York, NY, 10012, United States of America, lrhue@stern.nyu.edu
Coauthor(s): Arun Sundararajan
Year: 2013
Abstract: The visibility afforded by social media generates anticipatory social externalities that shape consumer purchase patterns and preferences. Evidence suggests that social media encourages consumers to diversify their portfolio of conspicuous purchases. Furthermore, the responses to consumer posts are shaped by the existing social network ties and mold the consumer’s norms.
