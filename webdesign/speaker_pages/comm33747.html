Title: Labor-mix and Flexible Response to Demand Spikes

Author: Saravanan Kesavan, University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill, McColl Building, Chapel Hill, NC, 27519, United States of America, skesavan@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Bradley Staats, Wendell Gilland

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this paper, we investigate how labor-mix affects financial performance and service quality. Using data from 451 stores over two years of a large U.S. retailer, we show that the labor-mix of temporary workers exhibits an inverted U-shaped relationship with store profitability and temporary labor-mix is related to lower values of service quality. We also find that employee engagement positively moderates the negative relationship between temporary labor mix and service quality.
