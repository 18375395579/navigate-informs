Title: Impacts of Information Transmission and Human behaviors in Epidemics: A Spatial Game Model
Author: Chih-Hang(John) Wu, Associate Professor, Kansas State University, 2037 Durland Hall, Manhattan, KS, 66506, United States of America, chw@ksu.edu
Coauthor(s): David Ben-Arieh, Songnian Zhao
Year: 2013
Abstract: Information transmission plays an essential role in the dynamics of infectious disease transmission since individuals decides their behaviors based on the current information. Two types of impacts from information transmission to the spread of epidemics, called local impact and global impact, and the corresponding stability analysis are studied in this paper.
