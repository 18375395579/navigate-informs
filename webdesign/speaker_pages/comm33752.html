Title: Combating the “Broken Windows?of Arson
Author: Douglas Thomas, Building and Fire Research Laboratory, National Institute of Standards and Tech, 100 Bureau Drive, Mailstop 8603, Gaithersburg, MD, 20899, United States of America, douglas.thomas@nist.gov
Coauthor(s): Jeffrey Prestemon, David Butry
Year: 2008
Abstract: From criminology, we know that indicators of urban decay provide would-be criminals with signals identifying neighborhoods with lower crime detection and apprehension rates. We model the incidence of arson as a function of “broken window?indices and identify several factors that affect future incidents. Changes in these indices, through neighborhood watches, police patrolling, or community beautification, can combat other, less controllable, but predictable factors, such as weather.
