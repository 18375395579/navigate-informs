Title: Socio-technical Decision Analysis
Author: Lawrence Phillips, Facilitations Limited, 1 Ladywell Court, 22 East Heath Road, London, NW3 1AH, United Kingdom, larry_phillips@msn.com
Year: 2007
Abstract: Although decision analyses are often conducted to resolve difficult technical issues, they can, with careful design, additionally resolve social issues, such as building trust, increasing communication, creating shared understanding, providing authority to recommendations and gaining commitment to the way forward. This paper explains how decision analysis can be carried out to achieve social goals as well as resolve technical issues.
