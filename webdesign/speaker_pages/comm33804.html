Title: Field Experiment in B2B Auctions

Author: Wedad Elmaghraby, Associate Professor, University of Maryland, RH Smith School of Business, Van Munching Hall Room 4352, College Park, MD, 20742-1815, United States of America, wedad_elmaghraby@rhsmith.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Anand Gopal, Ali Pilehvar

Year: 2013

Abstract: Although product assortment problem has received plenty of attention in the marketing and operations literature in recent years, it is yet unexplored in the auction literature. We report on the results of a field experiment that we ran on the auction site of one of the nation’s largest online auction wholesale liquidation sites. The field experiment was aimed at understanding how the number of auctions, the assortment of products and the starting price of an auction impact the final price.
