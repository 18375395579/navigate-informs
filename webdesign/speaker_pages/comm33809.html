Title: A New Paradigm for Project Management Education

Author: Gary Mitchell, Assistant Professor of Operations Management, Pamplin School of Business, University of Portland, 5000 N. Willamette Boulevard, Portland, OR, 97203, United States of America, mitchelg@up.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: In recent years, project management (PM) has become increasingly important to organizations of all types. An effective PM course relates to all business disciplines and therefore is likely to have a greater appeal than most MS courses. In this presentation, we discuss how PM may be the perfect context for introducing business students to MS tools that support and encourage the critical thinking a project manager needs, while stimulating greater interest in other MS courses.
