Title: Optimal Policies for Hepatocellular Carcinoma Surveillance: A Dynamic Approach

Author: Qiushi Chen, Georgia Institute of Technology, H. Milton Steward School of Industrial, 765 Fest Dr., 321 Main Building, Atlanta, GA, 30318, United States of America, chenqiushi0812@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Turgay Ayer, Jagpreet Chhatwal

Year: 2013

Abstract: Surveillance of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) among patients with hepatitis C can reduce the burden of disease; however, the optimal screening strategies remain unknown. We develope a mixed-integer programming (MIP)-based approach and evaluate the cost-effectiveness of HCC surveillance policies from the society's perspective. We estimate parameters from clinical studies and provide risk-stratified surveillance policy by disease severity and patient's age.
