Title: New Directions in False-name-proofness

Author: Vincent Conitzer, Assistant Professor, Duke University, LSRC, Box 90129, Durham, NC, 27708, United States of America, conitzer@cs.duke.edu

Coauthor(s): Liad Wagman, Naoki Ohta, Yasufumi Satoh, Atsushi Iwasaki, Makoto Yokoo

Year: 2008

Abstract: A mechanism is false-name-proof if it is never beneficial for an agent to use more than one identifier. Yokoo et al. have studied false-name-proofness in detail in combinatorial auctions and their variants. I will talk about some of our recent work that applies the concept in other settings, such as voting and coalitional games.
