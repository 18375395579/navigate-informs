Title: New Challenges in Electricity Price Modeling: Emissions, Renewables and Market Coupling

Author: Michael Coulon, Princeton University, ORFE Department, Princeton, NJ, United States of America, mcoulon@princeton.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Many electricity markets have recently undergone various fundamental changes linked to new regulations. These include the introduction of emissions markets, the growth of renewables and ongoing cross border integration (particularly in Europe) via a mechanism called market coupling. Such key changes provide major obstacles for traditional reduced-form models of power price dynamics. We discuss these challenges and propose some alternative structural approaches to capturing these new features.
