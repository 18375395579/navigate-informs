Title: Modelling Soccer Matches Using Bivariate Discrete Distributions with General Dependence Structure

Author: Phil Scarf, Dr, University of Salford, Centre for OR and Applied Statistics, Salford Business School, Salford, M5 4WT, United Kingdom, p.a.scarf@salford.ac.uk

Year: 2007

Abstract: The outcome of a soccer match can be recorded in a number of ways: as scores (goals for, goals against); or with more detail such as shots for, shots against, possession statistics, and other within match events. We will briefly consider the former. For the latter, we generate bivariate distributions using copulas, and fit these to data from the English Premier League. Our aim is to explore the effect of playing strategy on match outcome.
