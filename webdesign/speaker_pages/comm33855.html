Title: Can Airport Congestion Be Anticipated?

Author: Tony Diana, Manager, Information Systems, FAA, 800 Independence Avenue, Washington, DC, 20591, United States of America, tony.diana@faa.gov

Year: 2008

Abstract: Airport congestion occurs when an airport fails to bring under control its mutually interdependent operational subsystems. The study reveals that the odds of congestion in 2007 at the three largest New York airports increased with taxi times and total capacity utilized. Therefore any initiative designed to reduce congestion needs to focus on landside operations, which may further decrease capacity at arrival and departure.
