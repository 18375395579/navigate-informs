Title: Routines and Hospitals' Performance Path: evolution along Conformance and Experiential Quality
Author: Claire Senot, The Ohio State University, 2100 Neil Ave, Columbus, United States of America, senot_1@fisher.osu.edu
Coauthor(s): Aravind Chandrasekaran, Peter T. Ward
Year: 2013
Abstract: Using the theoretical lens of routines, we investigate the relationships between hospitals' structural characteristics (size & task complexity), infrastructural mechanisms (agency) and initial progression along conformance and experiential quality dimensions of the process of care. Findings reveal that size and complexity are negatively related to simultaneous improvement. However, a partial mediation through agency helps countering this effect.
