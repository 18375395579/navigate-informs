Title: New Product Shortages: Supply Chain Failures or Smart Marketing Strategies?
Author: Laurens Debo, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, laurdebo@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Francesca Gino, Peter Boatwright
Year: 2008
Abstract: Shortages of new, innovative products do not only create frustrated buyers, they also generate a lot of free buzz. Manufacturers have been accused in the past of deliberately creating shortages in order to create a buzz and spreading the word better than expensive advertisement campaigns would do. In this talk, we discuss whether these are proper accusations or not.
