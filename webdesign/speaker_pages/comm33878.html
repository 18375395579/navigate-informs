Title: Sustainable Rural Telehealth Innovation ?A Public Health Case Study

Author: Rajendra Singh, Doctoral Student, Georgia State University, Robinson College of Business, 35 Broad Street, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30302, United States of America, raj.singh@ceprin.gsu.edu

Coauthor(s): Max Stachura, Elena Khasanshina, Lars Mathiassen

Year: 2008

Abstract: This research examines the process of integrating telehealth into the clinical and administrative routines of a rural public health district. We use the theory of punctuated equilibrium as expressed in the encounter-episode framework to present and make sense of the innovation process. Results suggest that extensive collaboration within and across institutional boundaries, and a combination of technology-push and opportunistic-pull can help create sustainable rural telehealth innovation.
