Title: Transaction Cost Analysis of Supply Chain Logistics: Firm Based versus Port Centric
Author: Dong-qing Yao, Professor, Towson University, 8000 York Road, Towson, MD, 21252, United States of America, dyao@towson.edu
Coauthor(s): John Liu, Ziping Wang
Year: 2013
Abstract: We develop a transaction cost frontier model. Key findings are: 1) Logistics efficiency is critically dependent on both intra-firm asset specificity and inter-firm environment heterogeneity with port as transaction facility. 2) There is a logistics transaction cost economics that an adaptive advantage can be gained through port-centric industrialization of supply chains, which leads to geographically concentrated horizontal specialization and differentiation.
