Title: User Interface Support for Group Productivity

Author: Mary Czerwinski, Research Area Manager of the Human-Centered Computing (HCC) groups, Manager of the Visualization and Interaction (VIBE) Research Group, Microsoft Research, One Microsoft Way, Redmond, WA, 98052, United States of America, marycz@microsoft.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: Current and future technologies, including various wearables and sensing devices, ensure that robust communications and information transmissions can occur almost anywhere, any time, within a workgroup. Our ability to log, collect, and visualize event data has become more sophisticated, allowing us to analyze trends and identify patterns across many areas of individual andgroup behaviors. In this paper, I discuss our research approach to these trends.
