Title: Resource Allocation for Malaria Prevention
Author: Cesar Gutierrez, Georgia Institute of Technology, 1016 Howell Mill Rd Apt 2510, Atlanta, GA, 30318, United States of America, cgutierrez3@mail.gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Michele Cataldi, Cesar Gutierrez, Jeff Hull, Julie Swann, Christina Cho, Phillip Kim, Andrew Park
Year: 2008
Abstract: The project’s purpose was to develop a process for allocation and deployment of resources used for malaria prevention in Africa. Using Swaziland as a pilot, the team quantified at-risk populations and times for malaria and intervention costs for spraying. They developed recommendations for number and placement of facilities with scheduled deployment for labor, chemicals, and trucks. The team designed an interface to test and visualize the benefits of adapting the methodologies throughout Africa.
