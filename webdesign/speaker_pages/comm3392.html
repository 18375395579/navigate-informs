Title: Global Thermal Coal Flow Optimization Using a Network Construct
Author: Ashley Arigoni, PhD student, Colorado School of Mines, 1500 Illinois Street, Golden, CO, 80401, United States of America, aarigoni@mymail.mines.edu
Coauthor(s): Cameron Turner, Alexandra Newman
Year: 2013
Abstract: Thermal coal is one of the most important global energy sources; it is widely available, inexpensive, and provides dependable power. In the past decade, the thermal coal shipping industry has become vital as coal has undergone a commodization process to accommodate growing demand from new industry players. This presentation examines the thermal coal shipping industry via a network construct, with the goal of gleaning insight into areas for improvement of the current shipping industry.
