Title: Using Terrorism Risk Perception to Better Understand the Public’s Response to Disasters

Author: Heather Rosoff, Doctoral Candidate, University of Southern California, CREATE, School of Policy, Planning, & Developmen, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, rosoff@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Richard John

Year: 2008

Abstract: Assessing the impact of terrorism involves understanding the public’s perception of risk. We conducted a study of how the cognitive structure is related to individuals?perception of risk from terrorism and disaster events. Hierarchical Linear Modeling analyses were conducted to focus on individuals?evaluation of risk as a function of other features of the events (e.g. probability, fear, familiarity, etc.).
