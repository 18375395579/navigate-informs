Title: Deterministic MIP: An End to Chaos in Parallel MIP

Author: Roland Wunderling, ILOG, Ober-Eschbacher Str. 109, Bad Homburg, HE, 61352, Germany, roland@ilog.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: Traditional parallel MIP solvers have been available for a long time, yet two issues prevented them from being used by default: The requirement of expensive special hardware, and the non-reproducibility of results and run-times. Today issue 1 has been eliminated by the advent of multi-core CPUs as a standard and issue 2 by the new CPLEX deterministic parallel MIP solver. We will discuss its design and compare its performance to both sequential and traditional parallel MIP.
