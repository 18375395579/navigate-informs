Title: A Brief History of OR/MS in the Development of Containerization

Author: Ernest Koenigsberg, University of California at Berkeley, W A Haas School of Business, Berkeley, CA, 94115, United States of America, ekoenigsb@aol.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: Fifty to sixty years ago the concept of containerization was ready to be developed into the reality we know today. It took leadership, analysis, and technology to make it happen. We briefly review the history of containerization and note the role that OR/MS played in it. OR was essential in determining such factors as the size and speed of the ships, the size of the fleet, the dimensions of the containers, and the equipment used in terminals and the method of operation of the container terminals. Progress in OR models and techniques has matched the growth of intermodal transportation.
