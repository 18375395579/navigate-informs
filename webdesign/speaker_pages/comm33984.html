Title: Eradication: Evaluating Permanent Disease Elimination as an Outcome
Author: Kimberly Thompson, Harvard School of Public Health, 677 Huntington Ave., 3rd Floor, Boston, MA, 02115, United States, kimt@hsph.harvard.edu
Year: 2007
Abstract: This paper provides a historical and dynamic look at medical technologies for managing polio, explores the issues associated with applying static cost-effectiveness modeling approaches in the context of dynamic diseases, suggests the need for living economic models for interventions that appropriately change with time, and demonstrates important dynamics in the decisions related to eradication.
