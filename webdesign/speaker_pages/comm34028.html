Title: Weight a Minute: What might the Alternatives Say?

Author: Robert Dees, Instructor and Analyst, US Army, Department of Systems Engineering, 4th Floor, BLDG 752, West Point, NY, 10996, United States of America, robert.dees@usma.edu

Coauthor(s): Terry Bresnick, Gregory Parnell, Matthew F. Dabkowski

Year: 2008

Abstract: When we practice Value-Focused Thinking, we attempt to answer the question “What do we want?? At some point, we must transition to the questions of “What can we do?and “What should we do?? We start by thinking about our values and finish by deciding between alternatives. In our efforts to make opaque decision situations clear and enhance decision quality, we attempt to illuminate the differences between alternatives. As we move to decide, our alternatives?range of values across multiple objectives may help to illuminate these differences and provide discriminatory power. We present a possible technique for updating of weights in a multi-objective model once we move from “what we want?to “what we should do.
