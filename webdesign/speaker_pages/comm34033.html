Title: Artificial Intelligence and Algorithms to Optimize Expensive Black-Box Functions

Author: Deng Huang, Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, PO Box 999, MS-K6-08, Richland, WA, 99352, United States, deng.huang@pnl.gov

Coauthor(s): Rogene Eichler West, Theodore Allen

Year: 2006

Abstract: When the objective function is black-boxed and expensive, we analyze all available data points before the next move. Also we utilize low-fidelity data to reduce evaluation cost. These strategies mimic human actions in scientific discovery. Two methods are illustrated: Multiple Fidelity Sequential Kriging Optimization (MFSKO) and Hierarchical Bayesian Optimization Algorithm (hBOA). Application examples include radiation detector design and material discovery.
