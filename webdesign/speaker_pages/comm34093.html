Title: Firm Performance in Dynamic Environments: The Role of Operational Slack and Operational Flexibility
Author: Manpreet Hora, Georgia Institute of Technology, 800 West Peachtree Street, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30075, United States of America, Manpreet.Hora@scheller.gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Jeremy Kovach, Pankaj Patel
Year: 2013
Abstract: This study examines operational slack and operational flexibility under two components of environmental dynamism - unpredictability and instability. We collect data on 964 publicly traded firms in 23 industries from the years 1998 to 2007 (representing 8,473 firm year observations). Using panel data analysis, we find that operational flexibility is beneficial in highly unpredictable markets, whereas operational slack is beneficial in highly unstable markets.
