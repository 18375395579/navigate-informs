Title: A Peer-to-peer Network Security Pricing Model to Increase Networks?Resistance to Virus Propagation
Author: Daniel Rice, Loyola College in Maryland, 4501 N. Charles Street, Baltimore, MD, 21210, United States, drice2@loyola.edu
Coauthor(s): George Wright
Year: 2007
Abstract: This research presents a peer-to-peer (P2P) network security pricing model (NSPM) that promotes the growth of P2P networks having an increased resistance to malicious code propagation. The P2P NSPM integrates findings from recent developments in complex network theory with an incentive-compatible pricing model. Prices are a function of the Pearson coefficient. Early results demonstrate that the P2P NSPM impacts the overall structure of the network, creating more secure P2P networks.
