Title: Mentoring High School, Undergraduate & Graduate Students - Lessons Learned by an Assistant Professor
Author: Jun Zhuang, Assistant Professor, University at Buffalo, SUNY, 317 Bell Hall, Buffalo, NY, United States of America, jzhuang@buffalo.edu
Year: 2013
Abstract: Mentoring students could be both an extremely frustrating and an extremely rewarding process, especially for junior faculty without extensive experiences. In this talk, we will share some lessons learned by an assistant professor from mentoring a large group of high school, undergraduate and graduate students on research in the past five years. We will discuss the strategies of recruiting, mentoring, maintaining, and graduating students, including those from underrepresented groups.
