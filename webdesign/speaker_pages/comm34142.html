Title: A Change-point Approach to Estimate Post-disaster Built Environment Recovery

Author: Henry Lester, PhD Candidate, The University of Alabama, Box 870205, Tuscaloosa, AL, United States of America, leste019@crimson.ua.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Construction increases in disaster-prone areas dictate disaster planning to safeguard at risk populations during the recovery phase of the disaster life cycle and this planning necessitates a temporal recovery metric. This paper presents a change-point approach to estimating post-disaster built environment recovery. The approach examines spatiotemporal residential construction variability to determine built environment rapidity providing a temporal metric for operative disaster planning.
