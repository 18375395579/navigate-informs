Title: Cost-effective Enrichment Strategies for Alzheimer's Clinical Trial Screening by Data Mining

Author: Mona Haghighi, University of South Florida, 12760, University Club Dr., Tampa, FL, 33612, United States of America, monahaghighi@mail.usf.edu

Coauthor(s): Shuai Huang

Year: 2013

Abstract: The development of anti-amyloid preventative treatments, which hold great promise of preventing Alzheimer's disease, rely on a cost-effective screening method for identifying amyloid positive subjects for clinical trials, which is currently lacking. We investigate how data mining approaches can help the development of cost-effective enrichment strategies.
