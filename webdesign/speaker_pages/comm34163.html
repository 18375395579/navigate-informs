Title: Role of Project Organization Choice on Architectural Misalignment in Distributed Technology Projects

Author: Anant Mishra, University of Minnesota, 321 19th Ave S, Minneapolis, United States, amishra@csom.umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Kingshuk Sinha

Year: 2007

Abstract: Architecture misalignment occurs during the integration phase of a project when task modules that share design interfaces and functional dependencies are not aligned, and do not function well together. Based on data collected from 665 projects between January to March 2007, we investigate (i) the direct effect of project organization choice on architectural misalignment and (ii) how project organization choice moderates the relationship between architecture misalignment and project performance.
