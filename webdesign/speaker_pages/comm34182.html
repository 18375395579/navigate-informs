Title: High Vehicular Mobility Improves the Performance of Information Flow in Vehicle Ad Hoc Networks

Author: Lili Du, RPI, dul@rpi.edu

Coauthor(s): Satish Ukkusuri

Year: 2007

Abstract: High vehicular mobility distinguishes Vehicular Ad Hoc Networks (VANET) from general mobile ad hoc networks. The analytical models in this research characterize the reachability of a defined Information Flow Network (IFN) while incorporating vehicular lane-changing behavior and relative speed. The closed form expressions for the expected node degree as well as the reachability in an IFN are provided. Simulation models are developed to validate the analytical results.
