Title: Volatility and Valuation of Early Stage Firms with Jump Risk
Author: Nalin Kulatilaka, Wing Tat Lee Family Professor of Management, Professor of Finance, Boston University, School of Management, 595 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, MA, 02215, United States, nalink@bu.edu
Coauthor(s): Feng Shu
Year: 2006
Abstract: We model early-stage firms as sequences of call options leading to mature firms, thus, volatility is critical to proper valuation. Ignoring jumps due to innovations or failure results in over-estimates of volatility. The impact on firm value also depends on whether the option is away-from or at-the money (investment requirement). Our results explain why volatility of firms undergoes discrete reductions when clearing investment stages and provide new insights regarding over- and under-valuation.
