Title: New Business Models in Information Economy: GDP and Case Studies in Korea

Author: Hosun Rhim, Professor, Korea University Business School, Anam-dong Seongbuk-gu, Seoul, 136-701, Korea, Republic of, hrhim@korea.ac.kr

Coauthor(s): Min Choi, Kwangtae Park

Year: 2007

Abstract: This paper examines the structure of Korean information economy in 1990, 1995, and 2000 using M.U. Porat's research framework in 1967 as the foundation and assesses the growth experienced by different industries and sectors over the years. The study shows that the share of information economy in total GDP grew from 51.92% in 1990 to 59.03% in 2000 implying that the structural changes may accompany newly emerging business models in information economy.
