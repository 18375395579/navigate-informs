Title: How Can Operations Management Methods Reduce System-wide Food Waste and its Environmental Impact?

Author: Alexandra Heeney, PhD student, Stanford University, 566 Arguello Way, Apt 117, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, aheeney@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Warren H. Hausman, Dariush Rafinejad

Year: 2013

Abstract: Worldwide, 30-40% of food is wasted at all levels of the supply chain - approximately 1.3 billion tonnes - and every kilo or tonne of that food generates at least 4.5 times that amount of carbon dioxide emissions which could have been avoided. By taking a supply chain perspective, this talk explores how to model the food distribution, taking into account environmental externalities, and how improving system operations or influencing strategic consumer behavior could help mitigate food waste.
