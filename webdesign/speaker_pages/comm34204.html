Title: Information Strategies for Information Age: In Search of a Theory of IT-Enabled Market Transparency

Author: Alok Gupta, Carlson School Professor of IDSc, University of Minnesota, 321 19th Avenue South, 3-365 CSOM, Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States, alok@umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Robert Kauffman, Nelson Granados

Year: 2007

Abstract: The Internet has brought about significant changes in the availability of market information in many industries. Firms are increasingly able to design electronic selling mechanisms that re-veal, conceal, bias, and distort market information. We present a research agenda on the topic of market transparency for information-intensive industries where many firms are developing in-novative technologies to implement strategies exploiting the quantity and quality of information provided to consumers.
