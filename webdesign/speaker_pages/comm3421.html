Title: Biological and Chemical Terrorism Risk Analysis

Author: Terry O'Sullivan, Postdoctoral Research Associate, University of Southern California, CREATE Center, RTH 314, Los Angeles, CA, 90089-2902, United States, osulliv@usc.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Biological & chemical terrorist agents are unique, modern catastrophic threats facing Western societies. This presentation frames related policy and research problems for risk assessment, and discusses the overall structure of existing & planned preparation & response to biological and chemical terror. It concludes by outlining ongoing bioterrorism cost-benefit modeling projects at USC, such as analyzing the BioWatch detection program & the Cities Readiness Initiative SNS response pilot.
