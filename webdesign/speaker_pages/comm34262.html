Title: Television Advertising and Online Search

Author: Mingyu (Max) Joo, Assistant Professor, The Ohio State University, 2100 Neil Ave., Columbus, OH, 43210, United States of America, joo.85@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ken Wilbur, Bo Cowgill, Yi Zhu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Despite a 20-year trend toward integrated marketing communications, advertisers seldom coordinate TV and search ad campaigns. We find that TV ads for financial services brands increase both the number of related Google searches and searchers?tendency to use branded keywords in place of generic keywords. The elasticity of a brand’s total searches with respect to its TV advertising is .17, an effect that peaks in the morning.
