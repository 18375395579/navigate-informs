Title: Safety vs Capacity- The Safety Operating Characteristic for NextGen
Author: Sherry Borener, Senior Scientist, AVS/FAA, 800 Independence Avenue, Washington, DC, 200008, United States of America, Sherry.Borener@faa.gov
Year: 2008
Abstract: The relationship bewteen the safe, desired and delivered number of flight operations per day defines a Safety Operating Characteristic. If we normalize events against the number of operations delivered in the NAS we obscure the impact of safety constraints. Capacity and number of operations delivered are distinct, and informative. The SOC can be applied to forecast the potential improvement in enabled operations due to NextGen improvements.
