Title: Applying Game Theory to Humanitarian Supply Chains: Literature Review and Future Research Direction

Author: Timothy Muggy, PhD Student, Kansas State University, Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Eng, 2037 Durland Hall, Manhattan, KS, 66502, United States of America, lmuggy@ksu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jessica Heier Stamm

Year: 2012

Abstract: Humanitarian logistics systems are often characterized by the presence of multiple decision makers who act according to their own objectives, but whose choices impact the entire system. Applications of game theory in this context are increasing due to its ability to model interactions of decentralized agents, yet many opportunities remain to improve humanitarian logistics systems through the use of game theory models. We synthesize existing literature and identify directions for future research.
