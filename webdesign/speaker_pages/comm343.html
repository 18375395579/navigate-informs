Title: Team Structure in Collaborative Projects

Author: Morvarid Rahmani, PhD Candidate, UCLA, 110 Westwood Plaza, Los Angeles, 90095, United States of America, morvarid.rahmani.2013@anderson.ucla.edu

Coauthor(s): Guillaume Roels, Uday Karmarkar

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this paper, we study how teams should be structured in dynamic collaborative projects. Consider, for instance, a team working on developing a new product or service. Should the team organization be flat, letting every member of the team make decisions on the project, or should it be hierarchical, letting a project manager make all decisions on the project?
