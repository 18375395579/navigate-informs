Title: Scheduling the Brazilian Soccer Tournament: Solution Approach and Practice
Author: Celso Ribeiro, Universidade Federal Fluminense, Niterói, 22410-240, Brazil, celso@ic.uff.br
Coauthor(s): Sebastian Urrutia
Year: 2013
Abstract: The Brazilian soccer tournament is a compact, mirrored double round-robin tournament played by 20 teams in each of its two divisions. It is possibly the world's most attractive soccer tournament. Devising optimal schedules is crucial to players, teams, fans, sponsors, and administrators. Fair and balanced schedules are a major issue. We describe the problem formulation and its solution approach. We also report on the practical experience gathered after scheduling the tournament for three years.
