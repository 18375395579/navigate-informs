Title: In the Opponent’s Shoes: Modeling Terrorists?Judgments

Author: Sumitra Sribhashyam, London School of Economics, Houghton Street, London, United Kingdom, S.Sribhashyam@lse.ac.uk

Coauthor(s): Gilberto Montibeller

Year: 2013

Abstract: One way to anticipate terrorists?actions is to consider their judgments when modeling their potential decisions so proper counter-terrorism measures may be adopted. We review and discuss the suitability of assumptions made across such models from a descriptive point of view. We explore the modeling implications by drawing knowledge from fields surrounding terrorism. We present a way of addressing some of these areas: dynamic priorities, utilities and beliefs using MCDA and System Dynamics.
