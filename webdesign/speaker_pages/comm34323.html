Title: Some Solutions for Baseball Manager's Problems
Author: Marina Polyashuk, Associate Professor of Mathematics, Northeastern Illinois University, 5500 N. St. Louis Ave., Chicago, IL, 60625, United States of America, M-Polyashuk@neiu.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: This paper focuses on several decision making situations facing a Major League Baseball (MLB) team manager in his everyday work: selection of the 9-player set for a batting lineup on any given game day, deciding on the batting order, and selecting a substitute player while a game is in progress. In order to offer decision making tools for solving these problems, we will apply multiple criteria methods, graph theory, and statistical analyses.
