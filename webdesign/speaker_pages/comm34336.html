Title: Analysis of Energy Bridge Technology in the Liquefied-natural-gas Industry

Author: Erkut Sonmez, PhD Candidate, Carnegie Mellon University Tepper School of Business, 5837 Hobart st apt 2, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States of America, esonmez@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Nicola Secomandi, Alan Scheller-Wolf, Sunder Kekre

Year: 2008

Abstract: According to forecasts, local natural gas production will be soon unable to meet the demand in industrialized countries; imported liquefied natural gas (LNG) will play an imported role to close this gap. Increasing LNG imports will require additional regasification capacity. Energy-Bridge (EB) is a new offshore LNG regasification technology. In this paper, we analyze and compare conventional onshore, EB and transshipment based EB systems in terms of LNG throughput and capacity investment.
