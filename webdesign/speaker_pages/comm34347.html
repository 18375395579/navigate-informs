Title: Strategies to Improve Urban Freight Systems

Author: Johanna Amaya, Graduate Research Assistant, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, 110 Eighth Street, JEC 4037, Troy, NY, 12180, United States of America, amayaj@rpi.edu

Coauthor(s): Jose Holguin-Veras, Cara Wang, Miguel Jaller, Ivan Sanchez-Diaz, Carlos Gonzalez-Calderon, Jeff Wojtowicz, Michael Browne, Suzann Rhodes, Stacey Hodge

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper discusses National Cooperative Freight Research Program project #38 which provides practitioners with strategies to improve efficiency in urban freight systems. Public sector strategies were identified and classified into eight groups: Infrastructure Management; Parking/Loading Areas Management; Vehicle-Related Strategies; Traffic Management; Pricing, Incentives and Taxation; Logistical Management; Freight Demand/Land Use Management; and Governance. The role of OR will be discussed.
