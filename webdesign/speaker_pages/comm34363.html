Title: Coordination Challenges in Hospital Care
Author: Yan Xiao, University of Maryland Medical School, Baltimore, MD, yxiao@umaryland.edu
Coauthor(s): Peter Hu, Douglas Martz, Richard Dutton
Year: 2008
Abstract: Some of the challenges facing workers in hospitals may be summarized as those in coordination of knowledge, activities, and intentions. Detailed field studies have demonstrated that a number of artifacts, most physical, are used to articulate activities at different sites. These findings suggest both the variety of information needs for coordination. In this presentation, field study results and prototypes will be used to highlight the potential to support coordination in hospital care.
