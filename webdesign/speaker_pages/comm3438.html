Title: Transmission Congestion and Competition on Power Generation Expansion

Author: Pinar Kaymaz, Graduate Student, Auburn University, 207 Dunstan Hall, Auburn, AL, 36849, United States, kaymapi@auburn.edu

Coauthor(s): Jorge Valenzuela, Chan Park

Year: 2006

Abstract: Generation expansion planning involves decisions on location and capacity of new generation, which may lead to add or relieve congestion in transmission lines and to increase or reduce competition in deregulated markets. In this paper, the interaction between competition and transmission congestion on power generation expansion is studied. Results from a 5-bus power network and the IEEE 24-Bus system are presented and discussed.
