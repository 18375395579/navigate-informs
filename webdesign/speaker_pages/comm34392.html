Title: The Impact of Digital Technologies on Government Cultural Policy
Author: Sean Marston, Ph D Student, University of Florida, PO Box 117169, Gainesville, FL, 32611-7150, United States of America, sean.marston@cba.ufl.edu
Coauthor(s): Juan Feng, Hsing Cheng, Gary Koehler
Year: 2008
Abstract: The advances in digital technology create new avenues, such as the internet, for consumers to access foreign entertainment programs bypassing the government protection methods. We build a unified analytical framework to study the impact of digital technology on traditional cultural protection policies, including quota, tariff and subsidy.
