Title: The Roles of Capacity and Competition on Airline Scheduled Time and Delay
Author: Yan Dong, Assistant Professor, University of Maryland, R. H. Smith School of Business, 3425 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 20742-1815, United States of America, yandong@rhsmith.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Kefeng Xu, Robert Windle
Year: 2008
Abstract: From operations management and competition perspectives, this paper examines the relationships among operating environment (capacities and market competitiveness), operating decisions (scheduled time) and customer service (delay) in airline industry. After controlling for factors such as distance, hub, and financial position, our study finds that the observed scheduled times and increasing delays are a result of both competition and capacity constraints, signifying their importance in services.
