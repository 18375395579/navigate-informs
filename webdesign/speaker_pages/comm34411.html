Title: Evaluating Epidemic Control Strategies Using BioWar, a City-scale Multi-agent Simulation

Author: Virginia Bedford, Carnegie Mellon University, School of Computer Science, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213-3891, United States, vbedford@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Bruce Lee, Kathleen Carley

Year: 2007

Abstract: Many strategies have been proposed to prevent and/or control a potential mass infectious disease epidemic/pandemic such as influenza. These include mass vaccination, anti-viral distribution, and social distancing policies such as school and work closures, quarantine, and isolation. Using BioWar, a multi-agent, city-scale model that incorporates social networks, we designed an influenza epidemic simulation and virtual experiments to test the efficacy and impact of these potential strategies.
