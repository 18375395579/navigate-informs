Title: Network Flow in Emergency Response

Author: Jarrett Chapman, North Carolina A&T State University, 1601 East Market Street, Greensboro, NC, 27411, United States, jc016446@ncat.edu

Coauthor(s): Funda Samanlioglu, Lauren Davis

Year: 2007

Abstract: Motivated by the lack of logistics in the wake of hurricane Katrina, this research determines an effective strategy to pre-position and allocate supplies before and after an event. A stochastic linear programming model is developed to minimize the cost associated with satisfying demand, taking into consideration uncertainty associated with demand and damage to the supply caused by the event. The benefits associated with pre-positioning versus non-pre-positioning will be illustrated.
