Title: Manufacturing Sourcing in a Global Supply Chain: A Life Cycle Analysis

Author: Shiliang Cui, Doctoral Student, The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut Street, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, scui@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Morris Cohen

Year: 2013

Abstract: Today, while outsourcing to low-wage countries such as China continues to be a major component of global business strategy for large U.S. manufacturing companies, many have begun to rethink off-shoring of production to foreign locations. Why are firms placing such huge bets on “re-shoring? To provide an answer, we propose to develop a modeling framework that will incorporate the major stages of a product’s life cycle.
