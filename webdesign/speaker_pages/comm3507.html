Title: Peer Influence and Information Quality: The Case of Stock Micro-blogging

Author: Chong Oh, Assistant Professor, Eastern Michigan University, 421 Owen Building, Ypsilanti, MI, 48197, United States of America, coh1@emich.edu

Coauthor(s): Olivia R. Liu Sheng

Year: 2013

Abstract: This study examines peer influence and Information Quality (IQ) in the context of individual investor characteristics in stock microblogging. We discovered that individual characteristics that relate to peer influence is not synonymous with IQ. Individuals with both high reputation and high engaging effort relate positively to peer influence but negatively to IQ. Those who are better at diffusing information via retweets are likely to associate with low IQ.
