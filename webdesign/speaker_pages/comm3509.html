Title: Operations Decisions and the Value of the Firm: Perpetuities in a World Without Bankrupcy Cost

Author: Santiago Kraiselburd, Executive Director/Professor, Zaragoza Logistics Center/Instituto de Empresa, Avda Gomez Laguna 25, 1ra planta, Zaragoza, ZA, 50009, Spain, skraiselburd@zlc.edu.es

Coauthor(s): Alejandro Serrano

Year: 2007

Abstract: Authors within the world of Operations Management have usually focused their efforts on minimizing operational costs or maximizing profits from operations. On the other hand, researchers within the financial world have developed methods to calculate the value of firms. In this paper, we unite both perspectives. Specifically, this paper answers the question of what is the order quantity that maximizes the value of a company for shareholders when the cost of capital is not exogenous to operations.
