Title: Building Flexibility in US Healthcare Delivery through Medical Tourism

Author: Muer Yang, Assistant Professor, University of St. Thomas, 1000 LaSalle Avenue, SCH 435, Minneapolis, MN, 55403, United States of America, yangmuer@stthomas.edu

Coauthor(s): Sameer Kumar

Year: 2012

Abstract: Medical tourism is a large and growing phenomenon. In 2011 the medical tourism market was an estimated $20 billion, up from under $2 billion in 2005. The combination of cheap price and good medical care has caused an increasing number of Americans to choose medical tourism. We develop a mathematical model illustrated through discrete event simulation to show the impact of medical tourism on the cost and effectiveness of US healthcare delivery system.
