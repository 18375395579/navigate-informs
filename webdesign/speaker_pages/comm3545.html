Title: Novelty and Collective Attention

Author: Fang Wu, HP Labs, 1501 Page Mill Rd, Palo Alto, CA, 94304, United States, fang.wu@hp.com

Coauthor(s): Bernardo Huberman

Year: 2007

Abstract: To understand how attention to novel items propagates and eventually fades among large populations, we have analyzed the dynamics of collective attention among one million users of an interactive website ?digg.com - devoted to thousands of novel news stories. The observations can be described by a dynamical model characterized by a single novelty factor which decays with a stretched-exponential law, suggesting the existence of a natural time scale over which attention fades.
