Title: Cloud Computing Vendor Selection for the City of Pittsburgh

Author: Enrique Mu, MBA Program Director, Carlow University, 3333 Fifth Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, ENRIQUEM@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Howard Stern

Year: 2012

Abstract: Like many government agencies, the technology department at the City of Pittsburgh has had to deal with shrinking budgets and increased demands for state-of-the-art technology like cloud computing. The City employed the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) methodology to select the most innovative and cost-effective cloud computing solution provider. Lessons learned and issues with respect to this collaboration and public group decision-making in the context of new technology will be discussed.
