Title: Impact of Incomplete Information on Optimal Dynamic Portfolio

Author: Haleh Valian, PhD candidate, Rutgers University, 96 Frelinghuysen Road, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, valian@rci.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Davood Golmohammadi, Mohsen Jafari

Year: 2008

Abstract: This paper examines the impact of uncertainty about parameters of asset pricing model on the dynamic portfolio decision. The state of this system is estimated from noisy observations by applying filtering theory. Moreover, the sequence of optimal actions of an agent who takes the error of predicted variables into account is compared to an agent who is blind to it. The result shows that uncertainty of parameters usually induces risk averse agents to choose the higher trading volume.
