Title: Characterizing Nonlinearities in Interdependent Network Resilience

Author: Christopher Zobel, Virginia Tech, 1007 Pamplin Hall, Blacksburg, VA, 24061, United States of America, czobel@vt.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: The complexity of the interactions between different components of interconnected systems will typically lead to nonlinear recovery profiles following a network disruption. This non-linearity may manifest itself in different ways, however, with different levels of efficiency being represented by different characteristic curves. In this paper, we consider such differences with respect to their ability to reflect interdependent behavior in a network after a disrupting event has occurred.
