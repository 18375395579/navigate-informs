Title: Interactive Decision Tool to Forecast Impacts of Technological Change in the Passenger Vehicle Fleet

Author: David Gerard, Executive Director, Center for the Study & Improvement of Regulation, Engineering & Public Policy, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, dgerard@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Fischbeck

Year: 2006

Abstract: This talk will describe an accessible, flexible, interactive tool that allows stakeholders to examine the environmental/health implications of vehicle technologies using: dynamic models that estimate emissions of criteria pollutants; a reduced-form model to estimate ambient air quality in US metropolitan areas; and an assessment of health impacts.
