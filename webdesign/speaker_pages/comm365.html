Title: Statistical Decision Making for Optimal Budget Allocation in Crowdsourcing

Author: Xi Chen, PhD Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, xichen@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Qihang Lin, Dengyong Zhou

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this talk, we discuss an important statistical decision problem in crowdsourcing: the optimal budget allocation for labeling task. Using tools from Bayesian statistical decision theory, we formulate the allocation problem as a Bayesian Markov Decision Process (MDP). To address the computational challenge, we propose a new approximate policy, called optimistic knowledge gradient policy, which is a consistent policy and leads to superior empirical performance.
