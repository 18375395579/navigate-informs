Title: Online Care vs. Traditional Care: Substitution or Complementary Effects?

Author: Changmi Jung, PhD candidate, Carnegie Mellon University, 4800 Forbes Ave., Room 3009, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, changmi@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Rema Padman, Ateev Mehrotra

Year: 2013

Abstract: Advances in information technology are enabling a transformation from traditional face-to-face encounters between clinicians and patients to online medical consultations, and empowering patients to self-manage their health by accessing their clinical information via portal sites. In this study, we examine empirical evidence of substitution vs. complementary effects, and preliminary results support the substitution effect of eVisit and complement effect of portal usage on office visit frequency.
