Title: A Micro-simulation Model of U.S. Mental Health and Juvenile Justice System

Author: Michael Foster, Professor of Maternal and Child Health of Health Policy and Administration of Biostatistics (adjunct), University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Rosenau Hall, Campus Box# 7445, School of Public Health,UNC-CH, Chapel Hill, NC, 27599-7445, United States of America, emfoster@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Miao (Joyce) Jiang, Nilay Argon

Year: 2008

Abstract: Juvenile crimes have serious consequences to the individuals, families, and society as a whole. Much of the juvenile crime is drug-related and the need for drug treatment is high in juvenile justice system. This paper applies simulation tools to model the passage of young people through juvenile justice system, mental health system, and the community, and evaluates various impact for different policy scenarios of shifting resources between mental health treatment and juvenile detention.
