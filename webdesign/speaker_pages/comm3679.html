Title: Personalized Pricing to Mitigate Showrooming

Author: Shivaram Subramanian, IBM Research, 1101 Kitchawan Road, TJ Watson Research Center, Yorktown Heights, United States of America, subshiva@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Pavithra Harsha, Markus Ettl

Year: 2013

Abstract: Showrooming is the practice of browsing products at a store and then buying them online, possibly at another retailer. The current practice of price-matching employed by retailers has yielded mixed results. Retailers therefore need to better engage with customers while they are in the store. We propose a real-time, mobile-enabled, personalized coupon optimization method to increase profitability and minimize lost sales. We provide illustrative computational examples and discuss the results.
