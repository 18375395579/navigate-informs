Title: Going Out of Business: Applying Management Science to Retail Chain Liquidation

Author: Nathan Craig, Doctoral Candidate, Harvard Business School, Soldiers Field Rd, Boston, MA, 02163, United States of America, ncraig@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Ananth Raman

Year: 2012

Abstract: Retail chain liquidation is the time-constrained sale of inventory located at multiple retail locations. The retail industry depends on chain liquidation to allow investors to recover funds from failed ventures and to enable managers of going concerns to divest stores in efforts to improve performance. This paper introduces techniques for increasing the efficiency of retail chain liquidation. Further, this paper demonstrates the performance of these techniques across multiple liquidations.
