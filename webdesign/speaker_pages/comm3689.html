Title: Incentive-compatible Resource Allocation in Wireless Networks and Two-commodity Markets

Author: Tara Javidi, UCSD, 9500 Gilman Dr., La Jolla, CA, 92103, United States, tjavidi@ucsd.edu

Coauthor(s): Jennifer Price

Year: 2007

Abstract: Many resource allocation schemes are known to suffer from incentive compatibility issues when users hold private information and the interests of users are not aligned with the interests of the system. We focus on an alternate approach to pricing that exploits natural incidences of two-commodity markets in realistic wireless networks - for example, leveraging users?demand for downlink bandwidth to regulate their use of uplink bandwidth.
