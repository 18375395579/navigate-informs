Title: 10 Habits of a Successful Assistant Professor

Author: Esra Buyuktahtakin, Assistant Professor, Wichita State University, 1845 Fairmount St, Wichita, 67260, United States of America, Esra.Buyuktahtakin@wichita.edu

Coauthor(s): Wedad Elmaghraby, Ronald Askin, Jane Ammons, J. Cole Smith

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this discussion, panelists from engineering and business departments will share several good and bad habits that can affect the success of a tenure track assistant professor. Panelists have experiences in winning many prestigious awards, getting federal and industrial grants, and publishing in reputable journals. Not only they are successful in their career but also they have mentored many academicians and helped them become successful in their academic careers. They will also share interesting stories based on their experiences, which would teach important lessons to junior faculty.
