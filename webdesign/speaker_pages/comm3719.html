Title: Active Equity Managers in the U.S.: Do the Best Follow Momentum Strategies?

Author: Woo Chang Kim, Ph.D Candidate, ORFE, Princeton University, E312, ORFE, E-Quad, Princeton University, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States, wookim@princeton.edu

Coauthor(s): John M. Mulvey

Year: 2007

Abstract: Empirical evidence shows that momentum strategies have outperformed passive equity benchmarks, especially since publication of the momentum effects. This paper discusses the role of momentum in the performance of active equity managers. We show that the performance of the best managers in the growth and core domains are highly correlated with momentum strategies at the industry level, whereas the worst performing managers has low correlation with momentum.
