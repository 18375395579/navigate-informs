Title: Lessons Learned: Designing an IT Artifact While Creating a Health Experience
Author: Fay Cobb Payton, Associate Professor, North Carolina State University, COM, Box 7229, Raleigh, NC, 27695, United States of America, fcpayton@ncsu.edu
Coauthor(s): Khalia Braswell, Jeremy Currence, Kamar Galloway
Year: 2012
Abstract: In developing a website for African-American women that advocates prevention and awareness for HIV/AIDS, three things are important to consider: social media integration, tracking the social mushroom, and branding the user experience. As research scholars, we use a participatory design process to pinpoint the specific health needs of the African-American female community.
