Title: Lockheed Martin Space Systems Company Optimizes Infrastructure Project-portfolio Selection
Author: Cigdem Gurgur, Professor, Division of Economics and Business, 1500 ILLINOIS STREET, Golden, CO, 80401, United States of America, cgurgur@mines.edu
Coauthor(s): Tom Morley
Year: 2008
Abstract: Our solution, by multi-attribute utility theory and chance-constrained programming, ensures that high-value projects are selected to maximize the financial performance and that the portfolio does not exceed the available budget and that the carryover of the unspent funds to the next fiscal year does not exceed predetermined limits. We used Microsoft Excel to ensure broad accessibility, transparency, user interaction, improved data collection and asset management, and ease-of-use by managers.
