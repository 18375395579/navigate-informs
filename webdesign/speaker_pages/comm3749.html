Title: Communicating Uncertainty for Public Evacuation Decisions

Author: Eva Regnier, Associate Professor, Naval Postgraduate School, 699 Dyer Road, Monterey, CA, 93940, United States of America, eregnier@nps.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Hurricane forecasts include uncertainty about the size, intensity, path, and timing of the storm, all of which affect preparation and evacuation decisions. This research investigates what is known about human perception and response to uncertainty about the timing of events, as it applies to methods for communicating hurricane forecast uncertainty.
