Title: Evaluation of Targeted Influenza Vaccination Strategies via Population Modeling

Author: John Glasser, Mathematical Epidemiologist, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 1600 Clifton Road, NE, Mail Stop A-34, Atlanta, GA, 30333, United States, jglasser@cdc.gov

Coauthor(s): Denis Taneri, Jen-Hsiang Chuang, Jianhong Wu, Peet Tüll, William Thompson, James Alexander

Year: 2007

Abstract: In realistic age-structured models, we compared vaccinating infants and adults aged 65+ years, the current strategy, with vaccinating schoolchildren, an oft suggested alternative. This alternative reduces mortality among the very young and old more than vaccinating them. Insofar as anti-viral medications also prevent infection or reduce the duration or magnitude of infectiousness, early treatment of ill schoolchildren would be most efficient, mitigating the evolution of resistance.
