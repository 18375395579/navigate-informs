Title: Performance Obstacles in Intensive Care Units
Author: Ayse Pinar Gurses, agurs001@umaryland.edu
Coauthor(s): Pascale Carayon
Year: 2006
Abstract: We investigated the impact of performance obstacles on workload, quality of working life (QWL) and quality of care in ICUs. A cross-sectional, survey study was conducted among 300 nurses from 17 ICUs. Obstacles (e.g., misplaced charts, inadequate information) were significantly related to higher workload, lower QWL and quality of care. High workload increased stress and tension of ICU nurses and had a negative impact on quality of care. Findings have implications for ICU redesign efforts.
