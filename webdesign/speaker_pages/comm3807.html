Title: Job Allocation Methods to Optimize Communication and Cooling Costs in HPC Data Centers

Author: Ayse Coskun, Boston University, 8 Saint Mary's St., ECE Department, Boston, MA, 02215, United States of America, acoskun@bu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jie Meng, Fulya Kaplan

Year: 2013

Abstract: Nearly half of the energy in the computing clusters is devoted to cooling. Existing thermally-aware job allocation policies do not consider the communication among the threads of an application while making decisions. However, performance of multi-threaded high performance computing (HPC) applications is strongly affected by the distances among the communicating nodes. This talk discusses a novel job allocation method to minimize the communication cost while also reducing the cooling energy.
