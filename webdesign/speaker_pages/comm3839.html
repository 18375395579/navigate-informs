Title: Working with Cheats: The Role of Deception in Principal Agent Settings
Author: Sanjiv Erat, University of California at San Diego, serat@ucsd.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: Many principal agent relationships involve information asymmetry of the effort that the agent exerts. While traditional economic theory predicts the sub-optimality of compensations based on unobservable efforts, actual practices, such as the use of billable hours, suggest that compensation mechanisms may be based on agent reported metrics. In this presentation, I shall present evidence of two facets of lying aversion: the agent's aversion to lie, and the principal's aversion to be lied to.
