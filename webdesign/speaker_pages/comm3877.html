Title: Do the Costs of Cooperation Drive the Gale of Creative Destruction? : Commercialization Strategies

Author: Aaron Chatterji, Associate Professor, Duke University, 100 Fuqua Drive, Box 90120, Durham, NC, 27708, United States of America, ronnie@duke.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: We explore commercialization strategy from the perspective of the incumbent firm, and consider how the incumbents?willingness to cooperate with the inventor influences patterns of entrepreneurship and collaboration.Using data from the medical device industry, we empirically demonstrate that when the incumbents?costs of cooperation are exogenously increased, a subset of inventors are more likely to pursue entrepreneurship and less likely to collaboratively develop new technologies.
