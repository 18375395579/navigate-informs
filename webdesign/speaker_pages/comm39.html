Title: The Effect of Near Miss Events on Intertemporal Choice Behavior

Author: Florian Federspiel, PhD Candidate, IE Business School, Maria de Molina 12, Bajo, Madrid, 28006, Spain, ffederspiel.phd2014@student.ie.edu

Coauthor(s): Matthias Seifert

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the impact of near misses (i.e. events that almost lead to disastrous outcomes and are only averted due to chance circumstances) on intertemporal choice behavior. Using an experimental research design motivated by Andreoni and Sprenger (2012), we systematically test how individuals' preference for risk after experiencing such near miss events may lead to violations of the assumptions underlying discounted expected utility (DEU).
