Title: Decision Postponement in Supply Chains: Value of Flexibility and Utility of Waiting

Author: Mirko Kremer, mirko.kremer@bwl.uni-mannheim.de

Coauthor(s): Elena Katok, Stefan Minner, Luk Van Wassenhove

Year: 2007

Abstract: We report on the results of a laboratory experiment designed to answer the following question: How accurate are judgments of the value of decision postponement in a supply chain context? Relative to the normative benchmark commonly assumed in model-based research on this topic, we find that decision makers are consistently willing to pay too much for avoiding the mismatch costs associated with making inventory decisions under uncertainty.
