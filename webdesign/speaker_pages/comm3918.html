Title: The Tradeoff between Investments in Infrastructure and Forecasting in the Face of Hurricane Risk

Author: Seong Dae Kim, Ph.D. Candidate, Texas A&M University, 340D Zachry Engineering Center, 3131 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843, United States of America, sdkim@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Eric Bickel

Year: 2008

Abstract: Given limited funding, one must decide whether to invest in infrastructure to speed evacuation in an emergency, or in forecasting technology to predict more accurately the timing and intensity of the event. We use dynamic programming and Markov processes to quantify the benefits of investing in infrastructure or investing in forecasting technologies. By integrating the emergency management and forecasting systems into a single dynamic model, we are able to find an optimal mix of the investments.
