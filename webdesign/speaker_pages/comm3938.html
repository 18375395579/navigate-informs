Title: Dynamic Matching Markets with Search Friction
Author: Nick Arnosti, Stanford University, Stanford, CA, United States of America, narnosti@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Yashodhan Kanoria, Ramesh Johari
Year: 2013
Abstract: It is often costly for agents in matching markets to determine whether potential partners are interested in forming a match. This creates friction in the marketplace, lowering welfare for all participants. We introduce a dynamic model that allows us to quantitatively study this effect. We demonstrate that simple interventions by the market operator (such as restricting applications or increasing the application cost) can dramatically benefit both sides of the market.
