Title: Controls for Sarbanes-Oxley Compliance for Spreadsheets: What Still Needs to Be Done?

Author: Linda Leon, Associate Professor, Loyola Marymount, University, One LMU Drive, Los Angeles, CA, 90045, lleon@lmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Dolphy Abraham

Year: 2008

Abstract: We present an analysis of the literature on error prevention and detection in spreadsheets as it relates to compliance with Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) regulations. Post-SOX the literature has shifted to the need for a variety of controls. Since end-user developed spreadsheets are relied on throughout an organization, formal techniques to control for quality are required. We discuss organizational controls and end-user roles, including formal software development techniques, needed to comply with SOX.
