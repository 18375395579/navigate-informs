Title: Evaluation of Strategies to Mitigate Contagion Spread using Social Network Characteristics
Author: Mario Ventresca, University of Toronto, 5 King's College Road, Toronto, On, M5S 3G8, Canada, mventresca@gmail.com
Coauthor(s): Dionne Aleman
Year: 2012
Abstract: We study the effects of graph-based mitigation strategies with respect to their ability to contain disease spread. Evaluation is performed on a social network constructed from census data of the Greater Toronto Area of 5 million individuals. One outcome of the analysis highlights the pitfalls of betweenness and centrality measures for maximum mitigation given an unknown initial spreader. Our analysis focuses on pandemic disease, but the approach is applicable to any network contagion.
