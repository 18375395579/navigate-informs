Title: Optimization Models for Improvements to Civil Infrastructure Systems Subject to Natural Hazards

Author: Brian Piper, North Carolina State University, 2500 Stinson Drive, Raleigh, NC, United States of America, bepiper@ncsu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ranji Ranjithan

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present optimization models to improve the resilience of a civil infrastructure system (CIS) subjected to natural hazards. The engineering improvements considered reduce the risk to the CIS by either limiting consequences or reducing their associated probabilities. The uncertainties inherent to future natural hazards are represented by hazard scenarios. The models are applied to realistic data from a coastal community to demonstrate the value of a system-wide approach to the CIS improvements.
