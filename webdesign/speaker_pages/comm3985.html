Title: Role of Operations in Predicting Future Financial Performance: Empirical Study of Airline Industry

Author: Jim Cannon, University of Utah, 1645 E. Campus Center Drive, Salt Lake City, UT, 84112, United States, jim.cannon@business.utah.edu

Coauthor(s): Taylor Randall, Christian Terwiesch

Year: 2006

Abstract: Using 32 quarters of data from the airline industry, we examine the critical role that operations play in predicting future firm financial performance. The theory supporting the predictive relationship between financial metrics and future earnings is predicated on critical assumptions regarding the operational performance motivating the financial metrics. Our findings have implications for both operations and financial management who are concerned with maximizing firm value.
