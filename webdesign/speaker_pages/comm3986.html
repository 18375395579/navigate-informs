Title: Retail Information Processing and Store Design Strategy: Testing a Model of Alignment

Author: Jeff Shockley, PhD Student, Clemson University, 101 Sirrine Hall, Department of Management, Clemson, SC, 29634, United States of America, tshockl@clemson.edu

Coauthor(s): Lawrence Fredendall, Aleda Roth

Year: 2008

Abstract: This study examines the operational alignment of delivery system design strategies with the information requirements of retail service encounters. Using survey data from 175 stores in the Southeastern US, we develop and test a model of design strategy coalignment. We find that higher overall employee and customer satisfaction is strongly associated with the retailer's ability to align job and structural design strategies with the information requirements of store service encounters.
