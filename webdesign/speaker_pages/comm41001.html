Title: Pricing Co-production in Services: Is it Better to Charge or to Barter?

Author: Francesco Moresino, Professor, Haute Ecole de Gestion de Genève, HESSO, 7 Route de Drize, Genève, 1227, Switzerland, francesco.moresino@hesge.ch

Coauthor(s): Emmanuel Fragnière

Year: 2013

Abstract: We propose a model that extends Lovelock’s tripod for the pricing of a service. In addition to the three elements proposed by Lovelock (competition, cost structure, and client’s perceived value), we show that the price of a service should also take into account coproduction. We demonstrate that the co-producer can either be rewarded with cash or paid in nature. In this latter case, the co-producer is paid by a service especially tailored for her and coproduction can be seen as barter.
