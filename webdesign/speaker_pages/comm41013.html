Title: Realignment in the NHL as a Quadratic Assignment Problem
Author: Brian Macdonald, Assistant Professor, Math Department USMA, Thayer Hall, West Point, NY, 10996, United States of America, brian.macdonald@usma.edu
Coauthor(s): William Pulleyblank
Year: 2012
Abstract: The NHL has been planning realignment since the Atlanta Thrashers moved to Winnipeg, becoming the Jets. The Jets remained in the Southeast Division, creating an excessive amount of travel for those teams. We ask the question: Which teams should be in which divisions in order to minimize a weighted sum of the distances between each pair of cities? This optimization problem can be framed as a quadratic assignment problem. We report on ``good league structures under a variety of conditions.
