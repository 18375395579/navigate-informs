Title: Improving the Scalability of Parallel Applications through Genetic Algorithm based Task Reordering

Author: Ramanan Sankaran, Computational Scientist, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, MS 6008, PO Box 2008, Oak Ridge, TN, 37831, United States of America, sankaranr@ornl.gov

Coauthor(s): Mike Brown, Jordan Angel

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the impact of task placement on the parallel performance of a turbulent combustion solver (S3D) and a molecular dynamics code (LAMMPS), on the Titan Cray XK7 supercomputer at the Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility. A new strategy using genetic algorithm that takes into account the parallel application's communication pattern is presented. The technique provides a reordered placement, within the set of nodes allocated by the job scheduler, to minimize the communication overhead.
