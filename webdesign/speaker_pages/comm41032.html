Title: The Optimal Admission Threshold in Observable Queues with State Dependent Pricing
Author: Sherwin Doroudi, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, sdoroudi@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Christian Borgs, Jennifer Chayes, Mor Harchol-Balter, Kuang Xu
Year: 2012
Abstract: We consider a model where delay sensitive customers seek service from a server with an observable queue. It is known that in this setting revenue is maximized by a state dependent pricing policy with a threshold, whereby customers are barred from entry once the queue reaches a certain length. We are the first to find the optimal threshold in closed form. Our closed form uses the Lambert W function and leads to a very simple formula for optimal revenue.
