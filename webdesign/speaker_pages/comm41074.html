Title: Real-time Integrated Airport Surface Controlling Model
Author: Qing Wang, University of South Florida, 4202 E. Fowler Ave., ENC 3300, Tampa, FL, 33620, United States of America, qingwang@mail.usf.edu
Coauthor(s): Yu Zhang
Year: 2013
Abstract: In this study, we propose a real-time integrated airport surface movement controlling model. Given a set of arrival and departure flights in a specified planning horizon, the model determines 4D trajectories of aircraft that minimizes total taxi time and maximize runway throughput subject to operational constraints and safety requirements. The main decision variables include the push-back time at gates, passage time of aircraft in taxiways, and the ordering of landing and takeoff flights.
