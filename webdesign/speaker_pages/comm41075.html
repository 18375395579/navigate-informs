Title: A Maximum Expected Covering Problem for Locating and Dispatching Two Types of MEDEVAC assets

Author: Ben Grannan, Virginia Commonwealth University, 1015 Floyd Ave., Richmond, Va, United States of America, grannanbc@vcu.edu

Coauthor(s): Nathaniel D. Bastian, Laura McLay

Year: 2013

Abstract: Military emergency medical systems save lives by responding to casualty events and evacuating high-risk casualties to a medical treatment facility with air and ground MEDEVAC assets. We propose a novel MIP model to optimally locate air and ground MEDEVAC assets and assign the assets to casualty events. The objective is to maximize the proportion of high-priority casualties responded to within the Golden Hour. Hypercube correction factors remove the assumption of asset independence.
