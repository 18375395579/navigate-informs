Title: Modeling for Equitable and Effective Food Distribution in North Carolina under Capacity Constraints

Author: Irem Sengul, North Carolina State University, Raleigh, NC, United States of America, isengul@ncsu.edu

Coauthor(s): Julie Ivy, Reha Uzsoy

Year: 2013

Abstract: In partnership with the Food Bank of Central & Eastern North Carolina, which distributes food to a 34-county service area, our objective is to determine the equitable and effective distribution of donated food among the people at risk for hunger. This presentation focuses on deterministic network flow models for optimal food distribution and their robust counterparts while considering the effect of distribution capacities. We obtain optimal policies for food distribution and capacity allocation.
