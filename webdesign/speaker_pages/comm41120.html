Title: Balancing Production, Inventory, and Delivery Costs in Paper Manufacturing
Author: Neil Geismar, Texas A&M University, 320 Wehner, College Station, TX, United States of America, NGeismar@mays.tamu.edu
Coauthor(s): Nagesh Murthy
Year: 2012
Abstract: Supply chain scheduling in the paper industry minimizes the sum of production, inventory, and delivery costs. A single plant serves multiple clients. Each customer’s order contains multiple jobs; each job has its own weight and width. The plant combines jobs from different customers into production sets of uniform weight and limited total width. Distribution is via a limited number of capacitated railcars. Inventory is expensive, because of product's bulk.
