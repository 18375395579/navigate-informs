Title: An Algorithm for Multi-level Network-design Models Arising in Evacuation Problems

Author: Yen Tang, Ph.D Candidate, University of Florida, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States of America, yentang@ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Jean-Philippe Richard, J. Cole Smith

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the problem of constructing arcs in a network to minimize the cost needed to evacuate a population in the face of disaster, assuming that nature is an intelligent agent. We consider four variants where the disaster is/is not restricted to fixed locations, or when the number of evacuated people either does or does not depend on the disaster¹s location. We formulate a three-level programming for this problem, along with a finitely convergent algorithm for their solution.
