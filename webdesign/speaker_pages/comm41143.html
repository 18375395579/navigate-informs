Title: An Analysis of Block-time, Delay Propagation and Schedule Optimization Using Stochastic Models of Airline Networks

Author: Mazhar Arikan, University of Kansas, School of Business, Lawrence, KS, United States of America, mazhar@ku.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: This thesis consists of three levels of analysis: 1) an empirical study of the impact of airline flight schedules on flight delays, 2) building stochastic models of airline networks to propose more reliable air-travel infrastructure, and 3) optimizing the allocation of scheduled block-times to flights to reduce flight delays. Our recommended solution in the last part show that a major US airline could reduce its total network delay cost and its total network arrival delay minutes significantly.
