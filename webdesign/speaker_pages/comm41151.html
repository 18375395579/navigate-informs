Title: Relaxed Support Vector Machines and Extensions

Author: Onur Seref, Assistant Professor, Virginia Tech, 1007 Pamplin Hall, Blacksburg, VA, 24061, United States of America, seref@vt.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Relaxed support vector machines (RSVM) are variations of support vector machines, which feature a restricted budget for penalty-free slack, which is usually consumed by samples that would otherwise incur high penalty. RSVM have previously been proposed for binary classification and multiple instance classification. We propose extensions of RSVM to one-class and multi-class cases, and to support vector regression. We compare results on synthetic data and public benchmark datasets.
