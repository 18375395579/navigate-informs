Title: Multi-Objective Network Protocols

Author: Ashish Goel, Stanford University, Terman 311, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States, ashishg@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Sung-Woo Cho

Year: 2006

Abstract: Different variants of the congestion control protocol TCP maximize different utility functions. But could there be a single protocol that is good for all reasonable utility functions? We first present a centralized algorithm that is an O(log n) approximation, simultaneously, for all canonical utility functions. Then we present a TCP-like protocol which achieves the same bounds. Our protocol requires per-flow state at routers; whether this is necessary is an intriguing open problem.
