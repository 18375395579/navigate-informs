Title: Information Relaxation and Dual Formulation of Controlled Markov Diffusions
Author: Enlu Zhou, Assistant Professor, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 201D Transportation Bldg., 104 S. Mathews Ave., Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, enluzhou@illinois.edu
Coauthor(s): Fan Ye
Year: 2013
Abstract: Information relaxation and duality in Markov decision processes (MDPs) have been studied recently. We extend this dual formulation to controlled Markov diffusions: in a similar way we relax the constraint of adapted policy and impose penalty on early access of the future information. We establish the weak and strong duality, and complementary slackness results. We explore the structure of the optimal penalties and expose its connection between MDPs and controlled Markov diffusions.
