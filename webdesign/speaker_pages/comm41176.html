Title: Methods for MPEC Planning Problems in Energy

Author: Steve Gabriel, Assistant Professor, Department of Civil & Environmental Engineering, University of Maryland, 1143 Martin Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, sgabriel@umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Antonio Conejo, Yohan Shim, Raquel Garcia-Bertrand

Year: 2006

Abstract: Certain energy planning can be considered as mathematical programs with equilibrium constraints (MPEC). Consider electric power markets where upper-level variables choose which generators operate in a given time period to satisfy desirable properties (e.g., minimum profit conditions). The lower-level variables relate to the production levels of the active generators, the loads and locational prices of the network. We examine new Benders-like methods for solving these difficult problems.
