Title: The Diseconomies of Pooling in a Discretionary Task Setting: An Investigation of ED Length of Stay

Author: Hummy Song, PhD Student, Harvard Business School, Morgan Hall 480, Soldiers Field Road, Boston, MA, 02163, United States of America, hsong@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Anita Tucker, Karen Murrell

Year: 2013

Abstract: We conduct an empirical investigation on the impact of pooling demand on throughput time in a discretionary work setting. We propose that the benefits of a reduction in social loafing outweigh the expected efficiency gains from pooling, which are commonly predicted by queuing theory. Using an Emergency Department’s patient-level data (N = 233,781) over 4 years, we find that patients?lengths of stay were longer when the ED’s work system had pooled demand as opposed to dedicated demand streams.
