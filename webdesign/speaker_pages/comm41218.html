Title: A Game Theoretic Model for Airline Revenue Management and Competitive Pricing
Author: Karl Isler, Head of RM Strategy and Operations Research, Swiss International Air Lines Ltd, ZRHLX/NYS/ISKA, PO Box, Zuerich-Airport, CH, 8058, Switzerland, karl.isler@swiss.com
Coauthor(s): Henrik Imhof
Year: 2008
Abstract: We consider two airlines with one flight leg each and fenceless fare structures. We simulate the competitive dynamics when both airlines use an elasticity forecast derived from the observation of their own bookings and an exact optimization algorithm disregarding competition. We show that for continuous fares the game theoretic model has a unique, pure strategy, sub game-perfect Nash equilibrium. Both the simulation and the equilibrium show that for larger capacities prices will spiral down.
