Title: Service Systems with Adaptive Servers

Author: Mohammad Delasay, PhD candidate, University of Alberta, PhD program office, School of Business,, University of Alberta, Edmonton, Al, T6G2R6, Canada, delasays@ualberta.ca

Coauthor(s): Armann Ingolfsson, Bora Kolfal

Year: 2013

Abstract: Healthcare servers do not work at a constant speed—they may speed up to clear a queue and slow down when fatigued. We develop a quasi-birth-and-death model of a queueing system in which service rates change with the system load (fraction of busy servers) and overwork (number of users served). We demonstrate that ignoring adaptive server behavior in staffing can result in inconsistencies between planned and realized performance and can lead to unstable, suboptimal, or oscillatory staffing.
