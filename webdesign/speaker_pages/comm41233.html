Title: Altruistic Utility Functions for Joint Decisions
Author: Ralph Keeney, Research Professor, Duke University, 101 Lombard St., #704W, San Francisco, CA, 94111, United States of America, keeney@duke.edu
Coauthor(s): David Bell
Year: 2008
Abstract: Altruistic joint decisions occur when individuals each have a preference to please the others involved. We define primitive utility functions and altruistic utility functions for each individual. Given reasonable assumptions, the group altruistic utility function is additive over the individual’s altruistic utility functions and multiplicative over their primitive utility functions.
