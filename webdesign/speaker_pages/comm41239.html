Title: Theory of Expressiveness in Mechanism Design with Uses in Combinatorial Auctions and Sponsored Search

Author: Tuomas Sandholm, Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, Computer Science Department, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, sandholm+@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Michael Benisch, Norman Sadeh

Year: 2008

Abstract: In many mechanism design settings it has been observed that increasing the expressiveness of the participants increases efficiency in practice. We show this holds generally: we derive a domain-independent upper bound on efficiency, which increases with expressiveness. We present instantiations in combinatorial auctions and sponsored search, with some experiments.
