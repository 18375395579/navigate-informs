Title: Facility Location in Humanitarian Relief

Author: Burcu Balcik, Postdoctoral Fellow, Norhwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, United States of America, burcub@u.washington.edu

Coauthor(s): Benita Beamon

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider facility location and stock pre-positioning decisions for a humanitarian relief chain responding to quick-onset disasters. Our model, which is a variant of the maximal covering location model, integrates facility location and inventory decisions, considers multiple item types, and captures budgetary constraints and capacity restrictions. We present computational results and illustrate how the proposed model works on a realistic problem.
