Title: Effects on Benefit of Split Loads for the Pickup and Delivery Problem
Author: Maciek Nowak, Assistant Professor, Georgia Southern University, P. O. Box 8154, Statesboro, GA, 30460, United States, mnowak@georgiasouthern.edu
Coauthor(s): Chip White, Ozlem Ergun
Year: 2007
Abstract: A decrease in cost has been shown when split loads are allowed for the Pickup and Delivery Problem. We show how the following effect the benefit of split loads: load size, load size variability, origin and destination clustering, and the frequency with which loads share origins or destinations. These effects are shown for both randomly generated problem instances and a real world case.
