Title: Scheduled Maintenance Planning for a Per-seat On-demand Air Transportation Service

Author: Gizem Keysan, Georgia Tech, 765 Ferst Drive, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, gizem@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): George Nemhauser, Martin Savelsbergh

Year: 2007

Abstract: Our study focuses on two aspects of maintenance planning without fixed flight schedules: the long-term capacity decisions for the maintenance facility and the short-term decisions regarding the jets to be maintained. The aim is to use the maintenance cycle of the jets efficiently while providing a smooth workflow into the maintenance facility and ensuring the capacity of the facility is not exceeded. We use a look ahead strategy over a rolling time horizon to determine the jets to be maintained.
