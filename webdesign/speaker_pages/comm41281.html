Title: Ranking and Selection Techniques with Overlapping Variance Estimators

Author: Christopher Healey, Doctoral Student, H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering, Georgia Tech, 765 Ferst Drive, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States, chealey@isye.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): David Goldsman, Seong-Hee Kim

Year: 2007

Abstract: Some ranking and selection (R&S) procedures for steady-state simulation require an estimate of the asymptotic variance parameter of each system. We show that the performance of such procedures depends highly on the quality of the variance estimates that are used. We study the performance of R&S procedures with two new variance estimators - overlapping area and overlapping Cramér-von Mises - which show better long-run performance than other estimators previously studied in R&S problems.
