Title: Dominance Rules for Parallel Machines Scheduling Problems

Author: David Savourey, University of Technology of Compiegne, Centre de Recherches de Royallieu, Compiègne, FR, 60200, France, david.savourey@hds.utc.fr

Coauthor(s): Antoine Jouglet

Year: 2007

Abstract: We present some new dominance rules for parallel machines scheduling problems to minimize total cost, such that (weighted) sum of completion times or total (weighted) tardiness. Let P be a set of sheduled jobs. The idea is to examine under which it will always be more interesting to schedule a job i after P rather than another unscheduled job j. Numerical results comparing two tree-based searches, with or without these dominance rules, will be presented.
