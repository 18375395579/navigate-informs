Title: The Allocation of Indivisible Objects via Rounding

Author: Rakesh Vohra, Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University, Evanston, IL, 60201, United States of America, r-vohra@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Thanh Nguyen

Year: 2013

Abstract: Allocating indivisible objects arises in spectrum licenses, landing slots at airports and assigning students to schools. This paper proposes a technique for making allocations based on rounding a fractional allocation. Under the assumption that no agent wants to consume more than k items, the technique can be interpreted as giving agents lotteries over approximately feasible integral allocations that preserve the ex-ante efficiency and fairness properties of the initial fractional allocation.
