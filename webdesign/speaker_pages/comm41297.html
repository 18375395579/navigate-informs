Title: A Risky Two Stage Nash Game for Modeling Investment

Author: Danny Ralph, University of Cambridge, Judge Business School, Cambridge, United Kingdom, d.ralph@jbs.cam.ac.uk

Coauthor(s): Yves Smeers

Year: 2008

Abstract: We present a Nash game where the 1st stage involves investment in both electricity generation capacity and financial instruments (hedges), with production to occur in an uncertain 2nd stage environment. Electricity generators are risk averse, and seek to minimise their cost (or maximise profit) across both stages. The cost of future production is assessed via risk measures instead of expectations. This is a new game in which (risk neutral) probabilities are endogenous ?found at equilibrium.
