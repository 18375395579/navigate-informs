Title: Effects of Relief Center Design and Routing Strategies on Aid Delivery Performance
Author: Merve Ozen, Research Assistant, University of Wisconsin Madison, 3160 Engineering Centers Building, 1550 Engineering Drive, Madison, WI, 53706, United States of America, mozen@wisc.edu
Coauthor(s): Ananth Krishnamurthy
Year: 2013
Abstract: Disasters are catastrophic events and cause extensive damage and loss of lives. Distributing relief supplies efficiently in the immediate hours is critical. This paper investigates the effects of relief center design, routing strategies and staffing policies on the efficiency of aid delivery. Relief centers are modeled as a network of multi-class finite capacity queues with state dependent routing. Numerical investigations identify the combinations that result in fastest aid delivery to victims.
