Title: Demand Estimation for Point of Care CD4 Diagnostics

Author: Megan O'Brien, Research Director, CSHOR, Clinton Foundation HIV/AIDS OR Initiative, 383 Dorchester Avenue, Suite 400, Boston, MA, United States of America, mobrien@clintonfoundation.org

Coauthor(s): Justin Cohen

Year: 2008

Abstract: Point of care (POC) CD4 count technologies in the development pipeline have the potential to expand and streamline HIV treatment in resource-limited settings. We estimated the likely demand ceiling and created uptake algorithms for different clinical settings to estimate demand at various product price points. We then used resource estimation models to describe the potential impact of POC products on system-wide costs, resource allocation, and throughput for HIV care and treatment programs.
