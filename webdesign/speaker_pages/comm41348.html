Title: A Model for Optimally Transporting Emergency Medical Service Patients to Hospitals

Author: Benjamin Grannan, Virginia Commonwealth University, PO Box 843083, Richmond, VA, 23284, United States of America, grannanbc@mymail.vcu.edu

Coauthor(s): Laura McLay

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present a Markov decision process model for determining how to optimally dispatch ambulances to patients and then transport patients to distinguishable receiving locations (hospitals). Through this model, we investigate how the revelation of information about a patient over time affects the interrelated dispatch and transport decisions. Particular attention is paid on the impact of initial customer classification errors. The optimal policy is compared to two myopic policies.
