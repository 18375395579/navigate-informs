Title: Forecasting Movie Demand Decay Via Functional Models of Prediction Markets

Author: Natasha Foutz, Assistant Professor of Marketing, McIntire School of Commerce, 340 Rouss & Robertson Hall, University of Virginia, Charlottesville, VA, 22904, United States of America, ynf8a@comm.virginia.edu

Coauthor(s): Wolfgang Jank, Gareth James

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose a novel functional data-mining approach applied to early and dynamically evolving trading prices of a prediction market to forecast demand during not only the release week but also the decay rate in subsequent weeks. We illustrate our approach in the context of pre-release forecasting of release-weekend revenues of Hollywood motion pictures
