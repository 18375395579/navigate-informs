Title: Selectee Lane Queueing Design for Passenger Checkpoint Screening

Author: Rajan Batta, Professor, Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering, University at Buffalo (SUNY), 420 Bell Hall, Buffalo, NY, 14260, United States of America, batta@eng.buffalo.edu

Coauthor(s): Xiaofeng Nie, Colin Drury, Li Lin

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose a selectee lane queueing design model to study how to efficiently utilize the selectee lane. We consider how to assign passengers from different risk classes to the selectee lane based on how many passengers are already in the selectee lane. The objective is to maximize the checkpoint screening system's probability of true alarm. A rule-based heuristics is proposed and some computational experiments are performed.
