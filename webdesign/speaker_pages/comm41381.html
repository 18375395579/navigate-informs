Title: Stochastic Programming Based Priority Allocation Rules for the M System
Author: Marty Reiman, Bell Labs, 600 Mountain Ave., Murray Hill, NJ, United States of America, marty@research.bell-labs.com
Coauthor(s): Mustafa Dogru, Qiong Wang
Year: 2008
Abstract: The M system is an assemble-to-order inventory system with two components and three products. Applying a stochastic programming (SP) based approach that we previously developed, we identify four cost parameter regions for the M system, each of which has a distinct priority allocation rule. We consider allocation policies with and without component reservation. Our allocation rule, coupled with base-stock levels obtained from the SP, performs well in numerical experiments.
