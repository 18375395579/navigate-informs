Title: Scheduling Operating Rooms with Uncertain Surgery Times
Author: Emre Demirezen, State University of New York at Binghamton, School of Management, Binghamton, NY, United States of America, edemirezen@mays.tamu.edu
Coauthor(s): Subodha Kumar, Michael Pinedo, Chelliah Sriskandarajah
Year: 2013
Abstract: We develop an operating room scheduling model that deals with uncertainty in surgery durations and determines the sequence of surgeries and their start times such that the total cost of earliness, tardiness, and overtime is minimized. We assume that as soon as a surgery finishes, the next surgery can be started. This assumption is relevant in some settings, yet our solution also serves as a bound to the problems in settings where a surgery cannot start earlier than its scheduled start time.
