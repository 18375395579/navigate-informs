Title: Combining Optimization and Metaheuristics to Schedule Major League Baseball Umpires

Author: Michael Trick, Professor, Tepper School, Carnegie Mellon, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, trick@cmu.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: The average Major League Baseball umpire will handle more than 140 games over a 182 day season, traveling more than 30,000 miles. There are a host of conflicting objectives and constraints that makes finding a suitable schedule a difficult process. I will outline ways that optimization and metaheuristic approaches can work together to find playable schedules.
