Title: Impact of Cost Uncertainty on the Location of Facilities in a Supply Chain

Author: Seokjin Kim, Assistant Professor of Management, Millersville University, Department of Business Administration, P.O. Box 1002, Millersville, PA, 17551-0302, United States, skim@millersville.edu

Coauthor(s): Mozart Menezes, Rongbing Huang

Year: 2007

Abstract: We develop a location model for a distribution center on a demand-populated unit line between two suppliers to minimize the production, inbound and outbound transportation costs of one unit of a product. We first assume that two suppliers?production costs are deterministic, and then consider a probabilistic version where the production cost of one supplier follows a distribution. We propose properties of optimal locations and also characterize some distributions under which the properties hold.
