Title: Application of GIS and Optimization to the Distribution of Emergency Medical Supplies in an Urban Area

Author: Harry Bowman, Center for Risk and Economic Analysis of Terrorism Events, 3710 McClintock Avenue, Room 314, University of Southern California, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States, hbowman@usc.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: In the event of a major disease outbreak or bioterror event, local officials must plan for emergency distributions of medicine to the population. Dispensing locations must be selected, and supplies delivered to those locations before dispensing. Geographic analysis and optimization techniques can be applied to several parts of this problem.
