Title: Two-Stage Myopic Dynamics in Network Formation Games
Author: Esteban D. Arcaute, Stanford University, Stanford University, Mail Code 4042, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, arcaute@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Ramesh Johari, Shie Mannor
Year: 2008
Abstract: We consider two-stage myopic dynamics for network formation games (NFG), where utility to a node is a function of the distance to all other nodes. Since our NFG is a generalization of Myerson's announcement game, we use pairwise Nash stability (PNS) as the solution concept. We prove that, although the price of anarchy of the static game is unbounded, our dynamics converge to PNS networks with constant efficiency ratio. For some utility functions, our dynamics converge to efficient networks.
