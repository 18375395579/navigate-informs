Title: What Is a Good External Risk Measure?

Author: Steven Kou, Columbia University, 312 Mudd Building, Dept. of IEOR, 500 West 120th Street, New York, NY, 10027, United States, sk75@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Chris Heyde, Xianhua Peng

Year: 2007

Abstract: Instead of the coherent risk measure, which assumes subadditivity, we propose a data-based external risk measure, called natural risk statistic, that is characterized by a new set of axioms based on subadditivity only for comonotonic random variables, which is consistent with the prospect theory in psychology. The natural risk statistic includes the VaR as a special case and therefore shows that VaR, though simple, is not irrational.
