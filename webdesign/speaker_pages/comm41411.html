Title: Evaluating Risk-Benefit Tradeoffs in Seafood Consumption: A Decision-Analytic Approach

Author: Ketra Schmitt, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, ketra@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Sandra Baird, Mitchell Small, Sara Eggers

Year: 2006

Abstract: Risk-benefit trade-offs inherent in seafood consumption render the standard approach of limiting exposure to a risky substance inappropriate. Evaluating these trade-offs requires that risks and benefits be quantified and incorporated into a multi-endpoint risk-benefit model and integrated into a decision-analytic model to derive optimal fish consumption decisions for different sub-populations.
