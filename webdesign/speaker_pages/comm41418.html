Title: Profit-Maximizing and Socially Optimal Pricing of Advanced Traveler Information Services

Author: Lei Zhang, Assistant Professor, Oregon State University, School of Civil & Construction Eng., 220 Owen Hall, Corvallis, OR, 97331, United States, lei.zhang@oregonstate.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: This paper theorizes users?willingness to pay for advanced traveler information services (ATIS). For each user, information provided by ATIS is valuable if its price is lower than the subjective gain from the information, and lower than the perceived cost of other information-acquisition means. The profit-maximizing and socially optimal prices of ATIS, which correspond to different levels of ATIS market penetration, are analyzed and computed for a real-world transportation system.
