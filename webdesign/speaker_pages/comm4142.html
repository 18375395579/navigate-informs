Title: Setting Inventory Policy via Stochastic Programming with Application to the W Model

Author: Qiong Wang, Member of Technical Staff, Bell Labs, 600 Mountain Avenue, Murray Hill, NJ, United States of America, chiwang@alcatel-lucent.com

Coauthor(s): Mustafa Dogru, Marty Reiman

Year: 2007

Abstract: We solve a stochastic programming model to set base-stock levels for an assemble-to-order system. We show the solution is a lower bound on the total inventory cost of all policies. For the W model, we combine the base-stock setting with a priority backlog clearing policy. We implement our approach by simple search algorithms. Numerical experiments show that within a large subset of parameter space, the outcome is close to the lower bound and converges to it as the lead time increases.
