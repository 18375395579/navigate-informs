Title: A Stochastic Programming Approach to a Multi-or Surgery Allocation Problem

Author: Sakine Batun, Research Assistant, University of Pittsburgh, Department of Industrial Engineering, 1048 Benedum Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States, sab79@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrew Schaefer, Brian Denton

Year: 2007

Abstract: We present a stochastic programming model to solve the multiple operating room (OR) scheduling problem with surgeon constraints, setup times, and uncertain surgery durations. We consider performance measures such as fixed cost of opening ORs, overtime and surgeon idle time. We present computational results based on real data.
