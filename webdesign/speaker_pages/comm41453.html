Title: A Risk-averse Differential Game Approach to Multiagent Routing and Formation with Stochastic Targets

Author: Khanh Pham, Air Force Research Laboratory, Space Vehicles Directorate, 3550 Aberdeen Ave., S.E., Kirtland Air Force Base, NM, 87117, United States of America, Khanh.Pham@kirtland.af.mil

Year: 2012

Abstract: This talk considers a class of distributed stochastic multiagent systems where local interconnections among self-directed agents are determined by the use of graph theory. Associated with self-directed agents are finite-horizon performance measures for conflict-free routing and cohesive target tracking. It is shown that risk-averse agents effectively employ decentralized compensators driven by local noisy observations and timely implement output-feedback Nash policies for distributed decisions.
