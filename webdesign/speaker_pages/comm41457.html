Title: Provably Near-Optimal Approximation Algorithms for Capacity Expansion Problems

Author: Van Anh Truong, Cornell University, 288 Rhodes Hall, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States, vat3@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Robin Roundy

Year: 2006

Abstract: We present the first multi-dimensional balancing algorithms for capacity expansion problems with correlated and non-stationary demands. Our approach is computationally efficient for large numbers of machines and products, and guaranteed to have cost no worse than 2 times optimal. We depart from traditional considerations of clusters, or groups of machines purchased together, by introducing a novel quasi-separable resolution scheme for decomposing the cost to the system by machine types.
