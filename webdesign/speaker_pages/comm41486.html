Title: Fixed Pie or Fixed Variable?
Author: David Budescu, Professor of Psychology, University off Illinois, 603 E. Daniel street, Champaign, IL, 61820, United States of America, dbudescu@uiuc.edu
Coauthor(s): Ali Abbas, Hsiu Ting Yu, Ryan Haggerty
Year: 2008
Abstract: When encoding subjective probabilities of continuous variables judges can specify (a) cumulative probabilities for a set of values, or (b) values that correspond to particular cumulative probabilities. We report results of an experiment comparing these approaches. Judges estimated probability distributions using both methods. A variety of results favor the fixed value method: The judgments are more consistent, valid, accurate and faster. Most judges prefer this method.
