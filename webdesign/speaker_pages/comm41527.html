Title: Routing of Public Transit Vehicles in Urban Emergency Evacuation

Author: Fatemeh Sayyady, Mississippi State University, 350 McCain Engineering Building, Mississippi State, MS, 39762, United States, fs131@msstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Sandra Duni Eksioglu

Year: 2006

Abstract: We developed an off-line dynamic public transit routing plan to pick up and deliver evacuees to the safety. We formulated the problem as a network flow problem. In the planning stage, we generated a number of dissimilar paths between every origin-destination pair based on the current urban infrastructure. Given the impact of the disaster on the current infrastructure and given the public response time, we identified routes for transit vehicles to minimize the total evacuation time.
