Title: Emergency Inventory Pooling
Author: Jordan Tong, Assistant Professor, University of Wisconsin-Madison, 975 University Avenue, Madison, WI, 53706, United States of America, jtong@bus.wisc.edu
Coauthor(s): Jing-Sheng Song, Fang Liu
Year: 2012
Abstract: Organizations contract with vendors to always maintain a minimum level of inventory for disaster preparedness. We consider the problem of how to manage this emergency inventory among multiple vendors. Instead of stockpiling, we show that a type of virtual pooling we call emergency inventory pooling can reduce costs. We identify the optimal policy and derive an algorithm for implementation. Finally, we examine for which firms and products emergency inventory pooling is most valuable.
