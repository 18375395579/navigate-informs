Title: Importance Measures for Social Network Links
Author: Mary Helander, IBM Research, TJ Watson Research Center, 1101 Kitchawan Road, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States, helandm@us.ibm.com
Coauthor(s): Kate Ehrlich, Sarah McAllister
Year: 2006
Abstract: In this paper, we consider the mathematical importance of individual links in a social network. Relative to various network-level metrics, we mathematically define importance measurements for arbitrary links. We consider these measurements relative to the problem of identifying best (or worst) links to remove (or add) to a social network, and we discuss the implications for what we call robust and frail social networks.
