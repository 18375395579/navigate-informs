Title: Increasing Network Reliability by Introducing Warehouses
Author: Christian Klaus, PhD Student, Naval Postgraduate School, 1 University Circle, Monterey, CA, 93943, United States of America, cklaus@nps.edu
Coauthor(s): Nedialko Dimitrov
Year: 2012
Abstract: Humanitarian assistance is often shipped using available space on regularly scheduled transportation routes. We model the selection of warehouse locations to increase shipment reliability as a two-stage stochastic program where we first install warehouses, then the transportation routes are revealed and the shipment is executed if possible. Computing the network's reliability given warehouse locations is #P hard. We discuss some computational methods for finding good warehouse locations.
