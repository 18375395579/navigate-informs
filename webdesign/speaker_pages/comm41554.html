Title: A Note on Queue-size Scaling for Input-queued Switches

Author: Yuan Zhong, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 235 Albany Street, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, zhyu4118@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Devavrat Shah, John N. Tsitsiklis

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider queue-size scaling in an input-queued switch. In the main result of the paper, we provide a new class of scheduling policies under which the long-run average total queue size scales as power 2.5 of the port number n, in a certain limiting regime of interest. This is a substantial improvement upon prior works, where the same quantity scales as power 3 of n. Our result also advances toward a conjecture about optimal queue-size scaling in input-queued switches.
