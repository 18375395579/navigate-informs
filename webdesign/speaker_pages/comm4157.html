Title: A Mixed Integer Programming Model for Locating and Dispatching Ambulances

Author: Sardar Ansari, Virginia Commonwealth University, PO Box 843083, Richmond, VA, 23284, ansaris2@mymail.vcu.edu

Coauthor(s): Maria Mayorga, Laura McLay

Year: 2012

Abstract: In this presentation, we propose a novel mixed integer programming (MIP) model for identifying how to locate and dispatch ambulances through district design. The model maximizes the coverage level, i.e., the fraction of high-priority calls that can be responded to within nine minutes. The model locates ambulances at stations, assigns each call location to open ambulance stations according to a priority list, and balances the workload across the ambulances.
