Title: Using Strategic Idleness to Improve Customer Service Experience in Service Networks

Author: Jianfu Wang, PhD Candidate, University of Toronto, Rotman School of Management, 105 St. George Street, Toronto, ON, M5S3E6, Canada, jianfu.wang08@rotman.utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Dmitry Krass, Opher Baron, Oded Berman

Year: 2012

Abstract: In service networks the perception of waiting depends on how it is distributed among different stations. To better capture customers' perception of service quality managers should consider the probability of long waits in each station. In a queueing network with several stations, a policy that allows idling may reduce the probability of long waits. We demonstrate the advantage of Strategical Idleness by applying Threshold Based Policies in a network with two single-server queues in tandem.
