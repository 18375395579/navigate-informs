Title: Managing Bottlenecks in Port and Overland Transport Networks for Food Aid

Author: Mallory Soldner, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, msoldner@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Jarrod Goentzel, Ozlem Ergun

Year: 2012

Abstract: Based on experience with WFP in East Africa, port bottlenecks can restrict the flow of life saving food aid. We introduce a canonical framework for building acyclic queuing networks to model port and overland transportation networks. Steady state equations and approximations are used to define cost and time bottlenecks. We classify how these bottlenecks shift in the network depending on parameters and decisions. Finally, we consider how adding capacity to the network impacts efficiency.
