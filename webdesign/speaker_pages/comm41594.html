Title: Distributionally Robust Multistage Inventory Models with Moment Constraints

Author: Linwei Xin, PhD Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, lwxin@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): David A. Goldberg, Alexander Shapiro

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study a multistage distributionally robust inventory problem, when only the mean, variance, and support are known for the demand. It is known that if the policy maker is allowed to recompute her policy choice after each stage(i.e. dynamic formulation), a basestock policy is optimal. In contrast, if the policy maker is not allowed to recompute her policy choice(i.e. static formulation), far less is known. We define weak and strong time consistency in this setting and provide novel conclusions.
