Title: Edge Coloring and Decompositions of Weighted Graphs
Author: Mohit Singh, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15232, United States of America, mohits@andrew.cmu.edu
Year: 2008
Abstract: We consider a generalization of the edge coloring problem in bipartite graphs motivated by the study of Clos Networks. Given an edge-weighted bipartite graph G=(V,E), the task is to find a proper weighted coloring of the edges with as few colors as possible. We give an algorithm for the weighted bipartite edge coloring problem which returns a proper weighted coloring using at most 2.25 n colors where n is the total weight incident at any vertex. This is joint work with Uri Feige.
