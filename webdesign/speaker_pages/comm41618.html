Title: Planning the Alternative-fuel Station Infrastructure under Demand Uncertainty

Author: Yu-jiun (June) Tsai, Texas A&M University, College Station, TX, United States of America, yjt2009@neo.tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ismail Capar, Jorge Leon

Year: 2012

Abstract: Flow refueling location model (FRLM), which is used to locate a fixed number of fuel stations on a road network , assumes the traffic flow between origin and destination (O-D) pairs known in advance and the fixed traffic flow is estimated. In this work, we propose a new model to address traffic flow uncertainty in the input data instead of point estimates. We utilize a scenario based approach with the objective of maximizing the traffic flow under the worst case scenario.
