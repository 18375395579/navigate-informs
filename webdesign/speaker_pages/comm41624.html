Title: Online Learning in Multi-tiered Queueing Networks

Author: Stephen Barr, Graduate Student, University of Washington, UW Foster School of Business, Box 353200, Seattle, WA, 98195-3200, United States of America, stevejb@uw.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: In multi-tiered queueing networks, we have traditionally been concerned with long-run average performance. However, a particular customer reveals information about themselves with every decision, and this information, combined with the state of the system, can be used to predict the customers?remaining path and optimally allocate resources to maximize system performance. Utilizing machine learning, I investigate methods to optimize the real-time performance of a multi-tiered queueing network.
