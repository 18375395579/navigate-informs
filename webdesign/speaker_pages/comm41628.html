Title: Reducing Railroad Hazardous Materials Transportation Risk Through Route Rationalization

Author: Athaphon Kawprasert, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 1201 Newmark Lab, MC-250, Room B118, 205 North Mathews Avenue, Urbana, IL, 61820, United States, a_kawprasert@hotmail.com

Coauthor(s): Christopher Barkan

Year: 2007

Abstract: This paper combines a railroad hazardous materials risk analysis with a linear programming model to consider the potential reduction in risk from rationalization of a hazardous materials transportation route structure. An “optimal?route structure can be developed for the origins and destinations for any particular hazardous material. The set of optimized routes may vary depending on the particular objective function such as minimization of mileage, train accidents, or population exposure.
