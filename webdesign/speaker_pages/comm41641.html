Title: Congestion and Emission Mitigation on Signalized Networks
Author: Yuqi Sun, Industrial and Manufacturing system Engineering, the Pennsylvania State University, 240 Leonhard, University Park, United States of America, yus139@psu.edu
Coauthor(s): Terry Friesz, Hongcheng Liu, Tao Yao, Ke Han
Year: 2013
Abstract: We seek an optimal fixed-cycle, dynamic-split signal control strategy on a road network. The goal is to reduce both congestion and vehicle emission, assuming travelers behave selfishly. The on-and-off signal timing is replaced with a time-dependent parameter imposed on each junction. An emission model that incorporates both vehicle acceleration/deceleration and the Gaussian dispersion function is proposed. The problem of optimal signal strategy is solved as a bi-objective MPEC.
