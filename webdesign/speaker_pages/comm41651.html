Title: Stochastic Control of Runway Configurations and Service Rates at JFK Airport

Author: Alexandre Jacquillat, PhD Student, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, E40-234a, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, alexjacq@mit.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper optimizes the tactical utilization of airport capacity in the face of queue stochasticity and operating stochasticity. We minimize congestion costs by jointly controlling runway configurations and arrival and departure service rates, as a function of flight schedules, capacity estimates, observed congestion and operating conditions. Stochastic queue dynamics are combined to a dynamic programming framework. The combination of exact and approximate algorithms enables on-line implementation. The application at JFK suggests potential congestion costs savings of 15% to 20%.
