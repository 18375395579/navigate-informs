Title: Globally Optimized Sphere Packings: Models and Illustrative Results
Author: Frank Kampas, Senior Developer, WAM Systems, 600 W. Germantown Pike, Suite 230, Plymouth Meeting, PA, 19462, United States, fkampas@wamsystems.com
Coauthor(s): Janos D. Pinter
Year: 2007
Abstract: A general sphere packing is to find the tightest possible arrangement of a given collection of spheres in a given container. The container can be e.g. a sphere, or a rectangular box. Tightness can be measured by the volume of the container, or by the pairwise distance between the sphere centres, or by a linear combination of these two objectives. We present a general formulation of such models in Mathematica and solve model instances using our MathOptimizer Professional software.
