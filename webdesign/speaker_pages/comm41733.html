Title: Chance Constrained Optimal Power Flow for Topology Uncertainty

Author: Russell Bent, Los Alamos National Laboratory, Lanl, Mail Stop C933, PO Box 1663, Los Alamos, NM, 87545, United States of America, rbent@lanl.gov

Coauthor(s): Eli Chertkov, Daniel Bienstock, Earl Lawrence, Scott vander Wiel

Year: 2013

Abstract: Optimal power flow (OPF) is used to aid operators of electric power systems to identify the least cost generation to satisfy demand for electric power. OPF relies on deterministic information about the state of a power grid. However, much of this information is derived from state estimation that may provide erroneous data. We use recent advances in quantifying the uncertainty associated with topology state estimation and include that uncertainty into OPF formulations as chance constraints.
