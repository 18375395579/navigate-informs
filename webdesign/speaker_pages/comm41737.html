Title: Mechanism Design for Scheduling with Uncertain Execution Time

Author: Angelina Vidali, Duke University, Durham, NC, United States of America, angvid@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Vincent Conitzer

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the problem where a task must be executed, there are multiple machines/agents that can potentially perform the task, and our objective is to minimize the expected sum of the agents' processing times. Each agent does not know exactly how long it will take him to finish the task; he only knows the distribution from which this time is drawn. We construct a mechanism that makes it an ex-post equilibrium for the agents to both truthfully report their distributions and exert full effort.
