Title: Inventory Control in the Presence of Correlated Demands in Service Parts Supply Chains

Author: Collin Chan, PhD Student, School of Operations Research and Information Engineering, Cornell University, 287 Rhodes Hall, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, csc45@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Peter Jackson, John Muckstadt

Year: 2008

Abstract: Correlated demands for service parts arise in equipment overhaul operations such as jet engine repair. Planning stock levels and allocating inventory properly is of paramount importance to the timely and cost effective completion of these overhauls. We present DP formulations of these planning and allocation problems, and apply ADP methods to solve them. Computational results show the advantage of using a dynamic stocking policy as opposed to myopic first-come-first-serve overhaul strategies.
