Title: Probability Elicitation, Scoring Rules, and Cooperation Among Forecasters
Author: Casey Lichtendahl, Assistant Professor of Business Administration, University of Virginia, Darden School of Business, P.O. Box 6550, Charlottesville, VA, 22906, United States, lichtendahlc@darden.virginia.edu
Coauthor(s): Robert L. Winkler
Year: 2007
Abstract: We develop the notion of a joint scoring rule (a collection of interdependent scoring rules) and show that proper joint scoring rules can be generated on a project-by-project basis when eliciting probabilities from independent forecasters. These rules mimic the cooperative stakes business shares would naturally induce. We discuss the extent to which dependent forecasters deviate from the truth under slightly revised stakes.
