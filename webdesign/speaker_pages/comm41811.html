Title: Factors Enabling Single Engine Taxi Procedures at Airports: Case Studies with Airline Data

Author: Megan Ryerson, Assistant Professor, University of Tennessee, Department of Civil and Environmental En, 113 Perkins Hall, Knoxville, TN, 37996, United States of America, mryerson@utk.edu

Coauthor(s): Dua Abdelqader

Year: 2013

Abstract: While fuel savings can be achieved through single engine aircraft taxi procedures, such procedures are not consistently utilized. We investigate how runway configurations, crossings, and taxi distance factor into the decision to perform a single engine taxi. From the statistical analysis utilizing actual airline fuel consumption data we find enablers of single engine taxi to be a relatively long taxi distance, full information about taxiway congestion, and runways dedicated to departures.
