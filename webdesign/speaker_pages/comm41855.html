Title: The Cost-effectiveness of Supervised Consumption Facilities in Toronto, Canada

Author: Ahmed Bayoumi, Associate Professor, University of Toronto, St. Michael's Hospital, 30 Bond St., Toronto, ON, M5B 1W8, Canada, ahmed.bayoumi@utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Jennifer Jairam, Carol Strike, Greg Zaric, Eva Enns

Year: 2012

Abstract: We evaluated the cost effectiveness of establishing sanctioned facilities where people inject drugs under supervision in Toronto using a dynamic compartmental model accounting for the spread of HIV and Hepatitis C. One facility would cost $31,781/QALY compared to none; 2 facilities would cost $36,993/QALY. At thresholds of $50 and $100,000/QALY, it would be cost-effective to establish 3 and 4 facilities, respectively. Results were sensitive to assumptions population size, costs and efficacy.
