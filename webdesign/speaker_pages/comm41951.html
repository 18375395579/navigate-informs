Title: Darwinian Evolution in Parallel Universes: A Parallel Genetic Algorithm for Variable Selection

Author: Mu Zhu, Associate Professor, University of Waterloo, 200 University Ave W, Waterloo, ON, Canada, m3zhu@uwaterloo.ca

Coauthor(s): Hugh Chipman

Year: 2007

Abstract: In many applications, it is desirable to identify a few important variables that affect a certain outcome of interest. The genetic algorithm (GA) seems a natural tool for solving such a problem. In this talk, I will first demonstrate that the GA is actually not terribly effective for such a purpose. I will then propose a simple “twist.?The idea is to run a number of GAs in parallel without allowing each GA to fully converge, and to consolidate the information from the individual GAs in the end.
