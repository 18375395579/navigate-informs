Title: Purchase of Information in a System with Parallel Queues

Author: Ricky Roet-Green, Tel Aviv University, Israel, 1855 Saint Francis st., Apt.1113, Reston, VA, 20190, United States of America, rgricky@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Refael Hassin

Year: 2008

Abstract: Consider a customer who randomly selects one of two servers and inspects its queue length. The customer can now choose between two options: join this queue, or buy information about the length of the other queue and then join the shorter line. The model has a unique symmetric Nash equilibrium strategy, and it is characterized by cascades. These cascades reflect a behavior in which the actions of other customers serve as signals for the present customer.
