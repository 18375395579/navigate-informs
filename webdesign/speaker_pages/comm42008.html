Title: Scheduling and Routing for a Bus-based Evacuation with Constant Evacuee Arrival Rate
Author: Victor Pereira, PhD Student, Virginia Tech, Dept. of Industrial and Systems Eng., 250 Durham Hall, Blacksburg, VA, 24061-0118, United States of America, vpereira@vt.edu
Coauthor(s): Douglas Bish
Year: 2012
Abstract: In this presentation, a variant of the vehicle routing problem for bus-based evacuation planning is introduced. The problem assumes that evacuees arrive at a constant rate, and seeks a schedule of pick-ups and the routes for a fleet of buses that minimizes their waiting time at various pick-up locations. In addition, the effect of service level over the waiting time is explored and upper and lower bounds on the fleet size and an upper bound on the number of arc traversals per bus are proposed.
