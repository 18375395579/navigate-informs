Title: Electricity Markets for Unpredictable and Uncertain Participants
Author: Geoffrey Pritchard, University of Auckland, Private Bag 92019, Auckland, Au, New Zealand, g.pritchard@auckland.ac.nz
Coauthor(s): Andy Philpott, Golbon Zakeri
Year: 2008
Abstract: We discuss a stochastic-programming-based method for scheduling electric power generation subject to uncertainty. Such uncertainty may arise from either imperfect forecasting or moment-to-moment fluctuations, and on either the supply or the demand side. The method gives a system of locational marginal prices which reflect the uncertainty, and these may be used in a market settlement scheme in which payment is for energy only. We can show that this scheme is revenue-adequate in expectation.
