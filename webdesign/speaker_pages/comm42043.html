Title: Modeling Interactions between Microgrids, Utilities, and Power Markets: A Regulator’s Perspective

Author: Venkat Prava, PhD Student, The Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, MD, United States of America, vprava1@jhu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ben Hobbs, Chiara LoPrete

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study development of microgrids (MG) in two settings: a regulated vertically integrated utility and a distribution utility/degulated bulk power market. Cost and technology scenarios are considered for each. For each of setting and scenario we use cooperative game theory to determine whether entry would take place and be consistent with market efficiency. Market failures could block efficient entry, or incent inefficient entry. Regulatory mechanisms to ensure efficient outcomes are analyzed.
