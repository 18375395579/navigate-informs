Title: Dynamic Cost-Per-Action Mechanisms and Applications to Online Advertising

Author: Hamid Nazerzadeh, Stanford University, Terman Eng. Center #395, Stanford, CA, United States, hamidnz@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Amin Saberi, Rakesh Vohra

Year: 2007

Abstract: We examine the problem of allocating a resource repeatedly over time among a set of agents. The utility that each agent derives from consumption of the item is private information to that agent, and prior to consumption, may be unknown to that agent. We describe a mechanism based on a sampling-based learning algorithm that under suitable assumptions is asymptotically individually rational, asymptotically Bayesian incentive compatible and asymptotically ex-ante efficient.
