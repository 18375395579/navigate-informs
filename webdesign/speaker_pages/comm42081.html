Title: Carrier-carrier Collaborative Networks: A Network Design Formulation
Author: Salvador Hernández, Research Assistant, Purdue University, 550 Stadium Mall Drive, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, shhernan@purdue.edu
Coauthor(s): Srinivas Peeta
Year: 2007
Abstract: A Less-than-truckload (LTL) carrier has demand it cannot fully serve for multiple origins and destinations. It seeks to collaborate with other carriers who may share all, part, or none of its network. The goal of the LTL carrier is to identify the routes that minimize the sum of the collaborative path costs. A mixed integer formulation is presented which precludes load splitting between arcs. Issues are identified, and solutions are presented and discussed on varying network sizes.
