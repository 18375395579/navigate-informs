Title: A Markov Decision Process Model for Ambulance Allocation

Author: Kenneth Chong, Ph.D. Candidate, Cornell University, ORIE, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, kcc66@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Shane Henderson, Mark Lewis

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a queueing model of an Emergency Medical Service (EMS) system that operates two types of ambulances—“advanced?and “basic”— and serves two emergency call priority levels. Ambulances can be assigned to calls of either priority level, but undesirable allocations are penalized. We formulate this problem as a Markov Decision Process (MDP); its value function is, in general, neither concave nor submodular. We characterize, under certain conditions, the structure of the optimal policy.
