Title: Optimization with Uncertainties for the Location and Allocation of Medical Supplies in an Emergency

Author: Huseyin Onur Mete, PhD Student - Research Assistant, University of Washington, 6330 NE Radford Dr 3726, Seattle, WA, 98115, Turkey, mete@u.washington.edu

Coauthor(s): Zelda Zabinsky

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider the location and allocation of medical supplies in an emergency. In the preparedness phase, we determine the warehouses to be active in the city and their inventory levels by a Stochastic Program to provide fast distribution to hazardous areas. In the response phase, we use the Stochastic Program to prepare a Mixed Integer Program to quickly dispatch the supplies and route vehicles with updated information. We include a case study of two earthquake scenarios in the Seattle area.
