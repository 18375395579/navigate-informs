Title: The Benefits of Averaging When Quantile Forecasts Bracket the Truth
Author: Casey Lichtendahl, University of Virginia, Darden School of Business, 100 Darden Blvd., P. O. Box 6550, Charlottesville, VA, 22906, United States of America, lichtendahlc@darden.virginia.edu
Coauthor(s): Robert L. Winkler, Yael Grushka-Cockayne
Year: 2013
Abstract: Bracketing explains why averaging point forecasts performs no worse than the average point forecaster. We extend this notion to probabilistic forecasts. We assume each forecaster reports multiple quantiles and is evaluated with a linear scoring rule. We show that if there exists a u such that at least two of the forecasters' u-quantiles bracket the truth, then averaging quantile forecasts performs better than the average quantile forecaster; otherwise, it performs equally well.
