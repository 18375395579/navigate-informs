Title: Empirical Markov Decision Processes and Applications

Author: William Cooper, University of Minnesota, Department of Mechanical Engineering, 111 Church Street SE, Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States of America, billcoop@me.umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Bharath Rangarajan

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider Markov decision processes with unknown transition probabilities and unknown expected cost functions, and study a method for estimating these quantities from data. The procedure is based on taking expectations with respect to empirical distribution functions of the data. Once the estimates are in place, the method obtains a policy by solving the obtained empirical MDP as if the estimates were correct. We discuss performance guarantees and applications including revenue management.
