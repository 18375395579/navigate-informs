Title: Priority-based Assignment and Routing of a Fleet of Unmanned Combat Aerial Vehicles

Author: Rakesh Nagi, Professor and Chair, University at Buffalo, 438 Bell Hall, Department of Industrial and Systems Eng, Buffalo, NY, 14260, United States of America, nagi@buffalo.edu

Coauthor(s): Vijay Shetty, Moises Sudit

Year: 2007

Abstract: The strategic routing of a fleet of Unmanned Combat Aerial Vehicles (UCAVs) to service a set of predetermined targets from a prior surveillance mission is considered. Targets are characterized by their priority level and min/max service levels, and payload capacities of UCAVs and the range have to be respected. A Tabu Search Heuristic is developed to coordinate two problems: A target assignment problem and a vehicle routing problem, which in turn splits into multiple decision TSPs.
