Title: Stock Return Serial Dependence and Out-Of-Sample Portfolio Performance

Author: Victor DeMiguel, Professor, London Business School, Regent's Park, London, NW1 4SA, United Kingdom, avmiguel@london.edu

Coauthor(s): Raman Uppal, Francisco J. Nogales

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study whether investors can exploit stock return serial dependence to improve portfolio performance. First, we show that a vector autoregressive (VAR) model captures daily stock return serial dependence in a statistically significant manner. Second, we show that both arbitrage (zero-cost) portfolios and investment (positive-cost) portfolios that exploit serial dependence in stock returns outperform the traditional portfolios, even in the presence of transaction costs of up to 10 basis points.
