Title: Constructing Investor Risk Preferences From Data
Author: Vishal Gupta, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Ave, E40-135, Operations Research Center, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, vgupta1@mit.edu
Coauthor(s): Dimitris Bertsimas
Year: 2012
Abstract: Specifying risk preferences is critical to financial applications; yet, preferences are unobservable. Thus, we ask investors to self-describe as “conservative?or “risky.? In this work we take a novel, data-driven perspective. Using ideas from inverse optimization, we construct distortion risk measures that are consistent with an investor's historical holdings. Computational evidence suggests the method is robust to data error and misspecification.
