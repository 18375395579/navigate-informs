Title: Cost of Priority - Is Triaging Preferable in an Emergency Department?

Author: Arvind Sainathan, Ph.D Candidate, University of Rochester, Simon School, Rochester, NY, United States, sainathana2@simon.rochester.edu

Coauthor(s): Gregory Dobson

Year: 2007

Abstract: The ED systems with and without triage are compared where, first with an exogenous budget, waiting costs are minimized and then the total of labor and waiting costs are minimized. We conclude that there are 4 cases favoring triage: intermediate fraction of urgents, reasonably high cost ratio, low triage nurse’s salary and tight budget.
