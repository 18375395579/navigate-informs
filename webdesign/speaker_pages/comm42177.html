Title: Battery Scheduling under Various Objectives

Author: Johann Hurink, University of Twente, P.O. Box 217, Enschede, Netherlands, j.l.hurink@utwente.nl

Coauthor(s): Thijs van der Klauw

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider scheduling an energy storage to match a given energy production profile to a predefined target profile. This problem has applications in smart grids where storage can be used to control the output of e.g. wind or PV. We treat the objectives to minimize the number of charging cycles or the total energy flow through the battery and show the boundary between polynomial solvable and NP-hard problems. We also investigate the case where multiple batteries are used in parallel.
