Title: Best-Reply Mechanisms

Author: Michael Schapira, The Hebrew University, Jerusalem, Israel, mikesch@cs.huji.ac.il

Coauthor(s): Aviv Zohar, Noam Nisan

Year: 2007

Abstract: In many setting strategic agents are instructed to follow the best reply dynamics (economic markets, Internet protocols). This gives rise to many questions: Is best-reply dynamics guaranteed to converge to a stable state? What happens in asynchronous settings in which the communication between agents can be delayed or dropped? Is it in the best interest of the agents to follow best-reply dynamics? We characterize a subclass of games, which contains all dominance-solvable ones. For this subclass we prove that the best-reply dynamics converges within polynomial time to a pure Nash equilibrium. Moreover, we show that convergence is assured even in asynchronous settings in which communication between players may be delayed or lost. We then study best-replies strategies in the context of mechanism design. We characterize a narrower subclass of games for which we prove that best-reply strategies are ex-post-Nash incentive-compatible. We provide several examples: stable-roommate problems, network-routing games, and first-price auctions.
