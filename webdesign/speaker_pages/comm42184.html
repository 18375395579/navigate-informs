Title: Inapproximability of Pure Nash Equilibria in Congestion Games

Author: Alexander Skopalik, PhD Student, RWTH Aachen, Lehrstuhl für Informatik 1, Aachen, Germany, skopalik@cs.rwth-aachen.de

Coauthor(s): Berthold Vöcking

Year: 2007

Abstract: A natural and convincing notion of approximation for equilibria in congestion games assumes that agents are ambivalent between strategies whose delay differs by less than a factor of alpha. We show that computing an alpha-approximate equilibrium is PLS-complete, for any alpha > 1. Thus finding an approximate Nash equilibrium is as hard as finding an exact Nash equilibrium or solving any other problem in PLS.
