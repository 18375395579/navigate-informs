Title: A Strong IP Formulation of the the Ground Delay Problem with Uncertain End Time

Author: Charles Glover, Doctoral Student, University of Maryland, 9307 Spring House Lane, Apartment H, Laurel, MD, 20708, United States of America, cnglover@math.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Michael Ball

Year: 2008

Abstract: A ground delay program (GDP) is implemented for the duration of bad weather at an airport. This schedules the arriving flights according to the airport's reduced capacities. The Ration-by-Distance (RBD) algorithm has been proven to minimize the expected delay of a GDP, when the end time is uncertain. Here, we formulate an Integer Program for the GDP with uncertain end time and show that the RBD algorithm provides an optimal solution to the LP Relaxation of this formulation.
