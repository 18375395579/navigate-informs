Title: The Effect of Local Scheduling in a Load Balancing Game
Author: Adam Wierman, Assistant Professor, California Institute of Technology, 1200 E. California Blvd, Pasadena, CA, 91125, United States of America, adamw@caltech.edu
Coauthor(s): Ho-Lin Chen, Jason Marden
Year: 2008
Abstract: Load balancing is a common distributed architecture. In this paper, we show that the degree of inefficiency in load balancing designs is highly dependent on the scheduling discipline used locally at each of the servers. Traditionally, the local scheduler performs Processor Sharing and the degree of inefficiency is bounded by the number of servers. However, if the local scheduler does Shortest Remaining Processing Time the degree of inefficiency can be independent of the number of servers.
