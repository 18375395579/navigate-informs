Title: Parallel Machine Scheduling to Minimize Overtime and Waste Costs
Author: Nickolas Freeman, PhD student, University of Alabama, Box 870226, Tuscaloosa, AL, United States of America, nkfreeman@cba.ua.edu
Coauthor(s): John Mittenthal, Sharif Melouk
Year: 2012
Abstract: We consider a parallel machine scheduling problem with sequence dependent setup times and costs, where production waste and processing times depend on machine assignments. We use an MIP formulation to minimize overtime and waste costs which captures trade-offs between these cost components. We also develop two heuristic solution approaches and bounds on their solution quality. Experiments show our approach outperforms more traditional scheduling objectives with respect to the costs considered.
