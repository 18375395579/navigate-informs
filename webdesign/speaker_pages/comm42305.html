Title: Distributed Vehicle Routing for Street-level Map Scanning

Author: Dongdong Ge, Stanford University, 37 Angell Ct Apt 201, STANFORD, CA, 94305, United States of America, dongdong@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): John Carlsson

Year: 2008

Abstract: Recently, large-scale instances of the Chinese Postman Problem (CPP) have become of significant practical importance for internet-based map companies in scanning roads to generate street-level views of a location in a map. We describe a two-stage method for distributing CPP among a group of vehicles that balances workloads between vehicles and attempts to minimize the time spent at intersections.
