Title: Comparison of Constant-stress ALT and Step-stress ALT under Time Constraint

Author: David Han, Assistant Professor, University of Texas at San Antonio, One UTSA Circle, San Antonio, TX, 78249, United States of America, david.han@utsa.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: By running an experiment at higher stress levels, accelerated life tests (ALT) quickly yield information on lifetime parameters. In constant-stress ALT, a unit is tested at a fixed stress level while step-stress ALT allows increasing stress levels at pre-fixed time points. In this talk, optimal k-level constant-stress and step stress ALT are compared for exponential distribution under time constraint. The objective is to quantify the advantage of step-stress ALT relative to constant-stress one.
