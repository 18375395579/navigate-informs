Title: A Workload Averaging Principle for Tandem Polling Stations

Author: Otis Jennings, Professor, Duke University, 1 Towerview Dr, Durham, NC, 27705, United States of America, otisj@duke.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Consider a sequence of multiclass, two-station queueing systems in heavy traffic. Jobs visit station 1, a polling station that employs exhaustive service and incurs no setup time, and then station 2, which has an arbitrary non-idling service discipline. We prove that although the sequence of diffusion-scaled, immediate workload processes for station 2 does not converge in general, the sequence does in fact admit an averaging principle.
