Title: The Impact of Hospital Mutual Aid Agreements on Emergency Stockpiling

Author: Po-Ching DeLaurentis, Purdue University, 315 N. Grant St., West Lafayette, IN, United States, chenp@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Dulcy Abraham, Mark Lawley

Year: 2007

Abstract: Our research analytically tests the hypothesis that aggregating hospital inventories through a mutual aid agreement mechanism in a pandemic influenza scenario can lower the probability of short-term stock-out among the participating facilities. Furthermore, this work will focus on finding the optimal stockpile levels and ordering policies of a single critical item in individual hospitals with minimum or minimum acceptable shortage.
