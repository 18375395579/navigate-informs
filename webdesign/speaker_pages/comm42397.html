Title: Maintenance Policies for Multiple Machines with Technician Constraints in a Stochastic Environment

Author: Raha Akhavan-Tabatabaei, Assistant Professor, Universidad de los Andes, Cra 1 Este #19A -40, Bogota, 00, 1111, Colombia, r.akhavan@uniandes.edu.co

Coauthor(s): Juan Sebastian Borrero Angarita

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider the problem of dynamic allocation of technicians to perform preventive maintenance (PM) operations on degrading machines in order to minimize the expected PM, repair, and waiting work in process costs. We assume Markovian processes for the degradation and load, stochastic lengths for the PM and repair operations and that each technician is able to serve only a particular set of machines. Near-optimal policies are obtained using approximate dynamic programming techniques.
