Title: Stocking the Amazon: Inventory Planning in Large-scale Internet Retail

Author: Chris Provan, PhD Student, Cornell University, 206 Rhodes Hall, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States, cap73@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Paat Rusmevichientong, John Muckstadt, Russell Allgor, Marcus Hum, Ping Xu, David Murray

Year: 2007

Abstract: Our work explores the unique inventory problems that arise in the large-scale internet retail environment of Amazon.com. Areas of investigation include inventory allocation in multi-echelon supply chains with capacity constraints, ordering policies and stock-out decisions in a multiple demand class environment, and workload smoothing under seasonal dynamic demands. We present an innovative network design which takes advantage of economies of scale to address these questions.
