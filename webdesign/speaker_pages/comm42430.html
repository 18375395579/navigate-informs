Title: Dynamically Optimizing the Administration of Vaccines from Multi-dose Vials

Author: Maryam Hasanzadeh Mofrad, University of Pittsburgh, 1048 Benedum Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States of America, mah249@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Lisa Maillart, Bryan A. Norman, Jayant Rajgopal

Year: 2012

Abstract: We examine the question of when to open multi-dose vials of vaccines given that partially used vials have a limited shelf life. Under the objective of maximizing the expected number of vaccinations administered between consecutive stock replenishments, we formulate and a Markov decision process model that determines when to cease vaccine administration as a function of time of day, vial inventory and the number of days until replenishment. Structural and numerical results are presented.
