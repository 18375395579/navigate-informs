Title: Internalizing Emission Externality on Road Networks

Author: Yafeng Yin, Assistant Professor, University of Florida, 365 Weil Hall, Box 116580, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States, yafeng@ce.ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Siriphong Lawphongpanich

Year: 2006

Abstract: This paper addresses issues relevant to internalizing traffic emission externality on road networks. We show that the first-best congestion pricing schemes do not necessarily lead to less emissions, and then prove there always exists a nonnegative charging scheme that induces a flow distribution with minimum emissions. We provide a bound on the percent reduction in emissions achievable by any charging scheme, and then offer methods for computing charges that alleviate congestion and emissions.
