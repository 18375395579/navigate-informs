Title: Design of a Reverse Logistics Network for Product Returns

Author: Nizar Zaarour, Northeastern University, 360 Huntington Avenue, Boston, MA, 02115, United States of America, zaarour.n@neu.edu

Coauthor(s): Emanuel Melachrinoudis, Marius Solomon, Hokey Min

Year: 2008

Abstract: Product returns are an unavoidable cost of doing business. Few studies have addressed the problem of determining the number/location of initial collection points and the location/allocation of centralized return centers. Thus, we present a mixed-integer linear programming model for the design of a reverse logistics network for product returns. The objective function considers facilities costs, inventory carrying and handling costs and shipping costs with quantity discounts and distance penalties
