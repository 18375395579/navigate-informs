Title: Spanning Trees with variable degree bounds
Author: Luis Gouveia, Univ Lisbon, Campo Grande, Lisbon, Portugal, legouveia@fc.ul.pt
Coauthor(s): Pedro Moura, Amaro Sousa
Year: 2013
Abstract: We describe a minimum spanning tree problem with variable degree bounds and where the cost of each edge depends on the degree of its end nodes and where edges with a higher cost enable higher degrees. This problem is motivated by the design of wireless networks. We propose three classes of models using different sets of variables and compare from a theoretical perspective as well as from a computational point of view, the models and the corresponding linear programming relaxations.
