Title: New Approach to the Maintenance of Long Range Radars

Author: Jasenka Rakas, Ph.D, University of California at Berkeley, Nextor, 107B McLaughlin Hall, Berkeley, CA, 94720, United States, jrakas@berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Chenyun Wang

Year: 2007

Abstract: Currently, FAA is facing a significant budget deficit that impacts maintenance funding for many of its facilities. In this paper, a reliability-centered maintenance model, based on Markovian transition probabilities and dynamic programming, is introduced to minimize the life cycle maintenance costs for FAA’s Long Range Radars. We also explore the feasibility of privatizing maintenance tasks to companies that can perform maintenance activities.
