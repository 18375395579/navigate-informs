Title: Optimizing Differential Blood Screening Assays to Reduce Transfusion Risk

Author: Ebru Bish, Associate Professor, Virginia Tech, Dept of Industrial and Systems Engg, Blacksburg, VA, 24061-0118, United States of America, ebru@vt.edu

Coauthor(s): Susan Stramer, Douglas Bish, Ryan Xie

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study the problem of selecting screening tests, from a set of FDA-approved tests, to detect infectious agents in donated red blood so as to minimize the probability of an adverse transfusion event. The tests are imperfectly reliable, and are characterized in terms of their specifity and sensitivity. We focus on policies that allow for differential testing, characterize the structure of an optimal solution, propose an effective algorithm, and provide insights based on real data.
