Title: Statistics for Sustainably Selective Welfare Functions

Author: Kobi Abayomi, Asst. Professor, Georgia Tech, Groseclose Bldg, Atlanta, GA, 30030, kaa21@duke.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Chichilnisky’s criteria defines a sustainable welfare function as both purely finitely additive and integrable. Classical welfare optimization yields welfare functions that are singular to infinite time or negligible probability events. I introduce characterizations of statistical estimators for the divergence from a sustainable development path - or utility stream - via a copula similar representation of Kullback-Leibler divergence as a statistical equivalence for the criteria.
