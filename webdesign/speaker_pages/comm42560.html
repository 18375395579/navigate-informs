Title: Selecting Unique Designs in Complex Design Spaces

Author: Abhishek Shrivastava, Ph.D Student, Texas A&M University, Industrial and Systems Engineering, 3131 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843, United States, akshriv@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Yu Ding

Year: 2007

Abstract: Real problems usually have interacting variables giving rise to design points that can not be represented by a simple set of variables. Even when these can be represented with graphs, multiple graphs may represent the same real design point. We apply a canonical labeling based approach to find the unique graphs. In particular, we solve two problems - cataloging good fractional factorial designs, and selecting equipment configuration for a phone quality testing system.
