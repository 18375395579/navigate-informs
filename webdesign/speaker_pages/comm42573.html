Title: Bio-inspired Emergent Behavior: Can It Be Applied to Catastrophic Incident Response?

Author: Marjorie Greene, Senior Program Manager, Science Applications International Corporation, 1710 SAIC Drive, McLean, VA, 22102, United States of America, marjorie.j.greene@saic.com

Year: 2008

Abstract: This presentation suggests an approach to effective communications when local disasters spread to the state and federal levels. Building on research into biologically-inspired emergent behavior of social insects, it illustrates how communications paths during catastrophic incidents can be derived from ant routing algorithms in ant colony optimization.
