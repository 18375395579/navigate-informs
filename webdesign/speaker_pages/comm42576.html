Title: Spatial and Temporal Tradeoffs in Transit Stop Location Problems

Author: Sha Mamun, Graduate Student, University of Conneticut, 261 Glenbrook Road, Unit 2037, Storrs, CT, 06269, United States of America, msm08014@engr.uconn.edu

Coauthor(s): Nicholas Lownes

Year: 2012

Abstract: A transit network design problem is presented to optimize transit service efficiency by adjusting service frequency and transit stop locations. A bi-level mathematical programming model is constructed to allow tradeoffs between the spatial design (stop location) and the temporal operations (frequency setting). The stop locations are optimized by minimizing the cost components in the lower level, while the upper level optimized the transit line frequencies by maximizing network connectivity.
