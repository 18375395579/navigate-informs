Title: Analysis of Changes in Freight Distribution from Major Capacity Expansion Projects
Author: Sanghyeon Ko, Ph.D Student, University of Illinois at Chicago, 842 W. Taylor Street B19, Chicago, IL, 60607, United States of America, sko21@uic.edu
Coauthor(s): Kouros Mohammadian, Behzad Karimi
Year: 2013
Abstract: The U.S. major ports receive 80% of total container freight and redistribute to the states. The Panama Canal and the port of Prince Rupert in Canada will double their capacity from 2015 and 2020 and assuming to change receiving calls at ports from current status. Thus, optimization of the container flow from Asia will be analyzed considering commodity, port capacity, inland modes, and shipping costs. Results found that both expansions affected distribution from Asia to the U.S. major ports.
