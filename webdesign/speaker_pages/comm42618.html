Title: Dynamic Multi-Priority Patient Scheduling with Uncertain Demand
Author: Jonathan Patrick, PhD, University of British Columbia, 1215 Duthie Avenue, Burnaby, BC, V5A 2R5, Canada, jonathan.patrick@sauder.ubc.ca
Coauthor(s): Martin L. Puterman, Maurice Queyranne
Year: 2006
Abstract: We present a method for scheduling multi-priority patients to a diagnostic facility in a public health setting. Rather than maximize revenue, the resource manager seeks to allocate capacity so that waiting time targets are met. Our research involves modeling the scheduling process as an MDP and applying approximate dynamic programming methods. We present the optimal linear approximation for two cost structures as well as the resulting booking policy for achieving the waiting time targets.
