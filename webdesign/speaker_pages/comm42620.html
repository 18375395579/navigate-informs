Title: A Many-server FWLLN for Queueing Networks with Time-varying Parameters and Markovian Routing
Author: Ahmet Korhan Aras, Ph.D. student, North Carolina State University, 2152 Burlington Labs, 2500 Stinson Drive, Raleigh, NC, 27695, United States of America, akaras@ncsu.edu
Coauthor(s): Yunan Liu
Year: 2013
Abstract: A many-server heavy-traffic FWLLN is established for the (G_t/GI/s_t+GI)^m/M_t queueing network allowing customer abandonment, general service times, time-varying arrival rates, time-varying staffing levels, and customer feedbacks (after service completions) in a Markovian fashion. This limit theorem, extending the FWLLN for single queues, provides a rigorous mathematical justification and solid theoretical basis for a previously proposed deterministic network fluid approximation.
