Title: Maximal Accurate Forests From Distance Matrices

Author: Constantinos Daskalakis, Postdoc, Microsoft Corporation, costis@eecs.berkeley.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We present a method for distance-based phylogenetic inference, which is novel in two respects. It is the first method (to our knowledge) to guarantee accuracy when knowledge about the model tree, i.e., bounds on the edge lengths, is not assumed. Second, our algorithm guarantees that, with high probability, no false assertions are made; instead, the algorithm produces a maximal edge-disjoint subforest of the model tree.
