Title: Value of Information for Managing Inventory and Routing with Expediting

Author: Necati Ertekin, Texas A&M University, 320N Wehner 4217 TAMU, College Station, TX, 77843-4217, United States of America, nertekin@mays.tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Michael Ketzenberg, Richard Metters

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a stochastic inventory routing problem where a supplier serves multiple customers using a single vehicle. We study the value of inventory level information in integrating and coordinating inventory management and vehicle routing. We formulate a Markov decision process model and propose a heuristic where the key assumptions are that expediting occurs when demand exceeds capacity, expediting affects future routing decisions, and inventory levels are known prior to decision making.
