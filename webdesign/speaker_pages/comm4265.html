Title: Detecting r-robust 2-clubs in Graphs under Probabilistic Edge Failures

Author: Esmaeel Moradi, Oklahoma state University, 322 Engineering North, Industrial Engineering & Management, Stillwater, OK, 74075, United States of America, esmaeel.moradi@okstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Foad Mahdavi Pajouh, Baski Balasundaram

Year: 2012

Abstract: A subgraph of diameter at most k is called a k-club, a relaxation of cliques for k larger than 1. Extending this definition by requiring at least r distinct paths of length at most k, between all pairs of nodes results in the r-robust k-club model. This talk will present preliminary results on a conditional value-at-risk (CVaR) based model to detect large r-robust 2-clubs in random graphs with probabilistic edge failures.
