Title: Structure of US Air Travel Demand
Author: Dipasis Bhadra, Principal, MITRE/CAASD, 7515 Colshire Avenue, McLean, VA, 22102, United States of America, dbhadra@mitre.org
Year: 2008
Abstract: Has the relationship that drive demand between origin and destination cities in the US scheduled commercial air travel changed since 9/11 and other exogenous shocks? In this presentation, I use a time series model to discern systematic and non-systematic factors underlying the structural relationships integrating demand for air travel to fare, economic and demographic factors.
