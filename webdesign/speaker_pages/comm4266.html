Title: Evaluation and Optimization of a One-Warehouse, N-Retailer Distribution System with Reverse Flows

Author: Gregory DeCroix, Professor, University of Wisconsin–Madison, Wisconsin School of Business, 975 University Avenue, Madison, WI, 53562, United States of America, gdecroix@bus.wisc.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study a distribution system facing stochastic demands and returns. We propose heuristic inventory policies--based on local or centralized control and different cost approximations--and find these are nearly optimal within the classes considered. Centralized control performs better on average, but the advantage is small when the number of retailers is small or large. Also, it is better to receive returns at the retailers (warehouse) when the number of retailers is relatively small (large).
