Title: Large-scale Contiguous Geographic Clustering
Author: Ahmad Jarrah, Assistant Professor, Department of Management & Marketing, College of Business, University of Wyoming, Laramie, WY, 82072, United States of America, jarrah@uwyo.edu
Coauthor(s): Jonathan Bard
Year: 2007
Abstract: The focus of this problem class is that of partitioning a region into geographically contiguous sub-areas while optimizing an objective measure and adhering to capacity constraints. The motivating application is that of large pickup-and-delivery networks involving thousands of loads each day. Modeling paradigms, solution schemes and computational experiences are discussed.
