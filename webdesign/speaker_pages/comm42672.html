Title: Service Disciplines in Location Set Covering Problems with Stochastic Demands and Congestion
Author: Seokjin Kim, Assistant Professor, Department of Business Administration, Millersville University, P.O. Box 1002, Millersville, PA, 17551-0302, United States, skim@millersville.edu
Coauthor(s): Oded Berman, Dmitry Krass
Year: 2006
Abstract: We consider the problem of minimizing the number of servers to be located on a network and achieving the service level at each node at least a required level. Markovian demands originate and infinite capacity queues are positioned at each node. Servers can be stationed at nodes and serve calls within a pre-specified service radius. We investigate the effect of dynamic and static disciplines, and further develop location models for emergency medical services with two call classes.
