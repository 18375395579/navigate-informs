Title: An Empirical Analysis of Real-time Heuristics for the Dynamic Traveling Salesman Problem
Author: Barrett Thomas, Assistant Professor, University of Iowa, W272 Pappajohn Business Bldg, Iowa City, IA, 52242- 199, United States of America, barrett-thomas@uiowa.edu
Coauthor(s): Emanuele Manni, Gianpaolo Ghiani
Year: 2008
Abstract: We compare real-time dynamic insertion heuristics to sample-scenario planning under varying degrees of dynamism. With the objective of maximizing the number of late-requesting customers served, the goal of our research is to show under what conditions the more computationally intensive and more managerially demanding sample-scenario approach offers significant benefits. We also explore the benefits of stochastic information in the development of a priori tour for the insertion approach.
