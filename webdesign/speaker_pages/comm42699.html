Title: Transforming the Army's Rapid Fielding Initiative

Author: Scott Crino, Assistant Professor, Mahan Hall, Bldg 752, West Point, NY, 10996, United States of America, scott.crino@usma.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: The Army’s Rapid Fielding Initiative (RFI) is the process by which new equipment is distributed to soldiers either at home station or in a theater of operations. Equipment is shipped from over 50 suppliers around the United States to a warehouse on the east coast where it is packaged into sets. Sets are shipped to the end user stationed at one of over 40 locations around the world. This paper investigates the use of Lean Methods to significantly improve efficiency of the RFI process.
