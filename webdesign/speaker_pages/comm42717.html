Title: The Gradient Oriented Polar Random Search

Author: Loo Hay Lee, Associate Professor, National University of Singapore, 10 Kent Ridge Crescent, Singapore, Singapore, iseleelh@nus.edu.sg

Coauthor(s): Haobin Li, Ek Peng Chew

Year: 2012

Abstract: Involving gradient information in a random search is believed to have better performance than both gradient-based and metaheuristic local search. However, the main difficulty is to incorporate and control randomness in a direction instead of a point. This paper makes use of generalized polar coordinates and corresponding random distributions, so as to design a brand new Gradient Oriented Polar Random Search (GO-POLARS) that is proved to satisfy the conditions for strong local convergence.
