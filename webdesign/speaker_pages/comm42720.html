Title: Staffing and Routing to Incentivize Servers in Many-server Systems

Author: Ragavendran Gopalakrishnan, University of Colorado-Boulder, Boulder, CO, United States of America, raga@colorado.edu

Coauthor(s): Sherwin Doroudi, Adam Wierman, Amy Ward

Year: 2013

Abstract: Traditionally, research focusing on the design of scheduling and staffing policies for service systems has modeled servers as having fixed (possibly heterogeneous) service rates. However, service systems are often staffed by people. Then, the rate a server chooses to work is impacted by the system’s scheduling and staffing policies. We consider the impact of “strategic servers?on system performance in many-server systems. (There is a companion talk in the M&SOM cluster.)
