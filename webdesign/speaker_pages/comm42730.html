Title: A Link between Sequential Semi-anonymous Nonatomic Games and Their Large but Finite Counterparts

Author: Jian Yang, Associate Professor, Rutgers University, 1 Washingtong Park RM 1084, Newark, NJ, 07102, United States of America, jyang@business.rutgers.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We show that a Markov equilibrium of a sequential semi-anonymous nonatomic game can be adopted by players in corresponding large but finite games to achieve near-equilibrium payoffs. Such an equilibrium is parsimonious in form--oblivious of past history and blind to other players' current status, and is easy to execute. We propose an asymptotic resemblance concept between two sequences of probability measures to achieve this result.
