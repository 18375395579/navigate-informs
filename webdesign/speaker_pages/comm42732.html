Title: Distance-based Supertrees
Author: David Fernández-Baca, Professor, Department of Computer Science, Iowa State University, Ames, IA, 50011, United States of America, fernande@cs.iastate.edu
Coauthor(s): Jianrong Dong, Mukul S. Bansal
Year: 2008
Abstract: A phylogenetic tree represents the evolutionary history of a set of species: leaves correspond to species and internal nodes represent hypothetical ancestors. We study the problem of aggregating a collection of phylogenetic trees for partially overlapping subsets of a set of species into a single supertree that contains all the species. We formulate this question as a median problem by defining various distance measures between trees.
