Title: Order-picking Path Optimization with Multi Picks Per Route using Pallet Stackability in a Warehouse

Author: Ahmed Hassan Aly, Teaching Assistant and PhD Student, Industrial Engineering Department at Clemson University, 813, College Avenue, Apt # 11, Clemson, SC, 29631, United States of America, ahassan@clemson.edu

Coauthor(s): William Ferrell

Year: 2008

Abstract: Order-picking typically accounts for about 65% of warehouse operating costs and traveling compromises 55% of the order-picking time. This research focuses on the real situation where order line items are in pallet quantities, multiple picks are allowed and the pallets can be stacked. A model is developed to determine optimal or near optimal solutions.
