Title: Controlling the Autonomously Reconﬁguring Stochastic Factory

Author: Mark Yim, Associate Professor, University of Pennsylvania, 297 Towne Bldg, 220 S. 33rd Street, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, yim@grasp.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Hod Lipson, Eric Klavin, Daniela Rus

Year: 2008

Abstract: Natural systems create deterministic structures from a variable materials. Robustness and adaptability result from this stochastic nature. We will demonstrate a novel paradigm for robust construction and adaptive reconﬁguration of physical systems, under uncertainty and variable resources. We envision processes with resources and targets subject to stochastic ﬂuctuations. Attributes: autonomy, stochastic behavior, adaptation, distribution of sensing, control, and actuation.
