Title: Inferring Quality from a Queue
Author: Laurens Debo, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, laurdebo@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): Christine Parlour, Uday Rajan
Year: 2008
Abstract: We provide a model in which a queue communicates the quality of a good to consumers. Agents arrive randomly at a market, and observe the queue length and a binary private signal about the good. Service departures from the queue are also random. Agents decide whether to join the queue and obtain the good or to balk. We study the equilibrium arrival rate and system parameters.
