Title: Fast Approximate Choice of Record Length for Large Encrypted Database

Author: David Shallcross, Applied Communication Sciences, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, dshallcross@appcomsci.com

Year: 2012

Abstract: To conceal actual record lengths in encrypted databases, we consider choosing a fixed record length. Actual records are to be split and padded to form multiple records of the chosen length. We want to minimize the total memory consumed. An exact optimum can be easily be found in time quadratic in the maximum record length. We now present an approximate algorithm that runs in less time, and also some results on the sensitivity of this approximation to database updates.
