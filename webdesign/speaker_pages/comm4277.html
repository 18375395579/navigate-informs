Title: Passenger Timetabling/Planning Tools Framework

Author: Carl VanDyke, Partner, Oliver Wyman, One University Square, Suite 100, Princeton, NJ, 08540, United States of America, Carl.VanDyke@oliverwyman.com

Year: 2013

Abstract: Passenger timetabling must take into account many issues including the scheduling of trains, allocation of carrying capacity against customer demand, make-up of the trains given the equipment fleet, assignment of crews, scheduling of recovery trains, allocation of slots over the rail lines, planning of platform occupancy, utilization of yards, consumption of fuel & electricity, etc. A structured framework for the timetabling process will be presented, and existing tools identified.
