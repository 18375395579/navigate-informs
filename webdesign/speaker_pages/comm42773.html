Title: Tradable Emission Credits in Stochastic user Equilibrium with Heterogeneous Users: A Market-based Approach to Achieve Air Quality Standards

Author: H. M. Abdul Aziz, PhD Student, Purdue University, 150 Arnold Drive, Apt15, West Lafayette, IN, 47906, United States of America, haziz@purdue.edu

Coauthor(s): Satish Ukkusuri

Year: 2012

Abstract: This research proposes a market based approach for meeting the air quality standards in transportation networks. The externality due to emission is compensated through emission credits (initially distributed among the system users). A stochastic user equilibrium based model (with heterogeneous users) is developed and the existence of unique equilibrium credit price is proved. Finally, a solution algorithm is provided to implement the credit-based scheme in large transportation networks.
