Title: Risk-aware Scheduling and Real-time Charging for Plug-in Electric Vehicles

Author: Lei Yang, Postdoc Researcher, Arizona State University, Tempe, AZ, 85287, United States of America, lyang55@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Vincent Poor, Junshan Zhang

Year: 2013

Abstract: Risk-aware scheduling and real-time charging for electric vehicle (EV) is studied. The aim is to optimize jointly the EV charging cost and the load mismatch risk between the forecast EV load and the actual load due to EVs' random driving activities. To mitigate the computational complexity resulting from the large number of EVs in the system, an estimation algorithm based on importance sampling is developed, which can reduce the overall charging cost by using only a small number of samples.
