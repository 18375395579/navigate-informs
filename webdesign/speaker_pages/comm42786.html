Title: Mean or Median: An Implication for BIS Trading Book Capital Requirements

Author: Xianhua Peng, Assistant Professor, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, Department of Mathematics, Hong Kong, Kowloon, Hong Kong - PRC, maxhpeng@ust.hk

Coauthor(s): Steven Kou

Year: 2013

Abstract: We propose a new risk measure called median shortfall. We show that (i) the median shortfall captures tail risk in an alternative way to that of the expected shortfall; (ii) the median shortfall is more robust than the expected shortfall with respect to small changes in the data; (iii) the estimation error of the median shortfall is much smaller than that of the expected shortfall. Hence, it is better to use the median shortfall than to use the expected shortfall in setting capital requirements.
