Title: A Multilevel Simulation Method to Portfolio Risk Measurement

Author: Nan Chen, Assistant Professor, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shatin, N.T., Hong Kong, Hong Kong - PRC, nchen@se.cuhk.edu.hk

Coauthor(s): Yanchu Liu

Year: 2012

Abstract: This paper proposes a Monte Carlo method to estimate various nolinear risk measures for financial portfolios. We present a multilevel scheme to resolve the tradeoff between the bias and the variance of the estimator. It shows that the complexity of our method is O(\epsilon^{-2} (log\epsilon)^2) in the cases of Lipschitz risk measures and O(\epsilon^{-7/3}) in the cases of loss probabilities and other non-smooth risk measures, if we require the target MSE to be less than \epsilon^2.
