Title: Optimization of Product Placement in a Retail Environment

Author: Wei Ke, Columbia University, wke10@gsb.columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Garrett van Ryzin

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider the impact of physical locations on the sales of a product assortment. Specifically, we hypothesize that the demand of every retail product has two components--intrinsic and impulse. Products with a dominant impulse demand component tend to be strongly influenced by their locations in the store. We propose some heuristics to solve the NP-hard placement optimization problem and consider the design of an in-store experiment to effectively tease out the placement effect.
