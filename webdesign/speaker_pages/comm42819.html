Title: Randomized Longest-queue-First Scheduling for Large-scale Buffered Systems

Author: Tonghoon Suk, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr NW, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, tonghoons@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Ton Dieker

Year: 2013

Abstract: Motivated by a desire to analyze the tradeoff between performance and complexity of the randomized longest-queue-first scheduling algorithm in parallel-queuing systems with n buffers, we establish mean-field limit theorems as n goes to infinity. The limit theory developed in this presentation leads to a diffusion approximation for queue-length processes, and we show through simulation experiments that the resulting approximation is already quite accurate for moderate values of n.
