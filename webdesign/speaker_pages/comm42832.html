Title: Securing the Containerized Supply Chain: An Economic Analysis of C-TPAT

Author: Nitin Bakshi, Doctoral Student, Univ. of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut Street, Ste 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, nbakshi@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Noah Gans

Year: 2007

Abstract: Companies that comply with the Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism (C-TPAT) take extra security measures with their shipping containers and, in return, enjoy preferential treatment at entry points into the US. We model C-TPAT as a Stackelberg game, characterize the structure of the equilibrium, and provide comparative statics results.
