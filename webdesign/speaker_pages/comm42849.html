Title: Mutual Information Based Matching for Causal Inference on Observational Data

Author: Alexander Nikolaev, Assistant Professor, University at Buffalo (SUNY), 312 Bell Hall, Buffalo, NY, 14260-2050, United States of America, anikolae@buffalo.edu

Coauthor(s): Lei Sun

Year: 2013

Abstract: An optimization-driven approach is presented for making causal inference with observational data. Comparing units non-randomly exposed to treatment against those unexposed, the paper motivates algorithms that work to minimize the mutual information (MI) between the covariate vectors of compared units and the treatment variable. This approach is model-free, flexible and accurate. Optimality conditions are derived by treating non-linearity in the MI function. Computational results are reported.
