Title: Optimizing a Reciprocal Workforce
Author: Kay-Yut Chen, Hewlett-Packard, 3000 Hanover St, Palo Alto, CA, 94304, United States of America, kay-yut.chen@hp.com
Coauthor(s): Haitao Li, Alex Zhang, Pano Santos, Claudia Márquez-Nava
Year: 2013
Abstract: We integrate the “gift exchange?idea, where employees put in extra effort to reciprocate better pay, into a workforce sizing and wage determination problem. The productivities of employees are modeled as increasing nonlinear functions of wages. Numerical experiments reveal that cross training is valuable and a higher variation of productivity functions result in lower costs. The goodness of the solution and required time are sensitive to the size, and the degree of nonlinearity of the system.
