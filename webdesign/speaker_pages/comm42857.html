Title: Optimal Arrival Stream Spacing for Continuous Descent Approach (CDA)

Author: John-Paul Clarke, Associate Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, 270 Ferst Drive NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332-0150, United States of America, johnpaul@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Liling Ren, Senay Solak

Year: 2008

Abstract: We describe an optimization model and solution methodology to determine target spacing values for arriving aircraft in a Continuous Descent Approach (CDA) implementation. Assuming a sequence of metering points during the arrival procedure and given probability distributions of deviation in trajectories, the problem deals with the determination of target spacing values such that the total expected fuel burn cost required to achieve desired spacing is minimized.
