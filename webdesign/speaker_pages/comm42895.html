Title: Prior-Independent Mechanisms for Scheduling

Author: Balasubramanian Sivan, PhD Student, University of Wisconsin-Madison, 1210 W Dayton St., Madison, WI, 53706, United States of America, balu2901@cs.wisc.edu

Coauthor(s): Shuchi Chawla, Jason D. Hartline, David L. Malec

Year: 2013

Abstract: Consider the problem of scheduling on jobs on unrelated selfish machines to minimize the completion time of the last job, i.e., makespan minimization with unrelated selfish machines. We assume that job sizes are stochastic. We design simple truthful mechanisms that under various distributional assumptions provide constant and sublogarithmic approximations to expected makespan. Our mechanisms are prior-independent in that they do not rely on knowledge of the job size distributions.
