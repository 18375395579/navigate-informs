Title: Multiple Part-type Production in Robotic Cells: The Equivalence of Two Real-world Models

Author: Milind Dawande, Associate Professor, University of Texas at Dallas, School of Management, Richardson, TX, 75083, United States, milind@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Chelliah Sriskandarajah, Michael Pinedo

Year: 2007

Abstract: We address the scheduling of operations in a robotic cell producing multiple part-types. The objective is to obtain a schedule that maximizes the throughput. We show the equivalence of two real-world models. The first is a single-gripper cell with a unit-capacity output buffer at each machine. The second is a bufferless dual-gripper cell.
