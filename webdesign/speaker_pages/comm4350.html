Title: Monotonicity of Thresholds and Switching Curves in Queueing Control
Author: Flora Spieksma, Leiden University, Niels Bohrweg 2, Leiden, Netherlands, spieksma@math.leidenuniv.nl
Year: 2013
Abstract: Optimality of threshold policies in arrival control of single server queues is often proved through the successive approximations algorithm for Markov Decision Processes. Interestingly enough, the same algorithm can be used to show monotonicity of the threshold as a function of the iteration step. This allows to obtain bounds for the optimal threshold. Similar phenomena occur in two-dimensional optimal control models where a switching curve, instead of an index policy, is optimal. We discuss various examples.
