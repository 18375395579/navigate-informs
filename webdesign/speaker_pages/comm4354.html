Title: A Divide-and-conquer Analysis of Very Large Asymmetric Vehicle Routing Problems

Author: Dauwe Vercamer, Ghent University, Ghent, 9000, Belgium, Dauwe.Vercamer@ugent.be

Coauthor(s): Birger Raa, Dirk Van den Poel

Year: 2012

Abstract: When working with extremely large datasets in real-life VRPs, solution neighborhoods must be kept simple in order to obtain reasonable process times. By dividing the set into smaller samples, more elaborate neighborhoods can be explored or the total process time can be reduced. However, this also limits the optimization. Our research investigates the impact of using these smaller samples on the solution quality and provides approaches for overcoming the downsides of the division.
