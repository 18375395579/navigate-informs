Title: Finding the Most Important Edges in a Network

Author: Kevin Hutson, Furman University, Greenville, SC, United States of America, kevin.hutson@furman.edu

Coauthor(s): Michael Berry, Tim Chartier, Amy Langville

Year: 2013

Abstract: As teams play during a season a network encoding wins and losses of teams can be constructed. There are several ranking methods that can be used on this win-loss network to produce a ranking of teams. In this talk, we present several methods to determine which edges (games) in the network have the most influence in determining such a ranking order of the teams. We apply these methods to college football to determine the most important, or influential, games in that season.
