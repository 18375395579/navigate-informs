Title: Order Allocation Planning for Dell's US Manufacturing Network

Author: Gareth Williams, MIT Operations Research Center, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States, gpw@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Jeremie Gallien

Year: 2007

Abstract: Dell Computers recently expanded its US desktop manufacturing network to reduce supply chain costs and lead-times. While Dell’s current planning process allocates demand to factories using a static map minimizing outbound costs, it recognizes the opportunity to rely instead on dynamic allocation maps minimizing inbound, outbound, and direct labor costs. We present an optimization model developed with Dell to exploit this opportunity and results showing significant savings potential.
