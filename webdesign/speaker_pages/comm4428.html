Title: The Performance of New Warehouse Aisle Designs with Turnover-based Storage Policies

Author: Russell Meller, University of Arkansas, 4207 Bell Engineering, Department of Industrial Engineering, Fayetteville, AR, 72701, United States of America, rmeller@uark.edu

Coauthor(s): Letitia Pohl, Kevin Gue

Year: 2008

Abstract: Turnover-based storage policies can reduce the average travel time in traditional warehouses. The question we investigate is whether these policies can be effectively applied to the new warehouse aisle designs, flying-V and fishbone. We use a heuristic search to determine the turnover-based layout in a warehouse with the new aisle designs. Our results indicate that the average travel time is reduced by applying turnover-based storage policies in the new warehouse aisle designs.
