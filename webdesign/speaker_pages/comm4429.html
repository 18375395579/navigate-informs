Title: Economic and Emissions Implications of Load-based, Source-based and First-seller Emissions Trading Programs under California AB32

Author: Yihsu Chen, University of California, Merced, 5200 N. Lake Rd., Merced, CA, United States of America, yihsu.chen@ucmerced.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrew Liu, Benjamin F. Hobbs

Year: 2008

Abstract: In response to Assembly Bill 32, California is considering three types of carbon emissions trading programs: load-based, source-based, and first-seller. We formulate a market equilibrium model for each of the approaches, considering transmission limitations and emissions trading, analyze their properties, and show the equivalence of the three approaches. The equivalence results show that load-based trading has no inherent advantage compared to other systems in terms of costs to consumers, contrary to claims elsewhere.
