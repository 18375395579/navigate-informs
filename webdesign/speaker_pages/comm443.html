Title: On Fluid and Diffusion Approximations for Finite Horizon Single-server Queueing Models
Author: Harsha Honnappa, University of Southern California, Electrical Engineering, Los Angeles, CA, 90291, United States of America, honnappa@usc.edu
Coauthor(s): Rahul Jain, Amy Ward
Year: 2013
Abstract: We consider a finite horizon single-server queueing model in which n customer arrival times are sampled from a given distribution F, and service times are i.i.d.. We establish that the diffusion limit for this model, as n becomes large, is a function of a Brownian motion and a Brownian Bridge process, and is specified using the directional derivative of the one-dimensional Skorokhod map. The same diffusion limit arises for a class of conditioned “conventional?single-server queues.
