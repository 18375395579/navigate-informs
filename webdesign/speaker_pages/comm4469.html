Title: Stability of Join-the-Shortest-Queue Networks

Author: Jim Dai, Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Dr., Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States, dai@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): John Hasenbein, Bara Kim

Year: 2007

Abstract: This talk is on stability behavior in a variant of a generalized Jackson queueing network. In our network, some customers use a join-the-shortest-queue policy when entering the network or moving to the next station. We provide examples showing that distributional information other than mean values and tie-breaking probabilities is needed in order to characterize the stability of such systems. A byproduct of our analysis is a new method for using the fluid model of a queueing network to show non-positive recurrence of a process.
