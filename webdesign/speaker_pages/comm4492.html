Title: Estimating Interacting Processes with the Principle of Maximum Causal Entropy

Author: Brian Ziebart, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, bziebart@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Drew Bagnell, Anind Dey

Year: 2012

Abstract: The principle of maximum entropy prescribes joint and conditional probability distributions with optimal worst-case predictive guarantees. This work leverages directed information theory to extend the maximum entropy approach to settings where predicted variable sequences interact with known processes. We demonstrate the approach by applying it to predict people's behavior in decision settings, where the known process corresponds to a known state transition dynamics distribution.
