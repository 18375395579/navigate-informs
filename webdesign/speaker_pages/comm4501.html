Title: Analytical Framework for Assessing Physical Security Systems

Author: Greg Graves, LTC, U.S. Military Academy, Department of Mathematical Sciences, 646 Swift Road, West Point, NY, 10996, United States, gregory.graves@us.army.mil

Year: 2006

Abstract: Security systems seek to prevent or mitigate potentially catastrophic consequences. We present an analytical framework for the assessment of alternative security systems. The problem of separating preferences among alternatives without a unique probability distribution on reward is considered. Through a novel application of linear programming and sensitivity analysis, insights are gained regarding vulnerability and priorities for information acquisition.
