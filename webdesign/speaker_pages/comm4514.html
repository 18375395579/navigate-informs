Title: Dynamic Scheduling of a GI/GI/1+GI Queue with Many Customer Classes

Author: Jeunghyun Kim, University of Southern California, Marshall School of Business, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, jeunghyun.kim.2015@marshall.usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Amy Ward

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider a dynamic scheduling problem for a GI/GI/1+GI queue with many customer classes in which there is a class-dependent abandonment cost. The objective is to minimize average cost by dynamically choosing which class the server should next serve. We formulate and solve an approximating Brownian control problem with non-linear drift that incorporates the entire abandonment distribution of each customer class, and then interpret the solution as a scheduling control in the original problem.
