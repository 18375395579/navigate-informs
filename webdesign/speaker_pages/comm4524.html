Title: Multi-stage Scenario Tree Generation for Reliable Production Planning of Integrated Sites

Author: Bruno A. Calfa, Graduate Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, bacalfa@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ignacio E. Grossmann, Anshul Agarwal, John M. Wassick, Scott J. Bury, Joe J. Czyzyk

Year: 2013

Abstract: This work considers a network of highly interconnected chemical plants that are geographically distributed and that are subjected to uncertainties in demands and plant reliability. We describe NLP and LP approaches to quantify the uncertainty in the historical data to systematically generate scenario trees for multistage stochastic programming. The approaches can be used when the uncertainty consists of independently observed data or stochastic processes.
