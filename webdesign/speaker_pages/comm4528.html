Title: A Cargo Prioritization Model for Inland Waterway Disruptions

Author: Jingjing Tong, University of Arkansas, 4207 Bell Engineering Center, Fayetteville, United States of America, tong@uark.edu

Coauthor(s): Heather Nachtmann

Year: 2012

Abstract: For an ongoing project funded by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security, we are developing a cargo prioritization model that can provide timely knowledge regarding what barges should be prioritized for offloading when the inland waterway is closed or reduced due to a disruption. Our presentation focus is the formulation of a non-linear binary integer cargo prioritization model that considers terminal capacity, barge draft, cargo value, and commodity type.
