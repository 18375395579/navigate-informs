Title: Locating Monitors using Continuous- and Discrete-space Optimization Models

Author: Andrew Romich, University of Florida, P.O. Box 210020, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States of America, aromich222@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): J. Cole Smith, Guanghui Lan

Year: 2012

Abstract: The potential for an adversary to transport illicit material through an area of interest is a prevalent risk. In response to this threat, we examine the problem of placing stationary monitors in a continuous space to minimize an adversary's maximum probability of evasion. We first construct a two-stage MINLP. Then, we provide an algorithm to optimally locate the monitors in a continuous space and a heuristic that achieves a near-optimal bound utilizing a discretized set of monitor locations.
