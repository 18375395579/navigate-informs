Title: Generation of Realistic Networks by Multi-scale Perturbations

Author: Alexander Gutfraind, University of Illinois at Chicago, 1603 W. Taylor Street Rm. 834, MC 923, Chicago, Il, 60612, United States of America, agutfrai@uic.edu

Coauthor(s): Lauren A. Meyers, Ilya Safro

Year: 2013

Abstract: Many computational problems require synthetic networks with realistic properties. I will introduce a novel open source package for network generation termed MUSKETEER. It constructs new networks starting from a template, which is processed through a cycle of multiscale mappings, while receiving large perturbations to its structure. The generated replicas exhibit realistic structural, global and emergent properties, including clustering, assortativity, average distance and cascade size.
