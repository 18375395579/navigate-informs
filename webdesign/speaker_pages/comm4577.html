Title: On Replacements of a Vital Component to Extend System Life using Finite Spares
Author: Gozde Icten, University of Pittsburgh, University of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, PA, United States of America, gozdeicten@yahoo.com
Coauthor(s): Steven Shechter, Lisa Maillart
Year: 2008
Abstract: We examine the problem of scheduling replacements of a vital component using a finite number of identical spare parts in a multi-state, discrete-time Markovian deterioration setting. Whenever the vital component fails, a catastrophic system failure occurs and the problem terminates. So, our objective is to maximize the total expected lifetime of the system. We formulate this problem as a Markov decision process and explore the structural properties of the optimal policy.
