Title: Supply Chain Scheduling under Multi-unit Competition Based on a Game Theory

Author: Lixin Tang, Northeastern University, The Logistics Institute, Shenyang, 110004, China, lixintang@mail.neu.edu.cn

Coauthor(s): Feng Li

Year: 2012

Abstract: This talk studies scheduling model naturally arising in supply chain through multi-unit each of which has own scheduling requirement and objective. We model this problem as a non-cooperative game between these units where each unit is responsible for assigning all jobs belonging to a particular unit to the other unit. The local scheduling of these jobs is defined by a coordination mechanism. We proof that there always exists a Nash equilibrium and adopt the price of anarchy as a measure.
