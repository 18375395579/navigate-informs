Title: Two Simple Variations of Top Trading Cycles

Author: Thayer Morrill, North Carolina State University, Raleigh, NC, United States of America, thayer_morrill@ncsu.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: Top Trading Cycles is widely regarded as the preferred method of assigning students to schools when the designer values efficiency over fairness. However, there is a flaw in Top Trading Cycles: agents are allowed to trade for objects they are guaranteed to receive. Such a trade is not necessary and may cause a distortion in the fairness of the assignment. We introduce two simple variations of Top Trading Cycles in order to mitigate this problem.
