Title: Excursion-based Universal Approximations for the Erlang-A Queue in Steady-state

Author: Itai Gurvich, Northwestern University - Kellogg School of Management, Evanston, IL, United States of America, i-gurvich@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Junfei Huang, Avishai Mandelbaum

Year: 2012

Abstract: We re-visit the Erlang-A queue and propose a universal diffusion approximation (rather than limit) that applies simultaneously to all of the existing many-server heavy-traffic regimes: QED, ED, QD and NDS. The approximation yields accurate estimates for steady-state metrics. In our proofs, we do not use the steady-state distribution of the Erlang-A queue. Rather, we relate the excursions of the underlying Birth-and-Death process to properly defined excursions of the universal diffusion.
