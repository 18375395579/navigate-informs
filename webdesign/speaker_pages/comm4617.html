Title: Optimizing the Hinterland Transportation of Containers for Inbound and Outbound Flows

Author: Stefano Fazi, School of Industrial Engineering & Innovation Sciences, Operations, Planning, Accounting, and Co, TU Eindhoven, Eindhoven, Netherlands, S.Fazi@tue.nl

Coauthor(s): Tom van Woensel, Jan Fransoo

Year: 2013

Abstract: The hinterland container supply chain, from ports to inland terminals, entails the allocation of containers to different means of transport, considering their costs, capacity and speed. We present a model that optimizes the allocation of containers, both in and out the port, in order to guarantee an effective transportation and a good utilization of the fleet.
