Title: Detecting Viruses in Large Contact Networks

Author: John Hasenbein, Graduate Program in ORIE, The University of Texas at Austin, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, jhas@mail.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): David Morton, Jinho Lee

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider the problem of detecting a virus propagating through a contact network. We are interested in large social networks graphs with millions of nodes. The objective is to place a constrained number honeypots at a subset of nodes in order to either minimize the expected time until detection or maximize the probability of detection by a certain time. To obtain realistic problem instances we mined a database containing sampled call data from one of Asia's largest service providers.
