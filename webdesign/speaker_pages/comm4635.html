Title: Decision Theory and Imperfecting Information

Author: Chris Keller, Assistant Professor, East Carolina University, College of Business, 3136 Bate Building, Greenville, NC, 27858, United States of America, kellerc@ecu.edu

Coauthor(s): Scott Dellana

Year: 2012

Abstract: In decision theory, a simple expected value of perfect information is the weighted maximum outcome for each random variable less the maximum expected value of the alternatives. This paper considers varieties of inverted formulations for disinformation, i.e., the maximum expected value of the alternatives less the probabilistically weighted minimums of the outcomes for each random variable. Three topical cases are considered for the valuation and exploitation of disinformation.
