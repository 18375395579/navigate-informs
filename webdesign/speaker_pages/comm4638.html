Title: Aerial Sensor Flight Formation for Ground Target Tracking

Author: Michael Hirsch, Principal Engineer, Raytheon, PO Box 12248, M/S 61, St. Petersburg, FL, 33733, United States of America, Michael_J_Hirsch@raytheon.com

Coauthor(s): Chris Eck, Hector Ortiz-Pena

Year: 2008

Abstract: Fusion from disparate sources, in general, produces a more complete representation than any one sensor could provide. However, some sensor geometries might provide a ‘better?fused representation than others. We consider the following: Given a set of aerial sensors, each tracking the same moving ground target and sharing the kinematic information they sense, determine the flight pattern for the aerial sensors such that the resulting fused track of the ground target has minimal location error.
