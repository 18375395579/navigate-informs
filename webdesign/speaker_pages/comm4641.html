Title: Optimal Scheduling in a Multiserver Stochastic Network

Author: Mark S. Squillante, IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, Mathematical Sciences Department, 1101 Kitchawan Road, RT 134/PO Box 218, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States of America, mss@watson.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Mayank Sharma, Mohsen Bayati

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider an optimal scheduling problem in a stochastic network consisting of 2 customer classes and 2 server classes. This problem has received considerable attention in the context of fluid and diffusion scalings of the network. Our goal is to determine the optimal policy and its properties in the original stochastic network, comparing these resuls with those obtained under fluid or diffusion scalings. We believe our approach can be generally applied to a broad class of scheduling problems.
