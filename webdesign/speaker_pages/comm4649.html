Title: Geometry, Problem Stability, and Complexity in Convex Optimization
Author: Jorge Vera, Professor, Pontificia Universidad Catolica de Chile, Campus San Joaquín, Vicuna Mackenna 4860, Santiago, 02142, Chile, jvera@ing.puc.cl
Coauthor(s): Robert Freund
Year: 2008
Abstract: We consider the problem: given only a separation oracle for a convex set S, compute a point in S. We examine the computational complexity of this problem and its relation to the geometry of S as well as to the stability of S under perturbation/deformation. We show that all of these three interests are inter-related in precise ways, thus showing that “stability? “complexity?and “geometry?are the same for this problem.
