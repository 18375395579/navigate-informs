Title: Defense Against K-best Interdiction Attacks

Author: Cole Smith, Associate Professor, ISE, University of Florida, Weil Hall 303, PO Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States of America, j.cole.smith@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Kevin Wood

Year: 2008

Abstract: While traditional defender-attacker models protect against a worst-case interdiction action, there has been very little consideration regarding defense against a profile of actions that an attacker might make. This talk regards exact multi-level optimization algorithms to quantify trade-offs in defense against a worst-case attack, versus defense against a set of k most-effective interdiction actions, where each of the k actions is assumed to be equally likely.
