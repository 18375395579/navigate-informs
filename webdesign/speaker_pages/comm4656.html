Title: Aircraft Routing Under the Risk of Detection

Author: Michael Zabarankin, Assistant Professor, Stevens Instiute of Technology, Castle Point on Hudson, Hoboken, NJ, 07030, United States, Michael.Zabarankin@stevens.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: The deterministic problem for finding an aircraft’s optimal risk trajectory under the risk of detection by radars or similar sensors has been formulated. The model considers an aircraft’s trajectory in 3D space and represents the aircraft by a symmetrical ellipsoid with the axis of symmetry directing the trajectory. Analytical and discrete optimization approaches for routing an aircraft with variable radar cross-section subject to a constraint on the trajectory length have been developed.
