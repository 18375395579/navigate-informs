Title: Dynamic Activity-Travel Networks: A Framework to Model Transportation Demand-Supply Interactions
Author: Gitakrishnan Ramadurai, Renssalaer Polytechnic Institute, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Troy, NY, 12180, United States, ramadg@rpi.edu
Coauthor(s): Satish Ukkusuri
Year: 2006
Abstract: A new network representation framework, Activity-Travel Networks, is proposed where nodes are activity centers that are joined by travel links. Activities are represented by arcs that originate and terminate in same node. Each individual chooses an activity-travel sequence that maximizes his/her utility. A dynamic equilibrium is reached when all users have identified their ‘best?activity-travel sequence and any unilateral change results in reduction in one’s utility.
