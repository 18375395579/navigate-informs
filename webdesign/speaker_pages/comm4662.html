Title: Decentralized Optimization and Correlation Decay

Author: Theophane Weber, MIT Operations Research Center, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, E40-131, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, theo_w@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): David Gamarnik, John Tsitsiklis

Year: 2007

Abstract: Consider a network of cooperating agents, each of which has to take a one-shot decision. All agents seek to optimize a global function decomposable as a sum of edge functions, where the value of each depends on the decisions at the endpoints. Is it possible for agents who know the network within a distance r to compute a decentralized decision? How is performance affected by the distance r? We look at these questions using a correlation decay technique in an average-case probabilistic setting.
