Title: A MIP Model for Strategic Evacuation Planning with Pedestrian Guidance and Bus Routing

Author: Mojtaba Heydar, Department of Industrial and Manufacturing Engineering, University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, P.O. Box 784, Milwaukee, WI, 53211, United States of America, mheydar@uwm.edu

Coauthor(s): Yue Liu, Matthew Petering

Year: 2013

Abstract: We present a MIP for strategic emergency planning in an urban area where a large number of pedestrians depend on buses for evacuation. The model (1) guides evacuees through a “pedestrian network?of streets and crosswalks to designated pick-up points and (2) routes a fleet of buses on a “vehicular network?from depots to pick-up points in order to transport evacuees to shelter areas. A heuristic method is developed to solve large-scale problem instances in a reasonable amount of time.
