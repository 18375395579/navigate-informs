Title: Methods for Assessing Inventory Pooling Effects in Facility Location

Author: Michael Bucci, Ph.D. student, North Carolina State University, 3300 Drexel Hill Court, Apex, NC, 27539, United States of America, mbucci@nc.rr.com

Coauthor(s): Donald Warsing, Michael Kay, Reha Uzsoy

Year: 2008

Abstract: We investigate several approaches for computing inventory levels that account for pooling effects in facility location models with uncertain demand. We assess the “Square Root Law,?a more general concave cost function, and the explicit inventory cost calculation, comparing overall solution cost/structure, solution time, and computational effort. We then extend our analysis to explore inventory pooling effects in models with inter-customer correlation in demand.
