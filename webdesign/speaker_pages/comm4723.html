Title: Stochastic Speed Optimization for Continuous Descent Arrival Spacing

Author: Marcus Lowther, Research Engineer, Georgia Institute of Technology, 270 Ferst Dr. NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, marcus.lowther@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): John-Paul Clarke, Liling Ren

Year: 2008

Abstract: Successful implementation of continuous descent arrivals requires precise spacing of aircraft at a metering point near the top of descent. We present the details of and computational results for a stochastic program to determine the fleet-wide, fuel burn-optimal speed changes required upstream of the metering point to achieve the desired spacing.
