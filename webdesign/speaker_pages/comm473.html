Title: Dynamic Pricing in a Changing Environment

Author: Omar Besbes, University of Pennsylvania, The Wharton School, 3730 Walnut Street, 551 Jon M Huntsman Hall, Philadelphia, PA, United States, obesbes@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Assaf Zeevi

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider a dynamic pricing problem in an environment where the customers' willingness-to-pay distribution is not known and in addition may change over time. We propose a general class of policies which are characterized by three main modules: surveillance (detection), re-estimation (learning) and re-optimization (pricing). Trade-offs between these modules are analyzed and performance guarantees are derived for the proposed policies.
