Title: Production Scheduling for Fast Pyrolysis Fractionation with Lot-sizing
Author: Qi Li, Iowa State University, 0076 Black Engineering, Ames, IA, 50011, United States of America, qili@iastate.edu
Coauthor(s): Guiping Hu
Year: 2013
Abstract: Fast pyrolysis fractionation is a prominent technology for cellulosic biofuel production. In the process, a series of condensers and ESPs are used to recover bio-oil into fractions. The fractions could go through different upgrading processes. In this study, a production scheduling problem considering the trade-off between proportional production costs, set-up costs, and inventory costs is studied. Mixed integer linear programming model with lot-sizing is formulated and case study is presented.
