Title: The Influence of Supply and Demand Fluctuations on Travel Time Reliability

Author: Lei Zhang, Assistant Professor, Oregon State University, School of Civil & Construction Eng., 220 Owen Hall, Corvallis, OR, 97331, United States, lei.zhang@oregonstate.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Travel demand exhibits temporal variations. Transportation facilities may experience reduced capacity due to (non)recurrent events. This paper analyzes the influence of these supply-demand uncertainties on travel time reliability using the behavioral user equilibrium model. An important practical implication/complication from the findings is that reducing supply or demand uncertainty alone could exacerbate travel time variation and worsen travel reliability.
