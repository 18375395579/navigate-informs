Title: Optimizing Common-Period Exam Scheduling at the U.S. Air Force Academy

Author: Steven Clark, Instructor, 2354 Fairchild Drive, USAF Academy, CO, 80840, Steven.Clark@usafa.af.mil

Coauthor(s): Andrew Armacost

Year: 2006

Abstract: Exams for most core-curriculum courses at the Academy are scheduled during a common exam period. To avoid over-scheduling cadets, the Registrar’s office used a greedy heuristic to de-conflict the academic departments and determine a schedule. We present an improvement to this approach that uses a mixed-integer optimization formulation to minimize schedule conflicts and meet department preferences. The model was successfully incorporated into the Academy’s scheduling system in the Fall of ?4.
