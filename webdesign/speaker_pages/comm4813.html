Title: The Periodic Location Routing Problem with Operational Choice

Author: Luis de la Torre, PhD Candidate, Northwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, C210, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, ledelatorre@u.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Karen Smilowitz, Vera Hemmelmayr

Year: 2013

Abstract: We introduce variants of periodic location routing with several dimensions of operational choice: eligible depots are selected from the customer set; the capacity of the depots is a variable independent of depot location; and the frequency of node visits is a decision variable. We present a mathematical formulation and analyze the challenges introduced by operational choice. We use this analysis to develop solution methods, and present a case study on recycling for food pantries in Chicago.
