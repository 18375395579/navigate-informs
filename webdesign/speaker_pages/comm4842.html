Title: Queue Joining Strategies when Expected Service Time and Value are Unknown

Author: Laurens Debo, University of Chicago, Chicago, IL, United States of America, laurens.debo@chicagobooth.edu

Coauthor(s): Senthil Veeraraghavan

Year: 2012

Abstract: When the expected service time and service value are not perfectly known to a consumer base, but, positively correlated, both posterior value and waiting cost increase in the queue length. We show that this leads to non-monotone queue joining strategies.
