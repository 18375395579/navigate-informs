Title: Airline Schedule Planning in a Dynamic Scheduling Context

Author: Cynthia Barnhart, Professor, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, 1-235, Cambridge, MA, United States, cbarnhart@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Hai Jiang

Year: 2006

Abstract: Airlines are moving toward a dynamic scheduling paradigm where elements of the original schedule are re-optimized in the booking process as final demand situation evolves. We present models to design an original schedule with a greater degree of flexibility that facilitates future re-optimizations. The goal is to improve schedule robustness toward demand variability. Experiments are conducted using data from a major US airline to demonstrate the effectiveness of our models.
