Title: Designing Intervention Scheme for Public-Interest Goods Under Random Supply

Author: Ece Demirci, Ph.D. Student, Bilkent University, Department of Industrial Engineering, Ankara, 06800, Turkey, edemirci@bilkent.edu.tr

Coauthor(s): Nesim Erkip

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the problem of designing an intervention scheme for public-interest goods (health-related products, energy efficient appliances, etc.). We consider a system composed of a newsvendor firm that is subject to random supply and a central authority with fixed budget. The central authority regulates the system by two intervention tools: advertising, and research and development investments. We use bi-level programming for modeling the system and provide some structural properties.
