Title: Optimal Control of a Two-server Queueing System with Breakdowns

Author: Jeffrey Kharoufeh, Associate Professor, University of Pittsburgh, Department of Industrial Engineering, 1048 Benedum Hall, 3700 O'Hara Street, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States of America, jkharouf@pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Erhun Ozkan

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider controlling a two-server Markovian queueing system with heterogeneous servers, at least one of which is unreliable. Our objective is to dynamically route customers at arrival, failure, and repair epochs to minimize the long-run average number of customers in the system. We show the existence of an optimal threshold-type policy that depends on the number in queue and the status of the servers.
