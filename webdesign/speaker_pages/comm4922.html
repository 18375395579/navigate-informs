Title: Optimizing Routes for the Transportation Disadvantaged at Center for Transportation Excellence, NY
Author: Rajan Batta, Professor, Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering, University at Buffalo (SUNY), 420 Bell Hall, Buffalo, NY, 14260, United States of America, batta@eng.buffalo.edu
Coauthor(s): Elif Tokar-Erdermir
Year: 2008
Abstract: We develop routing algorithms for clients in a bussing environment. The clients are transportation disadvantaged, e.g. handicapped, elderly, mentally disabled. There are two route categories, fixed and on-demand. There are special constraints in addition to classical vehicle routing problem with time windows. Developed algorithms are based on cheapest insertion and tabu search heuristics. The algorithms are used for both static(offline) and dynamic(online) planning of the routes.
