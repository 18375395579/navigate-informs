Title: Dynamic Pricing to Control Loss Systems with Quality of Service Targets
Author: Robert Hampshire, Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, PA, United States, hamp@andrew.cmu.edu
Coauthor(s): William Massey, Qiong Wang
Year: 2007
Abstract: A wide range of service systems can be modeled as loss systems: agent staffing for call centers, provisioning bandwidth for private line services, and congestion pricing for parking spaces. A major objective for the manager is to find a pricing policy that maximizes total revenue while meeting the quality of service targets desired by the customers. Our pricing solution is a function of the number of customers in the system and a ``future congestion tax.
