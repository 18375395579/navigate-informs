Title: Comments on the Complexity of Revenue Maximization under Two Movie-screening Policies

Author: Tharanga Rajapakshe, University of Texas at Dallas, 800 West Campbell Road, Richardson, Tx, 75080-3021, United States of America, tharanga@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Milind Dawande, Chelliah Sriskandarajah, Inna Drobouchevitch

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider the problem of the selection and scheduling of movies for a multiplex to maximize the exhibitor's cumulative revenue over a fixed planning horizon. We investigate two popular screening policies: preempt-resume and non-preempt. We show that the problem of optimizing under the non-preempt policy is polynomially solvable while that under the preempt-resume policy is strongly NP-hard. Using a comprehensive test bed of realistic instances, we compare the revenues from these two policies.
