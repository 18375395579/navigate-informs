Title: Asymptotically Optimal Bayesian Sequential Change Detection and Identification Rules

Author: Savas Dayanik, Princeton University, Department of Oper. Res. and Fin. Eng., Olden St, E-Quad, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States of America, sdayanik@princeton.edu

Coauthor(s): Warren Powell, Kazutoshi Yamazaki

Year: 2008

Abstract: We study the problem of sequential detection and identification of a sudden unknown and unobservable change as quickly and accurately as possible. We propose a sequential decision rule that declares and identifies a change as soon as the posterior probability of a change type exceeds some fixed threshold. We show that the rule is asymptotically optimal under Bayesian and fixed-error formulations as the unit sampling cost and misdiagnosis probability go to zero, respectively.
