Title: Achieving Benefit of Variety with Limited Shelf Space

Author: Nan Xia, University of Southern California, Marshall School of Business, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States, Nan.Xia.2008@marshall.usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Yehuda Bassok

Year: 2006

Abstract: Some retail stores have to provide low variety due to limited shelf spaces. When competing with regular retailers who provide a large variety, they may use temporal variety (change the displayed variant over time) and low price to attract customers. Our research investigates the cost and benefit of the temporal variety strategy and shows that under certain conditions, retailers using this strategy might be more profitable than the regular retailers using spatial variety strategies.
