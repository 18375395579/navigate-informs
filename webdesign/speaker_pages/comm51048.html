Title: The Signaling and Incentive Effects of Supplier Awards

Author: Ruth Beer, PhD Candidate, University of Michigan, Ross School of Business, 701 Tappan, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, ruthbeer@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Stephen Leider, Hyun-Soo Ahn

Year: 2013

Abstract: Many firms recognize the importance of supplier performance by giving out a “Supplier of the Year?award to reward their suppliers?efforts. We show, both theoretically and experimentally the existence of two contrary effects: On one hand, these symbolic awards may have motivational effects on the recipient’s behavior. On the other hand, giving out an award to a supplier gives a signal to other buyers that this supplier is good, intensifying the competition to get the good supplier.
