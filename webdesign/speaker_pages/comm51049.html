Title: Advance vs. Late Selling: Information Advantage, Pooling Flexibility, or Wasteful Production?

Author: Rachel Chen, University of California at Davis, Graduate School of Management, Davis, CA, United States, rachen@ucdavis.edu

Coauthor(s): Hemant Bhargava

Year: 2007

Abstract: When buyers are uncertain about product valuations for future consumption, the seller incur an information disadvantage by allowing consumers to delay purchase decisions with updated valuation information (which is unknown to the seller). Yet, late selling can benefit the seller when customers are heterogeneous, by increasing sellers' flexibility in pooling different consumer types.
