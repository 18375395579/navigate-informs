Title: Sales Force Incentives for New and Remanufactured Products

Author: Jeremy Kovach, Georgia Institute of Technology, 800 West Peachtree St. NW, Atlanta, GA, 30308, United States of America, Jeremy.Kovach@mgt.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Sumitro Banerjee, Atalay Atasu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Firms are increasingly offering remanufactured products alongside new products in the same market. We develop optimal sales force incentives for a single sales agent responsible for new and remanufactured products as well as for separate sales agents responsible for each of the products. Furthermore, we investigate how these compensation schemes change depending on the organizational structure, perceived quality of remanufactured products, demand uncertainty, and sales agent risk aversion.
