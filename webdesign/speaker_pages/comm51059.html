Title: The Economic Lot-sizing Problem with Market Selection

Author: Wilco van den Heuvel, Assistant Professor, Erasmus University Rotterdam, Burg. Oudlaan 50, Rotterdam, Netherlands, wvandenheuvel@few.eur.nl

Coauthor(s): Joseph Geunes, Edwin Romeijn, Thomas Sharkey, Erhun Kundakcioglu, Albert P.M. Wagelmans

Year: 2007

Abstract: In the economic lot-sizing problem with market selection we are given a number of markets, where each markets has a revenue and a sequence of demands. The objective is to find the subset of markets that maximizes profit. The profit of a subset of markets consists of the revenues minus the minimum lot-sizing cost to satisfy the demands of the selected markets. We discuss the NP-completeness of the problem, and we develop polynomial time algorithms for some special cases of the problem.
