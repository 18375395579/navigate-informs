Title: Subcontracting Strategies with Stochastic Third-party Capacity and Tardiness Sharing Contracts
Author: Tolga Aydinliyim, Assistant Professor, Decision Sciences Department, Lundquist College of Business, University of Oregon, Eugene, OR, 97403, United States of America, tolga@uoregon.edu
Coauthor(s): George Vairaktarakis
Year: 2007
Abstract: A manufacturer cannot process all his workload before the customer due date and subcontracts part of his workload to a third-party with stochastic prior commitments. We study two different contracts; (i) a price-only contract, and (ii) a tardiness penalty sharing contract. The second contract coordinates the chain, Pareto-dominates all non-coordinating contracts if the contract parameters are negotiated jointly, and achieves higher chain efficiency even when there is coordination failure.
