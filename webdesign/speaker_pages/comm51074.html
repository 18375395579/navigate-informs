Title: Brand Drug Distribution: Fee-for-Service vs. Direct-to-Pharmacy
Author: Yao Zhao, Associate Professor, Rutgers Business School, 1 Washington Park, Newark, NJ, 07102, United States of America, yaozhao@andromeda.rutgers.edu
Coauthor(s): Katie Lacocca, Adam Fein
Year: 2012
Abstract: The U.S. pharmaceutical supply chain is under drastical transformation from asset-based Fee-for-Service agreement to Direct-to-Pharmacy agreement that transfers the ownership of channel inventory to the manufacturer. Such a transition generated a heavy debate among industry observers. Using mathematic models and real world examples, we compare these agreements for the pharmaceutical supply chain as a whole and its individual participants.
