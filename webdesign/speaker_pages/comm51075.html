Title: B2C Trade-in Program Design and the Role of Retailers

Author: Fei Qin, PhD Candidate, University of Cincinnati, Carl H. Lindner College of Business, Cincinnati, OH, 45221, United States of America, qinfi@mail.uc.edu

Coauthor(s): Uday Rao, Michael Fry

Year: 2013

Abstract: B2C trade-in programs where used consumer products can be returned for cash payment or store credits are increasing in popularity for broad product categories. We study the structure of trade-in programs to understand their effectiveness and efficiency. In comparing decentralized supply chain performance, we show the fraction of returns going to the secondary retail market to be the key for the trade-in firm's incentives to coordinate the used-product collection and the secondary retail.
