Title: Electronic Commerce and Local Competition
Author: Anindya Ghose, Assistant Professor, Stern School of Business, New York University, 44 West Fourth Street, KMC 8-94, New York, NY, 10012, United States, aghose@stern.nyu.edu
Coauthor(s): Avi Goldfarb, Chris Forman
Year: 2006
Abstract: We examine how changes in the local supply of goods in the offline world changes consumer purchase behavior online. The emergence of new online retailing channels may act as a substitute for the benefits of urban concentration both by offering lower prices and by providing increased product variety. We explore these questions using data from Amazon on the top twenty books, and DVDs for over 8626 unique locations over 10 months. We discuss implications for the Long Tail.
