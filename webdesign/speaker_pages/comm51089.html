Title: Stock-Outs and Consumer Search Behavior for Products with Quality Uncertainty

Author: Laurens Debo, Assistant Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, Tepper School of Business, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, laurdebo@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Garrett van Ryzin

Year: 2006

Abstract: A stock-out contains information for a consumer who is uncertain about the quality of the product. It is an indication that the product is likely of high quality as many other consumers have bought the product. This may motivate the consumer to search for the product in other stores. We discuss how the equilibrium consumer search and purchasing behavior depends on the costs of information gathering for the consumer and the store replenishment rates.
