Title: Optimizing Tooling Investments Across Internal and External Organizational Boundaries

Author: Blake Johnson, Consulting Professor, Stanford University, 12881 Via Grimaldi, Del Mar, CA, 92014, United States, blakej@stanford.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Most automotive components have custom specifications that necessitate investments in custom tooling. Because tool lead times are significant, OEM planning groups coordinate with suppliers to guide their tooling decisions that define capacity, price and liability for OEM procurement groups. Differences in information and incentives between these three parties create biases that impact performance, but which may be addressed with appropriately structured contingent performance commitments.
