Title: Inventory Investment and Sales Reporting for Publicly Traded Firms

Author: Guoming Lai, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon Univ, 5000 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, guomingl@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Lin Nan, Laurens Debo

Year: 2008

Abstract: We study a two-period inventory management problem with correlated demand. The inventory manager's compensation is partially based on the firm's stock price which is influenced by the reported sales revenues. With better information about the real demand, the manager may manipulate the stock price by shipping more than the real demand to downstream customers and reporting higher than real sales revenues.
