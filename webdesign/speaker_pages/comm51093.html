Title: Contracting for On-time Delivery in the U.S. Influenza Vaccine Supply Chain

Author: Tinglong Dai, Assistant Professor, Johns Hopkins University, 100 International Dr, Baltimore, MD, 21202, United States of America, dai@jhu.edu

Coauthor(s): Fuqiang Zhang, Soo-Haeng Cho

Year: 2013

Abstract: Motivated by the contracts used in the influenza vaccine industry, we study a supply chain contracting problem under uncertainties in product design, delivery, and demand. In this supply chain, it is critical for a vaccine manufacturer to choose the right production quantity at the right time. We show that several well-known contracts fail to coordinate this supply chain. Thus we propose new coordinating contracts and compare their performance with the contracts used in the industry.
