Title: Supply Chain Intermediation When Retailers Lead

Author: Elodie Adida, University of California at Riverside, School of Business Administration, Riverside, United States of America, elodieg@ucr.edu

Coauthor(s): Nitin Bakshi, Victor DeMiguel

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study a three-tier supply chain, where the middle tier consists of intermediaries who compete to mediate between competing retailers and capacity-constrained suppliers. Our model portrays the retailers as Stackelberg leaders. We find that intermediaries should avoid specialty and commodity products, efficiency can be achieved with the right balance of competition at different tiers, and retailers benefit from using intermediaries when it is costly for them to identify or validate suppliers.
