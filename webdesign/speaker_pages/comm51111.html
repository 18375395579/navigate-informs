Title: Co-opetition in Services: The Boardwalk Phenomenon

Author: Srinagesh Gavirneni, Cornell University, Johnson School of Management, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, nagesh@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Jim Dai, Xuchuan Yuan

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider the setting in which service firms (e.g. restaurants) that compete on price and/or waiting time and cooperate on entertainment effort that reduces the cost of waiting for the patrons. We study monopoly and duopoly settings and in the latter, we consider both individual and joint entertainment efforts. We show that by cooperating on entertainment, the competing service firms are able to achieve efficiency levels equivalent to that of monopoly settings.
