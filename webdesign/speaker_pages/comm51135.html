Title: An Analysis of Revenue Sharing Contracts when Retailers Cheat

Author: Eda Kemahlioglu Ziya, UNC-Chapel Hill, CB # 3490, Chapel Hill, NC, United States of America, Eda_KemahliogluZiya@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Hans Sebastian Heese

Year: 2008

Abstract: Revenue sharing contracts are widely used to align the incentives of different channel members. Under a typical revenue sharing contract, if it is impossible or prohibitively expensive for the manufacturer to verify the retailer’s sales reports, the retailer has incentive to under-report sales and cheat the manufacturer out of part of its profit. We develop a simple analytical model to investigate the impact of cheating on the different supply chain constituents.
