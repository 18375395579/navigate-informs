Title: The Effects of Inventory Misplacement on Supply Chains

Author: Almula Camdereli, Georgetown University, McDonough School of Business, Georgetown University, 37 & O Streets, Washington, DC, 20057, United States of America, zac2@georgetown.edu

Coauthor(s): Jayashankar Swaminathan

Year: 2007

Abstract: In this paper, we explore the effects of increasing the proportion of inventory availability at the retailer on the profits of the retailer and the manufacturer in vertically integrated, uncoordinated and coordinated decentralized supply chains. We assume that the retailer is subject to demand distributed according to a general distribution. Further, we explore the cases where a random fraction of items gets misplaced in the aforementioned settings.
