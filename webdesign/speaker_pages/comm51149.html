Title: Dynamic Pricing with Options

Author: Guillermo Gallego, Professor, Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, Columbia University, New York, NY, United States, gmg2@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Ozge Sahin

Year: 2006

Abstract: We present a finite capacity, finite horizon perishable inventory model in discrete time where costumer classes with different willingness to pay distributions arrive sequentially and update their valuations until the end of the horizon. We use Dynamic Programming to compute incentive compatible call options for each customer class to maximize expected revenues and compare revenues with options to revenues under a low-to-high pricing strategy.
