Title: Data Mining, Automated Bidding and the Cost of Information

Author: Wolfgang Jank, University of Maryland, 4322 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, wjank@rhsmith.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Louiqa Rashid, Shu Zhang, Avigdor Gal

Year: 2008

Abstract: We discuss an automated bidding strategy for online auctions. The strategy is based on a data mining model for predicting price in competing auctions in a forward-looking manner. In order to make accurate decisions, the model needs information as input, ideally real-time information that is most up-to-date. In this research, we investigate the costs of such information in making bidding decisions and their impact on extracting maximum consumer surplus.
