Title: Reverse Auctions with Social Identity

Author: Sherry Li, Assistant Professor, University of Texas - Dallas, 2610 N. Floyd Road, School of Economics, Richardson, TX, 75083, United States, sherry.xin.li@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Ernan Haruvy, Kutsal Dogan

Year: 2006

Abstract: In reverse auctions where the buyer has latitude to select the winner, social preferences may influence winner selection as well as the price offer. In a laboratory study, we induce social identity with preferences over paintings. Subjects in the role of sellers repeatedly make price offers and buyers can select any offer. Our investigation aims to outline the impact of the induced social identities on price offers and matches.
