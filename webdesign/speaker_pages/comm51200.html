Title: Managing Service Systems Via Hidden Queues: The Role of Strategic Customer Behavior

Author: Eren Cil, Assistant Professor, University of Oregon, 1208 University of Oregon, Eugene, OR, 97403-1208, United States of America, erencil@uoregon.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a firm that can hide parts of its waiting lines as in touristic attractions. When the firm commits to a decision and informs customers about it, the firm hides a smaller part of its queue than it would do in a market with non-strategic customers. On the other hand, when customers do not know the exact decision of the firm, the firm hides its queue more aggressively as a response to strategic customer behavior. In fact, the firm may choose to hide its queue almost completely.
