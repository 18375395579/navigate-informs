Title: Product Line Extensions and Technology Licensing with a Strategic Supplier

Author: Steve Gilbert, Sam P. Woodson, Jr. Centennial Memorial Professor in Business, The University of Texas at Austin, The McCombs School of Business, Dept. of IROM, Austin, TX, 78793, United States of America, steve.gilbert@mccombs.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Liwen Chen, Yusen Xia

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider how the introduction of a low-end version of an existing product can affect an OEM's interactions with a strategic supplier. We demonstrate how the low-end product line extension can induce a more favorable pricing from the supplier, and then show that, depending upon the cost structure and relative performance of the product line extension, the OEM may benefit even more from the low-end line extension if it is produced by a rival, even if no licensing revenue can be collected.
