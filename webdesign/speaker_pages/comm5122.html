Title: Dynamic Pricing and Inventory Management under Advance Selling

Author: Michelle M.H. Seref, Doctoral Student, Operations Management, Decision Information Sciences, University of Florida, Stuzin Hall 361-E, Gainesville, FL, 32611, United States, michelle.hanna@cba.ufl.edu

Coauthor(s): Aydin Alptekinoglu, Selcuk Erenguc

Year: 2006

Abstract: Advance selling allows customers to purchase a product prior to consumption. Customers decide whether to advance purchase or wait until the spot period based on their expected future valuation, the advance sales price and the spot price. We consider a single inventory order decision made at a deterministic leadtime during the advance sales period. We seek to find the optimal order quantity, dynamic advance sales pricing policy, and spot price that maximize the firm’s total expected profit.
