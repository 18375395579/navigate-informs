Title: Progress in Optimal Redistribution Auctions

Author: Vincent Conitzer, Assistant Professor, Duke University, LSRC, Box 90129, Durham, NC, 27708, United States of America, conitzer@cs.duke.edu

Coauthor(s): Mingyu Guo

Year: 2008

Abstract: In auctions without a seller, a natural goal is to redistribute as much of the revenue as possible to the bidders without losing incentive compatibility. I will talk about our recent progress on this topic, including our work on redistribution mechanisms that are undominated or optimal in expectation, as well as redistribution mechanisms that allocate inefficiently to achieve an increase in the total amount redistributed (which more than compensates for the inefficiency).
