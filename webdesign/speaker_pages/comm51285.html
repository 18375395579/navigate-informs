Title: Remanufacturing Under Take-back Legislation: Cost Benefits or Potential Competition?

Author: Gokce Esenduran, Kenan-Flagler Business School, UNC at Chapel Hill, CB# 3490, McColl Bldg., Chapel Hill, NC, 27599, United States, gokce_esenduran@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Eda Kemahlioglu-Ziya, Jayashankar Swaminathan

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider an OEM with and without in-house remanufacturing capabilities under multiple take-back legislative scenarios. When the OEM cannot remanufacture, we allow a third-party remanufacturer in the market. Questions we raise include:When should an OEM with in-house remanufacturing capability remanufacture under different legislations?If the OEM cannot remanufacture, when should he sell cores to a remanufacturer?Does imposing remanufacturing targets always increase the remanufacturing level?
