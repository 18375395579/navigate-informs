Title: Recycling as a Secondary Market Strategy
Author: Isil Alev, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, isilalev@gatech.edu
Coauthor(s): Vishal Agrawal, Atalay Atasu
Year: 2013
Abstract: We focus on the economic and environmental implications of recycling policy requirements on secondary market strategies and product design choices of durable good monopolist. Using well-established consumer and firm characteristics, our work shows that EPR policy may result in very different consequences than intended. Additionally, we analyze the effect of recent international export control mechanisms and find that partial bans cause adverse effects both in developed and developing countries.
