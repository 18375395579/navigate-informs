Title: Intermediation in Salvage Markets with Strategic Consumers

Author: Pnina Feldman, Haas School of Business, UC Berkeley, 545 Student Services Bldg #1900, Berkeley, CA, 94720, United States of America, feldman@haas.berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Kate Ashley

Year: 2012

Abstract: Motivated by the proliferation of third-party salvage firms such as Overstock.com, we consider the operational benefits that such firms can provide to sellers. The setting is a variation on the classic newsvendor: a seller can either sell surplus units directly to customers, or sell to an intermediary at a lower price. We find that if customers are strategic, the longer supply chain may perform better, even if the intermediary has no cost or marketing advantages.
