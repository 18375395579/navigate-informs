Title: Optimizing Capital Investment Decisions at Intel Corporation

Author: Feryal Erhun, Stanford University, Stanford, CA, United States of America, ferhun@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Karl Kempf, Erik Hertzler, Timothy Rosenberg, Chen Peng

Year: 2012

Abstract: Intel spends over $5B annually on manufacturing equipment. With increasing lead times from equipment suppliers and increasing difficulty in forecasting market demand, optimizing capital investment decisions is a significant managerial challenge. We describe the Velocity Program and the DMEP framework, which have benefited Intel hundreds of millions of dollars in documented cost savings and at least $2B in revenue upside for a manufacturing process transition during a period of economic crisis.
