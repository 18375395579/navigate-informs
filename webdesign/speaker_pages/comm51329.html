Title: Investments in Remanufacturibility: A Supply Chain Perspective
Author: Sasidhar Malladi, Iowa State University, 1400 Gateway Hills Park Drive, 802, Ames, IA, 50014, United States, sasi@iastate.edu
Coauthor(s): Jo Min
Year: 2006
Abstract: We consider a producer who sells both newly manufactured and remanufactured products to a service provider, who in turn utilizes them to meet her, own customers?demand. Furthermore, the manufacturer has the option to invest in technology so as to increase the degree of remanufacturability, the fraction of products that are remanufactured. We investigate how the optimal degrees of remanufacturability, prices and profits vary with respect to channel power as well as the product life.
