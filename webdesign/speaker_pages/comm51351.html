Title: Maximizing Supply Chain Profits with Selective Channel Strategies
Author: Ulrich Thonemann, Professor, Department of Supply Chain Management and Management Science, University of Cologne, Albertus-Magnus-Platz, Koeln, K, 50923, Germany, Ulrich.Thonemann@uni-koeln.de
Coauthor(s): Marcel Sieke, Ralf Seifert
Year: 2007
Abstract: Demand variability is an important challenges that companies are facing. Companies that poorly manage demand variability report high supply chain costs and low profit levels. In this paper we make a conscious decision about admitting demand variability to the supply chain. By observing the impact of demand patterns on the efficiency of the supply chain, we derive an optimal distribution channel strategy that trades off between market size and demand variability.
