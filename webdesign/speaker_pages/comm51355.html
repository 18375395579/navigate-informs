Title: Optimal Dynamic Pricing for Tandem Queueing Systems

Author: Xinchang Wang, PhD Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, Main 316, 755 Ferst Drive, NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332-0205, United States of America, xwang336@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Sigrun Andradottir, Hayriye Ayhan

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study tandem queueing systems with two stations and finite buffers, in which the queue manager quotes prices for incoming customers from a set of discrete prices. Our goal is to determine the dynamic pricing policy that maximizes the long-run average profit. We show that the optimal policy exhibits a monotone structure, in which the quoted prices have greater dependency on the queue length at station 1 than at station 2.
