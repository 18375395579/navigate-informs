Title: Efficiency of Simple Penalty Contracts When Suppliers Can Improve Reliability

Author: Woonam Hwang, PhD Student, London Business School, Regent's Park, London, NW1 4SA, United Kingdom, whwang.phd2009@london.edu

Coauthor(s): Nitin Bakshi, Victor DeMiguel

Year: 2012

Abstract: Firms facing supply uncertainty often use simple penalty contracts to induce greater supplier effort for improving reliability. We investigate the efficiency of a unit penalty contract under three broad categories of supply risk: random capacity (RC), random disruption (RD) and random yield (RY). We find that a unit penalty contract always coordinates the supply chain under RC and RD, but it does not under RY, and the performance depends on the timing of the reliability investment.
