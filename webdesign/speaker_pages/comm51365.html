Title: Bargaining in the Supply Chain and Its Implication to Coordination of Competing Supply Chains

Author: Opher Baron, University of Toronto, 105 Saint George Street, Toronto, ON, M5S3E6, Canada, Opher.baron@Rotman.utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Oded Berman, Desheng Wu

Year: 2007

Abstract: A significant part of the supply chain coordination literature focus on inducing the supply chain to act as a Vertically Integrated (VI) one rather than a Manufacturing Stackelberg (MS). We investigate an industry with two supply chains that Bargain on the Wholesale price and show that both the MS and the VI are special cases of this contract. We characterize the unique Nash Equilibrium for the strategies of both chains and, surprisingly, show that VI of both chains is never equilibrium.
