Title: Impact of Consumer Returns on Supply Chain Coordination

Author: Ana Muriel, Associate Professor, University of Massachusetts Amherst, 160 Governors Drive, Amherst, MA, 01003, United States, muriel@ecs.umass.edu

Coauthor(s): Rocio Ruiz Benitez

Year: 2006

Abstract: We consider a two-echelon supply chain with a single manufacturer and a single retailer that faces stochastic and/or price-dependent demand in a single period. Both retailer and manufacturer incur costs associated with consumer returns. We show that higher profits and better coordination can be achieved when the players acting in a decentralized fashion do not consider any information about consumer returns as they make their pricing and ordering decisions.
