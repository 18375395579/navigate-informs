Title: The Potential for Cannibalization of New Product Sales by Remanufactured Products

Author: Kate Li, Doctoral Student, Penn State University, 419A Business Building, University Park, PA, 16802, United States, jzl120@psu.edu

Coauthor(s): Daniel Guide

Year: 2006

Abstract: We discuss the potential problem of cannibalization of new product sales by remanufactured products. Previous literature usually assumes perfect substitutability between new and remanufactured products, or no substitution. We present the results from an experimental study that explores the willingness to pay for remanufactured and new products for a class of consumer products.
