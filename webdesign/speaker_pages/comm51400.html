Title: Comparative Analysis of Distribution Channels for Innovative Durable Goods
Author: Genaro Gutierrez, Associate Professor, The University of Texas at Austin, McCombs School of Business, Austin, TX, 78712, United States, Genaro.Gutierrez@McCombs.utexas.edu
Coauthor(s): Xiuli(Sophia) He
Year: 2006
Abstract: We compare the desirability of direct, indirect, and hybrid channel structures for the distribution of a durable good for which consumer demand follows a price sensitive Bass-type diffusion process. We examine the desirability each of these distribution channel structures as a function of the relative brand/retailer influence on the consumer, distribution costs, inventory costs, and after sale profit opportunities such as sale of service contracts and sale of complementary products.
