Title: Group Identity and Forecast Sharing in Supply Chains

Author: Ulrich Thoneman, University of Colgone, Germany, Ulrich.Thonemann@uni-koeln.de

Coauthor(s): Felix Papier, Torsten Gully

Year: 2013

Abstract: A production planner decides on order quantities and relies on demand forecasts from a demand planner for this decision. The more effort the demand planner invests, the better is the forecast. We analyze the effect of group identity on the effort that the demand planner invests. We develop a game-theoretical model and test the model in the lab. We find that increasing group identity increases the effort invested by the demand planner and that the production planner anticipates this effect.
