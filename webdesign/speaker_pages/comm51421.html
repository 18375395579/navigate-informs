Title: Component Procurement Strategy in Assemble-To-order Systems with Demand Forecast Updates

Author: James Cao, Assistant Professor, University of Saskatchewan, Edwards School of Business, Room 88 PotashCorp Centre, 25 Campus Dr., Saskatoon, S7N 5A7, Canada, cao@edwards.usask.ca

Coauthor(s): Rick So

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper examines the value of demand forecast updates in an assemble-to-order system in which the components have different procurement lead times. The manufacturer procures the components in sequence, and thus can take advantage of the latest forecast updates when ordering components with shorter lead times. We compare the expected profit with the no-updating case to determine the operating environment under which demand forecast updates could be most beneficial.
