Title: Controlling for Price Endogeneity in Airline Passenger Itinerary Choice Models

Author: Stacey Mumbower, Graduate Research Assistant, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, stacey.mumbower@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Laurie Garrow

Year: 2012

Abstract: Although price endogeneity can lead to unrealistic and misleading model coefficient estimates, few studies have explored endogenous airline prices. A unique set of data collected from airline websites is used to build airline passenger itinerary choice models. An instrumented variable approach is used to control for factors that influence price (such as prices of other competitors and presence of a low cost carrier), allowing models to estimate the unbiased effect of price on consumer decisions.
