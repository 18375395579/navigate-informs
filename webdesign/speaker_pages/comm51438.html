Title: A Behavioral Investigation of Service-Based Supplier Competitions

Author: Karen Donohue, Associate Professor, Carlson School, University of Minnesota, 321 19th Avenue S., Minneapolis, MN, 55455, United States, KDonohue@csom.umn.edu

Coauthor(s): Saif Benjaafar, Elena Katok

Year: 2006

Abstract: We compare two types of supplier competitions that are proven, in theory, to be equally effective ways for a buyer to elicit high service quality from his supply base. We study the two mechanisms in the laboratory to determine whether these similarities hold true with human decision-makers. We also test the effect of the number of competitors and different service cost structures on service performance.
