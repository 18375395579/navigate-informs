Title: Designing Subsidies with Industry Response Dynamics: Commitment vs. Flexibility
Author: Ruben Lobel, Assistant Professor, Wharton School, Philadelphia, United States of America, rlobel@wharton.upenn.edu
Coauthor(s): Georgia Perakis, Maxime Cohen
Year: 2012
Abstract: Governments use consumer incentives to promote new technologies and stimulate investments from the private sector (eg. solar panels). We model the interaction between a government and an industry player in a multi-period setting under uncertain demand. We show how the timing of decisions will affect production and subsidy levels. In particular, a flexible subsidy policy will be on average more expensive for the government than a committed long-term policy.
