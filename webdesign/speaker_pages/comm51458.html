Title: Information Sharing in Supply Chains: An Empirical and Theoretical Valuation

Author: Ruomeng Cui, Northwestern University, 2001 Sheridan Road, Evanston, United States of America, r-cui@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Achal Bassamboo, Jan Van Mieghem, Gad Allon

Year: 2013

Abstract: We provide an empirical and theoretical assessment of the value of sharing downstream information. According to recent work, the upstream forecast accuracy improvement is zero under a large set of parameters. We use the data from a CPG company to show the opposite result. Out theoretical model allows the decision maker to deviate away from a given inventory policy. These “decision deviations?turn out to lead to information losses, resulting in strictly positive value of downstream information.
