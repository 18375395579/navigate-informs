Title: On Scheduling and Deadline Differentiated Pricing of Deferrable Demand

Author: Eilyan Bitar, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, United States of America, eyb5@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Yunjian Xu

Year: 2013

Abstract: A large fraction of total electric load is inherently deferrable in time. In the following, we propose a novel market, where consumers consent to deferred electric power service in exchange for a reduced price of energy. The longer the willingness to defer, the larger the reduction in price. We jointly characterize scheduling policies and differentiated prices that are both efficient and incentive compatible, in that every consumer would like to reveal her true deadline to the supplier.
