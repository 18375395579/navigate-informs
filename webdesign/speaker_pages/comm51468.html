Title: Product Customization and Customer Service Cost: An Empirical Analysis

Author: Anuj Kumar, PhD Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 4800 Forbes Avenue, 243 HBH, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, akumar1@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Rahul Telang, Sunder Kekre

Year: 2008

Abstract: We examine the link between product customization and firm’s cost to serve customers via call center at a major US health insurance firm. Process of customization is such that it increases users?familiarity with his coverage and improves the fit with his medical needs - reduces his incentives to call the firm’s call center. Our results show that users with customized policies call 30% less frequently than users with standard plan ?customized products may be operationally cheaper to serve.
