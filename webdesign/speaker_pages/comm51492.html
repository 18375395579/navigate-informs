Title: Price Dispersion in Electronic Markets: New Evidence from Transaction Prices

Author: Anindya Ghose, Assistant Professor, Stern School of Business, New York University, 44 West Fourth Street, KMC 8-94, New York, NY, 10012, United States, aghose@stern.nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Yuliang Oliver Yao

Year: 2006

Abstract: We use transaction prices for more than 30,000 products, collected from both the electronic and traditional markets to re-evaluate price dispersion. We find price dispersions in the electronic market is as small as 0.03%, a level substantially lower than those reported in the literature. We estimate the consumer surplus gain from the use of the electronic market is about $181 millions per year.
