Title: Valuing Product Mix as an Option: Investment, Production and Pricing
Author: Zhongjun Tian, Ph.D Candidate, Washington University, Campus Box 1133, One Brookings Drive, St. Louis, MO, 63130, United States, tian@wustl.edu
Coauthor(s): Panos Kouvelis
Year: 2007
Abstract: We study a firm's joint decisions of investment, production and pricing. At the beginning of the time horizon, the firm invests in a capacity which can be used to produce two substitute products of which the total demand evolves in time. At sometime near the end of the horizon, the firm makes commitment decisions on both quality level of and volume of capacity committed to each product. At the end of the horizon, the firm observes the total demand and makes the production and pricing decisions.
