Title: The Effects of Social Relationship on Supply Chain Networks Vulnerability and Risk Management

Author: Jose Cruz, Assistant Professor, University of Connecticut, School of Business, 2100 Hillside Road Unit 1041, Storrs, CT, 06269-1041, United States of America, Jose.Cruz@business.uconn.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We analyze the effects of levels of social relationship on a supply chain networks. We propose a performance measure for the evaluation of supply chain vulnerability. The model investigate the interplay of the heterogeneous decision-makers in the supply chain and compute the equilibrium pattern of product transactions, prices, and levels of social relationship. The results show that high levels of relationship lead to lower overall cost, lower price and higher product transaction.
