Title: Inducing Retailer Forecasting: Rebates versus Returns

Author: Terry Taylor, Associate Professor, Columbia University, Graduate School of Business, 3022 Broadway, Uris 417, New York, NY, 10027, United States, tat2002@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Wenqiang Xiao

Year: 2006

Abstract: We examine how a retailer’s ability to improve her demand information by exerting forecasting effort affects what type of contract a manufacturer should offer. We show whether a manufacturer should offer a contract that rewards the retailer for selling units (rebates) or a contract that rewards the retailer for not selling units (returns).
