Title: Offshoring and Transfer Pricing Decisions of Global Firms in the Presence of Tax Advantages

Author: Masha Shunko, CMU, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, mshunko@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Laurens Debo, Srinagesh Gavirneni

Year: 2008

Abstract: We study offshoring decisions of global firms in the presence of tax advantages. We consider transfer pricing as an income shifting tool that allows to benefit from tax differential and that also serves as an incentive tool within the firm. We show that the optimal offshoring policy for a centralized firm has an all-or-nothing structure. Under information asymmetry, the firm benefits from decentralizing pricing or offshoring decisions, in which case the policy changes to partial offshoring.
