Title: Optimization of Customer & Supplier Logistics at a Large Automotive Parts Manufacturer
Author: Hakan Yildiz, Assistant Professor, Michigan State University, Department of Supply Chain Management, 370 N. Business Complex, East Lansing, MI, 48824, United States of America, yildiz@msu.edu
Coauthor(s): R. Ravi
Year: 2008
Abstract: We undertake an integrated study of inbound and outbound logistics at a leading automotive parts manufacturer. We identify the opportunity for significant cost savings over the current system by matching opposite flows of material and containers. We consider the problem from a supply chain coordination perspective, where the parts manufacturer makes all the transportation arrangements for its customers and suppliers based on the results of the centralized optimum solution.
