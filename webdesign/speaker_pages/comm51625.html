Title: Slotting and Pricing Decisions in a Durable Product Supply Chain

Author: Xiuli He, UT-Dallas, School of Management, SM 30, Dallas, TX, 75080, United States, xiulihe@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Suresh Sethi

Year: 2007

Abstract: Consider a supply chain in which a manufacturer sells an innovative durable product to a retailer. The product demand follows a Bass-type diffusion process and is determined by the market influences, retail price, and shelf-space allocated to it. We consider the myopic and far-sighted retailer profit strategies. We characterize the optimal dynamic shelf space allocation and optimal pricing policies. Surprisingly, the manufacturer, and even the retailer, may be better off with a myopic strategy.
