Title: Signaling by Price in a Congested Environment

Author: Laurens Debo, Associate Professor, University of Chicago, Chicago, IL, 60637, United States of America, laurens.debo@chicagobooth.edu

Coauthor(s): Uday Rajan, Senthil Veeraraghavan

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider the informational role of a queue when a firm can also adjust its price to signal its quality to uninformed consumers. We exhibit equilibria in which the queue almost perfectly reveals the quality. Here, short queues are associated with the low-quality firm and long queues with the high-quality one. Queues can be more efficient signaling instruments than prices.
