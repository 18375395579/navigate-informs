Title: Concurrency of Online Auctions
Author: Wolfgang Jank, Assistant Professor, University of Maryland, Robert H. Smith School of Business, College Park, MD, 20742, United States, wjank@rhsmith.umd.edu
Year: 2007
Abstract: We consider the phenomenon of concurrency and how it affects bidding and winning in online auctions. Concurrency arises from auctions that take place over the same time period and interact with each other. Interaction can occur in the form of bidders participating in simultaneous auctions or in the form of sellers evaluating their competition and adjusting selling strategies. In this work we consider several ways of measuring, visualizing and modeling concurrency in online auctions.
