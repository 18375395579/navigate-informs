Title: Comparison of Product Take-back Compliance Schemes

Author: Eda Kemahlioglu-Ziya, UNC-Chapel Hill, CB 3490, Chapel Hill, NC, United States of America, eda_kemahliogluziya@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Gokce Esenduran

Year: 2013

Abstract: Under take-back legislation, the most common compliance scheme is collectively with cost allocation by market share. An alternative is individual compliance. We compare these schemes based on cost to the manufacturer and on environmental benefits. We show that high collection targets and large market shares among partner firms make collective compliance more cost effective. We provide insights on the tradeoff between collection rate targets and the effectiveness of incentives for recyclability.
