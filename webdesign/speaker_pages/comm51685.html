Title: Bargaining Power and Supply Base Diversification

Author: Zhixi Wan, PhD Candidate, Stephen M. Ross School of Business, University of Michigan, 701 Tappan Street, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, wanzhixi@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Damian Beil

Year: 2008

Abstract: A buyer must select a set of suppliers to maintain as a supply base. Contracts are auctioned periodically and the buyer seeks to minimize long-term expected costs. Suppliers in the same region have correlated costs, and the buyer can mitigate the influence of correlated costs by choosing suppliers from different regions. We characterize the buyer's optimal supply base diversification strategy and show how it depends upon her bargaining power.
