Title: The value of sharing downstream data to improve forecasting: an empirical evaluation

Author: Ruomeng Cui, PhD Student, Northwestern University, 2001 Sheridan Rd, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, r-cui@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Jan Van Mieghem, Gad Allon, Achal Bassamboo

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study the value of sharing downstream information. We consider a two-stage supply chain model in which the retailer faces an autoregressive integrated moving average demand. The empirical value of information sharing is mostly positive. The key reason is the batching effect component. We shows that the demand propagates to a semi-ARIMA order process. The empirical value of information sharing is consistent with the numerical result from the theory.
