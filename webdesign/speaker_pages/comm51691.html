Title: Delegation vs. Control of Component Procurement

Author: Enis Kayis, Ph.D Candidate, Stanford University, Management Science and Engineering, Stanford, CA, United States, ekayis@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Feryal Erhun, Erica Plambeck

Year: 2007

Abstract: Should manufacturers delegate component procurement to their tier-1 suppliers, or control component procurement by contracting with both tier-1 and tier-2 suppliers? We consider a model in which suppliers have asymmetric cost information and manufacturers have alternative sources of supply with cost known only to them. We study how the complexity of contracts, the manufacturers?beliefs about the suppliers?costs as well as the underlying supply chain structure influences the optimal decision.
