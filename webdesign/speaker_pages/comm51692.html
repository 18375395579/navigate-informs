Title: Collaboration among Supply Chain Partners: Better Outcomes or More Conflict?
Author: Muge Yayla-Kullu, Asst. Prof. Operations Mgmt., RPI Lally School of Mgmt, 110 8th St, Troy, NY, 12180, United States of America, YAYLAH@rpi.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: The emerging area of supply chain alliances studies how firms work together for mutual benefit. In this paper, we investigate benefits of such an alliance for collaborative procurement. We take heterogeneity of the customer base into account when the products of firms are differentiated. In such a supply chain network, we find that joint consideration of sourcing, pricing, and demand generation objectives may result in non-trivial outcomes.
