Title: Analysis of Sales Force Effects and Pricing Decisions
Author: Itir Karaesmen, University of Maryland, Robert H Smith School of Business, College Park, MD, United States of America, ikaraes@rhsmith.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Wolfgang Jank, Shu Zhang, Wedad Elmaghraby
Year: 2008
Abstract: In this study, we analyze the sales transactions of a perishable-goods distributor. We investigate when sales force accept or reject pricing recommendations from an automated pricing optimization tool and what factors influence the transaction prices. We study the effect of margins previously quoted to the customers, product characteristics, length of the relationship with the customer, and sales force characteristics on the final sales prices.
