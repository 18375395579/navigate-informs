Title: Markdown Management at Sports Unlimited

Author: Masoud Talebian, University of Newcastle, Callaghan, Australia, Masoud.Talebian@newcastle.edu.au

Coauthor(s): Garrett van Ryzin

Year: 2013

Abstract: The case study focuses on the analytical aspects of designing markdown policies. For this purpose, it describes a retail sporting goods chain that operates nationally. Merchants (buyers) are responsible for buying items and setting initial prices. During the sales season, merchants evaluate items for possible markdowns and eventually mark them out-of-store so that they can be salvaged. Merchants currently rely on a price optimization tool, called PO, to manage markdowns. PO essentially identifies the items with low weekly sell-through rates as candidates for markdowns. Weekly sell-through is measured as the ratio of weekly sales to initial inventory. The firm’s current policy is to target an initial markdown of 50%.
