Title: Strategic Risk from Supply Chain Disruptions
Author: Zigeng Yin, PhD Candidate, Northwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, Tech C231, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, z-yin@northwestern.edu
Coauthor(s): Wallace Hopp, Seyed Iravani
Year: 2008
Abstract: Supply chain disruptions can result in both tactical (e.g., loss of short-term sales) and strategic (e.g., loss of long-term market share) consequences. In this talk, we present a model that captures the impact of supply disruptions on competing supply chains. We use our analytic and numerical results to highlight the situations that present the greatest risk and to identify policies for mitigating these risks.
