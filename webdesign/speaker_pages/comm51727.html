Title: Countervailing Power, Commitment, and Strategic Quality Improvement
Author: Rui Yin, Assistant Professor, Arizona State University, W. P. Carey School of Business, Tempe, AZ, 85287, United States of America, Rui.Yin@asu.edu
Coauthor(s): Yimin Wang
Year: 2013
Abstract: A critical factor that influences product quality is supply chain members' joint improvement efforts. In this research we explore two important financial and operational levers that may impact their joint improvement efforts: countervailing power and commitment. We examine the implications of these two levers on the manufacturer and supplier’s optimal quality improvement efforts and profits, when their efforts are complements or substitutes.
