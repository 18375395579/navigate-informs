Title: Disruption Risk and Optimal Sourcing in Multitier Supply Chains

Author: Erjie Ang, Stanford University, Graduate School of Business, Mail code 5015, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, erjieang@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Robert Swinney, Dan Iancu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We analyze a supply chain in which a manufacturer sources critical components from Tier 1 suppliers, who in turn source components from a possibly overlapping set of Tier 2 suppliers prone to disruption. We consider how the manufacturer can optimally source and contract with Tier 1 suppliers given this effect, and discuss robust sourcing strategies that the manufacturer can employ if its does not know the structure and degree of supplier overlap of the upper levels of its supply network.
