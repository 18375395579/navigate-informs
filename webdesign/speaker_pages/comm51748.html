Title: An Optimum Pricing Policy for a Multiclass Problem in the Airline Industry

Author: Daniel Felipe Otero Leon, Lecturer, Universidad de los Andes, Cra 1 Este #19A -40, Bogota, 00, 1111, Colombia, df.otero128@uniandes.edu.co

Coauthor(s): Raha Akhavan-Tabatabaei

Year: 2012

Abstract: In the airline industry, deciding the price of a ticket directly affects the future demand to buy that ticket. Depending on the willingness to pay of the customers, seats could remain empty or sold at a lower price at the departure date. We propose a stochastic dynamic pricing model to optimize the revenue of a given flight, applying phase type distributions and renewal processes to estimate the inter-arrival process and the probability that a customer buys a ticket.
