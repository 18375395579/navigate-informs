Title: Pricing and Operational Performance in Discretionary Services

Author: Raj Rajagopalan, Srajagop@marshall.usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Chunyang Tong

Year: 2012

Abstract: In discretionary services, the value or quality derived by a customer from a service depends upon the time the service provider (SP) devotes to the customer and the valuation differs across customers. We identify the optimal pricing scheme and explore the impact of two widely used pricing schemes, time-based billing and fixed fee, on many dimensions of service performance including SP's profitability, demand, utilization, and congestion level.
