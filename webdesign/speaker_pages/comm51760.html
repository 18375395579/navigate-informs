Title: Economic Hysteresis and Hydroponic Investments in Agriculture

Author: Timothy Richards, Arizona State University, trichards@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ram Acharya

Year: 2007

Abstract: Rising fuel prices cause higher prices for fresh produce on the U.S. East Coast. At some point, investing in local hydroponic production will become viable. Fuel price volatility creates a significant embedded real option value. This paper estimates the resulting hysteretic effect and develops a real-option investment-evaluation model. The real option value is nearly 25% of the investment amount, so fuel prices will have to rise further to make investments worthwhile.
