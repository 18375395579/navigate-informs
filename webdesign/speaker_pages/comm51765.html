Title: At Your Service?: Consumer’s Return Behavior and Retailer’s Profitability
Author: M. Ali Ulku, Assistant Professor, School of Business, Capital University, 1 College and Main, Columbus, OH, 43209, United States of America, aulku@capital.edu
Coauthor(s): Lynn C. Dailey, Muge H. Yayla-Kullu
Year: 2013
Abstract: Consumers expect that they can return goods if they are dissatisfied with them. Although they act as risk-relievers, such product returns significantly impact retailers?profits not only due to reduction in net sales, but also due to increased costs. In a retailer-consumer setting, we determine the profit-maximizing price and return period. We find that even with fraudulent consumers?negative effect on sales, a retailer can still achieve high profits with an optimally determined return policy.
