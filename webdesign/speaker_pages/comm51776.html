Title: Revenue Management in the Airline Industry: An Empirical Study

Author: Minho Cho, mc9@u.washington.edu

Coauthor(s): Yong-Pin Zhou, Ming Fan

Year: 2007

Abstract: In this paper, we use the data from Bureau of Transportation Statistics to empirically examine the revenue management practice in the airline industry. Specifically, we study how the operational policies affect pricing, operational performance and revenue. Our results show that operational policies such as capacity and code-sharing have significant impacts on price dispersion, load-factor, and revenue. We also find that route level revenue increases in price dispersion.
