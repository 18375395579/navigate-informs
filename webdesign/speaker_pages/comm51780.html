Title: The Negotiation Power of Groups
Author: Feng Chen, USC, Bridge Hall, 400F, Los Angeles, CA, 98119, United States, fchen@marshall.usc.edu
Coauthor(s): Yehuda Bassok
Year: 2007
Abstract: Negotiation power of individual is defined in several ways. It is quite common that individual players form groups and negotiate as teams. But it is not clear what the negotiation power of a team is. Typically 3 types of assumptions are made: The negotiation power of a team is equal to the average, maximum or minimum power of its members. In this study we report the result of laboratory experiment to determine the negotiation power of teams.
