Title: Supplier and Retailer Behavior and the Effectiveness of Coordinating Contracts

Author: Julie Niederhoff, Assistant Professor, Whitman School of Management, Syracuse University, 721 University Ave, Syracuse, NY, 13244, United States of America, jniederh@syr.edu

Coauthor(s): Panos Kouvelis

Year: 2007

Abstract: Supply chain coordinating contracts are theoretically equivalent when set by risk-neutral expected-profit-maximizing decision makers. However, individual effects of the decision maker interact with the situational effects of the contract framing to reveal differences in contract performance. We study this interaction to identify several key factors which drive the expected profit of the contract.
