Title: To Repair or to Replace? Optimal Repair Durations for Short Lifecycle Products
Author: Kristin Sahyouni, Northwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, Department of IEMS, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States, kristin@iems.northwestern.edu
Coauthor(s): Canan Savaskan, Mark Daskin
Year: 2006
Abstract: We consider a repair operation in which defective warranty items arrive to an OEM over a known period and must be repaired using spare parts procured from a third party supplier or replaced with a comparable next generation alternative. We show how facility costs, inventory holding costs, replacement costs, and variable repair costs affect a firm’s optimal decision. Extensions consider arrival rate uncertainty, supplier contracting options, and the value of information over the lifecycle.
