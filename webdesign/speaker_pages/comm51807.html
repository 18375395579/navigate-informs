Title: A Little Flexibility is All You Need: Optimality of Tailored Chaining and Pairing

Author: Achal Bassamboo, Managerial Economics & Decision Sciences, Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States of America, a-bassamboo@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Jan Van Mieghem, Ramandeep Randhawa

Year: 2008

Abstract: We introduce tailored pairing that merges and extends the concepts of chaining and tailoring in dynamic processing systems. We optimize the type and amount of flexibility using a Brownian approximation that is asymptotically correct. We show analytically that for most practical flexibility cost structures the optimal flexibility configuration invests a lot in dedicated resources, a little in bi-level flexibility, but nothing in level-k>2 flexibility, let alone full flexibility.
