Title: Fairness in Dual Channel

Author: Duygu Dagli, PhD Student, University of Texas at Dallas, Naveen Jindal School of Management, 800 West Campbell Road, Richardson, TX, 75080, United States of America, dxd115020@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Ozalp Ozer

Year: 2013

Abstract: Direct channels provide higher profit margins for manufacturers at the expense of channel conflict. This study focuses on the price competition between a manufacturer-owned direct channel and a retailer channel under asymmetric power structure. We determine when behavioral regularities, in particular fairness, play a key role in pricing strategies and conflict resolution.
