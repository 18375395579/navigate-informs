Title: On the Performance Of Supply Chain Contracts With Domestic Gray Market Externalities

Author: Mehmet Sekip Altug, Assistant Professor, George Washington University, 2201 G. Street NW, Washington, DC, 20052, United States of America, maltug@gwu.edu

Coauthor(s): Garrett van Ryzin

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a supply chain with one manufacturer and several authorized distributors that face uncertain demand and the potential of a domestic gray market emergence, which is possibly fed by local authorized distributors as a result of the incentives manufacturer provides. We characterize the emerging equilibrium in such domestic gray markets. We find out that wholesale pricing is quite robust to the presence of domestic gray markets so much so that manufacturers can even be better off.
