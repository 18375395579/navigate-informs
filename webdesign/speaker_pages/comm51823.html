Title: Designing Distribution Channels to Launch an Innovative Durable Product with Complementary Services
Author: Xiuli He, UT-Dallas, School of Management, SM 30, Dallas, TX, 75080, United States, xiulihe@gmail.com
Coauthor(s): Genaro Gutierrez
Year: 2007
Abstract: Consider a supply chain in which a manufacturer produces an innovative durable product (IDP) with complementary services and products. We assume a Bass-type diffusion process for the demand. The manufacturer decides on whether to launch the IDP through a direct, an indirect or a competitive channel. We characterize the impact of after-sales profits and the retailer market influences on the pricing decisions of the channel members as well as the manufacturer’s decisions on channel configuration.
