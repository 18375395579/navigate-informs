Title: Dynamic Spectrum Allocation for Cognitive Radio
Author: Richard La, Associate Professor, University of Maryland, A. V. Williams Bldg. Department of ECE, University of Maryland, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, hyongla@eng.umd.edu
Coauthor(s): Sung Chun
Year: 2008
Abstract: We study the problem of dynamically re-allocating underutilized spectrum in cognitive radio. Primary users can re-sell a portion of purchased spectrum when it is underutilized to recover parts of their cost. Interested primary users can cooperate among themselves to auction away available spectrum to secondary users that are assumed selfish. We investigate if there is an incentive for the primary users (sellers) to cooperate to increase their expected revenue.
