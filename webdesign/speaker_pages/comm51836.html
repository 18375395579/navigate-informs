Title: Choice of a Mitigation Strategy for Supplier Default Risk: Subsidies, Inventory or Diversification

Author: Luz Caudillo, University of Michigan, 660 Hidden Valley Dr. Apt 202, Ann Arbor, MI, 48104, United States, fuentesl@umich.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: We analyze the tradeoffs of diversification, subsidies and inventory using a model where a manufacturer can rely on either inventory or financial subsidies to control exposure to supplier default risk. We consider a setting where current demand is known (deterministic), for different scenarios: a) having one or two suppliers, b)considering one or two periods, or c) having known (deterministic) or uncertain future demand. In general, the higher the granted subsidy, the less supplies are ordered.
