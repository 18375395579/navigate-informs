Title: Internet Exchanges for Information Goods

Author: Michael Smith, Associate Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, 4800 Forbes Avenue, HBH 2105D, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, mds@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Rahul Telang

Year: 2006

Abstract: We use a unique dataset documenting new and used markets for CDs and DVDs at Ama-zon.com, to analyze the degree to which sales of these used products cannibalize new sales. Our results show that the cross price elasticity of new product sales with respect to used product prices is significantly higher for CDs and DVDs than for books. One implication of these find-ings is that used product markets pose a larger threat to the CD and DVD industries than they do to the book publishing industry.
