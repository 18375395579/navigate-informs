Title: The Impact of Competition in Green Technology Adoption

Author: Georgia Perakis, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Sloan School of Management, 100 Main Street, Cambridge, MA, United States of America, georgiap@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Maxime Cohen, Charles Thraves

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study competition among suppliers for green technologies when government gives subsidies to the consumers. We quantify who is benefiting from competition and when. The results differ depending on demand uncertainty and supplier asymmetry. Competition hurts suppliers and benefits the government but not always the consumers. We also discuss how governments should incentivize new entrants in this market.
