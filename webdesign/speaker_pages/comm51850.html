Title: Impact of Cash Conversion Cycles on Inventory Systems with Non-stationary Demand

Author: Wei Luo, Duke University, 100 Fuqua Drive, Durham, NC, United States of America, wei.luo@duke.edu

Coauthor(s): Kevin Shang

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider a cash-constrained firm that orders inventory to satisfy stochastic customer demand in a finite horizon. The firm provides trade credits to its downstream customer while receiving trade credits from its upstream supplier. The work sheds light on how firms should manage cash conversion cycles and assesses the value of cash flow information.
