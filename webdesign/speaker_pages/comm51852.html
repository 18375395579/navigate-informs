Title: Resilient Supply Chains: Diversification vs. Dedicated Supplier Procurement Strategies
Author: Volodymyr Babich, Assistant Professor, University of Michigan, 1205 Beal Avenue, IOE 2783, Ann Arbor, MI, 48105, United States, babich@umich.edu
Coauthor(s): Luz Caudillo
Year: 2006
Abstract: Using a dynamic, stochastic model which combines procurement and financial subsidies decisions, this paper examines the tradeoffs of a manufacturing firm between two strategies for managing supply default risks: diversification of the supplier base vs. financial subsidies to a few dedicated suppliers. We describe properties of the optimal policies and derive conditions under which the firm prefers capital concentration to diversification.
