Title: Customer Response to Short-Term Price Fluctuations
Author: Margaret Pierson, Assistant Professor, Tuck at Dartmouth, Hanover, NH, United States of America, mpierson@hbs.edu
Year: 2012
Abstract: Transactions at 15 gasoline stations in Massachusetts over two years are used to estimate consumer elasticity with respect to price on a transaction basis. A significant proportion of the observed transactions display high sensitivity to price. This customer behavior results in congestion at pumps during high prices, a counterintuitive result which constrains stations' price choices in practice.
