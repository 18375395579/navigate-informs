Title: Competing for Socially Conscious Consumers: The Role of Supply Chain Structure

Author: Robert Swinney, Associate Professor, Graduate School of Business, Stanford University, 655 Knight Way, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, swinney@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Ruixue Guo, Hau L. Lee

Year: 2012

Abstract: We analyze a duopoly in which two competing firms choose whether to adopt socially responsible manufacturing practices. Doing so attracts demand from competing firms-those consumers who have a preference for socially responsible production-but also increases costs. We consider the impact of supply chain structure on the firms?decisions, and discuss how the supplier and consumer population characteristics combine to influence the equilibrium degree of social responsibility in the industry.
