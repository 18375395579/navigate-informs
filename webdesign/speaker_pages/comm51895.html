Title: Google Search and Cross-channel Competition

Author: Tawei Wang, University of Hawaii at Manoa, 2404 Maile Way, BUSAD E602C, Honolulu, HI, 96822, United States of America, twwang@hawaii.edu

Coauthor(s): Wenqi Shen, Roger Debreceny

Year: 2013

Abstract: This study investigates whether online information seeking behaviors (captured by Google search volume) is associated with retailers' online sales revenues and how such online attention affects the sales revenues through the traditional channel. The main findings suggest that Google search volume can be used as a proxy for the general attention or interests towards a firm which in turn leads to opposite impact on sales revenues through online versus the traditional channel.
