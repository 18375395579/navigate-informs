Title: The Implications of Cost, Capacity and Competition on Product Line Selection

Author: Rui Yin, Assistant Professor, Arizona State University, Dept. of Supply Chain Management, W. P. Carey School of Business, Tempe, AZ, 85287, United States of America, rui.yin@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Chris Tang

Year: 2008

Abstract: We present a model in which a firm makes the optimal product line selection, production, and pricing decisions in the presence of utility-maximizing consumers. There are 2 substitutable products in the consideration set, where product 2 has a higher quality than product 1. We examine the impact of costs, capacity and competition on the firm's optimal product line selection, optimal price and optimal production quantity.
