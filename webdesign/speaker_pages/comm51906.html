Title: Retail Execution and Store Associate Incentive Design
Author: Nicole DeHoratius, Zaragoza Logistics Center, Edifico Náyade 5, C/ Bari 55 - PLAZA, Zaragoza, Spain, NDeHoratius@zlc.edu.es
Coauthor(s): Saravanan Kesavan, Adam Mersereau
Year: 2012
Abstract: We determine the extent to which a change in the design of store associate incentives at several pilot stores of a major retail chain drive store associate effort allocation and consequently store performance. Our results yield insights useful to a retail chain seeking to maximize long-term profitability rather than short-term sales when designing store associate incentive plans.
