Title: Does Field Price-discretion Improve Profits? Evidence from Auto Lending

Author: A. Serdar Simsek, Columbia Business School, 3022 Broadway, New York, NY, 10027, United States of America, ASimsek13@gsb.columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Garrett van Ryzin, Robert Phillips

Year: 2013

Abstract: In many markets, headquarters create a price list while local salespeople have discretion to negotiate prices for individual deals. We investigate how much (if any) pricing discretion to grant using two data sets –one from an online lender and one from an indirect lender with local pricing discretion. We find that local sales force adjusts prices in a way that improves profitability and this discretion introduces endogeneity into the pricing process, which leads to significant estimation bias.
