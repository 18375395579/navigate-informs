Title: Transient and Steady State Profitability of New/Remanufactured Product Substitution

Author: Ana Muriel, Associate Professor, University of Massachusetts Amherst, 160 Governors Drive, Amherst, MA, 01003, United States of America, muriel@ecs.umass.edu

Coauthor(s): Yue Jin, Yihao Lu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We investigate the profitability of offering remanufactured products for a monopoly firm in single-period and steady state, multi-period settings. We identify when customer segmentation drives the remanufacturing decision, so that the firm benefits from offering new products as remanufactured. While in the single period model the substitution decision depends solely on the new product cost, it depends on the weighted average of manufacturing and remanufacturing cost in the steady state.
