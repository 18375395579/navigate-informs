Title: A Supplier’s Response to Auditing and Incentives for Social and Environmental Performance

Author: Terry Taylor, University of California, Berkeley, Haas School of Business, Berkeley, CA, 94720, United States of America, taylor@haas.berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): Erica Plambeck, Qinqin Zhang

Year: 2012

Abstract: Firms audit their suppliers?labor conditions and environmental performance, and even provide financial incentives for such performance. Unfortunately, suppliers often respond with deception - by hiding noncompliance. We identify conditions under which higher auditing effort or financial incentives backfire - causing the supplier to invest less in compliance. We also identify conditions under which a buyer should commit to invite a third party to audit is supplier and to publicize violations.
