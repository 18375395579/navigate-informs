Title: Social Optimality and Duopoly Competition with Real Options

Author: Guillermo Gallego, Chairman, IEOR Department, Columbia University, 500 West 120th Street, New York, NY, 10027, United States, ggallego@ieor.columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Ozge Sahin

Year: 2006

Abstract: We consider a two period model with homogeneous customers who know the distribution of their valuations in period 1 and see realizations in period 2. We show that options are socially optimal and provide a range of consumer surplus over which options strictly dominate low-to-high pricing. We also consider a duopoly game between providers with finite capacity that compete by offering call options. We compare and contrast our results with the classical Bertrand-Edgeworth model without options.
