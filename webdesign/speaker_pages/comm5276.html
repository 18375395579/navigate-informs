Title: Revenue Management in Aircraft Leasing Business

Author: Qing Ding, Assistant Professor, Singapore Management University, LKC School of Business, 50 Stamford Road #04-01, 178899, Singapore, dingqing@smu.edu.sg

Coauthor(s): Brian Rodrigues, Kwan Eng Wee

Year: 2007

Abstract: We consider an aircraft leasing business problem. A group of airline companies may either purchase their own aircrafts or rent aircrafts from a leasing company. We study the benefit of risk pooling effect, which may be affected by the number of seats in a single aircraft; On the other hand, may be recoverd by using dynamic pricing to adjust the demands of airline tickets. We also explore the benefit from interaction of decisions among the purchasing, leasing and dynamic pricing.
