Title: Financing in Supply Chain Management

Author: Song (Alex) Yang, PhD Student, The University of Chicago Graduate School of Business, 5807 S Woodlawn Ave, Chicago, IL, 60637, United States of America, syang1@chicagogsb.edu

Coauthor(s): John Birge

Year: 2008

Abstract: In supply chains, it is common for one party, who normally has easier access to financial market, to finance the other party in various ways (trade credit, loans, etc.). In this talk, we discussed the advantages and limitations of supply chain financing, as well as associated problems, including incentives and credit risks, based on a model where firms make operational and financing decisions jointly.
