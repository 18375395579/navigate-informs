Title: The Effect of Modes of Acquisition and Retention Strategies on Customer Profitability

Author: Erin Steffes, Assistant Professor, Towson University, 8000 York Road, Towson, MD, 21252, United States of America, esteffes@towson.edu

Coauthor(s): B.P.S. Murthi, Ram Rao, Andrei Strijnev

Year: 2008

Abstract: We investigate how acquisition and retention strategies differ in their ability to attract and keep profitable customers in the credit card industry. Among the acquisition modes, Internet and direct mail generate more profitable customers than telemarketing and direct selling. We find that customers with loyalty cards and/or affinity cards are less profitable. Our work adds to the CRM literature and our results have managerial implications for marketing resource allocation.
