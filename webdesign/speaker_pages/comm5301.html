Title: Comparison of (s,S) and (R,T) Policies in Supply Chains with Fixed Costs and Information Sharing

Author: Jin Kyung Kwak, Ph.D Student, Cornell University, 401 M Sage Hall, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States, jkk37@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Srinagesh Gavirneni

Year: 2007

Abstract: We investigate how the retailer's inventory policy affects the total cost of a two-stage supply chain with a fixed cost at the retailer. When the retailer uses the locally optimal (s,S) policy, there is randomness in both order time and order quantity to the supplier. When the retailer uses the non-optimal (R,T) policy there is randomness only in order quantity. When the supplier has knowledge of the end-customer demand information, we hypothesize that the (R,T) policy is more cost effective.
