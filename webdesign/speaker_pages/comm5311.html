Title: An Empirical Study of Inventory Policy Choice and Inventory Level Decisions
Author: Justin Ren, Boston University School of Management, 595 Commonwealth Ave, Boston, MA, United States, ren@bu.edu
Coauthor(s): Sean Willems, Gokhan Usanmaz
Year: 2007
Abstract: This paper empirically investigates how managers set and maintain their inventory levels using field data collected from a large nation-wide dealer network. We find that even though dealers receive inventory stocking recommendations from the manufacturer, a multitude of economic and organizational factors jointly determine dealers?actual inventory policy choice and inventory levels. Those factors include demand pattern, holding cost, competition, management capability and infrastructure.
