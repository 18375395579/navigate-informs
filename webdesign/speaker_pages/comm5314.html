Title: The Impact of Supply Chain Structure on Responsible Sourcing

Author: Ruixue Guo, Doctoral Student, Stanford University, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, rguo@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Hau L. Lee, Robert Swinney

Year: 2013

Abstract: We analyze the sourcing decisions of firms that may choose between two types of suppliers: responsible suppliers that are costly but follow strict social and environmental responsibility standards, and normal suppliers that are less costly but may randomly experience responsibility violations. We explore how three structural elements of the supply chain influence the firm’s optimal sourcing decision: downstream competition, concentration of the supplier base, and supply chain flexibility.
