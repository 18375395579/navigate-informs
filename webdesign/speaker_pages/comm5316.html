Title: Warranty Pricing with Product Failures and Forward Looking Consumers: An Empirical Approach
Author: Jingqi Wang, Northwestern University, 2001 Sheridan Rd, Evanston, IL, 60611, United States of America, jingqi-wang@kellogg.northwestern.edu
Coauthor(s): Ozge Islegen, Baris Ata
Year: 2013
Abstract: We study warranty pricing for durable goods, considering product failures. Consumers solve a dynamic program to make purchases. We introduce the inclusive value processes to reduce the dimension of the estimation problem. We estimate consumers?preferences using the model and individual level transaction data. By conducting counterfactual experiments, we find that warranties are underpriced. Lastly, we explore the impact of changing product reliability on the retailer’s profit.
