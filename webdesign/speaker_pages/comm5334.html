Title: Channel Coordination and Incentives for Variety
Author: Garrett van Ryzin, Columbia University, Uris Hall, New York, NY, 10027, United States, gjv1@columbia.edu
Coauthor(s): Wei Ke
Year: 2006
Abstract: We look at a simple stylized model of multiple manufactures who sell differentiated products through a single retailer. Both pricing and product positioning decisions are modeled. We look at how various trading relationships affect the incentives for creating appropriate product variety and the resulting impact on channel coordination.
