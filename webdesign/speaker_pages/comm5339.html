Title: On the Strategic Effects of Requiring Minimum Purchases to Trigger Commissions
Author: Guillermo Gallego, Professor and Chairman, Columbia University, 326 Mudd Bldg, New York, NY, 10026, United States of America, ggallego@ieor.columbia.edu
Coauthor(s): Masoud Talebian
Year: 2008
Abstract: Consider a game between two suppliers with fixed capacity and a broker facing consumer demand. With fixed commission margins, providers have incentives to impose minimum purchase quantities. If providers also compete with commission margins the NE results in lower profits for the smaller provider and larger profits for the broker.
