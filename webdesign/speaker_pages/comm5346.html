Title: Quality Investment, Pricing, and Inspection Decisions in Supply Chains

Author: Murat Erkoc, Assistant Professor, University of Miami, College of Engineering, P.O. Box 248294, Coral Gables, FL, 33124, United States of America, merkoc@miami.edu

Coauthor(s): Haresh Gurnani, Mingzhou Jin

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider a decentralized supply chain where market demand depends on the supplier’s quality investment and the buyer’s inspection policies. The supplier chooses the quality level and the wholesale price, whereas the buyer sets the inspection policy and the resale price. Higher quality and inspection do not only enhance demand through improved consumer perception but also reduce external and internal failure costs caused by defective products.
