Title: Capacity Investment by Competitive Start-ups

Author: Robert Swinney, Graduate School of Business, Stanford University, 518 Memorial Way, Stanford, CA, 94305-5015, United States of America, swinney@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Gerard Cachon, Serguei Netessine

Year: 2008

Abstract: We analyze duopolistic capacity investment games in which firms may be either start-ups (subject to bankruptcy risk) or established firms (with no chance of bankruptcy). Firms build capacity in advance for a market facing a stochastic multiplicative demand shock. We consider how the presence of one or more start-ups affects total equilibrium capacity investment and price. We also endogenize the order of commitment to enter the market and address how start-ups affect market entry.
