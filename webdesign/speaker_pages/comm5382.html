Title: Allocation Policies based on Demand Aggregation in an Assemble-to-order System

Author: Yulan Wang, University of Michigan - Dearborn, 19000 Hubbard Drive, Dearborn, MI, United States of America, yulanw@umd.umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Fernando Bernstein, Gregory DeCroix

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider an ATO system in which multiple products are assembled from a common component and a set of product-dedicated components. Component capacities are chosen prior to the selling season, and the common component is allocated to the products based on observed demands. We propose a collection of allocation mechanisms involving varying degrees of demand aggregation and explore the impact of the allocation scheme and customer impatience on sales, proﬁts, and capacity decisions.
