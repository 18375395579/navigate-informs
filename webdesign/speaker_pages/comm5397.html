Title: Agricultural Supply Chain Planning under Yield Uncertainty

Author: Nur Cavdaroglu, post-doctoral researcher, Whitman School of Management Syracuse University, Whitman School of Management Syracuse Un, Syracuse, NY, 13244, United States of America, ncavdaro@syr.edu

Coauthor(s): Burak Kazaz, Scott Webster

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider the production planning problem in an agricultural supply chain with a single retailer and multiple farmers. The quality level of the product is directly proportional with the improvement effort (e.g. fertilization) during the cultivation phase and the price of the end-product changes accordingly with farmers' efforts. We analyze the problem and develop pricing mechanisms that coordinate the system and induce farmers' to invest in the same level of effort with the centralized system.
