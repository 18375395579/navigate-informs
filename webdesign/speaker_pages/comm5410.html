Title: Reverse Supply Chain Design for Multiple Channel Product Returns

Author: Aejaz Khan, University of Houston, Decision and Information Sciences, Bauer College of Business, Houston, TX, United States, amkhan4@mail.uh.edu

Coauthor(s): V. Daniel R. Guide, Jr.

Year: 2007

Abstract: Consider a manufacturer receiving returns of a product that may be sold both at a retail location and over the internet. Internet product returns could be returned either at the retail location or shipped to the retailer's distribution center. In this paper, we study three reverse supply chain designs and determine the major drivers that influence the choice of each design.
