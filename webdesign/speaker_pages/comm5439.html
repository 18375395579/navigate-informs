Title: Correcting the Externalities of Price Protection

Author: Karthik Sourirajan, IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, ksourira@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Roman Kapuscinski, Pu Huang, Markus Ettl

Year: 2007

Abstract: In high-tech industries where technological obsolescence is high, price protection has become a standard element of contracts between manufacturers and distributors. While price protection was intended to give distributors sufficient incentives to stock enough inventory, empirical evidence suggests that price protection often leads to overstocking. We analyze acceptable mechanisms to reduce excess inventory caused by price protection.
