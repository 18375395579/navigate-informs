Title: Pricing Differentiated Services in Cloud Computing Market

Author: Linna Du, PhD Student, University of Connecticut, School of Business, 2100 Hillside Road Unit 1041, Storrs, CT, 06269, United States of America, linna.du@business.uconn.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: The cloud computing market is featured as a large scale market for a non-storable commodity with instantaneously released capacity, differentiated services, stochastic demands and risk-averse vendor. We employ queueing network analysis to capture the system unique features and long run behavior. Our model helps business managers develop actionable pricing strategies for increasing the revenue rate, controlling the user’s service adoption and hedging against variability in realized services.
