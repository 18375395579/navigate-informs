Title: The Impacts of Inventory Sharing on Commodity Procurement

Author: Seung-Jae Park, Universityof Texas, Austin, 2110 Speedway Stop B6500, Austin, TX, 78712, United States of America, Seung-Jae.Park@phd.mccombs.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Guoming Lai, Sridhar Seshari

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider two firms that use a common commodity input to satisfy stochastic demands in a multi-period setting. The firms procure the commodity as well as sell excess inventory through either the spot or the forward market. The firms also share the commodity. We characterize the firms?equilibrium procurement policies. We show inventory sharing is always beneficial. We numerically find that the option to share inventory may either increase or decrease the firms?optimal inventory levels.
