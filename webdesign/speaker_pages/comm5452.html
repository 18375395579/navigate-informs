Title: The Roles of Bank and Trade Credits: Theoretical Analysis and Empirical Evidence
Author: Xiangfeng Chen, Associate Professor, Fudan University, School of Management, Shanghai, China, chenxf@fudan.edu.cn
Coauthor(s): Zhiguo Xiao, Gangshu Cai
Year: 2013
Abstract: This paper investigates the roles of bank and trade credits in a supply chain with a capital constrained retailer facing demand uncertainty. We evaluate the retailer's optimal order quantity and the creditors' optimal credit limits and interest rates in two scenarios. We further analytically prove that the relationship between bank and trade credits, and then empirically validate this prediction based on a panel of 674 firms in China over the period 2001--2007.
