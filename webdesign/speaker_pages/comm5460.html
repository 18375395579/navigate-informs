Title: Dynamic Revenue Management in Airline Alliances

Author: Christopher Wright, PhD Candidate, William E. Simon Graduate School of Business Administration, Carol Simon Hall 4-341, University Of Rochester, Rochester, NY, 14627, United States, wrightc1@simon.rochester.edu

Coauthor(s): Harry Groenevelt, Robert Shumsky

Year: 2007

Abstract: Currently, most airline alliances use static contracts for revenue-sharing. We propose and analyze several dynamic contracts that adjust sharing proportions based on the changing state of the system. Framing a two-airline alliance as a finite-horizon Markov game, we find the equilibrium policies for each contract. We compare revenues under these policies with those under static proration contracts and with those of a centralized controller, providing insight into when each performs well.
