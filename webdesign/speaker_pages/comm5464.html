Title: Demand Allocation Effects of Product-line Pricing with Advance Selling

Author: Mingyu (Max) Joo, Assistant Professor, The Ohio State University, 2100 Neil Ave., Columbus, OH, 43210, United States of America, joo.85@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ken Wilbur, Dinesh Gauri

Year: 2013

Abstract: Firms use product line pricing to manage demand not only across products but also across time periods. To understand the intertemporal effects, this paper estimates a nested logit model in the cruise industry based on descriptive analyses. The results show that the firm is able to use product line pricing to allocate demand across time periods. A policy experiment shows that intertemporal price variation accounts for 18% of revenues relative to optimal product line pricing alone.
