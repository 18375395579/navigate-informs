Title: Carrier Capacity Procurement and Transportation Cost under Demand Uncertainty

Author: Parijat Dube, IBM, Yorktown Heights, NY, United States of America, pdube@us.ibm.com

Year: 2013

Abstract: Customers engaging in long-term freight transportation contracts with forwarders do not have accurate estimates of demand they will foresee. Demand is often loosely known as expected demand over some aggregate time interval. Forwarders need to account for the uncertainty in freight demand over the contract period when procuring capacity from carriers. We develop models for demand over contract period and use these to study capacity procurement and transportation cost for the forwarders.
