Title: A Structural Analysis of Vehicle Design Responses to Corporate Average Fuel Economy Policy

Author: Ching-Shin Shiau, PhD Student, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Scaife Hall 402 Mechanical Engineering, Pittsburgh, PA, 15217, United States of America, cshiau@cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jeremy Michalek

Year: 2008

Abstract: We conduct a structural analysis of automaker vehicle design and pricing responses to CAFE policy in long-run oligopolistic equilibrium. We find that vehicle fuel economy responses follow a distinct pattern where high CAFE standards are ineffective without correspondingly high penalties for violation. Recent consumer and vehicle data suggest that vehicle decisions are more sensitive to gas prices than CAFE, and the new CAFE standards may be irrelevant if gasoline prices continue to rise.
