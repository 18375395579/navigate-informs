Title: Product Design in a Price-Setting Newsvendor's Decentralized Supply Chain

Author: Qin Geng, Graduate Student, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 350 Wohlers Hall, 1206 S. 6th Street, Champaign, IL, 61820, United States, qingeng@uiuc.edu

Coauthor(s): Nicholas Petruzzi

Year: 2006

Abstract: We explore product design and pricing issues in a supply chain characterized by uncertainty by developing and analyzing a model to investigate the following: How does demand randomness affect the optimal decisions and profits in a decentralized system? How do these decisions and profits compare to the optimal centralized decisions, and how does that comparison depend on demand randomness? How do the answers to the above questions depend on which decision maker designs the product?
