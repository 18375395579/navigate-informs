Title: Capacity Investment under Bankruptcy Risk with Forward-Looking Consumers

Author: Robert Swinney, Associate Professor, Graduate School of Business, Stanford University, 655 Knight Way, Stanford, CA, 94305, United States of America, swinney@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Song Alex Yang, Erica Plambeck

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider the capacity investment decision of a firm that is in financial distress and may enter bankruptcy. Consumers value firm survival and experience a loss in utility if bankruptcy occurs. Thus, forward-looking consumers anticipate the risk of bankruptcy and may delay a purchase to see whether the firm survives. We analyze optimal capacity decisions in this setting and determine the impact of such strategic customer behavior on the firm’s profit, survival probability, and capacity level.
