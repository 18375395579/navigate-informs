Title: How to Manage a Category Captain - Trust and Trustworthiness in Information Sharing, Advice Provision

Author: Upender Subramanian, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas at Dallas, Jindal School of Management, Richardson, TX, 75080, United States of America, upender@utdallas.edu

Coauthor(s): Ozalp Ozer, Yu Wang

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study a retailer who relies on the market knowledge of a manufacturer designated as the category captain? We examine channel performance and firms' profits under three arrangements: information sharing, advice provision and delegation. While standard theory predicts lack of cooperation, through laboratory experiments, we determine why and how each arrangement generates varying levels of cooperation by inducing different levels of trust and trustworthiness.
