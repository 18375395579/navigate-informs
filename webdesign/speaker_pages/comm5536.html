Title: Are Restrictions on Low Fare Customers Beneficial?

Author: Vijay Desai, Ph.D. Candidate, Industrial Engineering and Operations Research Department, Rm. 313, SEAS, Columbia University, 500 West 120th Street, New York, NY, 10027, United States of America, vvd2101@columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Guillermo Gallego

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider the network revenue management problem with MNL as consumer choice behavior model. We assume the consumer's utility function depends on schedule quality and price. By analyzing the DP value function we can show that the revenue is monotonically increasing in schedule quality. This result suggests that imposing artificial restrictions on lower fare customers like 14 day advance purchase, weekend stay are actually detrimental to revenue. We explore other extensions of this model.
