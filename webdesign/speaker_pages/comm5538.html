Title: An Empirical Study of Ordering Behavior of Retail Stores

Coauthor(s): Rob Broekmeulen, Tom van Woensel, Jan Fransoo

Year: 2007

Abstract: We study whether store managers in a supermarket chain deviate from orders generated by an automated replenishment system. Using orders, item characteristics, and POS data for 19,417 skus over 5 stores, we show that store managers systematically advance orders from peak days to preceding non-peak days, resulting in higher inventory but a more balanced handling workload. This behavior is consistent with their incentives, addresses system shortcomings, and predictably varies across items.
