Title: Outsourcing Service Processes to a Common Service Provider under Price and Time Competition

Author: Gad Allon, Assistant Professor, Northwestern University, Kellogg School of Management, 2001 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States, g-allon@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Awi Federgruen

Year: 2006

Abstract: In many industries, firms consider the option of outsourcing a service process associated with the goods or services they bring to the market. Often, competing firms outsource this service process to one or more common service suppliers. When they outsource to a common provider, this gives rise to a service supply chain. We develop analytical models to characterize the benefits and disadvantages of outsourcing in industries in which the retailers compete in terms of prices and waiting time.
