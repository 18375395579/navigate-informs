Title: The Relational Advantages of Intermediation

Author: Elena Belavina, Assistant Professor, University of Chicago, Booth School of Business, 5807 South Woodlawn Avenue, Chicago, IL, 60611, United States of America, Elena.Belavina@chicagobooth.edu

Coauthor(s): Karan Girotra

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper provides a novel explanation for the use of supply chain intermediaries. We find that even in the absence of the well-known transactional and informational advantages of mediation, intermediaries improve supply chain performance. In particular, intermediaries facilitate responsive adaptation of the buyers?supplier base to their changing needs while simultaneously ensuring that suppliers behave as if they had long- term sourcing commitments from buying firms.
