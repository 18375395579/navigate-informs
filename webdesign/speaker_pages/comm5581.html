Title: A Dynamic Network Game for the Adoption of Technologies of Uncertain Value
Author: Matt V. Leduc, Stanford University, Huang Engineering Center, 475 Via Ortega, Stanford, CA, 94305-4121, United States of America, mleduc@stanford.edu
Coauthor(s): Matthew O. Jackson, Ramesh Johari
Year: 2013
Abstract: We propose a class of dynamic network games to study the adoption of technologies of uncertain value. This value can be learned by experimenting or by free-riding on others?experimentation. For an important set of technologies, the often-used policy offering a price discount to early adopters is least efficient. We demonstrate that a referral incentive policy may cause both low and high-degree agents to experiment whereas middle-degree agents free-ride on the information they generate.
