Title: Inventory Replenishment and Demand Allocation Decisions for Multi-Sourced Items

Author: Abhilasha Katariya Prakash, PhD Student, Texas A&M University, 503 Nagle st, College Station, TX, 77840, United States of America, abhilashapk@neo.tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Eylem Tekin, Sila Cetinkaya

Year: 2012

Abstract: We consider a manufacturer using multi-sourced parts from a contractual vendor (CV) and a spot market (SM). The CV holds inventory at a vendor managed hub and charges a fixed unit price under a quantity commitment contract. Inventory procured from the SM with volatile prices is held at an advance purchase hub. The overall problem deals with when and how much to order from each source and how to allocate demand to the alternative hubs.
