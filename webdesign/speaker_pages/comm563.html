Title: Decentralization Cost in Scheduling - A Game Theoretic Approach

Author: Eran Hanany, Assistant Professor, Tel Aviv University, Department of Industrial Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Ramat Aviv, 69978, Israel, hananye@post.tau.ac.il

Coauthor(s): Yossi Bukchin

Year: 2007

Abstract: Decentralized organizations may incur inefficiencies due to scheduling based competition for limited resources. We analyze the `Decentralization Cost', the ratio between the Nash equilibrium cost and the cost attained at the centralized optimum. Solution properties and bounds on the decentralization cost are derived for a dispatching-sequencing model. A scheduling-based coordinating mechanism is then provided, ensuring that the centralized solution is obtained at equilibrium.
