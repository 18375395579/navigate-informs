Title: The Effect of Website Search Tools on Airline Customers' Purchase Behaviors

Author: Susan Hotle, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, United States of America, shotle3@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Laurie Garrow, Matthew Higgins

Year: 2013

Abstract: Website search tools that return prices over multiple departure and return dates help consumers find low fares and can enable customers to shift their travel dates. We hypothesize that these search tools result in demand shifts around advance purchase deadlines. In this study, we use search and purchase data from a single OTA to quantify the level of consumer surplus generated by these website search tools. Results show that there is a significant shift in demand due to the presence of advance purchase deadline. We measure consumer surplus using instrumented variable regressions, which control for endogeneity between supply and demand.
