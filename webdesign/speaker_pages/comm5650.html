Title: When Distressed Retailers Met Smart Customers: The Implication of Liquidation Sales
Author: Xiao Wu, Student, University of Chicago, Booth School of Business, 5807 South Woodlawn Ave, Chicago, IL, 60637, United States of America, xwu6@chicagobooth.edu
Coauthor(s): S. Alex Yang, Rodney Parker, John Birge
Year: 2013
Abstract: In this paper, we investigate a financially distressed retailer's inventory and pricing decisions in the presence of strategic consumers. The retailer has to make three decisions: initial price, initial stocking quantity, and liquidation price. Our results show that understanding the interaction between a firm's financial condition and the consumers it faces is very important. Ignoring this linkage could lead to substantial losses.
