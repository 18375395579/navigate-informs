Title: Quality and Advertising in a Vertically Differentiated Market

Author: Elie Ofek, Associate Professor, Harvard Business School, Boston, MA, 02163, United States, eofek@hbs.edu

Coauthor(s): Zsolt Katona

Year: 2007

Abstract: We examine firms' quality positions when consumers only consider products they are aware of. Under blanket advertising, we identify conditions for one firm to choose a high quality product, but because its rival positions close by, it selects a low ad expenditure to avoid fierce price competition. Under targeted advertising, we identify conditions such that both firms choose high quality products, but advertise to different segments. This can result in a middle pocket of unserved consumers.
