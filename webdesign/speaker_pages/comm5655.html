Title: Contingent Pricing in the Presence of Loss Averse Consumers with Stochastic Reference Points

Author: Sami Najafi-Asadolahi, Rotman School of Management, University of Toronto, Toronto, Canada, Sami.Najafi@rotman.utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Opher Baron, Ming Hu

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study a setting in which over repeated sales horizon a firm sells a product to a market with two types of consumers: regular consumers, who have a given valuation for the product and whose market size is uncertain; and bargain hunters, who have a lower valuation and are abundant. The regular consumers are loss-averse with stochastic reference points that reflect their experiences with historic prices. We show that the firm's optimal policy is a contingent pricing policy in a threshold form.
