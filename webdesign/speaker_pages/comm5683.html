Title: Root Causes of Product Expiration in the Consumer Packaged Good Industry
Author: Arzum Emel Akkas, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 77 Massachusetts Avenue, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, aakkas@mit.edu
Coauthor(s): David Simchi-Levi
Year: 2012
Abstract: Our work investigates the root causes of product expiration at retail stores using archival operations data from a large beverage company. Using binomial regression, we quantify the impact of product configuration, warehouse aging of products, warehouse congestion, inventory policy, and suboptimal shelf arrangements on product expiration observed at retail stores.
