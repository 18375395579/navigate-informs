Title: Drivers and Implications of Direct-store-Delivery in Distribution Channels

Author: Mumin Kurtulus, Assistant Professor, Vanderbilt University, OGSM, 401 21st Ave South, Nashville, TN, 37203, United States of America, mumin.kurtulus@owen.vanderbilt.edu

Coauthor(s): Canan Savaskan

Year: 2013

Abstract: Direct-Store-Delivery (DSD) is a channel arrangement in which a retailer allows manufacturer(s) to execute in-store operations such as product replenishment and merchandising at the retail stores. We investigate the economic drivers and implications of DSD for the retailers and manufacturers. We consider a distribution channel where two competing manufacturers sell their products to the consumers via a shelf-space constrained retailer and characterize the conditions under which DSD emerges.
