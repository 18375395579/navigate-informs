Title: Fleet Standardization and Airline Performance

Author: Martin Dresner, Professor, University of Maryland, R.H. Smith School of Business, College Park, MD, 20742, United States of America, mdresner@rhsmith.umd.edu

Coauthor(s): Li Zou, Chunyan Yu

Year: 2012

Abstract: A standardized fleet helps reduce airline operational costs. However, a standardized fleet may lack flexibility in terms of payload and range given a complex route structure, thus limiting revenue potential. A set of fleet standardization indices for US carriers is developed to examine how standardization affects costs and profitability. Results show that standardization reduces costs but also reduces revenues. The net effect on profitability depends on the cost-revenue tradeoff.
