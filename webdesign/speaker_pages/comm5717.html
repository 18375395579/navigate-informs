Title: Coordinating Product Launch Decisions in Pharmaceutical Industry

Author: Mehmet Sekip Altug, Assistant Professor, George Washington University, 2201 G Street, NW, 415 Floor, Washington, DC, 20052, United States of America, maltug@email.gwu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ozge Sahin

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider a pharmaceutical manufacturer that is planning the timing of a pioneer drug launch in a foreign country which will result in parallel import activity. We find out that the Generalized Nash-Bargaining problem leads to a unique equilibrium price and characterize the optimal launch time in closed form. The manufacturer may find it optimal to launch the product “immediately? delay it or not introduce it at all. We then study the price competition between the firm and local generics.
