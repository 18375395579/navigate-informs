Title: Trade-in Rebates for Price Discrimination or Product Recovery

Author: Vishal Agrawal, PhD Student, Georgia Tech, 800 W Peachtree St, Atlanta, GA, 30308, United States of America, gth767p@mail.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Mark Ferguson, Gilvan Souza

Year: 2008

Abstract: We study the impact of trade-in rebates on the firm's ability to price discriminate when there are remanufactured products. When there is no secondary market for a product, a firm can base the amount of a trade-in rebate on the customer's willingness-to-pay rather than on the condition of the returned unit since the customer lacks a reference. If the firm decides to sell remanufactured units, she loses this ability to perfectly price discriminate. We analytically study this trade-off.
