Title: Optimal Commodity Trading with a Capacitated Storage Asset

Author: Nicola Secomandi, Assistant Professor, Tepper School of Business, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, ns7@andrew.cmu.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: This talk investigates the optimal inventory trading policy with a capacitated storage asset and discusses managerial insights related to (1) the meaning of high-low commodity prices, (2) the benefit of coordinating inventory trading and operational decisions, (3) the shares of the asset value that can be attributed to price uncertainty and deterministic variability (seasonality), and (4) the performance of a policy obtained by sequential reoptimizations of a deterministic model.
