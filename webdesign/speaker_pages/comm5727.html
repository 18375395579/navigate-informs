Title: Coordinating Inventory Control and Pricing Strategies with Concave Ordering Cost
Author: Xin Chen, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, 104 S. Mathews Ave., Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, xinchen@uiuc.edu
Coauthor(s): Yuhan Zhang
Year: 2007
Abstract: We analyze a finite horizon, single product, periodic review model where pricing and production/inventory decisions are made jointly. Demand in each period is additive. Pricing and ordering decisions are made at the beginning of each period and all shortages are backlogged. Ordering cost is a concave function of the amount ordered. We show that under certain conditions that the generalized (s,S) inventory policy maximizing expected profit over the finite horizon.
