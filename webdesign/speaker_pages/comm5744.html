Title: Revenue Management under Randomly Evolving Economic Conditions
Author: Jian Yang, Associate Professor, Rutgers University, 94 Rockafeller Road, Piscataway, NJ, 08854, United States of America, jian.yang@rutgers.edu
Coauthor(s): Yusen Xia
Year: 2012
Abstract: We consider a dynamic pricing model in which the rate of the demand arrival process is dependent on both the price charged by the firm and the state of the world. The worldly state evolves in a Markovian fashion. We establish the value of being better informed on the the worldly state. When reasonable monotonicity conditions are met, we show that better present economic conditions will lead to higher prices.
