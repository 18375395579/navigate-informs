Title: Centralized vs. Decentralized Competition for Price and Lead-time Sensitive Demand

Author: Pelin Pekgun, PhD Candidate, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States, ppekgun@isye.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Pinar Keskinocak, Paul Griffin

Year: 2006

Abstract: We study two firms that compete on the basis of their price and leadtime decisions in a common market. We explore the impact of the decentralization of price and leadtime decisions, as quoted by the marketing and production departments, respectively, comparing three scenarios: (i) Both firms are centralized, (ii) Only one firm is centralized, (iii) Both firms are decentralized.
