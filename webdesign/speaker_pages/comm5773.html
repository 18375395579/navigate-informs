Title: Strategic Customers and Commitments in a Decentralized Supply Chain

Author: Ali Parlakturk, Assistant Professor, University of North Carolina-Chapel Hill, Kenan-Flagler Business School, Chapel Hill, NC, 27599, United States of America, ali_parlakturk@unc.edu

Coauthor(s): Mustafa Onur Kabul

Year: 2012

Abstract: Consider a decentralized supply chain serving forward-looking consumers in two periods. The supplier and the retailer dynamically set the wholesale and retail price to maximize their own profits. The consumers are strategic in deciding whether and when to buy the product. We show while a centralized system always benefits from making price and quantity commitments, this is not true for a firm in a decentralized supply chain due to how the other firm reacts to such commitments.
