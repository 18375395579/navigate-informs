Title: Coordinating Pricing and Supply of Public Interest Goods Using Rebates and Subsidies

Author: Gal Raz, Associate Professor, University of Virginia, Darden Business School, 100 Darden Blvd., Charlottesville, VA, 22903, United States of America, Razg@darden.virginia.edu

Coauthor(s): Anton Ovchinnikov

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper presents a framework for analyzing the impact of demand uncertainty and consumption externality on the government’s ability to coordinate the pricing and supply of public interest goods (such as electric vehicles) through the use of rebates and subsidies. We show that while a joint mechanism (using both subsidies and rebates) coordinates the system (but may often result in negative subsidy - a tax), simplified mechanisms (i.e., rebates or subsidy) do not coordinate the system.
