Title: Valuing Flexibility in Multi-generation New Product Development

Author: Yael Grushka-Cockayne, London Business School, Regent's Park, London, UK, United Kingdom, ygrushka.phd2003@london.edu

Coauthor(s): Janne Kettunen, Bert De Reyck, Zeger Degraeve, Ahti Salo

Year: 2007

Abstract: We value managerial flexibility in a new product development program consisting of multiple product generations, accounting for uncertainties regarding product performance and market requirements, and incorporating cannibalization affects across product generations. We derive managerial insights regarding possible decisions the firm can make throughout the program, in the form of product line abandonment, development enhancement and flexible launching times of each product generation.
