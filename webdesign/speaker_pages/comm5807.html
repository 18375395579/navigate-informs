Title: Pricing Restaurant Reservations

Author: Jaelynn Oh, University of Pennsylvania, The Wharton School, Philadelphia, United States of America, jaelynn@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Xuanming Su

Year: 2012

Abstract: Many restaurants offer reservations to delay sensitive customers and charge a fee for no-shows. Strategic customers make a reservation only when the expected utility of doing so is greater than that of walking in without a reservation. We study the restaurant’s pricing problem when serving both reservation holders and walk-in customers. We characterize equilibrium prices and profits and discuss model implications.
