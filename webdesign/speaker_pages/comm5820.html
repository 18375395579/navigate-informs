Title: Optimizing an NBA Team's Approach to Free Agency using the Knapsack Problem
Author: Sam Kirshner, Queen's School of Business, 143 Union Street, Kingston, Canada, skirshner@business.queensu.ca
Year: 2012
Abstract: This paper models an NBA team’s approach free agency as a knapsack problem. The free agents comprise the items and the team’s cap space corresponds to the size of the knapsack. As free agents are signed, both the talent pool to fill the team’s needs and the money in the market decreases. Thus, the optimal free agency strategy must considers the trade-off of losing a player to a competitor and acquiring the player at a price that preserves as much cap space as possible to sign additional players.
