Title: Dynamic Pricing with Reluctant Customers

Author: Victor Araman, AUB/NYU, 44 West Fourth Street, New York, NY, Lebanon, varaman@stern.nyu.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: Products proliferation and market competition make the consumer more reluctant in buying a new item. We study in this work how retail companies can benefit from available information about reluctant customers to adjust their prices accordingly. We assume a Poisson arrival of customers interested in some item who will spend a positive amount of time before deciding whether to buy or not. We compare such model to that of a generic one where customers are revealed only when they buy the item.
