Title: On the Threshold Purchasing Behavior of Customers Facing Dynamically Priced Perishable Products

Author: Yong-Pin Zhou, University of Washington, Business School, Seattle, WA, United States, yongpin@u.washington.edu

Coauthor(s): Ming Fan, Minho Cho

Year: 2006

Abstract: Dynamic pricing is a standard practice that firms use for revenue management. As price fluctuates with the changes in inventory and time, a strategic customer may postpone a purchase in anticipation of lower prices in the future. We analyze a threshold purchasing policy for the strategic customer, and use numerical studies to study its impact on both the strategic customer’s utility and the firm’s revenue. We find that in most cases the policy can benefit both.
