Title: The Impact of Wholesale Price Commitment and Retail Competition on the Introduction of Store Brands
Author: Ana Groznik, Indiana University, 1309 E 10th St, Bloomington, IN, 47405, United States, agroznik@indiana.edu
Coauthor(s): Seb Heese
Year: 2007
Abstract: Store brands present direct competition to national brands, thus often causing supply chain conflict. We consider the tradeoffs governing store brand introduction decisions of competing retailers, and we investigate how the resulting dynamics are affected by the national brand manufacturer’s (in)ability to credibly commit to a wholesale price.
