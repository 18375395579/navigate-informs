Title: Mitigating Global Supply Chain Risks through Corporate Social Responsibility

Author: Jose Cruz, Associate Professor, University of Connecticut, School of Business, Storrs, CT, 06269, United States of America, jose.cruz@business.uconn.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: We develop a decision model that captures supply side disruption risks, social risks, and demand side uncertainty within an integrated global supply chain and corporate social responsibility (CSR) modeling and analysis framework. We investigate the effects of CSR activities in a global supply chain and compute the equilibrium product flows, prices, and levels of social responsibility activities. The results show that CSR activities can potentially be used to mitigate global supply chain risk.
