Title: Pricing Games and Impact of Private Demand Information in Decentralized Assembly Systems

Author: Basak Kalkanci, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Boston, MA, United States of America, kalkanci@MIT.EDU

Coauthor(s): Feryal Erhun

Year: 2013

Abstract: We reveal new insights on the value of contract type (price-only versus complex), demand information and contract sequence to different players in decentralized assembly systems under asymmetric demand information. We find that individual suppliers do not necessarily benefit from a complex contracting equilibrium and a downstream supplier may prefer information asymmetry, especially under moderate demand variability. Unless there is a high demand risk, the first-mover advantage is prevalent.
