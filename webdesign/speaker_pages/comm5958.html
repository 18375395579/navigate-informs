Title: Responsive Pricing of Fashion Products: The Effects of Demand Learning and Strategic Consumer

Author: Mike Wei, Olin Business School, Washington University, St. Louis, United States of America, weim@wustl.edu

Coauthor(s): Fuqiang Zhang, Yossi Aviv

Year: 2012

Abstract: We provide a stylized Bayesian demand learning model to investigate the value of responsive pricing under strategic consumer behavior. In particular, we find that increasing market uncertainty affects the value of responsive pricing adversely due to the spread effect and the manipulation effect. Furthermore, it can be shown that the price commitment can boost the seller's revenue substantially, and the information can be much more valuable under strategic consumers than under myopic consumers.
