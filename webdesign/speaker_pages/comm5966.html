Title: Contractual Provisions to Mitigate Holdup: Evidence from Information Technology Outsourcing
Author: Anjana Susarla, University of Washington, asusarla@u.washington.edu
Coauthor(s): Ramanath Subramanyam, Prasanna Karhade
Year: 2007
Abstract: Contractual incompleteness combined with the need for relationship-specific investments gives rise to the holdup problem in IT outsourcing. We suggest that extensibility terms in contracts reduce the holdup problem. We also find that extensibility terms are important even when contracts are well designed with respect to the extent of initial contingencies. We also test for ex post efficiency of contract design by examining contract renewals between firms and vendors.
