Title: A Model of Assembly Supply Chain Complexity and its Relationship to Cost

Author: Hui Wang, University of Michigan, 2350 Hayward Street, 2250 GGBL, Ann Arbor, MI, 48109, United States of America, johnwang@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): S. Jack Hu, Goker Aydin

Year: 2008

Abstract: We propose a complexity measure for an assembly supply chain, based on the information entropy. Using two potential applications (choosing the product variety to be offered by the supply chain and choosing between two given supply chain configurations), we study the degree of consistency between decisions made according to the complexity and decisions made according to the cost. The complexity measure is found to be particularly useful when choosing the level of product variety.
