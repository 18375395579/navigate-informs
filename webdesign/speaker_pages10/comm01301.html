Title: DDDAMS-based Surveillance and Crowd Control via UAVs and UGVs

Author: Amirreza M. Khaleghi, PhD Student, University of Arizona, Systems and Industrial Engineering, The University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ, 85721, United States of America, amirreza@email.arizona.edu

Coauthor(s): Dong Xu, Young-Jun Son

Year: 2013

Abstract: A DDDAMS-based planning and control framework is proposed, which 1) devises a best control strategy via crowd detection, tracking, and motion planning in agent-based simulation, 2) controls real UAVs/UGVs via monitoring, data processing, task generation, and execution, and 3) adjusts modeling scales over time adapting to system performance and computations. Experimental results demonstrate system robustness against various configurations, crowd coverage improvement, and computational savings.
