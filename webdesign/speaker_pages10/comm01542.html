Title: Monitoring Progress of Branch-and-Bound Algorithms

Author: Osman Ozaltin, Graduate Student, University of Pittsburgh, 1048 Benedum Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States, ozaltn@yahoo.com

Coauthor(s): Brady Hunsaker

Year: 2006

Abstract: The most common methods to monitor the progress of branch-and-bound algorithms have drawbacks, particularly the fact that they do not improve monotonically. We consider new methods for monitoring the progress of branch-and-bound algorithms using the data extracted from the branch-and-bound tree. Our goal is to provide users with more useful information about the progress of the algorithm as well as the likelihood of it finding better solutions.
