Title: Physics-based Response Oriented Bayesian Empirical Surfaces

Author: Ravishankar Rajagopa, The Ohio State University, 1971 Neil Avenue, Columbus, OH, 43210, United States of America, rajagopalan.18@osu.edu

Coauthor(s): Theodore Allen

Year: 2008

Abstract: Polynomial model forms have been the norm of the researchers working on Response Surface Methodology (RSM) and regression. These model forms are often reasonably accurate within a region of interest. Yet, generally RSM models do not extrapolate accurately outside the experimental design space. Dimensional analysis involves the selection of a relatively small number of dimensionless system inputs and model forms chosen to exploit physics-based knowledge. In this research we propose Physics-based Response Oriented Bayesian Empirical Models (PROBEs) that combines the implied assumptions of both standard RSM and physics-based modeling. We demonstrate benefits using real casting and welding process design datasets.
