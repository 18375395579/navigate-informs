Title: Secondary Structure Prediction in Proteins with a New Dihedral Angle Measure

Author: Jim MacGregor-Smith, Professor, Dept of Mechanical and Industrial Eng., Elab Building, Univ of Massachusetts, 160 Govenor's Drive, Amherst, MA, 01003, United States, jmsmith@ecs.umass.edu

Coauthor(s): Moon Kim, Yunho Jang

Year: 2007

Abstract: Secondary structure prediction in proteins is a necessary prerequisite for tertiary prediction in the protein folding process. Based upon the combinatorial optimization properties of Steiner trees, a new dihedral angle measure is derived to aid in this process. Numerous experimental results with this new angle measure and its probability distribution are presented in order to characterize the alpha-helix and beta-sheet structures of proteins.
