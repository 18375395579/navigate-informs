Title: Learning Risk Measures Through Inverse Optimization

Author: Vishal Gupta, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, ORC, 77 Massachusetts Ave, Cambridge, MA, 02139, United States of America, vgupta1@mit.edu

Coauthor(s): Dimitris Bertsimas

Year: 2013

Abstract: Specifying risk preferences is critical in many applications. Descriptions, like ``conservative'' or ``risky,'' are notoriously uninformative. We take a data-driven perspective. Using inverse optimization, we construct a coherent risk measure from an investor's historical holdings that approximately describes her behavior. We illustrate our approach with a case study of US mutual funds, clustering funds by their fitted risk profiles and contrast to traditional Lipper ratings.
