Title: Broaching Process Monitoring based on Global and Cyclic Signals

Author: Ran Jin, Virginia Tech, Grado Dept of Industrial and Systems, Engineering, 111 Durham Hall, Blacksburg, VA, 24061, United States of America, jran5@vt.edu

Coauthor(s): Wenmeng Tian, Tingting Huang, Jaime Camelio

Year: 2013

Abstract: Broaching is a machining process to create specific shaped slots. Both global and local defects in manufacturing process may exhibit, while those defects are hard to be detected based on product quality or tool conditions in real practice. In this talk, we present a process monitoring framework for global and local defects based on pulling force signals. Both a simulation and a case study of broaching processes are performed to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
