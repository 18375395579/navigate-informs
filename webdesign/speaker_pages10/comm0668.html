Title: Use of Customer Desired Maximum Probability for Bayesian Limit Models

Author: Roy Creasey, Assistant Professor, Longwood University, 201 High Street, Farmville, VA, 23909, United States of America, creaseyrr@longwood.edu

Coauthor(s): Linda Wright, K Preston White, Cheryl Davis

Year: 2008

Abstract: Limit standards are probabilistic interval requirements for proportions. The focus in simulation literature is on determining the confidence interval, which is inappropriate for limit standards. Bayesian methods provide approaches that can be utilized for limit standard models. Instead of the typical methods used to establish the prior distribution, we consider a methodology developed in reliability analysis for defining a priori the maximum desired a posteriori probability of a proportion.
