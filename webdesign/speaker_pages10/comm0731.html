Title: Bayesian Site Selection for Gaussian Process Regression

Author: Arash Pourhabib, Research Assistant, Texas A&M University, TAMU 3131, College Station, TX, 77840, United States of America, arash.pourhabib@tamu.edu

Coauthor(s): Yu Ding, Faming Liang

Year: 2013

Abstract: One major difficulty attributed to Gaussian Process (GP) regression is its computational complexity. We propose a Bayesian Site Selection (BSS) method to approximate GP through utilizing latent variables. The BSS is then solved using reversible jump MCMC methods. The BSS provides accurate prediction while spending reasonable computation time.
