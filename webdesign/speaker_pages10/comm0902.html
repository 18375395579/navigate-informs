Title: Multiple Objective Optimization for Degrading Systems - Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) Pipeline

Author: David Coit, Industrial and Systems Eng, Rutgers University, Piscataway, United States of America, coit@rci.rutgers.edu

Coauthor(s): Sanling Song, Qianmei Feng

Year: 2013

Abstract: LNG pipeline reliability is analyzed based on dependent competing failure processes and multi-objective optimization. LNG pipeline failure can pose significant threat to people and property in vicinity of failure. Some failures are time independent, e.g., mechanical interference by third party, earthquake or overpressure, while other failures are time dependent e.g., corrosion or fatigue failures.
