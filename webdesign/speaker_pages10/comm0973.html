Title: Localization of Myocardial Infarction using Vectorcardiogram Octant Transition Network

Author: Trung Le, Oklahoma State University, 322 Engineering North, Stillwater, Ok, 74075, United States of America, trung.le@okstate.edu

Coauthor(s): Satish Bukkapatnam

Year: 2012

Abstract: Myocardial Infarction is a leading cause of death in the US. Detecting the location of MI in the early stages can significantly improve treatment efficiency and reduce mortality risks. We present an approach that uses the topological features of octant network reconstructed from VCG signals to detect and localize MI. Extensive tests using data of from Physionet indicate that transition features can differentiate healthy, inferior, anterior MI with >92% sensitivity and >93% specificity.
