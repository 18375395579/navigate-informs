Title: Robust Markov Decision Processes

Author: Wolfram Wiesemann, Imperial College, 180 Queen's Gate, London, SW7 2AZ, United Kingdom, ww@imperial.ac.uk

Coauthor(s): Ber?Rustem, Daniel Kuhn

Year: 2013

Abstract: The practical use of Markov decision processes (MDPs) can be affected by their sensitivity to unknown distributional model parameters that have to be estimated by the decision maker. To counter the detrimental effects of estimation errors, we consider robust MDPs that offer probabilistic guarantees in view of the unknown parameters. To this end, we construct a confidence region for the parameters, and we determine policies that attain the best worst-case performance over this confidence region.
