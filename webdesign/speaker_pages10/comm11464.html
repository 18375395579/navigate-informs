Title: A Game-Theoretic Perspective on Dynamic Network Equilibrium

Author: Stephen Boyles, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas, Austin, 1 University Station C1761, Austin, TX, United States of America, sboyles@mail.utexas.edu

Coauthor(s): Christopher Melson

Year: 2012

Abstract: Dynamic network equilibrium is much more complex than static equilibrium. Equilibrium may not be unique, and may not even exist in pure strategies, which is significant for simulation-based dynamic traffic assignment. This presentation discusses several small examples of these issues, and introduces a game-theoretic perspective which may be useful in resolving them.
