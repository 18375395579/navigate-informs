Title: Airport Congestion During Relief Operations

Author: Mike Veatch, Gordon College, Department of Mathematics, 255 Grapevine Rd, Wenham, MA, 01984, United States of America, Mike.Veatch@gordon.edu

Coauthor(s): Jarrod Goentzel

Year: 2012

Abstract: Scheduling the airlift of relief supplies into a damaged or small airport during a crisis is a major challenge. Adapting to the local conditions and coordinating the many international donors and airlift providers is complex. Alternative scheduling methods are proposed and assessed using queueing models with data from the Haiti earthquake.
