Title: q-Ad-hoc Hub Assignment due to Hub Outages

Author: Hyun Kim, Assistant Professor, University of Tennessee, Department of Geography, 1000 Philip Fulmer Way, Knoxville, TN, 37996, United States of America, hkim56@utk.edu

Coauthor(s): Megan Ryerson

Year: 2013

Abstract: If an outage occurs at a hub, transferring goods will be impeded unnecessarily. A recovery strategy could be the utilization of alternative hubs in an ad-hoc manner; this is, in essence, reverse network interdiction. We propose the q-Ad-hoc hub assignment model to re-organize the spatial structure of a disrupted hub network with unaffected hubs serving as ad-hoc replacement hubs and the establishment of alternative routes connecting supply and demand nodes with the ad-hoc ports.
