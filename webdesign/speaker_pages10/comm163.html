Title: Using Composite Variable Modeling to Solve Integrated Freight Transportation Planning Problems

Author: Sarah Root, University of Michigan, 1205 Beal Avenue, Ann Arbor, MI, United States, seroot@umich.edu

Coauthor(s): Amy Cohn

Year: 2006

Abstract: Developing plans for a small package carrier is a complex process, with many interdependent and constrained resources. As a result, the process is often decomposed into sequential steps, which can greatly impact solution quality. We present a modeling approach, solution techniques, and computational results to integrate three of these sequential sub-problems: the assignment of packages to trailer types, the routing and matching of trailers, and the balancing of empties.
