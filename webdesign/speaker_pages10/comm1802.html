Title: Mathematical Models for Transportation Lane Aggregation and Short Haul Lane Pricing

Author: Qi Yuan, Supply Chain Research Analyst, Niagara Bottling, 10312 Sparkling Dr Unit 1, Rancho Cucamonga, CA, 91730, United States of America, freestander@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Xiaoyan Zhu, Ryan Kieczykowski, Bhaskar Tatke

Year: 2013

Abstract: To solve the problem of customers?demand variability in transportation lanes, we developed an innovative model to aggregate the lanes using cluster-based method. We also proposed an improved pricing model to guarantee fair pricing on short haul lanes. As a result, the spot loads are greatly reduced due to decreased variability and carriers are also willing to reserve more consistent capacity for the shipping company, which in turn increases the customer service level.
