Title: Urban Area Crime Modeling: Spatiotemporal History, Opportunity, and Security Resource Allocation

Author: Daniel Salazar, University of Southern California, 3710 McClintock Ave, Los Angeles, CA, 90089-2902, United States of America, danielsalazaraponte@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Samrat Chatterjee

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present a probabilistic graphical model based approach to estimate the risk of robbery around a university campus. Model components include location and time of past incidents, opportunity related to pedestrian density and environmental conditions -like weather and lighting- as well as mobile preventative security resources. Optimization models for allocation of mobile security measures to reduce robbery risk are also discussed.
