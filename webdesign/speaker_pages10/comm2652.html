Title: George Dantzig Crosses the Potomac River, Twice!

Author: Saul Gass, Professor Emeritus, University of Maryland, 8809 Maxwell Drive, Potomac, MD, 20854, United States of America, sgass@rhsmith.umd.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: We recount how General George started his career in 1941 working for the U.S. Air Force’s Combat Analysis Division, Pentagon, during World War II, and his return in 1946 as Chief Mathematician of the Air Force’s Project SCOOP, the birth place of linear programming and the simplex method. George had been drawn into battle by his Pentagon colleagues who challenged him to figure out how the Air Force could mechanize its planning process in a world of desk calculators and IBM accounting equipment.
