Title: Mining Social Media: A Brief Introduction

Author: Pritam Gundecha, Arizona State University, Ira A Fulton Schools Engineering, Tempe, AZ, United States of America, Pritam.Gundecha@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Huan Liu

Year: 2012

Abstract: The pervasive use of social media has generated unprecedented amounts of social data. Social media data is vast, noisy, unstructured, and dynamic in nature, thus novel challenges arise. This chapter reviews the basics of data mining and social media, introduce representative research problems, illustrates the application of data mining to social media using examples, and describes some projects of mining social media for humanitarian assistance and disaster relief for real-world applications.
