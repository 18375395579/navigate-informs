Title: Shared Leadership: The Impact of the Occupy Movement on Decision Making in Organizations

Author: Adina Lav, Doctoral student, George Washington University, 2480 16th Street, NW #445, Washington, DC, 20009, United States of America, adinalav@gwmail.gwu.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Social and political movements around the globe witnessed over the past year are great examples of the changes taking place in how people learn from each other and express their leadership at the societal level. This oral presentation describes the Occupy movement and its organic development of consensus building and shared leadership and calls for organizations to broaden their perspectives of decision making by understanding new ways in which leadership and consensus-building takes place.
