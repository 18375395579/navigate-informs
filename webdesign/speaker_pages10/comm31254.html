Title: A New Lower Bound from Progressive Hedging

Author: Sarah Ryan, Iowa State University, Black Hall, Ames, United States of America, smryan@iastate.edu

Coauthor(s): Dinakar Gade

Year: 2013

Abstract: Nonconvexity and/or a restrictions on computation time may cause the progressive hedging (PH) algorithm to terminate with a feasible solution and a cost upper bound. A tight lower bound can provide reassurance on the quality of the PH solution. We describe a new method to obtain lower bounds using the information prices generated by PH. We show empirically, using these lower bounds, that for large-scale non-convex stochastic unit commitment problems, PH generates very good solutions.
