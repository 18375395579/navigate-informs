Title: Lifting Generalized Intersection Cuts

Author: Egon Balas, Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15206, United States of America, eb17@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Selvaprabu Nadarajah

Year: 2012

Abstract: Generalized or look-ahead intersection cuts are obtained by first generating a collection B of points on the boundary of a lattice-free convex set, then using the reverse polar of B to generate a set of deep cuts. Assuming that these cuts are generated in a subspace, we examine ways of lifting them into the full space.
