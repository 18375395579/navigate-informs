Title: Production Scheduling for Steel Making

Author: Jayant Kalagnanam, Research Staff Member, IBM T.J. Watson Research, 1101 Kitchawan Rd, PO Box 218, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10591, United States of America, jayant@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Andrew Davenport, Fatma Kilinc Karzan

Year: 2007

Abstract: Production scheduling in the hot metal shop has a flow shop flavor but with some variations. The scheduling is done on a rolling horizon and hence the job selection/packing aspect is introduced. In addition, there are proximity constraints on some of the resources that lead to a special structure. Another complicating constraint is the hot metal inventory that has to be maintained between a minimum and maximum level. We outline some formulations and an approach to address this problem.
