Title: A Primal-Dual Active-Set Method for Convex Quadratic Optimization

Author: Frank Curtis, Lehigh University, 200 W Packer Ave, Bethlehem, PA, United States of America, frank.e.curtis@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Zheng Han, Daniel Robinson

Year: 2012

Abstract: We present a primal-dual active-set method for convex quadratic optimization. The method is based on that proposed for bound-constrained problems by Hintermueller, Ito, and Kunisch. However, we have extended the ideas to handle general linear constraints. In contrast to classical active-set methods, the method makes rapid changes in the active set. Computational experiments show that even for large problems (>1000 variables), the method is able to converge in only a few (<100) iterations.
