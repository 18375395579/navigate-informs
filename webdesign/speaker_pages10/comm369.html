Title: Efficient Algorithms for Assessing Contiguity and Holes in Political Districting

Author: Douglas King, PhD Candidate, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Industrial and Enterprise Systems Eng., Urbana, IL, 61801, United States of America, kingdouglasm@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Edward Sewell, Sheldon Jacobson, Wendy Cho

Year: 2012

Abstract: Political districting is an intractable problem with significant ramifications for political representation. This problem can be formulated as a graph partitioning problem; satisfying contiguity constraints can require significant computation as the problem size grows. This talk discusses a scale-invariant algorithm for enforcing contiguity in local search approaches to political districting.
