Title: Cost Function Approximation Methods in Dynamic Programming

Author: Huizhen Yu, Postdoctoral Researcher, Univ. of Helsinki, P.O. Box 68 (Gustaf Hallstromin Katu 2b), University of Helsinki, Helsinki, 00014, Finland, janey.yu@cs.helsinki.fi

Coauthor(s): Dimitri Bertsekas

Year: 2007

Abstract: We overview approximate dynamic programming methods, which are suitable for very large-dimensional problems. The cost function is approximated by simulation and projection on a low-dimensional subspace generated by basis functions. Recent extensions involving Q-learning algorithms for optimal stopping problems, and general linear systems of fixed-point equations will also be discussed.
