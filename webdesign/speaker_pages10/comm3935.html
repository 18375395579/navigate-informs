Title: A Fast Heuristic Method for Global Optimization and MINLP

Author: John Chinneck, Professor, Carleton University, Systems and Computer Engineering, 1125 Colonel By Drive, Ottawa, On, K1S 5B6, Canada, chinneck@sce.carleton.ca

Coauthor(s): Mubashsharul Shafique

Year: 2013

Abstract: Our multi-start heuristic for global nonlinear optimization has these steps: random initial points, rapid movement towards feasibility, clustering the near-feasible points to identify disjoint feasible regions, a simple heuristic for improving the best point in each cluster, and a local optimizer. The method is parallelizable, well-suited to very large models, and fast. It is extended to MINLP by adding spatial and integer branching to return a solution using very few local optimizer launches.
