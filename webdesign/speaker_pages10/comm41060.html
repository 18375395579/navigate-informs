Title: Stochastic Mixed-Integer Programming Approach to Asset and Liability Management

Author: Stephen Stoyan, PhD Student, University of Toronto, 5 King's College Road, Toronto, ON, M5S 3G8, Canada, stoyan@mie.utoronto.ca

Coauthor(s): Roy Kwon

Year: 2008

Abstract: We present the problem of Asset and Liability Management (ALM) where the underlying portfolio contains an index-tracking fund. A general ALM model is defined whose assets meet a terminal financial obligation while upholding a number of important portfolio characteristics. The numerous sources of uncertainty are captured in a Stochastic Mixed-Integer Programming (SMIP) approach, where a Goal Programming technique is used to handle various portfolio objectives.
