Title: Two-level Mixed Integer Programming for Energy Conservation Measure Selection

Author: Bill Champion, Director/Ph.D. Candidate, EMG Corporation, University of Maryland, 222 Schilling Circle Suite 275, Hunt Valley, MD, 21031, United States of America, bchampion@emgcorp.com

Coauthor(s): Steven Gabriel

Year: 2013

Abstract: Many states, local governments and authorities have been issued directives and mandates that require a percentage reduction in energy consumption. Project selection is made difficult given real-world constraints, limited resources to implement savings retrofits, various suppliers in the market and other project financing alternatives. This paper will demonstrate how two-level mixed integer programming is used to optimize the selection of energy conservation measures.
