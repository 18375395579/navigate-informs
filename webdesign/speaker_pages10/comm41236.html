Title: Challenges in Applying Financial Modeling to the Supply Chain

Author: Alan King, IBM Thomas J Watson Research Center, PO Box 218, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10598, United States, kingaj@us.ibm.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: Financial modeling uses information from financial markets to make optimal decisions and to manage risk. Many supply chain problems such as project selection, production inventory management, or transportation and distribution involve significant elements of uncertainty. This talk reviews the fundamentals of financial modeling from a stochastic programming perspective, and discusses the challenges and opportunities of applying these techniques to supply chain problems.
