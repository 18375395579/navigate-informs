Title: Stochastic Policy Modeling of Wind Energy Benefits

Author: Jeremy Hargreaves, Ph.D. Student, The Johns Hopkins University, DoGEE, 313 Ames, 3400 No. Charles St., Baltimore, MD, 21218, United States of America, jjh@jhu.edu

Coauthor(s): Sebastiaan Hers, Benjamin Hobbs

Year: 2007

Abstract: Wind energy displaces fossil fuels and lowers emissions, but increases the volatility of net demand on thermal facilities. To quantify wind’s net benefits for policy analysis purposes, we propose a stochastic optimization model to simulate balancing energy requirements and day-ahead commitments of generators, considering uncertainty in load and generation. Markov chains are used to represent wind, load, and generator availability. An application to the Netherlands energy market is presented.
