Title: Use of U.S. EPA MARKAL Model for Better Decision Making in Renewable Energy Alternatives

Author: Ozge Kaplan, US Environmental Protection Agency, 109 T.W. Alexander Drive, Mail Drop: E305-02, Research Triangle Park, NC, 27711, United States, Kaplan.Ozge@epamail.epa.gov

Year: 2007

Abstract: The goal of this study is to place urban wastes in the context of national energy system while considering technological change. A variety of renewable energy technologies for waste and landfill-gas are included in the U.S. EPA MARKAL model to understand how they compete against technologies using fossil fuels to meet the energy demands. The variation of cost, emissions, technology penetrations in different emission reduction scenarios, and the robustness of the technology choices are analyzed.
