Title: Portfolio Risk Control under Optimization-based Stock Screening: Case of U.S. Market Sectors

Author: Chanaka Edirisinghe, Professor, University of Tennessee, College of Business, Knoxville, TN, 37996, United States of America, chanaka@utk.edu

Coauthor(s): Xin Zhang

Year: 2008

Abstract: Portfolio risk control is closely tied to the quality of selection of securities. This paper first describes a stock screening procedure based on optimization-based fundamental financial analysis of firms. Stock selections are subject to portfolio analysis under variety of risk metrics. Under tightened stock screening procedures, portfolio risks are well-managed even under relaxed risk control methods. Computational results are provided using US stock data of over 800 stocks for 9 major sectors.
