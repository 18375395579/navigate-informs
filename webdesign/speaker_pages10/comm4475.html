Title: Challenges in Security Risk Analysis

Author: Robin Dillon-Merrill, Assistant Professor, Georgetown University, 418 Old North, Washington, DC, 20057, United States, rld9@georgetown.edu

Coauthor(s): Robert Liebe

Year: 2007

Abstract: Security decisions must be based on threat scenario likelihoods, facility vulnerabilities, and the impacts of a successful attack in terms of mission loss, personnel loss, and economic loss. While the need for a threat x likelihood x consequence approach is well documented, implementing such a process is not simple. This presentation discusses several approaches to risk-based decision making as well as organizational consequences and challenges encountered implementing the processes.
