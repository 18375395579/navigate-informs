Title: A Framework for Managing Risk in Supply Chains

Author: Joseph Mazzola, Professor, Georgetown University, McDonough School of Business, 37th and O Streets, NW, Washington, DC, 20057, United States, mazzolaj@georgetown.edu

Coauthor(s): Robin Dillon-Merrill

Year: 2007

Abstract: Risks arising from natural and human-induced disruptions in supply chains must be managed effectively to ensure continuity of operations and continued competitiveness. We present a risk-management model based on an expanded supply-chain framework and discuss implications for the allocation of resources to more effectively manage these risks.
