Title: Lifetime Portfolio/Spending Decisions with Immediate Life Annuities

Author: Sam Bodily, Professor, Darden School, University of Virginia, 100 Darden Boulevard, Charlottesville, VA, 22901, United States, bodilys@virginia.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Investment and spending decisions in later life are optimized using a simulation model of uncertainties in longevity, inflation, and returns of various stock and bond categories. The role of immediate lifetime annuities is highlighted, and the best time to annuitize is studied, along with the effects of consumption risk tolerance, bequest motive, and consumption stability preferences.
