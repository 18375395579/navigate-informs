Title: Critical Regulatory Changes Made and Rejected that Supported Containerization of Ocean Intermodal Traffic

Author: Bertram E. Rifas, Director of Operations Research for Pacific Intermountain Express Company, Oakland, CA, bertramrifas@rcfn.com

Year: 2007

Abstract: It is an irony of political philosophy that the most highly regulated industry in the United States was the source for a worldwide technical revolution in the industry. That industry is the domestic maritime trade and the domestic overland rail and highway transportation trade. Even the intermodal ports were highly regulated. The most persistent constraints involved the eight foot wide vehicle Operations research in overland transportation helped reduce constraints among the states, cities, and national regulatory agencies. Examples are given.
