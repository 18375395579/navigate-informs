Title: On the Appropriate Objective Function for Post-Disaster Humanitarian Logistics Models

Author: Felipe Aros-Vera, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, Troy, NY, United States of America, arosvm@rpi.edu

Coauthor(s): Jose Holguin-Veras, Miguel Jaller, Johanna Amaya

Year: 2013

Abstract: The paper discusses the incorporation of welfare economic principles in post-disaster humanitarian logistic models to ensure that delivery strategies lead to the greatest good for the greatest number of people. This quest’s central goal is to take into account the human suffering associated with the lack of access to a good or service, which is referred as deprivation cost. The sum of logistic and deprivation costs represent the objective function of post-disaster humanitarian logistic models.
