Title: How do Consumers Value the Duration of Retailer Return Policies? Results From a Conjoint Study

Author: Prashant Yadav, Professor of Supply Chain Management, MIT-Zaragoza International Logistics Program, Gomez Laguna 25, Zaragoza, AR, 50009, Spain, pyadav@zlc.edu.es

Coauthor(s): Clara Agustin, Sweta Thota

Year: 2008

Abstract: There is little evidence in literature on how consumers value the duration of a retailer’s return policy. We conducted a conjoint study to estimate how the consumers?valuation of a return policy changes with its duration. In addition to estimating relative importance of return policies in the choice model, we also analyzed how product price and search cost influence this relative importance.
