Title: The Assortment Packing Problem: Multiperiod Assortment Planning for Short-Lived Products

Author: Victor Martinez de Albeniz, Associate Professor, IESE Business School, Av. Pearson 21, Barcelona, 08034, Spain, valbeniz@iese.edu

Coauthor(s): Felipe Caro, Paat Rusmevichientong

Year: 2012

Abstract: Retailers introduce new items to refresh product lines and maintain their market share. We study how a firm must decide, in advance, the release date of each product in a given collection over a selling season. Our formulation models the trade-offs among profit margins, preference weights, and limited life cycles. A key aspect of the problem is that each product is short-lived in the sense that, once introduced, its attractiveness lasts only a few periods and vanishes over time.
