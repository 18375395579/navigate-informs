Title: Reliability or Inventory? Contracting Strategies for After-sales Product

Author: Sang-Hyun Kim, Doctoral Student, The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut St. Suite 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States, shkim@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Morris Cohen, Serguei Netessine

Year: 2007

Abstract: In traditional sourcing arrangements for after-sales support of capital-intensive products, customers' payments to service providers were based on physical resources. In recent years, we have witnessed the emergence of a new service contracting strategy called Performance Based Logistics (PBL). Under PBL, the basis of supplier compensation is actual uptime of the product. We investigate how reliability and inventory decisions are made under the traditional and the PBL contracts.
