Title: The Implications of Customer Purchasing Behavior and In-store Display Formats

Author: Rui Yin, PhD Candidate, UCLA Anderson School, 110 Westwood Plaza, Suite B512, Los Angeles, CA, 90095, United States, ryin@anderson.ucla.edu

Coauthor(s): Christopher Tang

Year: 2006

Abstract: Consider a retailer announces both the regular price and the post-season clearance price at the beginning of the selling season. Throughout the season, customers arrive in accord with a Poisson process. We analyze the impact of two types of customer purchasing behavior (myopic or strategic) and two common in-store display formats (display all items or display items one by one) on the retailer's optimal expected profit and optimal order quantity.
