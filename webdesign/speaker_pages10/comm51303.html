Title: Channel Product Variety with Consumer Evaluation

Author: Ying Xiao, CKGSB, 1 E Changan Ave, Beijing, China, xiaoying@ckgsb.edu.cn

Coauthor(s): Yunchuan Liu

Year: 2013

Abstract: In this paper, we study the retailer's incentive to manage product variety and the profitabilities of the retailer and manufacturers when consumers do not have full information about the fitting of the brands but can choose to incur a cost to evaluate the products. We show that a manufacturer can benefit when the retailer introduces a competitive brand. We also discuss the motivation of product variety management in a centralized channel compared with a decentralized one.
