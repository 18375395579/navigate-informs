Title: On Measuring Supply Chain Resilience

Author: Nitin Bakshi, Doctoral Student, Univ. of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut Street, Ste 500, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, nbakshi@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Paul Kleindorfer, Howard Kunreuther

Year: 2007

Abstract: We study how characteristics of supply chains and markets in which a firm operates determine the impact of a major natural disaster. These characteristics include disaster preparedness, supplier-switching costs, interconnectedness, competition in the market for the final product, financial health of the firm, and historical trends in sales and profitability.
