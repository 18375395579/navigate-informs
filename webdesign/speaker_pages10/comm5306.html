Title: The Role of Component Commonality in Product Assortment Decisions

Author: Lei Xie, Duke University, One Towerview Drive, Durham, NC, United States of America, lx4@duke.edu

Coauthor(s): Fernando Bernstein, Gurhan Kok

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider a firm that produces multiple variants of a product. Each product is assembled from a common component and a dedicated component. We characterize the optimal assortment and derive the inventory levels for the common and dedicated components. We investigate the effect of commonality on product variety and compare its benefits under different demand characteristics.
