Title: Effectiveness of Synchronized Ordering in Decentralized Distribution Supply Chains

Author: Lucy Gongtao Chen, Department of Decision Sciences, NUS Business School, National University of Singapore, 1 Business Link, BIZ 1 Building #04-29, 117592, Singapore, gc84@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Srinagesh Gavirneni

Year: 2007

Abstract: We study a decentralized distribution supply chain with one supplier and many retailers that face exogenous end-customer demands. For this supply chain, we consider a synchronized ordering policy where all retailers order freely in the same period of an ordering cycle, and receive fixed shipments in the other periods. Using the total supply chain cost per period as our performance measure, we analyze when sychronized ordering outperforms balanced ordering and free ordering
