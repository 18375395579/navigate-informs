Title: Markovian Decision Model for Capacity Adjustment in the Pods of Humanitarian Logistics Operations

Author: Raúl Rodolfo Je Heras Garrido, PhD, Iberoamericana University, Mexico City, Paseo de la Reforma 880 Lomas de Santa F, DF, 01219, Mexico, raul.heras@ibero.mx

Coauthor(s): Marco Antonio Serrato García, Jose Holguin-Veras

Year: 2013

Abstract: The Humanitarian Logistics have challenges in the delivery of critical supplies that are characterized by unexpected fluctuations in demand, the uncertainty in the capacity of the supply chain, and the need to respond immediately. The Markovian Decision Model developed in this study supports the decision and establishes policy decisions for each stage to minimize human suffering through the incorporation of the deprivation costs and the adjustment of the capacity in the points of distribution.
