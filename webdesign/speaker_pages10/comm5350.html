Title: Multichannel Buying; Investigating Channel Choice for Heterogeneous Goods

Author: Richard Briesch, Associate Professor of Marketing, School of Business, Southern Methodist University, Dallas, TX, 75275, United States of America, rbriesch@mail.cox.smu.edu

Coauthor(s): Jaquelyn Thomas, Sandy Jap

Year: 2013

Abstract: Multichannel research has primarily focused on individual consumers purchasing homogenous products across multiple channel formats. This research investigates multichannel purchasing in markets for heterogeneous goods ?the product’s value and the purchase decision are dependent upon its physical condition and the assortment of goods offered. We examine whether it is possible that a channel could offer too much (little) assortment or products that are in too pristine (poor) physical condition?
