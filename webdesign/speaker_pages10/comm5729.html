Title: Optimal Pricing and Rebate Strategies in a Two-Level Supply Chain

Author: Soo-Haeng Cho, UCLA Anderson School of Management, 110 Westwood Plaza, Gold Hall, Suite B512, Box 951481, Los Angeles, CA, 90095, United States, scho@anderson.ucla.edu

Coauthor(s): Kevin McCardle, Christopher Tang

Year: 2007

Abstract: In many product categories, both manufacturers and retailers use rebate promotions. While rebates have been recognized as an effective tool, firms are debating whether to continue the rebate programs due to the high cost associated with rebates. In this paper, we analyze the rebate and pricing decisions of a supply chain comprising a manufacturer and a retailer. We provide insights about how the strategic behavior of both parties affects the conditions under which a firm should offer rebates.
