Title: Airline Product Variation: The Interplay between Perceived Quality and Resource Utilization

Author: Praowpan Tansitpong, Assistant Professor, State University of New York at New Paltz, VH303 1 Hawk Drive, New Paltz, NY, 12561, United States of America, tansitpp@newpaltz.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper explores how operational decisions regarding a quality-differentiated product line determines the overall success of a multiproduct firm in the marketplace. Airlines provide multiple quality products with large variation at difference prices for the first, business, and economy class. We empirically investigate the impact of number of airline products under market constraints, given limited capacity and resources.
