Title: A Model for Estimating Systematic Risk in Supply Chains

Author: Vishal Gaur, Professor of Operations Management, Johnson School, Cornell University, 321 Sage Hall, Ithaca, NY, 14853, United States of America, vg77@cornell.edu

Coauthor(s): Nikolay Osadchiy, Sridhar Seshadri

Year: 2013

Abstract: The demand uncertainty faced by a firm can be caused by idiosyncratic factors or systematic economic factors. We investigate how systematic risk propagates through supply chain networks using industry-level data for the manufacturing, wholesale trade, and retail trade sectors of the U.S. economy. Our results have implications for the relative value of operational and financial hedging strategies used by firms.
