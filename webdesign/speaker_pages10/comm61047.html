Title: Deterministic, Dominant Strategy Auctions for Deliberative Agents: A Characterization and an Impossibility Result

Author: David R.M. Thompson, University of British Columbia, 2366 Main Mall, Vancouver, BC, V6T 1Z4, Canada, daveth@cs.ubc.ca

Coauthor(s): Kevin Leyton-Brown

Year: 2008

Abstract: Can we make deterministic, dominant strategy auctions for agents whose private information is costly to discover? Given independent private values, only a series of take-it-or-leave-it offers will work. But even this mechanism will not have a dominant strategy equilibrium when values are interdependent or agents are capable of learning about each other's preferences. However, both of these restrictions can be overcome by randomizing.
