Title: Dynamic Pricing without Priors

Author: Rene Caldentey, New York University, Stern School of Business, 44 West Fourth Street, New York, United States of America, rcaldent@stern.nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ying Liu, Ilan Lobel

Year: 2013

Abstract: We consider the pricing problem faced by a monopolist selling to a population of consumers over a discrete-time horizon. Customers are heterogeneous along two dimensions: (1) willingness-to-pay and (2) arrival time. The seller has limited information about demand, knowing only the support of customers' valuation. We characterize a robust pricing policy, which minimizes the seller's regret defined as the difference between her payoff under full demand information and her realized payoff.
