Title: Blind Auctions and Predatory Trading

Author: Gerry Tsoukalas, University of Pennsylvania, Wharton School, Philadelphia, PA, United States of America, gtsouk@wharton.upenn.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: We analyze the blind auctions market in which a seller solicits private off-market bids for her portfolio. Fearing predation, the seller often runs the auction blind, in the sense that the underlying assets are only revealed ex-post to the winner. We study the secrecy vs. efficiency trade-off that ensues and characterize the optimal amount of information dissemination to the market. We show specific conditions under which transparency can be optimal, despite predatory behavior.
