Title: Minimax Complexity of Pricing in a Changing Environment

Author: Omar Besbes, Assistant Professor, The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania, 3730 Walnut Street, 545 JMHH, Philadelphia, PA, 19104, United States of America, obesbes@wharton.upenn.edu

Coauthor(s): Assaf Zeevi

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider a pricing problem in an environment where the customers?willingness-to-pay distribution changes at a time that is unknown to the decision-maker. We characterize the best possible profit loss, as measured relative to an oracle, and articulate mathematically the role of dynamic pricing policies in such settings.
