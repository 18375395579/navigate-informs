Title: Competitive Pricing Practices in the US Airline Industry

Author: Laurie Garrow, Assistant Professor, Georgia Institute of Technology, School of Civil and Environmental Engine, 790 Atlantic Dr, Atlanta, GA, 30332-0355, United States of America, laurie.garrow@ce.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Shawn Pope

Year: 2008

Abstract: This study analyzes competitive pricing practices using on-line data collected for 100 US markets for 1 month of departures and 4 week booking horizon. Impacts of multiple market factors on the average lowest nonstop price and price dispersion are examined. Average price and price dispersion are associated with several factors, including time until departure, presence of a low cost carrier, and amount of market concentration. Different pricing practices among carriers are also observed.
