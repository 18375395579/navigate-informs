Title: Payment Rules through Discriminant-based Classifers

Author: Ben Lubin, Professor, Boston University, 595 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, MA, 02215, United States of America, blubin@bu.edu

Coauthor(s): Felix Fischer, Pichayut Jirapinyo, John Lai, David Parkes, Paul Deutting

Year: 2012

Abstract: In mechanism design one typically seeks socially optimal mechanisms constrained by incentive compatibility. By replacing this constraint with the goal of minimizing expected ex post regret, we are able to adapt statistical machine learning techniques to the design of payment rules in settings with multi-dimensional types. Specifically, we train a support vector machine with a special discriminant function, such that it implicitly establishes a payment rule with desirable properties.
