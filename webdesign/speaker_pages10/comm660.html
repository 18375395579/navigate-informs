Title: Terrorism, Game Theory and Motivation

Author: William D. Rowe Sr., Senior Consultant, Project Performance Corporation, 1760 Old Meadow Road, McLean, VA, 22102, United States, wrowe10@comcast.net

Year: 2006

Abstract: Game theory addresses strategic approaches when facing rational opponents. Terrorists cannot be considered as rational opponents from an organized society’s viewpoint. The value functions for the opponents differ and result in a mismatch of measurement scales. Game theory structures provide a means of organizing the “terrorism game? A simple game structure is presented to illustrate the situation. The missing piece is the motivation of the players, requiring a need to map them.
