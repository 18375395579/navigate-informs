Title: The Impact of Environmental Policy on Profit-Driven Vehicle Design Optimization

Author: Jeremy Michalek, Assistant Professor, Carnegie Mellon University, Scaife Hall 323, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, jmichalek@cmu.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: Environmental policy, consumer preferences, and cost structures of current technology set the parameters that determine which vehicle designs are most profitable in a competitive marketplace. This study models the characteristics of optimal vehicle design under a range of policy incentive scenarios and examines the resulting impact on public and private preferences.
