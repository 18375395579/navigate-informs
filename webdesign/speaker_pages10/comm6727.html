Title: Decomposition Approaches for Planning Targeted Online Ad Campaigns

Author: John Turner, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Ave., Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States of America, jgt@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Alan Scheller-Wolf, Sridhar Tayur

Year: 2008

Abstract: Ad campaigns may be targeted toward certain demographic groups or geographic areas. Linear programming may be used to decide what rate to serve each campaign in each inventory type; however, 1) as targeting becomes sophisticated the number of inventory types grows combinatorially; and 2) the number of impressions of each inventory type is unknown ex-ante. We explore some decomposition techniques and provide experimental results applied to the scheduling of ads in online video games.
