Title: Positive Externalities and Optimal Scale

Author: Ramesh Johari, Stanford University, rjohari@stanford.edu

Coauthor(s): Sunil Kumar

Year: 2007

Abstract: We study a system where many identical customers use a single service. Each customer experiences both positive and negative externalities from other customers using the service. We study the ratio of Nash equilibrium welfare to socially optimal welfare, as well as the welfare of a single individual at the Nash equilibrium, as the number of customers in the system varies. Our main result is that both these quantities are simultaneously maximized at a positive optimal scale.
