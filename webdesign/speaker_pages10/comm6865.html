Title: Expectation-based Versus Potential-aware Automated Abstraction in Imperfect Information Games

Author: Andrew Gilpin, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, United States of America, gilpin@cs.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Tuomas Sandholm

Year: 2008

Abstract: Automated abstraction algorithms for sequential imperfect information games are a key component in developing competitive game theory-based agents. Previously, agents whose construction has used automated abstraction have only been compared under confounding effects. We conduct systematic experiments on Rhode Island Hold'em poker, comparing abstraction algorithms against each other, against optimal play, and against each agent's nemesis.
