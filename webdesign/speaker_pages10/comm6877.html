Title: An Empirical Analysis of Price, Quality, and Incumbency in Online Procurement Auctions

Author: Fang (Vivian) Zhong, Rotterdam School of Management, Room T09-22,, Postbox 1738, Rotterdam, Netherlands, FZhong@rsm.nl

Coauthor(s): D.J. Wu, Tunay Tunca

Year: 2007

Abstract: Using online reverse auctions to procure business services is growing in the practice of e-procurement. However, the impact of these auctions on the procurement outcomes is unclear. In this study, we demonstrate empirically that online auctions for business services can achieve sustainable cost reduction, without sacrifcing supplier quality. We also analyze the effect of incumbent status in supplier selection decisions and offer novel insights on the role of incumbency in online auctions.
