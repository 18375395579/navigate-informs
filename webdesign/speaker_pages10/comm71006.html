Title: Finite Time Bounds for Stochastic Approximation Algorithms

Author: Assaf Zeevi, Professor, Columbia Business School, New York, NY, 10027, United States of America, assaf@gsb.columbia.edu

Coauthor(s): Deniz Cicek, Mark Broadie

Year: 2008

Abstract: We consider the Kiefer-Wolfowitz stochastic approximation algorithm for sequentially estimating the point of maximum of an unknown function from noisy observations. We develop a new and simple proof that leads to general bounds on the mean squared error of the algorithm, and show that some assumptions adopted to date are superfluous. The analysis leads to ``speedup'' heuristics as well, improving the transient behavior of such algorithms.
