Title: Inventory Control with Satisfaction-dependent Future Demand

Author: Justin Azadivar, University of California - Berkeley, University of California - Berkeley, Berkeley, CA, United States, jazad@berkeley.edu

Coauthor(s): George Shanthikumar, Max Shen

Year: 2007

Abstract: Inventory control decisions are made based on assumptions and estimates of demand levels. But those decisions also affect demand levels, and a complete inventory control model recognizes the use of control decisions to both meet current demand and modify future demand. In this paper, we look at a simple inventory control model with a demand that responds to satisfaction of previous demand and propose a simple framework to determine the value of these control decisions.
