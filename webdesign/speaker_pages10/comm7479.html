Title: On the Dynamic Control of Matching Queues

Author: Itai Gurvich, Associate Professor, Northwestern University, 2001 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, United States of America, i-gurvich@kellogg.northwestern.edu

Coauthor(s): Amy Ward

Year: 2013

Abstract: We study the optimal control of matching queues with dynamically arriving jobs. The objective is to minimize cumulative holding costs over a finite time horizon. We introduce a multi-dimensional imbalance process that facilitates the construction of a lower bound. Imposing a match-pooling condition, we devise a discrete-review dynamic matching policy that asymptotically, as the arrival rates become large, achieves this imbalance-based lower bound.
