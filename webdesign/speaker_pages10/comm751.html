Title: Distributionally Robust Inventory Control when Demand is a Martingale

Author: Linwei Xin, PhD Student, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332, United States of America, lwxin@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): David A. Goldberg

Year: 2013

Abstract: In multi period inventory models, independence of random demands across different periods is a typical assumption in the literature. In this talk, we consider a distributionally robust inventory model in which the sequence of demands must take the form of a martingale with bounded support. We explicitly compute the optimal policy and draw some interesting conclusions about how much the independence and martingale assumptions can differ in terms of optimal value and policy.
