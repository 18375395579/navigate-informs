Title: Non-collaborative Systems with Setup Costs

Author: Tugce Isik, Georgia Institute of Technology, 765 Ferst Drive NW, Atlanta, GA, 30332-0205, United States of America, tugceisik@gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Sigrún Andradóttir, Hayriye Ayhan

Year: 2012

Abstract: We study a system of two tandem queues with a finite buffer and two flexible servers. The servers are unable to collaborate and constant setup costs are incurred when the servers move between stations. We investigate how the server assignment policy that maximizes the long-run average profit depends on the magnitude of the setup cost. The optimal policy is characterized for different buffer sizes.
