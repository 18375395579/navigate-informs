Title: Applying Service Theory and Work ReDesign in an Academic Internal Medicine Program

Author: Jessica Chen, Professor, UCSD School of Medicine, 9500 Gilman Drive, #0821, La Jolla, Ca, 92010, United States of America, j38chen@ucsd.edu

Coauthor(s): John Fontanesi

Year: 2007

Abstract: Service Theory and work redesign were used to improve services in an academic Internal Medicine program. Following detailed workflow observations using a standardized instrument, registration/intake process's and information flow were redesigned. With no additional expenditures, efficiency (ratio of patient-provider time to all other time in the clinic) improved more then 25%, patient and staff satisfaction by more then 30% and clinical outcomes more then 10%.
