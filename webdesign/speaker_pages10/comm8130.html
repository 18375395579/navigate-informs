Title: Technology-enhanced Care Management Reduces Depression Among Minority Diabetics:Preliminary Findings

Author: Shinyi Wu, Assistant Professor, University of Southern California, 3715 McClintock Avenue, GER 240C, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, shinyiwu@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Jeffrey Guterman, Pey-jiuan Lee, Irene Vidyanti, Caitlin Hawkins, Kathleen Ell, Pai Liu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Preliminary depression trial results are reported for a comparative study among low-income, primary care, minority diabetes patients. Depression care is guided by a collaborative team care protocol. In one study arm, 500 patients also receive automatic telephonic depression symptom assessment over time and their providers automatically tasked per protocol. At 6 months, patients in the technology arm have significantly lower PHQ-9 depression scores and are less likely to have major depression.
