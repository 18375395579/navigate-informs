Title: Inferring the Transmission Tree during an Outbreak Investigation

Author: Alexander Gutfraind, University of Illinois at Chicago, 1603 W. Taylor Street Rm. 834, MC 923, Chicago, Il, 60612, United States of America, agutfrai@uic.edu

Coauthor(s): Jaideep Ray, Richard Chen

Year: 2013

Abstract: During an outbreak of an infectious disease, public health authorities need to construct a transmission tree representing the chain of transmission starting with the index case that introduced the infection. We propose a novel and flexible MIP approach to solving this problem and demonstrate its performance on both synthetic and real instances.
