Title: Incorporating Patient Heterogeneity and Choice into Predictive Models of Health & Economic Outcomes

Author: Maria Mayorga, Clemson University, Raleigh, NC, United States of America, mayorga@clemson.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: The goal is to obtain better predictions of the effectiveness of population-level health interventions by incorporating consumer choice models and accounting for patient heterogeneity in predictive models of health and economic outcomes. An example is presented for colo-rectal cancer screening guidelines. The educational plan includes advising and mentoring activities that work with discovery-oriented projects involving multi-level student teams.
