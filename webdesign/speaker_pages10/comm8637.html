Title: Disbursement of Medical Supplies for Biological Emergencies

Author: Fernando Ordonez, Assistant Professor, University of Southern California, 3715 McClintock Avenue, GER-240, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States, fordon@usc.edu

Coauthor(s): Hongzhong Jia, Maged Dessouky, Zhihong Shen

Year: 2006

Abstract: A response to biological emergencies considers the delivery of federal push packages of medical supplies to meet the surge in demand. We investigate how to efficiently disburse these supplies from a local site to points of dispensing, where the population receives treatment. We separate the problem into a facility location and routing problems that incorporate the uncertainty present in emergencies and show the benefits of this formulation on an example of a mass vaccination in LA County.
