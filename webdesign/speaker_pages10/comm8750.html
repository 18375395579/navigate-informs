Title: Long Term Capacity Planning for a Residential Care Facility

Author: Martin Puterman, Professor, Sauder School of Business UBC, 2053 Main Mall #470, Vancouver, BC, V6T 1Z2, Canada, Martin.Puterman@sauder.ubc.ca

Coauthor(s): Marc Levine

Year: 2008

Abstract: As board members of a residential care facility in Vancouver, we faced the challenge of planning capacity expansion to meet the needs of constituents through 2021. To this end, we developed forecasting and queuing models to investigate the sensitivity of capacity to model inputs. Finding that capacity projections were most sensitive to length of stay (LOS), we used censored data methods to forecast LOS. Results were presented to the board and implementation planning has begun.
