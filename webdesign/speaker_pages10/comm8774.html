Title: Setting Surgical Schedules to Optimize Downstream Resource Utilization

Author: Patrick Jonathan, Professor, University of Ottawa, 55 Laurier Avenue, Ottawa, ON, K2G 3A6, Canada, patrick@telfer.uottawa.ca

Coauthor(s): Davood Astaraky

Year: 2012

Abstract: Determining an appropriate surgical schedule is a tough decision process that requires the administrator to assess competing priority classes and surgical types, variable surgical times and lengths of stay post-surgery. We seek to develop a model that optimally allocates surgeries into pre-determined surgical blocks in order to maximize utilization of operating room time and post-op beds while meeting priority specific wait time targets using an approximate dynamic programming approach.
