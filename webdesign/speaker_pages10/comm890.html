Title: Robust Dynamic Control of Living-Donor Liver Transplant Timing

Author: David Kaufman, Postdoctoral Associate, Industrial Engineering, University of Pittsburgh, 1048 Benedum Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15261, United States, davidlk@engr.pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Andrew Schaefer

Year: 2006

Abstract: Markov decision process (MDP) models for timing of living-donor liver transplants require the estimation of underlying transition probabilities from clinical data. Such estimation is a source of ambiguity that is inherent in many medical decision-making problems. We incorporate this ambiguity in a “robust?MDP formulation of the living-donor problem. We discuss choices of conditional measures based on real data for the resulting “inner problem?and implications for optimal control structures.
