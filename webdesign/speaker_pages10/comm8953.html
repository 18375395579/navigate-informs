Title: Reserving Capacity for Urgent Patients in Primary Care

Author: Gregory Dobson, Simon School of Business, University of Rochester, Rochester, NY, United States of America, dobson@simon.rochester.edu

Coauthor(s): Edieal Pinker

Year: 2008

Abstract: We examine the common practice of reserving slots for urgent patients in a primary care practice. Using a stochastic “dam?model of appointment scheduling, we optimize system performance accounting for revenue and the two service quality measures as a function of the number of reserved slots for urgent patients. Our results suggest that, performance may improve if no slots are reserved for urgent patients.
