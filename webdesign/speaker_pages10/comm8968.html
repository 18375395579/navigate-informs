Title: The Impact of Private Rooms on Hospital-Acquired Infection: A Longitudinal Study

Author: Liam O'Neil, Associate Professor, University of North Texas, Health Sciences Center, 3500 Camp Bowie Blvd, Fort Worth, TX, 76107, United States of America, liam.oneill@unthsc.edu

Coauthor(s): Vikram Tiwari, Anita Tucker

Year: 2013

Abstract: Data were obtained for 1.2 million patients undergoing 11 representative surgical procedures during 2004 - 2010. Adjusted rates of hospital-acquired infections were compared for 400+ hospitals with a high vs. low percentage of private rooms. Hospitals with mostly private rooms had significantly lower rates of hospital-acquired infections. The business case is examined for hospital renovations that include potential savings from reducing medical errors and improving patient satisfaction.
