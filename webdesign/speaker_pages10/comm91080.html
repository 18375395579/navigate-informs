Title: Congruence Gaps and Their Effect on Distributed Software Development Teams

Author: Mary Helander, IBM Research, T.J. Watson Research Center, Yorktown Heights, NY, 10603, United States of America, helandm@us.ibm.com

Coauthor(s): Kate Ehrlich, Giuseppe Valetto, Clay Williams, Stephen Davies

Year: 2008

Abstract: Software projects are frequently distributed across multiple sites. While this practice takes advantage of local expertise and differential cost structures, there is a hidden coordination cost. Modularity and even agile methods have had mixed success in addressing distributed development. We used a variation of organizational congruence that focuses on gaps in communication to examine coordination decisions in distributed development teams.
