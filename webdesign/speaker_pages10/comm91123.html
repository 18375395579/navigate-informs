Title: Quality and Price Competition for Screening Services

Author: Evrim Didem Gunes, Assistant Professor, Koc University, Rumeli Feneri Yolu, Sariyer, Istanbul, 34450, Turkey, egunes@ku.edu.tr

Coauthor(s): Stephen Chick, Luk N. Van Wassenhove

Year: 2006

Abstract: We examine the effect of market organization for screening for early disease detection on the quality and price of that service. Two providers offer screening and treatment services, and decide on the quality and the price of screening service. The equilibrium is characterized. A sensitivity analysis describes the effect of disease characteristics, such as prevalence, progress rate, and the service cost characteristics on the quality and price levels under competition.
