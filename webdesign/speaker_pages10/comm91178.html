Title: A Dynamic Model of Emotions, Attitudes, and IS Security Policy Violations

Author: John D'Arcy, University of Delaware, Newark, DE, United States of America, jdarcy@udel.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Research suggests that emotions play a prominent role in explaining employee deviance. In this study, we explore the dynamic nature of workplace events, emotions, and attitudes in predicting employees?deliberate IS security policy violations. The study is rooted in affective events theory and utilizes an experience-sampling methodology to assess within-individual variation in several of the study variables, including emotions.
