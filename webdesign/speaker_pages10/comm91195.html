Title: The Moderating Roles of Types of Recommender Systems and Products on Recommender System Acceptance

Author: Yen-Yao Wang, Doctoral Student, Michigan State University, North Business College Complex, 632 Bogue St. N270, East Lansing, United States of America, wangyen@bus.msu.edu

Coauthor(s): Anthony Townsend, Andy Luse, Brian Mennecke

Year: 2013

Abstract: This study investigates how consumers assess the quality of two types of recommender systems and also considers hedonic and utilitarian product characteristics on this issue. This study utilized a 2x2 crossover within-subjects experimental design involving a total of 80 participants. The results suggested that the type of recommender system significantly impacted many of relationships on behavioral intention. Surprisingly, the type of product did not impact relationships on behavioral intention.
