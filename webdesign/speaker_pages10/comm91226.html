Title: An Analysis of Injectable Drug Shortages: Manufacturer Incentives and Social Impact

Author: Sang Kim, Associate Professor, Yale University, New Haven, CT, 06520, United States of America, sang.kim@yale.edu

Year: 2013

Abstract: In recent years severe shortages of sterile injectable drugs have persisted in the U.S., creating life-threatening situations for many patients. There are many contributing factors, including Medicare reimbursement policy, high concentration of generics drug manufacturers, and frequent supply disruptions. We set up a stylized model of manufacturers' capacity decisions under supply disruptions that capture many aspects of the current problems and study the social impact of these decisions.
