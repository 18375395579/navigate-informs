Title: On Two Dimensional Product Differentiation and Use of Personalization Technology

Author: Sunil Wattal, Doctoral Candidate, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, swattal@andrew.cmu.edu

Coauthor(s): Rahul Telang, Tridas Mukhopadhyay

Year: 2006

Abstract: In this research, we examine the economics of personalization in the case of information goods. We consider a more general 2 dimensional model of product differentiation where consumers attach different importance to various product attributes. We also examine different market structures based on the locational differentiation. We report the equilibrium in terms of the ‘Quality-Fit?Ratio, which measures the relative strength of consumer preferences on each dimension of product differentiation.
