Title: Competitive Pricing in Service Systems Market

Author: Wooseong Kang, Assistant Professor, North Carolina State University, College of Management, CB# 7229, Raleigh, NC, 27695, United States of America, wkang@ncsu.edu

Year: 2008

Abstract: The purpose of this study is to examine how firms in a service systems market can maximize profits by coordinating their decisions on multiple service offerings. The service offerings are typically a combination of physical products, professional service components, and after-sale maintenance/upgrade services. We provide a framework and an analytic model to investigate both initial installation and on-going maintenance pricing decisions in a competitive environment.
