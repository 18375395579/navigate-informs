Title: Identity and Opinion: A Randomized Experiment

Author: Sean Taylor, New York University, 44 West 4th Street, New York, NY, United States of America, staylor@stern.nyu.edu

Coauthor(s): Sinan Aral, Lev Muchnik

Year: 2013

Abstract: Social media content such as tweets and comments are usually displayed with the identity of the user who creates them. The associated social context links content quality and creator identity, making it difficult to separate their effects on content interactions such as rating and discourse. We conducted a randomized experiment on a news discussion website, randomly omitting commenter identity for 5% of comments and allowing us to attribute interactions to identity features only.
