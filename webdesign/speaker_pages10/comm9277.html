Title: International Humanitarian Relief Logistics

Author: John Vande Vate, Professor & EMIL Executive Director, Georgia Institute of Technology, ISyE, 765 Ferst Drive, Atlanta, GA, United States, John.VandeVate@isye.gatech.edu

Coauthor(s): Marco Gutierrez

Year: 2006

Abstract: Humanitarian organizations employ 4 strategies in delivering relief: 1. Instantaneous: established presence in the region 2. Fast: Pre-positioned supplies sped to the victims 3. Framework: Agreements with suppliers to deliver provisions to regional response sites 4. Capacity Building: Build long-term capacity within the community We discuss roles the OR community can play in strengthening and aligning these strategies for greater effectiveness.
