Title: Ambiguous Problem Complexity, Group Synergy and Performance: An Experiment

Author: Elliot Bendoly, Associate Professor, Emory University, 1300 Clifton Road, Atlanta, GA, 30322, United States of America, elliot_bendoly@bus.emory.edu

Coauthor(s): Stylianos Kavadias, Svenja Sommer

Year: 2012

Abstract: Kavadias and Sommer (2009) describe a normative model predicting strong collaborative group problem solving in relatively simple settings, but weakness in increasingly complex ones. We revisit the issue through a computer-based experiment. The value of the normative model is strengthened through the consideration of member diversity, focus, control and accountability.
