Title: Designing Government Feed-In-Tariff Policies to Promote Green Technologies

Author: Saed Alizamir, PhD Student, Duke University, 100 Fuqua Drive, Durham, NC, United States of America, saed.alizamir@duke.edu

Coauthor(s): Francis de Vericourt, Peng Sun

Year: 2012

Abstract: Incentive policies such as Feed-In-Tariff (FIT) are used by governments to promote green technologies toward market integration. In this paper, we model the widespread of a new green technology and the contributing factors such as learning curve and diffusion process, using a dynamic program. We study the optimal design of FIT policies and gain insights into how the government should dynamically adjust the prices or tariff levels as the technology grows.
