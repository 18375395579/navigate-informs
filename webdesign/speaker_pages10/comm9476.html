Title: Targeting in Online Advertising Markets

Author: Nabita Penmetsa, Doctoral Candidate, University of Pittsburgh, Joseph M.Katz Graduate School of Busines, Mervis Hall, Pittsburgh, PA, 15260, United States of America, Npenmetsa@katz.pitt.edu

Coauthor(s): Esther Gal-Or

Year: 2013

Abstract: Internet platforms are collecting huge amount of data about customers, using various tracking technologies. This information can improve the effectiveness of online advertising and attract advertisers to the platform. However, consumer privacy concerns can limit the level of targeting that can be used by platforms. In the presence of this trade-off, we analyze the targeting and pricing decisions of competing platforms in a two-sided market model with heterogeneity on both sides of the platform.
