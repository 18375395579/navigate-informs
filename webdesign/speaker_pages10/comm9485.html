Title: Social Networks: Theory and Applications

Author: Yong Tan, University of Washington, Business School, Box 353200, Seattle, WA, 98195-3200, United States of America, ytan@u.washington.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: A social network is a map of formal/informal relationships among people, organizations, or other entities. It plays an important role in information dissemination and knowledge sharing, and is becoming increasingly popular in business research. This tutorial aims to cover social networks theory from the perspective of economics, sociology, and physics. Empirical methods and models will be introduced. Related work in the areas of Operations Management, Information Systems, Management, Marketing, and Finance will be discussed.
