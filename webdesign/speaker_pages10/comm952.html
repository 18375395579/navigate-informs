Title: Sell a Pig in a Poke or Let the Cat out of the Bag: Developing, Signaling, Learning and Screening Qu

Author: Shouqiang Wang, Assistant Professor, Clemson University, 131D Sirrine Hall, Clemson, SC, 29634, United States of America, SHOUQIW@clemson.edu

Coauthor(s): Gulru Ozkan

Year: 2013

Abstract: This paper considers a firm that develops and sells a new experience product, whose quality may not be observable by the buyer with uncertain perceived quality, and evaluates the quality, pricing and timing strategy of the seller that offers a free time-locked product trial. We synthetically study three informational roles of such a trial program in communicating the product quality: signaling, learning and screening; and their implications on the seller's new product development strategy.
