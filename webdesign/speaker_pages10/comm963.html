Title: How Does Problem Representation Influence Decision Performance and Attitudes?

Author: Michael Johnson, Associate Professor, Department of Public Policy and Public Affairs, John W. McCormack Graduate School of Policy Studies, University of Massachusetts Boston, 100 Morrissey Blvd, Boston, MA, 02125-3393, United States, michael.johnson@umb.edu

Coauthor(s): Jennifer Mankoff, Susan Fussell, Deanna Matthews

Year: 2007

Abstract: Current research on individual decision support to reduce negative environmental impacts relies on users to evaluate multiple suggestions for daily behavioral change. In this paper we examine the impact of length and level of information content of the suggestions on quality of decisions and satisfaction with the decision process. Experimental results provide specific guidance for decision support system development, especially for routine decisions performed quickly and often.
