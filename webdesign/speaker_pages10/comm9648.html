Title: Conversion and Churn in B2C, Ecommerce: An Analysis of Observed Behavior

Author: Mark Cotteleer, Assistant Professor, Marquette University, PO Box 1881, Milwaukee, WI, 53132, United States of America, mark.cotteleer@marquette.edu

Year: 2007

Abstract: Given a dependence on subscription and advertising revenue, B2C providers must understand factors associated with recruiting and retaining subscribers. This challenge is intensified by high costs of customer acquisition. We draw on observed customer behavior from a major content site to explain factors related to conversion and retention of subscribers, and present analyses of factors that contribute to customer propensity to convert from Trial to Subscription status.
