Title: Mining Product Discussion Forums for Market Structure

Author: Ronen Feldman, School Of Business Administration, Hebrew University, Jerusalem, ronenf@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Jacob Goldenberg, Oded Netzer, Lyle Ungar

Year: 2007

Abstract: Product discussion forums are a potential rich source of information about how users view different products. Researchers and practitioners are starting to extract user sentiment about products from user product reviews. Users often compare different products, stating which they like better and why. We present a case study in extracting information about comparisons between cars, and running shoes, describe an effective methodology, and show how it produces insight into how consumers view the running shoe market and the cars market.
