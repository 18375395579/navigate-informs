Title: Exploration of Parole as a Transient State Between Prison and Liberty in the Community

Author: Al Blumstein, University Professor and J. Erik Jonsson Professor of Urban Systems and Operations Research, Heinz School of Public Policy and Management, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213, United States, ab0q@andrew.cmu.edu

Year: 2006

Abstract: We examine cross-state variation in the parole process, whereby California is seen to churn their offenders considerably more than others, but their recommitments for new crimes in not anomalous. This study also involves extension of an earlier study of the factors contributing to the dramatic growth of the U.S. incarceration rate.
