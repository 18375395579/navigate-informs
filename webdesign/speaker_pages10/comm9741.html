Title: The Mediating Role of Electronic Word-of-Mouth between Firms?Strategic Actions and Performance

Author: Ling Xue, University of North Carolina at Greensboro, Greensboro, NC, 27402, United States of America, l_xue@uncg.edu

Coauthor(s): Cheng Zhang, Zhe Qu, Peijian Song

Year: 2013

Abstract: We address two research questions: (1) how do customers respond to firms?strategies by their e-WOM behavior and (2) how does customers?e-WOM behavior channel the impact of firms?strategic actions on their performance. Our empirical findings show that e-WOM generates an important chain effect between firms?strategic actions and their performance, and that the effect of firms?strategic actions on e-WOM is moderated by firms?online popularity.
