Title: Online Communities and Sustainability: An Empirical Investigation

Author: Dobin Yim, University of Maryland, 3300 Van Munching Hall, College Park, MD, United States of America, dyim@rhsmith.umd.edu

Year: 2012

Abstract: Fostering consumer-driven energy conservation efforts has sprouted “green?online communities. We examine how online social networks facilitate environmental sustainability through the visibility of sustainable behaviors and group-induced norm. We show that slower growing groups perform better in energy reduction, and the strength of ties and group characteristics that individuals belong to are important predictors of prosocial behaviors.
