Title: Earning Management and Operations Response

Author: Song (Alex) Yang, PhD Student, The University of Chicago Graduate School of Business, 5807 S Woodlawn Ave, Chicago, IL, 60637, United States of America, syang1@chicagogsb.edu

Coauthor(s): John Birge

Year: 2008

Abstract: In accounting literature, there is substantial evidence that managers manage earnings by manipulating real operations activities. While it is questionable if it is the ``right thing to do, it unveils some manager's incentive under the current market equilibrium. In this talk, we will investigate how optimal operational decisions should be made under those incentives.
