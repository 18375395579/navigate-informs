Title: Dynamic Managerial Capabilities and Adaptive Change

Author: Margaret Peteraf, Professor of Strategic Management and Organization, Tuck School of Business at Darmouth, 100 Tuck Hall, Hanover, NH, 03755, United States, Margaret.A.Peteraf@Dartmouth.EDU

Coauthor(s): Randal Reed

Year: 2007

Abstract: This paper explores the question of how the differential exercise of managerial choice can facilitate organizational adaptation and improve efficiency over periods of regulatory change. This is an adaptive mechanism that helps firms adjust to environmental change or maneuver over a rugged landscape. We view this as a type of dynamic managerial capability for achieving dynamic fit under changing conditions.
