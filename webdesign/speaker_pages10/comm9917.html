Title: When Practices Diffuse in a Bubble: Reverse Mergers and the Internet Wave

Author: Peggy Lee, WP Carey - ASU, 300 E. Lemon St., Tempe, 85287, United States of America, Peggy.Lee@asu.edu

Coauthor(s): Ivana Naumovska, Edward Zajac

Year: 2012

Abstract: We theoretically and empirically examine the diffusion process in a bubble context. In doing so, we synthesize information-based theories of imitation with institutional theory to explain the growth and decline of reverse mergers (RM) of internet firms during the time of the internet wave (1998-2000). Our results show a clear two-stage pattern, with early RMs done by established firms, and late RMs done by fledgling firms. We also find evidence of an early mover advantage.
