Title: Which Strategy to Adopt in Boom and Bust? A DEA Analysis on Software Companies: 1995-2004

Author: Shanling Li, Associate Professor, McGill University, 1001 Sherbrooke Street West, Montreal, PQ, H3G 1A5, Canada, shanling.li@mcgill.ca

Coauthor(s): Sandra Slaughter, Jennifer Shang

Year: 2006

Abstract: For software companies, the correct choice, implementation and evolution of a business strategy can provide considerable competitive advantages. This research sets out to discover exactly how different business strategies influenced the three capabilities: marketing, operations and R&D, in economic boom and bust. Both DEA window analysis and stochastic efficiency frontier approaches are used and compared. We derive managerial insights and discuss strategic implications.
