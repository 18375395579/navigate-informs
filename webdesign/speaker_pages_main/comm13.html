Title: Optimization of Prostate Cancer Screening Decisions

Author: Jingyu Zhang, North Carolina State University, Graduate Program in Operations Research, Campus Box 7913, Raleigh, NC, 27695-7913, United States of America, jzhang2@ncsu.edu

Coauthor(s): Hari Balasubramanian, Nilay Shah, Brant Inman, Brian Denton

Year: 2008

Abstract: Prostate Specific Antigen (PSA) testing is widely used in screening for prostate cancer, although the cost and quality of life benefit remains uncertain. In our talk, we propose a Markov Decision Process model to determine the optimal PSA threshold at which to refer a patient for biopsy. We describe structural properties of the problem and present numerical results based on real data from a large longitudinal data set.
