Title: Information Asymmetry in Direction and Volatility: Price Process and Transaction Level Analysis

Author: Joon-Hui Yoon, University of Florida, 303 Weil Hall, P.O. Box 116595, Gainesville, FL, 32611-6595, United States of America, jhfoever@gmail.com

Coauthor(s): Farid AitSahlia

Year: 2008

Abstract: Asymmetric information in equity and option markets is one reason why the Black-Scholes assumption of redundant options is violated. We develop a Nash-equilibrium strategy to be adopted by a market maker to successfully set bid-ask spreads of equity and option prices in order to make the informed trader refrain from taking advantage of private information and encourage the uninformed liquidity trader to trade.
