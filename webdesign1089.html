" 1 - A Dynamic Infrastructure Performance Model for Evaluating Maintenance Effectiveness and Poolability" Chih-Yuan Chu, Student, Northwestern University, 2145 Sheridan Road, Evanston, IL, 60208, United States, jameschu@northwestern.edu" Pablo L. Durango-Cohen" 2006"The paper uses state-space specifications of time series and develops dynamic, stochastic, multivariate infrastructure performance models that capture the effectiveness of maintenance activities, exogenous factors and uncertainty. A fully and a partially aggregated specifications are proposed. Their predictive power and poolability of data are evaluated by a benchmark model."
