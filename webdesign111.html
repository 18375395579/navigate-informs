" 2 - Teaching the Use of Mixed Integer Programming Models and Algorithms" Andrew Miller, University of Wisconsin-Madison, 3156 Engineering Centers Building, 1550 Engineering Drive, Madison, WI, United States of America, amiller@engr.wisc.edu" 2007"I will discuss my use of Xpress-MP to teach MIP in courses varying from required undergraduate to Ph.D. elective. Xpress has features that make it useful for teaching beginners, including 1) the modeling language Mosel, which makes it easy for beginners to convert algebraic formulations into code, and 2) graphical displays, which help explain the basics of branch-and-bound and -cut. While Xpress is the software I use, any package that provides these facilities could be used in the same way."
