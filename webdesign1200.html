" 2 - Bayesian Analysis of Linear Models with Related Predictors" James Delaney, Visiting Assistant Professor, Department of Statistics, Carnegie Mellon University, Baker/Porter Hall 132B, Pittsburgh, PA, 15213-3890, United States, jdelaney@cmu.edu" Roshan Vengazhiyil" 2007"In fitting linear models often interaction and polynomial terms of the original predictors are used. This can substantially increase the total number of predictors and can cause problems with frequentist estimation. A Bayesian approach can overcome these. Because the predictors are related, specifying a prior for the parameters in the linear model is not easy. In this work, we propose a simple approach to prior specification. The advantage of the approach is illustrated with real examples."
