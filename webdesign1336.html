" 3 - Bayesian Sequential Change Detection and Identification" Savas Dayanik, Assistant Professor, Princeton University, Engineering Quadrangle, Princeton, NJ, 08544, United States, sdayanik@Princeton.EDU" Christian Goulding" 2007"The problem of detection and identification of an unobservable change in the distribution of a random sequence is formulated via a hidden Markov model approach. The formulation is Bayesian, on-line, discrete-time, allowing multiple disorders with dependent observations, statistical dependencies between the change-time and change-type, and general discrete-time disorder distributions. Optimality results and numerical examples will be given."
