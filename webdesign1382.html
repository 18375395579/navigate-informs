" 3 - Various Atypical Heterogeneous Structures for SIR Models" Benjamin Morin, Postdoctoral Researcher, Arizona State University, School of Life Sciences, Box 874501, Tempe, AZ, 85287-4501, United States of America, brmorin@asu.edu" Charles Perrings" Eli Fenichel" Carlos Castillo-Chavez" 2013"SIR models that account for distributed susceptibility, adaptive contact/mixing rates, and affinity are studied. We highlight the use of a variety of techniques in the modeling heterogeneities within an SIR context. Model outcomes are evaluated under random mixing and alternative forms of heterogeneity: qualitative equivalence, relative disease severity, and tractability."
