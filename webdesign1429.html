" 1 - Biomechanics Data Mining to Support Human Performance and Injury Prevention" Kaizhi Tang, Research Scientist, Intelligent Automation, Inc., 15400 Calhoun Dr., Suite 400, Rockville, MD, 20850, United States of America, ktang@i-a-i.com" Atul Phadke" 2007"Biomechanics with its experimental, simulation and domain rules provides an excellent arena for data mining applications. The unique characteristics of biomechanics datasets including isolated feature space and multiple dependent variables call for innovative data mining solutions. This paper presents the research efforts of Intelligent Automation Inc. on biomechanics data mining, especially summarizes how the unique characteristic and requirements in biomechanics data mining are handled."
