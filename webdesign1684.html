" 1 - Models for Reallocation of Hospital Capacity and Ambulances for Efficient Disaster Mitigation" Jomon Aliyas Paul, Assistant Professor, Kennesaw State University, Coles College of Business, 1000 Chastain Road, Kennesaw, GA, 30144, United States of America, jpaul17@kennesaw.edu" Arun Jotshi" Kedar Sambhoos" 2008"Optimal allocation of hospital capacities and ambulances has profound impact on disaster mitigation and hence a problem of considerable importance. Congestion on roads is a function of disaster type and is a major deterrent for ambulance routing. We model congestion using stochastic simulation, which along with casualty clusters are used to reallocate hospital capacities via an optimization model. This is used for ambulance scheduling and routing. Results are demonstrated via case-studies."
