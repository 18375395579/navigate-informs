" 4 - The Value of Stochastic Modeling in Two-stage Stochastic Programs" Erick Delage, Assistant Professor, HEC Montréal, 3000 ch. de la Côte-Sainte-Catherine, Montreal, Qc, H3T2A7, Canada, erick.delage@hec.ca" Sharon Arroyo" Yinyu Ye" 2013"Decisions often need to be made with incomplete knowledge about some parameters of the problem. While formulating a stochastic model that captures accurately this knowledge can be a costly task, solving a stochastic program simply based on historical data can provide useful guidance. Unfortunately, one might worry of being misled by such a simplistic view. In this work, we propose tractable methods for bounding the improvements that could be achieved with a more accurate/expensive model."
