" 3 - A Probabilistically Constrained Model for Wildfire Initial Response Planning" Julian Gallego, Texas A&M University, Austin, TX, United States of America, kamizama77@tamu.edu" Lewis Ntaimo" Curt Stripling" 2013"We present a probabilistically constrained stochastic integer program model for wildfire initial response planning that incorporates both wildfire risk and the decision-maker’s risk averseness. The model uses wildfire risk indices based on the Texas Wildfire Risk Assessment system. We report on the application of the model to one of the Texas A&M Forest Service fire planning units in East Texas."
