" 2 - Resources for Results: Funding Social Development Projects through Market Mechanisms" Sripad Devalkar, Indian School of Business, Ac3 L1 Room 3114, Gachibowli, Hyderabad, 500032, India, sripad_devalkar@isb.edu" Milind Sohoni" 2013"Traditionally, a NGO raises funds from donors before implementing the project. This exposes the donors to a high level of uncertainty, resulting in challenges for the NGO to raise funds. In contrast, some NGOs have moved towards outcome based funding where a NGO implements the project in phases, using its own corpus to implement the initial phases, and securitizes the results from these phases to raise future funds. We examine the challenges of using a results based funding model for an NGO."
