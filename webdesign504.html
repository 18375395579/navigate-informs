" 3 - Robust Solutions to Human Adversaries in Security Domains" James Pita, University of Southern California, PHE 101, 3737 Watt Way, Los Angeles, CA, 90089, United States of America, jpita@usc.edu" Milind Tambe" Richard John" 2012"Recently, game theory has been shown to be useful for reasoning in security settings about how to optimally allocate limited security resources to best defend a set of critical assets. In real-world scenarios the adversaries considered are human adversaries who may not behave according to the standard assumptions of game-theoretic models. We investigate a number of robust approaches and human decision-making models to better address human decision-making in such critical security settings."
