" 1 - The Effect of Budgetary Restrictions on Breast Cancer Diagnostic Decisions" Mehmet Ayvaci, Assistant Professor, The University of Texas at Dallas, Jindal School of Management, 800 West Campbell Road, SM33, Richardson, TX, 75080-3021, United States of America, mehmet.ayvaci@utdallas.edu" Elizabeth Burnside" Oguzhan Alagoz" 2012"We develop a finite-horizon constrained Markov Decision Process to model diagnostic decisions after mammography where we maximize the total expected quality adjusted life years (QALYs) of a patient under resource constraints. Comparing to actual clinical practice, using optimal thresholds may result in 22% cost savings without sacrificing QALYs. Our modeling framework could be used for evaluating cost-effectiveness of diagnostic procedures."
