" 3 - Generalizing Sequential Sampling Experiments" Dashi Singham, Naval Postgraduate School, 1411 Cunningham Road, Monterey, CA, United States of America, dsingham@nps.edu" 2012"The sampling decision is crucial to the success of any simulation experiment. More samples imply better confidence and precision in the results, but require higher costs. A sequential confidence interval procedure can be used to determine when to stop collecting samples. We present a general framework for analyzing sequential rules and assessing their quality. Solutions for managing the tradeoff between improved coverage and replications costs are provided."
